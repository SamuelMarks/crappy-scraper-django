news briefs

The Federal Government will spend almost $1.1 billion in the next four years to promote business-based research, development and innovation as part of Prime Minister Malcolm Turnbull's Innovation Statement, unveiled in Canberra today. A key focus of the plan revolves around strengthening ties between the business community, universities and scientific institutions, including: a $200 million innovation fund which will co-invest in businesses that develop technology from the CSIRO and Australian universities; funds to help students in years 5 and 7 learn coding; early stage investors in start-up businesses to get a 20% non-refundable tax offset and a capital gains tax exemption; those investing in new early stage venture capital partnerships to get a 10% offset.
The Victorian Government has pledged $18 million towards the Regional Rail Connectivity Project to fix mobile blackspots along the rail lines. The Department of Economic Development, Jobs, Transport and Resources will commence a procurement process this month asking for mobile carrier partners to co-invest. It is anticipated that contracts will be awarded and construction of the mobile towers will commence in mid-2016.
The second annual edition of the Australian Industry Report produced by the Office of the Chief Economist has been released by the Department of Industry, Innovation and Science.
The National Broadband Network 's (NBN) Cyber Security Operations Centre is to be established in Melbourne. The Victorian Government has boasted that that the NBN is to create 700 new high-skill jobs in Melbourne over the next four years, with 400 over the first two years and 300 more thereafter to be generated by the project in construction, maintenance and technical support roles.
The Government is inviting submissions from entrepreneurs, academics, scientists, communities and individuals for its Pacific Humanitarian Challenge, which looks at preparing and responding to natural disasters in the Pacific. Submissions are open until Sunday 7 February 2016, with the winning submission to be announced and awarded up to $2 million at the World Humanitarian Summit in Istanbul in May 2016.
A report from the Creative Industries Taskforce has been delivered to the Victorian Government . The report, which draws on contributions from almost 10,000 members of Victoria's cultural and creative industries and the broad public, outlines 42 initiatives for Government consideration that the Taskforce believes would grow Victoria's $23 billion creative and cultural economy.
The Digital by default - which is one of three initiatives announced in the 2015-16 Budget as part of ' Reducing red tape measure - reforms to the Australian Taxation Office ' is now open for community consultation until Friday 15 January 2016.
Australian startup Kayban has developed the world's first anti-microbial healthcare range made from organic flaxseed oil following a collaboration with CSIRO .
NSW tenants can now electronically pay their rental bond straight to the State Government , bypassing real estate agents, landlords and paper-based processes. The service will be accessible on a mobile device and will also give tenants a view of their rental history online, for use with future rental applications.
ZipTel Limited (ASX: ZIP) has signed a binding commercial agreement with SpeedCast (ASX: SDA) to white label the Zipt application for an initial period of two years. The agreement will commence upon SDA granting a certificate of acceptance of the Zipt white label product in Q1 2016.

company watch

News Corp (ASX: NWS) has acquired a 25% stake in tradespeople marketplace, hipages . Founded in 2004 by David Vitek and Roby Sharon-Zipser, hipages directly employs 200 people. hipages which is headquartered in Sydney, previously raised $6 million in May 2014 from Right Click Capital, Ellerston Capital and Australian Ethical Investment, and a further $6 million in June 2015 from existing investors, along with Kestrel Capital.
Fairfax Media has taken a 25% stake in social network, Nabo joining other major shareholders including Seven West Media and Westpac backed Reinventure Group.
UK and Australian-based digital creative agency, The Project Factory (TPF), has been acquired by Way To Blue in an all cash deal. TPF Director, Jennifer Wilson, will oversee both the UK and Australian operations for TPF and the company will retain its branding and identity. Guy Gadney and Annette Parry, founding Directors of TPF, will be stepping down as Directors to pursue new ventures.
Brisbane based business telecommunications provider Over the Wire (ASX: OTW) has begun trading on the Australian Securities Exchange following a capital raising of $10 million.
Australian online platform LawAdvisor has secured a $230,000 investment from Startup Grind. As part of the partnership, Chris Joannou will become LawAdvisor's startup ambassador.
Code Club Australia has received $1 million from the Federal Government and the Telstra Foundation to help the organisation reach more schools in regional areas.
Tech23 2015 alumni Locomote has announced an increased investment from key-stakeholder and now majority shareholder, Travelport (NYSE: TVPT). Travelport has increased its stake in Locomote to 55% and has appointed CEO Sandra McLeod.
Cirrus Networks Holding Limited (ASX: CNW) has acquired Amcom L7 Solutions for a purchase price of $500,000 cash from Vocus Communications (ASX: VOC).
New Zealand Commerce Commission has approved the merger between Vocus Communications (ASX: VOC) and M2 Group (ASX: MTU).

people watch

President of the Business Council of Australia, Catherine Livingstone AO, will be the next Chancellor of the University of Technology Sydney . She is the current Chair of the Telstra Board. She was the Chief Executive of Cochlear Limited from 1994 to 2000 and Chair of CSIRO from 2001 to 2006.
