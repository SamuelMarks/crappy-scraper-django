news briefs

The Australian Research Council is to fund the $35 million Centre of Excellence in Exciton Science based at the University of Melbourne, which will develop next-generation energy and security technologies by manipulating light in unique ways. Other organisations involved in the Centre include RMIT University, Monash University, The University of Sydney, The University of New South Wales, CSIRO, Defence Science Technology Group, Reserve Bank of Australia, Chongqing Institute of Green and Intelligent Technology - Chinese Academy of Sciences, University of Ulm, Huazhong University of Science and Technology, University of California, Berkeley, University of Bayreuth and Shandong University, China.
Commonwealth Bank of Australia (ASX:CBA) and Barclays (LON: BARC) have joined forces to enable their customers to transfer money between Australia and the UK with just a mobile number.
The Federal Department of Industry, Innovation and Science has engaged Fujitsu Australia to deliver an end-to-end grants management solution for its Business Grants Hub. The solution will reduce time between a grant application and awarding and provide a clearer view of grant statuses, results and outcomes.
CSIRO has launched a Centre for Hybrid Energy Systems to research renewable and hybrid energy technologies. Hybrid systems deliver overall cost and efficiency benefits by combining two or more forms of energy generation compared with single-source systems.
Sydney now has an Internet of Things community network in the Industrial and Scientific Spectrum at 915MHz, allowing anyone within a 3-5km radius to connect their IoT device for free, in order to prototype, test, or develop solutions. The gateway is a partnership between KPMG, Meshed, Internet of Things Alliance Australia and International Towers Barangaroo.
Syntonic Limited (ASX:SYT) is partnering with Verizon Wireless to enable Syntonic's cross-carrier application, Freeway by Syntonic on Verizon's FreeBee Perks platform.
The Federal Government has established a $500 million Biomedical Translation Fund , committing $250 million over two years with the remaining $250 million to be matched by private investment. The Fund will invest in promising biomedical innovations with commercialisation potential.
The Australian Renewable Energy Agency has announced a multi-million dollar large-scale solar round, with twelve new large-scale solar photovoltaic (PV) plants set to be constructed across Australia.
More precise weather information for farm businesses and rural communities across the agricultural regions of WA will soon be available thanks to a Doppler weather radar to be launched in October 2016.
Tianqi Lithium Australia has got the green light to build a new $400 million lithium processing plant in Kwinana, WA.
The ACT Government has established a new partnership with local industry and research experts and ANU spin-out company Seeing Machines , to deliver research into driver assistance car technologies.
A new energy storage research program at ANU will receive up to $5 million from a fund established as part of the ACT Government’s reverse wind auctions.
After a successful 12 week trial, the VIC Government has deployed drones to tackle tasks such as koala tracking, monitoring of planned burns and assisting with the broader identification of hazardous trees.
Deakin University has partnered with RCR to develop a robotics-driven target system for the armed forces to train with to improve accuracy. The technology, dubbed the OzBot Raider, will be presented to the Australian Army next month. The joint venture with RCR will enable the system to be commercialised globally by allied forces.
Linfox staff will have access to Monash University’s virtual reality tools and advanced analytics under a new strategic partnership which will drive innovation across the supply chain network.
StartupAUS has made recommendations on supporting the AgTech sector in Australia in a recently released report , including developing a marketplace for AgTech products, establishing a joint R&D fund, and establishing a network of AgTech hubs. See all the recommendations here .

company watch

Mentorloop has closed a $300K funding round from a range of private investors. The funds will be used to further the startup’s product development and hire an in-house developer.
Brisbane-based company SurgicalPerformance received $100K of Ignite Ideas funding from the QLD Government to further develop its surgical audit software.

people watch

Martin Hosking, the founder of Redbubble has won the VIC Pearcey Entrepreneur Award . Hosking is to be an industry leader at Tech23 2016.
Global non-profit organisation, Girls In Tech launched its first Australian chapter this week in Melbourne headed up by Susan Brown, Head of Strategy and Consulting at Odecee and Meegan Jia-Good, founder of event design company The Good Event Company and an advisory board comprising representatives from digital companies including REA Group, Slack, Data Creative, Milk It Academy, Launchcode PR, Blue Chilli, Brolly, Weave Web Communications and General Assembly.
