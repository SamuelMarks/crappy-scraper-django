news briefs

West Perth-based Optika Solutions was awarded the 2016 National Innovation of the Year iAward for their data analytics platform, Akumen. Launched in 2014, Akumen is a cloud-based simulation and data modelling tool that operates on a whole of industry scale. A full list of iAwards winners can be found here .
Australian micropayment startup Tapview will be launching on two publications of Fairfax Media today. This partnership will allow on-site micropayments for individual articles and subscriptions.
A $20 million seed fund for Clean Energy has been launched by Artesian Venture Partners, drawing on finance and skills from the Clean Energy Finance Corporation and the Australian Renewable Energy Agency.
A $500 million Biomedical Translation Fund has been launched by the Australian Private Equity and Venture Capital Association . The fund will be seeded with $250 million from the government's Medical Research Future Fund with matched funding from the private sector.
The Australian Competition and Consumer Commission has outlined concerns about commercial agreements between nbn and Telstra in a report . A number of measures to mitigate unfair advantages have been agreed to by Telstra and nbn, including requiring Telstra to follow nbn priorities on activation and repair work.
Dr James Zhang from Deakin University’s Institute for Intelligent Systems Research and Innovation is the first researcher to join an SME under CSIRO’s SME Connect program. Zhang will help software company YTEK develop advanced training tools for soldiers, surgeons and pilots.
Three projects have received funding under the Victorian Intelligent Transport System Transport Technology Grant Program . Intelematics, developing tech to allow vehicles to pass through consecutive waves of green lights, will receive $765,000. Yarra Trams and Australian Road Research Board (ARRB) will receive $669,000 to trail a signalling system giving trams priority at traffic lights; and ARRB will partner with Connect East and LaTrobe University, receiving $578,000 over 18 months for their project to enable roadside infrastructure such as traffic lights and electronic speed signs to communicate with vehicles.
Deakin University will be the first Australian university to use Inspark courses, powered by Tech23 Alumni Smart Sparrow , in term 3 this year to deliver adaptive digital learning experiences. The partnership will enable Deakin students to receive interactive lessons tailored specifically to their abilities. Educators will be able to see built-in course analytics.
An on-road trial of a fully electric driverless shuttle bus has commenced on Perth roads. The shuttle bus will follow a 25 minute trial route over three weeks initially between Sir James Mitchell Park and the Old Mill in South Perth. Members of the public can register to ride the shuttle bus during the trial.
Ride-booking services are now legal in Queensland, under changes to personalised transport regulation rolled out across the state today.
Queensland’s Disaster Management Portal has been launched. The portal was developed by the Disaster Management Branch of Queensland Fire and Emergency Services, with funding provided by the Natural Disaster Resilience Program through the Department of Infrastructure, Local Government and Planning.
Subsidised smart battery storage will be available to Canberra homes and businesses with the ACT Government awarding $2 million in grant funding for eight companies to roll-out the technology.
Following an investigation by the Australian Competition and Consumer Commission, Expedia and Booking.com have both reached an agreement to amend price and availability parity clauses in their contracts with Australian hotels and accommodation providers.
The Improvement Foundation has joined forces with Telstra Health to release Elicio , a system which directly collects and aggregates real time data from general practices, hospitals, pharmacies, radiology and aged care software systems, providing clinicians with a view of their patients’ health journey beyond their own health service.
Communications Alliance and the Australian Mobile Telecommunications Association have proposed the creation of an industry guideline on making representations to consumers about the performance of broadband services, as the ACCC releases an issues paper on the communications sector and commences an inquiry into mobile roaming services.

company watch

Tech23 alumni Navisens has raised a $2.6 million seed round for its software-only location platform led by Resolute Ventures, with KEC Ventures, Bob Zipp from Amicus Capital, Dave Pell from Arba Seed Investment Group and angel investor Gokul Rajaram participating.
Nine Entertainment Co has made an offer to shareholders of CarAdvice.com, intending to hold a majority stake in CarAdvice and incorporating a path to 100% ownership within three years.
GBST (ASX:GBT) has secured a five-year contract to support a South-African owned retail investment business with its GBST Composer wealth management platform.
Empired's wholly owned New Zealand subsidiary, Intergen Limited is to provide 'Cohesion' Enterprise Content Management Service to the Department of Internal Affairs in New Zealand.

people watch

Former assistant minister for innovation Wyatt Roy has joined fintech fund H2Ocean as an independent director. He joins high-profile presenter David Koch and Beyond Bank chairperson Anne O’Donnell on the board.
LaunchVic has secured Kate Cornick as its CEO. Cornick was formerly Managing Director of Rision Ltd (ASX: RNL) and is well known in the industry from previous roles at University of Melbourne and as an advisor to Senator Conroy. She will also continue to serve on the Victorian Government’s Innovation Expert Panel.
Angela Clark has announced she is leaving the Australian Broadcasting Corporation, where she headed up the Innovation and Digital Network divisions. Clark is credited with the development and growth of the ABC’s iView service, ABC Kids and the ABC News website. She intends to continue her career in the commercial sector.
Sprooki has grown its board, appointing Bob McKinnon as Chairman and Jack Hanrahan as Non-Executive Director. McKinnon has held senior roles at Commonwealth Bank, Westpac and Lend Lease; Hanrahan was formerly General Manager of Vodafone Australia.
