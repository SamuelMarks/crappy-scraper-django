news briefs

AgriWebb - who is making farming smarter - has won the Tech23 2016 People’s Choice Award and the Data61 Digital Disruption Award, a cash prize of $5k. ResApp Health , whose innovation helps you diagnose a cough from your mobile phone, won the Innovation Excellence Award, while Plutora - whose DevOps innovation is growing at the rate of knots - nabbed the award for Greatest Potential.
T he University of Sydney is to provide a further $1 million in funding to INCUBATE which will see it increase its support to over 30 startups per year by 2018 (up from 16 currently). INCUBATE, which is run by James Alexander, will also be given a new co-working space for the expanded program in the Wentworth Building on City Road.
Congratulations to three companies within the ATP Innovations portfolio who were awarded $7.88 million from the $8.63 million NSW Department of Health Medical Devices Fund - Tech23 2015 Innovation Excellence Award recipient Nano-X Pty Ltd received $2.5 million for their smarter and smaller radiotherapy machine, while Tech23 2015 Award winner Respiratory Innovations received $1.3 million for Breathe Well , a device which provides breast cancer patients with breath hold instructions to reduce the risk of heart damage from radiotherapy.
Under a new agreement, Collaborate Corporation's (ASX: CL8) DriveMyCar business unit will promote the Subaru brand. The Levorg wagon will be available through a 'try before you buy program', through which the vehicle will be made available for rideshare use as part of DriveMyCar's agreement with Uber.
The Government will create a new Digital Transformation Agency (DTA) along with a Digital Transformation Advisory Board. The new agency will be responsible for ICT policy and ICT procurement functions, currently managed by the Department of Finance, and will "build on the success of the Digital Transformation Office". Nerida O'Loughlin is the new interim CEO for the DTA, while Paul Shetler is new Chief Digital Officer. The board will be chaired by Dr Martin Parkinson, Secretary of the Department of Prime Minister and Cabinet. The AIIA will act as the ICT industry representative.
The ACCC has released a draft decision to continue to regulate the wholesale ADSL service for another five years.
Dubber (ASX: DUB) and Simetric Telecom have signed an agreement to deploy a scalable mobile call recording solution to Simetric's UC customers and as part of its wholesale mobile platform which services UK telcos.
OBJ Limited (ASX: OBJ) has been granted two new US patents pertaining to its magnetic microarray technology for use in skincare and oral healthcare.
Imagine IM has signed a Memorandum of Understanding with Duromer Australia .
Commonwealth Bank is trialling virtual reality learning as part of its Start Smart program for Australian primary school students. The two-month pilot will use virtual reality technology and a storybook to 'reinforce financial education lessons'. Around 1500 Year 1 or Year 2 students from 24 primary schools will participate.
A smartphone app for surgeons has received $100,000 funding from the Advance Queensland Ignite Ideas Fund . SurgicalPerformance's app, the ' Surgeon Score Card ', is an extension of an existing online tool which allows surgeons to inform patients about surgical risks ahead of surgery and improve patient outcomes.
An agreement between GS1 Australia and Brandbank will enable the provision of a digital content creation and management service for Brandbank.
DigitalX Limited (ASX: DCC) has signed an agreement with AT&T Comunicaciones Digitales (AT&T Mexico). US consumers will be able to use DigitalX's AirPocket , a mobile bill pay and remittance product, for direct funds transfer.
Twitter will live stream the 2016 Melbourne Cup - the first Twitter live streaming deal outside of the US.
nbn has signed two four-year Design and Construction Master Agreements for new developments and fibre links. Existing delivery partners Fulton Hogan and Visionstream were announced after a competitive tender process.
expensemanager Software now integrates directly to MYOB Advanced.
The Australian Cyber Security Centre (ACSC) has released the ACSC Threat Report 2016 .
Optus will upgrade phone coverage at Toowoomba's Brisbane West Wellcamp Airport and will invest in 10 new mobile phone towers across greater Toowoomba over the next 18 months.
Swift Networks Group Limited (ASX: SW1) will deploy its hospitality entertainment solution to Seashells Hospitality Group.
Micro-donations platform provider Activistic Limited (ASX: ACU) will create a fundraising app for US Congressman Joe Garcia's 2016 Congressional campaign.
Two WA hospitals are trialling new video call services . Princess Margaret Hospital for Children and Royal Perth Hospital will use the new metro video calling system, and patients will be able to request future video call appointments after the trial.
Yalumba Family Vignerons is installing a 1.4 MW array of solar panels in Angaston, South Australia - the largest solar photovoltaic system at an Australian winery.
South Australia will invest $10 million in testing, research and development of connected and autonomous vehicle technologies . Grant applications will open on 14 November.

company watch

Singaporean based Australian entrepreneur David Sanderson is the founder of data analytics company Nugit , which has announced a funding round of US$5.2 million from Sequoia Capital India.
H2Ocean Limited has withdrawn its IPO from the market after not meeting the minimum subscription requirement.
Brisbane-based SimPRO has received a $40 million investment from New York growth equity firm Level Equity, who will become a minority shareholder.
IoT startup SpaceConnect has raised $400,000 from Blue Chilli .
Sydney-based Hyper Anna has signed up Westpac and IAG as they receive a $1.25 million investment from Reinventure and AirTree Ventures .
NZ-headquartered Crimson Consulting , which helps students navigate world admissions to universities, has raised $39.5 million from Tiger Global.

people watch

Following "a difference of opinion" on the future leadership of Vocus (ASX: VOC), Executive Director and Vocus founder, James Spenceley and Non-Executive Director and Amcom founder Tony Grist have resigned from the company's board. Grist and Spenceley had supported a change to the CEO, Geoff Horth, in early 2017 and a formal review of board leadership over the course of the year, but the proposal was not supported. Vocus Chairman David Spence expressed disappointment to see the departing Directors leave the board, but emphasised the importance of moving "forward with a fully cohesive Board and executive team that can, and will, provide the necessary unity and leadership required to steer the company through an important point in its history.” Vocus is on the hunt for replacement directors and a new Chief Financial Officer.
Sidekicker has appointed Sam McConkey as Chief Operating Officer.
