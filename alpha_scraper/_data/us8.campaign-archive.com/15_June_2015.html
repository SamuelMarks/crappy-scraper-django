company watch

Amcom (ASX: AMM) and Vocus Communications (ASX: VOC) have merged. The $12 billion entity was approved by a record show of shareholders who voted for the merger to go ahead, despite TPG ’s (ASX: TPM) attempt to thwart the deal.
Melbourne IT (ASX: MLB) has acquired a 50.2% stake in mobile app developer Outware Systems for $21.7 million with the option to acquire the remaining stock. Outware Mobile has 115 employees in Melbourne and Sydney. Danny Gorog, Eytan Lenko and Gideon Kowadlo who founded the business in 2009 will remain with the business in their current roles. In the FY 2015, Outware Mobile is forecast to generate revenues of $15.6m and EBITDA of $5.1m. Total transaction consideration (assuming Melbourne IT moves towards 100% ownership) is expected to be approximately $67 million, implying a forward EBITDA multiple of 8.6 times; tranches 2 and 3 depending on the financial performance.
Booking Boss has raised $2 million in a Series A funding round led by investors Michael and Daniel Dempsey, VC firm Acequia Capital and Scott Blume; former CEO of Wotif . Booking Boss was founded by Renee and Andy Welsh.
Wynyard Group Limited (NZX: WYN) has raised NZ$40 million through a share placement from institutional investors in Australia, New Zealand and Asia. The Wynard Group’s crime analytics software was born out of commercialisation deals done with the Australian Federal Police (to develop their investigative case management suite) and the New Zealand Police (digital forensic solutions). The company listed on the New Zealand stock exchange in 2013.
Mobile Embrace (ASX: MBE) has made a strategic investment in Tech23 2014 company Clipp , for a cash and script investment valued at $4.69 million. Under the deal, MBE has acquired a 31% stake in Clipp, with the option of a 72% ownership.
Moula has raised $30 million in a funding round led by Liberty Financial , NCN Investments and a group of private investors. Moula was founded by Aris Allegos, Andrew Watt and Piers Moller and offers loans to small to medium sized businesses.
iiNet (ASX: IIN) has set 27 July 2015 as the date for its shareholders to vote on the proposed acquisition of TPG (ASX: TPM). The Australian Competition and Consumer Commission is inviting submissions from interested parties on the acquisition with a final decision to be made on 20 August 2015.

news briefs

Buying off-the-plan apartments will be made easier through 3D experiences made possible by Scann3d ’s technology. Realestate.com.au has partnered with the Melbourne startup, Scann3d and property developer BPM to launch self-guided 3D walk-through for users to explore developments before they exist in the real world.
The Department of Communications is undertaking a review of the Australian Communications and Media Authority . The terms of reference for the review are available here .
Nitro has partnered with Google Drive , OneDrive and DropBox , post a US$15 million funding round in November 2014 to launch its new software Nitro Pro 10.
Woolworths is launching Woolworths Connect, a new telecommunications service offering two pre-paid mobile options utilising parts of Telstra 's 3G network. Woolworths Head of Telco is Jason Hair.
The Victorian Auditor General has handed down his report "Operational Effectiveness of the myki Ticketing System". In 2005, the state committed almost $1 billion to develop myki by 2007 to replace the ageing Metcard system, and operate myki for 10 years. Public Transport Victoria is currently planning to retender the contract for myki's continued operation which expires in 2016. The time taken to develop and implement myki increased from two years, to in excess of nine years with the budget increasing by around $550 million, or 55%.
Visionstream Australia Pty Ltd has entered into a five year Multi-Technology Integrated Master Agreement with NBN Co which is expected to exceed $270 million in the first year. Under the agreement, Visionstream will construct fibre to the node and fibre to premises across Queensland, New South Wales, Victoria and Tasmania.
The Regional Telecommunications Independent Review Committee , headed by Deena Shiff, has released a paper on Regional Telecommunications Review Issues and is calling for submissions from the public and interested shareholders on key issues affecting people in regional, rural and remote parts of Australia.
The University of Sydney and Dell have partnered to launch their first High Performance Computer service Artemis, which will allow researchers to leverage big data for research and cross-disciplinary collaboration.
NICTA has launched a Knowledge Hub: Transport and Logistics Living Lab, at the Australian Technology Park . It is the fifth Knowledge Hub to receive Government support with an investment of up to $1.7 million over two years.
