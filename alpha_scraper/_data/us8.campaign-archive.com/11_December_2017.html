NewsBriefs

Cicada Innovations held its second annual Australian Nobel Gala spotlighting incubatee success from Speedx, Logica Medical and Tech23 alumnus Flurosat - to name but a few. Bill Ferris, the Chairman of Innovation Australia, also launched the Innovation Experience Program (IXP) supported by the NSW Government, which places second year PhDs into Cicada Innovation's high growth startups for a period of 6 months.
New air monitoring equipment is being trialled for six weeks in the Latrobe Valley in Victoria to provide more information about air quality and improve the Environment Protection Authority’s ability to locate and watch smoke plumes.
New South Wales-based company RingIR will receive $4.6 million in Federal Government funding through the Defence Innovation Hub to develop and demonstrate a counter-improvised explosive device capability which uses highly sensitive laser spectrometry to locate and identify vapours from explosives.
Amazon’s Australian online store has finally opened.
A new e-waste processing centre at Officer in Melbourne’s outer south is expected to process 1000 tonnes of e-waste in its first year with capacity to divert 5000 tonnes from landfill.
Commencing in 2018, a new Cyber Security National Program at the Box Hill Institute in Victoria will partner with industry nationwide to provide on-the-job experience for students and address the national skills shortage in cyber security. The Box Hill Institute received $4.7 million in government funding to enhance its cyber security qualifications.
A new law introduced through the Enhancing Online Safety (Non-consensual Sharing of intimate images) Bill 2017 will empower the Office of the eSafety Commissioner to quickly remove intimate images published without consent and prevent further sharing.
Tech23 alumnus Bigtincan (ASX: BTH) has signed a reseller agreement with Verizon. The US company will resell Bigtincan Hub to its US customer base.
A portable brain scanner for early stroke detection, WoundVue's automated and advanced assessment of chronic wounds, and a high performance energy storage alternative to lithium ion batteries are amongst 15 highly diverse industry-led projects to receive a $33 million investment under the fourth round of the CRC Projects (CRC-P) Program , and will leverage an additional $98 million in cash and in-kind contributions from 97 project partners.
The Australian Small Business Advisory Services program will allocate $18 million in grants over three years to help provide relevant training, expert advice and targeted support to grow small businesses through digital platforms.
ACS and CSIRO's Data61 have signed a Memorandum of Understanding. Under the agreement, ACS and Data61 will provide thought leadership on emerging trends and focus on training Australia's workforce in new areas including data science, automation and AI.
Over 8,700 Optus customers will be compensated by Optus after they were misled about maximum speeds on Optus NBN plans.
A network of speakers will be installed by the Victorian Government at more than 90 key sites in Melbourne’s CBD to provide warnings and key information during emergencies.
Congratulations to Victorian group the International Campaign to Abolish Nuclear Weapons (ICAN), who have been awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in recognition of their efforts which led to 122 nations voting to adopt the United Nations’ Treaty on the Prohibition of Nuclear Weapons this year (Australia is not a participant in the treaty).
The search is on for a Victorian family to live in an energy-smart house with no rent and no power bills for a year. The House with No Bills will be built by Mirvac and will look, feel and operate just like a typical home, with roof insulation, solar PV panels, LED lighting, energy efficient appliances and smart metering and monitoring systems. The house will be built in partnership with the CSIRO’s Evergen team, and will feature CSIRO-developed intelligent energy management, which uses solar and batteries, along with Schneider electric smart home automation.
The Royal Adelaide Hospital will be the first in the country to have a 3D organ printer which promises to revolutionise treatment for people with Type 1 Diabetes. Developed by researchers from the University of Wollongong and set to be manufactured in South Australia, the printer will be capable of making pancreatic islets suitable for transplant.
A pilot program will provide training and accreditation for technology solution providers and consultants connecting businesses to the nbn broadband access network. NBN Co's pilot has commenced with Cisco distributor Westcon Comstor, IT support franchise Computer Troubleshooters Australia, Telstra and Vocus. The full partner program is expected to launch in 2018.
Australia’s first National Cities Performance Framework to track the progress and performance of our largest cities is now published at smart-cities.dashboard.gov.au . Key indicators for performance include job accessibility, housing price to income ratio, access to green space, business creation, employment growth and youth unemployment.
Tank Stream Labs is expanding to Perth as part of a partnership with EY, and will co-locate with EY's Perth branch.
Telstra Wholesale plans to offer a white label network , provisioning and service capability through an agreement with Inabox Group. The network will enable companies with a retail channel to develop new revenue streams by launching their own mass market telecommunications services.
Trials to mitigate coral bleaching on the Great Barrier Reef will use water mixing technology in an effort to reduce the impacts of thermal stress. To be carried out by the Reef and Rainforest Research Centre, the project is supported by $2.2 million in government funding.
A Letter of Intent to establish an Australian-United States Chief Technology Officer Dialogue with the President of the US Council on Competitiveness has been signed at a ceremony at Parliament House. The CTO dialogue intends to ‘give Australian technology and innovation leaders the opportunity to engage with American counterparts , sharing common challenges and opportunities in the innovation and technology space.’ The CTO dialogue will be held in 2018.
Australians are consuming more and more online content and services, according to ACMA's Communications report 2016-17 . Australians downloaded over 3.1 million terabytes in the June 2017 quarter, a 43% increase on the same period last year.
Using machine learning, ANU and CSIRO's Data61 have developed a job-searching machine to help universities prepare graduates for non-academic work and demonstrate the value of PhD graduate skills to industry.
Optus Business will provide telecommunications and managed services to Hastings Deering under a three year whole-of-business ICT contract .
Data#3 has won a competitive tender for outsourced managed services to support PanAust Limited. The five year contract will assist with pre-development and exploration opportunities for copper and gold in Laos, Papua New Guinea and Myanmar.
The Australian Innovation System Report 2017 has been released. Mark Cully, Chief Economist at the Department of Industry, Innovation and Science, emphasises the role of businesses in spearheading innovation-led growth in Australia.
A new multi-million dollar research institute, the Australian Institute for Machine Learning , will be established at the University of Adelaide. The South Australian Government will provide $7.1 million in funding, including $1.5 million prioritised for defence capability research.
ASX will replace CHESS using distributed ledger technology developed by technology partner Digital Asset.

CompanyWatch

York Butter Factory has acquired Melbourne coworking space Teamsquare. It will move the York Butter Factory community to the Teamsquare space on Bourke Street.
Family Zone Cyber Safety (ASX: FZO) has raised $5 million in an oversubscribed placement. Funds will support expansion in the New Zealand market and integration of the Linewize and Family Zone platforms.
Canberra-based Kord Defence will receive $655,000 of government funding through the Defence Innovation Hub to develop an innovative wireless systems control technology to enhance Army capability.
The ACCC will not oppose the proposed merger of Fox Sports and Foxtel. It found the transaction 'would not substantially lessen competition.'
TechMpire (ASX: TMP) - formerly Fortunis Resources Limited, a digital online performance-based marketing group - has announced a capital raising of $2-3 million .
Ookami Limited (ASX: OOK) is to acquire a strategic equity position of 18.23% for $933,240 in blockchain company Brontech . Telstra’s Muru-D, BlueChilli and Artesian Fund are all current shareholders. Emma Poposka, the founder and CEO of Brontech will join the Board of Ookami.
