news briefs

Brisbane City Council and Digital Brisbane will be opening a $5 million innovation start-up hub in Brisbane's CBD as part of the 'New World City Plan'. Announced by the Lord Mayor, Graham Quirk, The Capital will include a collaborative workspace run by Happy Panda Two (by the team at Little Tokyo Two) and Fishburners from Sydney.
The Victorian Government has signed an agreement that will see Oxford University's Global Cyber Security Capacity Centre (GCSCC) establish its first international office in Melbourne. The news collides with Data61, the digital research arm of the CSIRO , moving their lead national cyber security centre to Melbourne. The GCSCC office will be co-located with a new Oceania Cyber Security Centre, being set up with support from the Government and will bring together eight Victorian universities, the Melbourne-based Defence Science Institute and private sector partners.
Uber has been legalised in NSW following approval by the NSW Government . Taxi owners will be compensated for the drop in value of their licenses, which is equivalent to $20,000 for each owner of a taxi plate in NSW.
Congratulations to ASMS Innovators , Cartland Law and Technology for Women's Safety for winning the D3 Digital Challenge ' Keeping Women Safe '. A group of students were awarded $15,000 to develop a prototype of an educational app geared towards young people to be presented on the Tuesday 26 January.
The Digital Transformation Office (DTO) has given a glimpse of what Gov.au could look like with a blog outlining their journey to reserch and design their alpha prototype. In another blog post, the DTO outlines their plans follow the same Discovery/Alpha/Beta/Live process to design, build and deliver a way for suppliers of technology and digital services to be matched with buyers across government with the prototype version of the Digital Marketplace to be available in 2016.
Service Stream Limited (ASX: SSM) has signed a new four year Operations and Maintenance Mast Agreement with the nbn . While Telstra and the nbn have announced they have signed a Memorandum of Understanding to negotiate a significant contract for Telstra to support the nbn network build in areas currently covered by Telstra’s HFC footprint as well as additional work for Telstra under the nbn’s maintenance contracts.
Locals and visitors to Ballarat will now be able to connect to a free public Wi-Fi service across a number of outdoor public spaces as part of the Victorian Government 's five year pilot Wi-Fi Project. The $11 million pilot will be managed and maintained by TPG .

company watch

Flight Centre Travel Group (ASX: FLT) has acquired a 70% stake in BYOjet Group with the option to acquire 100% in 2018. BYOjet.com was launched in Australia in 2010 and expanded into New Zealand in 2012 and Singapore in 2014. The business was founded by Lenny and Manne Padowitz and later partially acquired by Disruptive Investment Group 's (ASX: DVI) Professional Personal Systems (PPS). Two Flight Centre representatives will join the board of PPS. BYOjet Founder Lenny Padowitz will continue to run the business as CEO, be on the Board of PPS and following the deal with his associates will hold 13.6% of the PPS stock. DVI will retain a 16.4% stock holding in PPS. The deal follows Flight Centre making a US$28 million offer to acquire StudentUniverse.com , a US based youth and student travel service.
Bevan Slattery retains 47% of the stock of Megaport (ASX: MP1) after a successful IPO raising over $25 million.
NEXTDC Limited (ASX: NXT) has raised $120 million.
VentureCrowd has raised $2 million and is to partner with Melbourne based Kentgrove Capital to allow investors to invest in Israeli startups through their service.
Auswide Bank has partnered with online marketplace MoneyPlace that will see it take a 20% equity stake in the company and provide $60 million in funding over five years. MoneyPlace will be using the funds to grow its consumer lending service.

people watch

Wayne Gerard, Founder of Queensland based, Tech23 alumni RedEye Apps has joined the board of StartupAUS . Peter Bradd, will finish his six-month tenure as CEO and become StartupAUS Chairman. Incumbent Chairman, Engineering Director for Google Australia and New Zealand, Alan Noble, will remain on the StartupAUS board. Bradd has partnered with founder of the Entourage, Jack Delosa, to establish The Beanstalk Factory. A full-time CEO for StartupAUS is expected to be announced early in 2016.
The Board of the Telecommunications Industry Ombudsman Limited has appointed Judi Jones as its new Ombudsman, succeeding Simon Cohen, who has taken up a senior role with the Victorian Government. Jones will assume responsibilities in March 2016.
The nbn has appointed Peter Ryan as its new Chief Network Engineering Officer effective immediately.
Singapore based Google Executive Michelle Guthrie will become the ABC 's first female Managing Director when she takes over from Mark Scott in May 2016.
Annalie Killian is leaving AMP after nearly 16 years. She is the Founder, Curator and Executive Producer of Amplify, which she has grown over the last ten years. Watch this space to see what Annalie does next!
