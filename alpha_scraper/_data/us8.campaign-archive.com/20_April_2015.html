news briefs

Service NSW has begun building its whole-of-government Payment Services Platform. Azuron , a Sydney based company, has commenced work on the initial stages of the project. The Platform is due to be in service for transactions delivered through Service NSW by the end of the year, and eventually made available as a service to all NSW Government agencies.
SimulationSurvivalGame picked up the Most global impact Award, People's Choice Award and was the first project nominated for global judging at the 2015 SpaceApps Challenge held in Adelaide. Launch Pad Board Game won the Greatest Opportunity for Commercialisation Award and was the second project nominated for global judging.
Our thanks to Lucy Feagins of the Design Files for bringing our attention to the "Real Australians Say Welcome" posters of Peter Drew. You can follow the social media responses via #realaustralianssaywelcome. And you can also buy a poster through Pozible .
Suncorp has partnered with Trov in the Australian market. San Francisco based Trov is an app which enables you to organise, record and value your belongings.
Melbourne IT and 2nd Watch have joined forces to bring Cloud Factory to Australia to help organisations who want to accelerate their journey to the Amazon Web Services (AWS) Cloud. Both Melbourne IT and 2nd Watch are AWS Premier Consulting Partners. 2nd Watch is venture-backed and headquarterd in Seattle, Washington.
Victorian Minister for Small Business, Innovation and Trade Adem Somyurek has signed a memorandum of understanding with Telstra to reduce Mobile Black Spots in Victoria.
Mint Payments (ASX: MNW) has partnered with Rewardle (ASX: RXH) to integrate its mobile payments platform with Rewardle’s business intelligence system.
Tasmania’s Glenorchy City Council is moving to the cloud with the help of TechnologyOne ’s (ASX: TNE) cloud platform, OneCouncil.
Applications for ilab ’s 3 month Germinate Accelerator program close 3 May 2015.
Following The Australian Sports Tech Company becoming an investor in The Australian Sports Technologies Network , Australian sport tech start-ups can express interest in gaining seed funding before 3 May.
David Marquet, author of Turn the Ship Around is to come to Australia for the first time to keynote at Agile Australia in June in Sydney. He’ll be sharing his leadership expertise, drawn from his experience taking the USS Santa Fe submarine from ‘worst to first’.

company watch

Telstra (ASX: TLS) has completed its acquisition of Pacnet Limited . Under the acquisition, Pacnet will slowly be integrated into Telstra and phased out as a brand.
99designs has secured $10 million in Series B funding led by Recruit Strategic Partners . The company plans to use the funds to expand internationally in Japan and other parts of Asia.
Social network startup Gamurs , which received its first grant of $30,000 in January as part of the NRMA Accelerator Program , has raised $350,000 in seed funding led by high net-worth individuals.
Vocus Communications (ASX: VOC) has acquired a 14.5% stake in Macquarie Telecom Group Limited (ASX: MAQ). The strategic investment was acquired under a physically-settled equity swap transaction for a consideration of $15.619 million on settlement of the swap transaction.
NewSat (ASX: NWT) has appointed PPB Advisory and McGrathNicol as Voluntary Administrators and Receivers respectively. The company has also filed for bankruptcy under Chapter 15 of the Bankruptcy Code in the United States.

people watch

Young and Well CRC 's founder and CEO, Associate Professor Jane Burns, is to be on the ABC's Q&A program on Monday 20 April, 2015. The Young and Well CRC unites young people with researchers, practitioners and innovators to explore the role of technology in improving mental health and wellbeing for young people aged 12 to 25.
Robert Nason is set to retire as Telstra ’s (ASX: TLS) Group Executive of Business Support. Nason will remain in the role for several months to provide support and assistance before departing in October.
David Liu has been appointed as Isentia 's (ASX:ISD) Chief Executive of Asia to drive growth across the region. He replaces Cameron Buckley who is returning from Singapore to his home of Melbourne to take on new challenges. Liu was most recently at Engine group network in Asia.
