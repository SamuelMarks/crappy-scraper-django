news briefs

realAs is the winner of the inaugural round of the Westpac Innovation Challenge. realAs has won a $40,000 cash prize and a six-month placement in the BlueChilli accelerator program to further develop its business, helping home buyers and investors find out what price properties will really sell for via a smartphone app.
In response to the Federal Government cuts to the ABC , the ABC will replace its Innovation Division with an ABC Digital Network Division, with the aim of prioritising online and mobile expenditure. Future initiatives announced are to be implemented as savings are realised: upgrading of iview; exploration of new video streaming and transaction-based services; investment in News Digital - creating a mobile-led newsroom in the ACT; and the extension of radio streaming to regional areas and the development of the personalised radio player.
Artesian Venture Partners has signed a five year partnership with KPMG .
Amcom has signed an agreement with the Northern Territory Government to supply additional data services to 141 sites across the Northern Territory.
Mint Wireless (ASX:MNW) has signed an agreement with Community CPS Australia Limited, trading as Beyond Bank Australia to supply payment solutions, including card not present services. Mint will receive a revenue share of all fees, including monthly fees, and transaction fees, processed through the mPOS payment solution.
The Queensland Government has launched a new ICT Renewal Action Plan to reflect current challenges and government priorities to pursue a cloud-first, ICT-as-a-service approach.
Telstra is trialing free Wi-Fi at Bourke Street Mall in Melbourne, Hyde Park in Sydney and King George Square in Brisbane as part of a plan to switch on two million hotspots across Australia over the next five years.
Fire and Rescue New South Wales (FRNSW) has selected IBM to deploy a new ‘as a service’ IT delivery platform to reinforce the delivery of critical services to people in New South Wales. The five year agreement will see IBM design, build and run the new platform and manage the migration of FRNSW workloads including Emergency Services SAP system to the NSW Government Data Centres.
Mike Walsh, of FED night fame (Fourth Estate Domain), is about to release his book The Dictionary Of Dangerous Ideas , outlining 88 scientific breakthroughs, emerging technologies and disruptive business models.
The Australian Communications and Media Authority (ACMA) is set to launch its Australian Internet Security Initiative - an online portal aimed at warning people of malware affecting devices. You can tune in to the free live webcast on Friday 28 November 2014 at 9:30am on the ACMA site.
The Joint Standing Committee on Electoral Matters has released its second interim report on the conduct of the 2013 Federal election. The committee has voted against the electronic voting due to high costs and security risks.
Fairfax Media and the The New York Times have announced that all current subscribers of The Sydney Morning Herald and The Age will receive six months free access to the The New York Times website.
Startup Weekends were held in Melbourne, Perth, Sydney, Gold Coast, Townsville and Cairns. In Melbourne at York Butter Factory , the winners were OnTheGreen and HelloRent, getsoundninja.com was the winner in Sydney.

people watch

Sam Mead has joined Perth accelerator AtomicSky as an Advisor.
AIMIA has appointed Rob Wong as its new Chief Executive Officer. Wong replaces David Holmes who resigned as CEO in August of this year.
Redflow (ASX:RFX) has welcomed Internode Founder and NBN Co Director Simon Hackett as its new Non-Executive Director.

company watch

Aconex Limited is expected to raise a total of approximately $140 million, based on an offer price of $1.90 per share. Of this amount, approximately $50 million will be raised by the issue of new shares by Aconex and approximately $90 million will be raised by the sale of shares by existing shareholders. Aconex co-founders, non-executive directors and executive management will retain a majority of their existing shares on completion of the IPO. Francisco Partners, the company’s largest shareholder with 24% of the total shares on issue, has agreed to sell all of its shares through the IPO. Aconex shares are currently expected to begin trading on the ASX on or around December 9, 2014, under the ticker code ACX.
Bigcommerce has raised $50 million in Series D funding. They plan to use the funds to invest in product development, grow sales and marketing, and expand into new markets. The financing was led by SoftBank Capital , a venture firm affiliated with SoftBank Corp., and included Telstra Ventures and American Express , as well as existing investors General Catalyst and Revolution Growth. Steve Murray, a partner at SoftBank Capital, will join the Bigcommerce board. In October, Bigcommerce announced a new partnership with Alibaba.com to streamline product sourcing for merchants. SoftBank Corp. is a one-third owner of Alibaba.
BigAir (ASX:BGL) has acquired Oriel Technologies for $4.21 million using cash and two additional earn-out payments based on the latter’s performance in FY15 and FY16. The total acquisition has been capped at $15 million.
Mobile Embrace (ASX:MBE) has acquired The Performance Factory for $3.2 million. The consideration also includes up to a further $4 million over two years and is subject to profit before tax targets through 2017.
