NewsBriefs

More than 60 data breaches were reported to the Federal Government’s privacy body in the first six weeks of the new mandatory notification scheme, with the majority caused by human error.
The new Digital Health Cooperative Research Centre will invest over $200 million to develop and test digital health solutions . The centre will bring together R&D programs involving 40 commercial and government organisations operating across the health, aged care and disability sectors, 24 established and startup technology, advisory and investment companies, and 16 Australian universities.
University of Technology Sydney has partnered with SAS and CISCO to launch a new research and teaching facility, the UTS-SAS-CISCO IoT Innovation Lab .
The Bureau of Communications and Arts Research has today released its assessment of the potential impacts of 5G on Australia’s productivity and growth. Key findings include that 5G is likely to have a positive effect on productivity growth across the economy and the benefits from 5G will be the wellbeing of consumers and households rather than just 'dollar values'.
BidEnergy Limited (ASX: BID) will provide end to end energy spend lifecycle management services for the supply of electricity and natural gas to The Salvation Army .
The NSW Government is seeking technology that will detect illegal mobile phone use on our roads.
Toowoomba Regional Council has become the first council in Queensland to join a trial to optimise electricity use through Yurika Virtual Power Plant, part of the Energy Queensland group of companies.
In partnership with Coca-Cola Amatil, BlueChilli has announced the launch of Amatil-X . The Xcelerate six month program will provide $38,000 pre-seed funding plus the potential opportunity to both pilot and scale with Coca-Cola Amatil. Xcelerate is looking for companies who are solving challenges in the arenas of personalisation and data; sustainability, wellbeing and health and logistics. Coca-Cola Amatil have set aside up to an additional $10 million to invest in selected startups who graduate from either the Xcelerate startup or scale-up programs.
Clean TeQ (ASX: CLQ) has signed a Cooperation Framework Agreement with Meili Ecological and Environmental Research and Design Institute (China). The agreement will focus on the promotion and and development of Clean TeQ’s water purification technologies in China through joint research projects.
Following on from the development of the innovation precinct at University of Melbourne , an autonomous mini shuttle bus is now active on campus. The driverless vehicle is the result of a three-year partnership with French company EasyMile. University of Melbourne Vice Chancellor Glyn Davis said the vehicle will provide researchers unprecedented access to autonomous technology, putting students at the forefront of research and development.
UNSW is partnering with Keypath Education with the launch of a suite of new fully online Master’s programs.
UNSW has opened a new Centre of Excellence led by Professor Andy Pitman, which will focus on understanding the processes that trigger extreme weather and climate events .
The NT government will establish a cyber security operations centre to identify and respond to cyber intrusions on agency IT systems in real-time. The centre will be funded as part of a $1.5 million investment to strengthen the territory’s cyber security posture.

PeopleWatch

Snowflake Computing has announced the appointment of Guy Baldwin as Director of Alliances and Channels , Asia Pacific and Japan based in Sydney.
Google Australia has appointed Mark Wheeler as Head of Large Customer Marketing. Wheeler was previously Digital Marketing Director at McDonald’s ANZ.
Twilio has established a presence in Australia appointing former Oracle and Symantec Executive, Richard Watson , as its first regional country director in Australia.
Roots Sustainable Agricultural Technologies Limited (ASX: ROO) has announced that Professor Raoul Bino, Professor Haim Rabinowitch and Professor Uzi Kafkafi will join its newly formed Scientific Advisory Board .
