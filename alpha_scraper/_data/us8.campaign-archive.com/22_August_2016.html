news briefs

Macquarie Park is set to have a brand new Innovation District backed by Macquarie University , Optus, Johnson & Johnson, Abbott, KonicaMinolta, NAB and AMP Capital. The Macquarie Park Innovation District (MPID) will be a collaborative ecosystem where researchers, entrepreneurs and business work together. The District is made up of 180 multinational enterprises, 200 SMEs, Macquarie University and Macquarie University Hospital, and is serviced by three train stations.
Muru Music , Pixeality , Kini Swimwear , Citizen Wolf , and Storyboard Social are the five creative tech startup finalists of QUT’s Creative 3 Pitch. The winner will represent Australia in the global Creative Business Cup in Copenhagen in November.
Congratulations to Tech23 Alumni CleverTar for winning Best Start-Up, Tech Innovation, at the 2016 Talent Unleashed Awards. Other winners included ODOCS for the social impact category and ResApp for Best Tech IPO/ VC raising.
The Australian Competition and Consumer Commission is considering submissions by Commonwealth Bank, Westpac, NAB, and Bendigo and Adelaide Bank on whether to allow them to collectively negotiate and boycott activities with Apple Pay and other third-party digital wallet providers in Australia. Other issues for collective negotiations include appropriate industry standards for digital wallets and the banks’ ability to pass on any fees charged by a third party digital wallet provider.
Meanwhile, Android Pay, Google's version of the digital wallet, will be available in all Service NSW centres.
Children’s Cancer Institute has deployed Yellowfin’s Business Intelligence and analytics software across its data sources to enhance the delivery of research, aimed at improving childhood cancer treatments. Deploying Yellowfin has also enabled researchers to quickly and independently analyse, share and act on a unified view of their operational data, allowing the Institute to improve effectiveness and efficiency throughout the organisation.
In other health news, patients and parents of sick kids at the Royal Children's Hospital can now access their medical records using the My RCH portal , the first electronic medical records system of its kind in Australia. The $48 million project allows parents to update health information, manage hospital appointments, see medications prescribed and instructions for taking them, view test and imaging results and read doctor notes. More than 1300 people have already registered to access the portal.
Ride sharing services like Uber are now legal in Tasmania after the Taxi and Hire Vehicle Amendment Bill 2016 was passed.

grant briefs

Twenty more grants have been awarded under the Entrepreneurs’ Programme with Tech23 Alumni companies Liquid Instruments being awarded $830k and InterfereX receiving $675k. ANU-backed Liquid Instruments won a swag of Awards at Tech23 2015 including People's Choice Award; the Data61 Digital Disruption Award and ATP Innovations – Success Multiplied Award.
The STEM⁺ Business Fellowship program, managed by CSIRO’s SME Connect , will provide $7.9 million worth of grants of up to $105k per annum to help small and medium businesses undertake two to three year projects with early career researchers from Australian research organisations.
Applications for round two funding of grants up to $3 million over three years under the Cooperative Research Centres Programme has been opened to support short term, industry-identified and industry-led collaborative research projects.
74 Australian small and medium businesses and researchers will receive $7k in priming grants under the Government’s $4.9 million Global Connection Fund to support collaborative meetings and projects with overseas partners that address industry needs.
Eligible businesses can apply for grants of up to $100k to develop ideas to meet five challenges under the Business Research and Innovation Initiative (BRII). The five challenges set by different Government agencies include: tracking the effect and value of information products; sharing information nationally to ensure child safety; and digitally enabled community engagement in policy and programme design. Successful ideas may receive follow up grants to develop a prototype or proof of concept.
The NSW Government has set aside $10 million to grow the State's network of incubators and accelerators, with an extra $3 million dedicated to direct grants for start-ups, as part of the Jobs for NSW Fund .

company watch

OpenAgent has raised $12 million in a round led by Westpac’s VC arm, Reinventure . Breakthrough Labs and Singapore-based VC Qualgro also participated in the oversubscribed round. This latest capital raise brings its total to $20 million. OpenAgent will use the funds to build new features into its platform.
Tech23 Alumni RedEye have won a $250k grant from Queensland's Ignite Awards Fund , to employ five more staff.
London-based media group Future plc (LSE: FUTR) has acquired Next Commerce for an undisclosed cash consideration. Next Commerce operates the getprice.com.au and pricepanda.com comparison shopping websites across the Australia and South East Asia markets, earning $3.3 million in FY15/16.

people watch

Former AIMIA CEO John Butterworth has been appointed head of Digital Services at Gruden . He will be responsible for growing Gruden’s customer base.
Ken Sloan has joined Monash University as Deputy Vice Chancellor and Vice President (Enterprise), focussing on creating partnerships with industry and government. He brings experience from his former role as Chief Operating Officer for the University of Warwick and his work with their industry engagement flagship, the Warwick Manufacturing Group - now a global benchmark for successful university and business collaboration.
Certus Solutions has appointed Rachel Wells to the newly created executive role of General Manager, People and Performance. Most recently Wells was Director HR, Strategic Change for Ministry of Social Development.
Xref (ASX:XF1) has appointed Brad Rosser as Non-Executive Chairman and Nigel Heap as Non-Executive Director, as Simon O’Loughlin resigned as Non-Executive Chairman last week.
