NewsBriefs

The Federal Government is investing $151.5 million in four new Cooperative Research Centres (CRCs), including the Food Agility CRC ($50 million) and the iMove CRC ($55 million). The Food Agility CRC, boasting 54 partners, will support Australia's food industry to grow through digital transformation. The bid consortium was led by the Knowledge Economy Institute at the University of Technology Sydney , along with QUT and Curtin University. Dr Anne Astin will be the inaugural Chair. The iMove CRC - led by Excellerate Australia - will explore digital and evolving vehicle technologies to improve traffic flow. iMove has 46 partners and will work in consultation with stakeholders including the Australian Logistics Council and the National Transport Commission.
The landslide election win in Western Australia has given Carnegie Clean Energy (ASX:CCE) a skip in its step with the confirmation of the $19.5 million funding for the first commercial wave farm in Australia, the Albany Wave Energy project.
UK urban regeneration developer The Berkeley Group is using Aconex (ASX: ACX) to manage the construction of three new schemes comprising the Albert Embankment Plaza.
A newly upgraded robot has been deployed with the Queensland Police Service's (QPS) Special Emergency Response Team. Smaller robots are also being used by police negotiators across the state. QPS and Deakin University are collaborating on this use of force option in software and engineering development.
CSIRO have used 3D printing to produce a titanium and polymer sternum (bone, cartilage and tissue) to suit individual patients.
Scientists at the Centenary Institute have discovered new cancer therapies for patients with difficult-to-treat cancers. The new therapy will be developed by MetabloQ, a privately held biotech company, and funded by the Medical Research Commercialisation Fund and UniSeed, with additional investment from CSIRO. Clinical trials for new cancer therapies will most likely start within three years.
Airobotics, an Israeli industrial drone company, has established its first overseas entity in Perth. It aims to service clients in the mining and resources sector. Metals and mining company South32 has deployed the automated drone platform at its Worsley Alumina site to inspect equipment, conduct aerial surveys and develop 3D maps.
AI company Flamingo Customer Experience has signed an agreement for a six-month paid trial with DirectMoney Limited (ASX: DM1). Flamingo is owned by Cre8tek (ASX: CR8).
LiveHire Limited (ASX: LVH) and CEB Australia have partnered to launch the Women in Tech portal , a diversity initiative which aims to attract 100,000 women to the tech sector.
Queensland public housing tenants will be able to access rooftop solar power under a new trial. Up to 2000 eligible tenants managed through Logan City's Woodridge Housing Service Centre will be able to receive cheaper power by entering into a solar power purchase agreement.
As part of a $2.3 million revitalisation project, Optus Business and South West TAFE have created 15 intelligent tailored Smart Classrooms for around 5000 students across campuses in Warrnambool, Hamilton and Portland.
Australia Post has chosen Hobart as the first capital city in the country to pilot new three-wheel electric delivery vehicles to deliver small parcels. The e-vehicles are already successfully used in Germany and Switzerland.
The first mobile base station under round 2 of the Mobile Black Spot Program has been switched on.
Data downloads have increased by 52% for fixed broadband and 69% for mobile, according to the ACCC's annual reports on prices and competition in the telco sector. Prices for internet, mobile and fixed line services have fallen overall by 1.5%, while prices for internet services increased by 2.7% on average.
Elon Musk has held talks with Prime Minister Malcolm Turnbull on 'energy storage and its role in delivering affordable and reliable electricity'. Their discussion followed Mike Cannon-Brookes overture on Twitter, when he asked: "how serious are you about this bet? If I can make the $ happen (& politics), can you guarantee the 100MW in 100 days?" Carnegie Clean Energy Limited (ASX: CCE) is also in discussions with the South Australian Government regarding the provision of utility scale battery energy storage solutions into the SA electricity network.
The Department of Defence has licensed a system for imaging stress in aircraft structures to Victorian company LRM Technologies.
Sky and Space Global (ASX: SAS) has signed a non-binding Memorandum of Understanding with Globalsat Group LLC to explore commercial and technical collaboration, which would provide SAS's narrow-band satellite communications services to Globalsat's end users in South and Latin America.
Peppermint Innovation (ASX: PIL) will test their MyWeps Mobile Remittance at the Central Bank of the Philippines.
La Trobe University is collaborating with Cisco and Optus for the first Cyber Games . Partners include Quantum Victoria, the Science Teachers Association of Victoria and Life Journey/Day of STEM. Cyber Games will simulate real world threat scenarios in a 'cyber gaming' environment for students.
Nokia has renewed its managed services agreement with Chorus for another three years.
The OECD's recently-released Economic Survey of Australia 2017 has criticised the NBN , stating that 'in fixed-line technology, the wholesaler (National Broadband Network) needs to address concerns that it is not lowering its prices sufficiently quickly as the market develops'. The survey raises the issue of the NBN's disproportionate influence over fixed access markets and urges greater technical and pricing flexibility for retailers.
A major shake-up at the ABC will see 150-200 staff leave the organisation and $50 million invested in a new Content Fund , which will operate via a competitive bidding process open to all employees to source new ideas for content. $15 million per year will be invested in regional jobs and extra digital and video output. ABC will also transition to a 'more audience-focussed content structure' over the next 12 months. As part of the 'transformation program', new appointments include: David Anderson to Director of ABC Television; Leisa Bacon as Head of the Audiences Team; Louise Higgins as CFO; Sam Liston as Chief Engagement Officer; and David Pendleton as Interim Chief Technology Officer.

CompanyWatch

Data analytics crowdsourcer Kaggle has been acquired by Google . Nicolas Gruen, who is the current chairman, presented Kaggle at Tech23 in 2011. Soon afterwards they announced investment of $US 11 million from high-profile investors including the co-founder of PayPal. CEO Anthony Goldbloom has stated that Kaggle will remain a distinct brand within Google Cloud.
NAB Ventures has led a series B funding round in US-based foreign exchange payments company Veem . The round totalled US$25 million, and included investments from GV, American VC firm Kleiner Perkins Caufield & Byers and SBI Investment Co. Ltd. The funding round was led by NAB Ventures General Partner Melissa Widner (Industry Leader at Tech23 in 2016).
Indian multinational company Jain Irrigation has invested in Australian agtech company Observant Technology. The investment deal will enable the companies to combine Observant's field monitoring and control technology with Jain's global irrigation manufacturing and precision agriculture technology platform.
Uniseed will establish a Follow-On Fund with $20 million allocated from existing university partners ($5 million each over the next 10 years). Uniseed operates at the Universities of Melbourne, NSW, Sydney, Queensland and the CSIRO.
Family Zone (ASX: FZO) has raised over $2.8 million in an oversubscribed placement, supported by a strategic investment from Fidelio Partners.
Microequities Venture Capital Fund has invested $1 million in brisbane startup Outfit . The marketing tech platform will create 10 new jobs and hopes to break into the US market.
An online mapping platform has received $60,000 in funding from the Northern Territory Government. Ecostat, developed by Station Innovation, remotely monitors properties and infrastructure via satellite images and its online mapping platform.
