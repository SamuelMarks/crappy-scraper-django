news briefs

The Federal Government will mount a public information and community engagement campaign to help change the culture around innovation and science in Australian businesses, engage young people and inspire future entrepreneurs and support the National Innovation and Science Agenda . Research the Government has undertaken shows - 64% of Australians agree we need to take more risks to get innovative; 77% of Australians believe innovation is not just for the technology industry; advances in health are viewed as the most important reason for Australia to innovate, followed by jobs and better living standards.
Chinese wireless telecom supplier Enice (ASX: ENC), which floated on the ASX in early November 2015 has won a two year tender notification valued at $8.53 million from China Mobile Group for the provision of DAS antennas CPP for 2016 and 2017.
Megaport Limited 's (ASX: MP1) US subsidiary has entered into a binding alliance agreement with Amsterdam Internet Exchange , B.V. (AMS-IX) to become the exclusive global elastic cloud interconnection partner for AMS-IX.
Enterprise software provider TechnologyOne (ASX: TNE) has claimed to be Australia’s largest software R&D company. TechnologyOne will invest $41 million in its Research & Development program in 2016, equating to 19% of its revenue.
iSelect Limited (ASX: ISU) plans to rectify internal operations with initiatives including the implementation of a new CRM system and integrated telephony technology and the expansion of the proprietary iConnect Platform across all business units.
New Zealand Police and Auror have partnered to work together to collaborate with retailers to combat crime.
The University of New South Wales (UNSW) has been approved under new legislation by the Civil Aviation Safety Authority to provide integrated Commercial Pilot Licence training. Advanced flight training will be conducted on two newly purchased flight simulators at UNSW’s Flying Operations Unit at Bankstown Airport in a partnership between UNSW Aviation and QantasLink . The Pacific Simulators 4.5 flight training device has a 180 degree wrap-around screen, and is representative of a two-pilot jet transport aeroplane with weight and performance characteristics similar to a Boeing 737-800.
REFFIND Limited (ASX: RFN) has signed up Staples , Bupa , Domain , Sunglass Hut , Gilbert + Tobin and Gadens to use its employee experience platform.
Tech23 alumni Locomote has signed an agreement with Allianz Global Assistance . The partnership will see Allianz's travel insurance services integrated into Locomote's corporate travel platform.
The South Australian Government is accepting tenders for its Electronic Court Management System to collect, store, and process court matters electronically, ensuring delivery of the right information to the right people at the right time. The system will be used throughout the entire court process and transition the court from paper to digital. The tender closes at 4.00pm Adelaide time on Friday 26 February 2016.

company watch

BuyMyPlace has signed a subscription agreement with KordaMentha Investments ahead of its reverse takeover of Killara Resources . You can find the Prospectus for the $5 million capital raising, here .
Microsoft has acquired the technology assets of Brisbane based company Event Zero for an undisclosed sum. Microsoft plans to expand and improve its built in management tools for Skype for Business Online in Office 365.
CapitalPitch has raised $600,000 seed round and is launching a Venture Fund to invest in the next 20 graduates for its accelerator program in 2016. The seed round was led by angel investors Trevor Folsom and Aaron Bertinetti. CapitalPitch plan to use the funds to finance a technology expansion and help build its investor network.
Amaysim (ASX: AYS) has completed the acquisition of Brisbane based MVNO company Vaya Pty Ltd .

people watch

Andrew Stead has been appointed Director Strategic Initiatives at IAG (ASX: IAG). Stead is the Non-Executive Director of Red Analytics and previously had commercialisation roles at Data61 and ATP Innovations .
Dr Heather Smith has been appointed Secretary of the Department of Communications and the Arts . Her role will commence Monday 25 January and will run for five years. Dr Smith is currently a Deputy Secretary in the Department of the Prime Minister and Cabinet where she recently headed the Prime Minister’s taskforce on innovation and led work on public data policy.
3P Learning Limited (ASX: 3PL) has announced its Managing Director and Chief Executive Officer Tim Power intends to step down from his role to pursue new opportunities effective immediately after leading 3P Learning for the last decade. Current Chief Financial Officer and Company Secretary Jonathan Kenny will be acting as Interim CEO.
Trent Innes is the new Managing Director of Xero Australia (ASX: XRO). Innes, who has spent nearly three years at Xero Australia as National Sales Director, will replace current Managing Director Chris Ridd. Ridd who has resigned after five years will continue to work as an advisor to the Xero executive team. Before joining Xero, Innes was the National Sales Manager of the Microsoft Business Group in Australia, managing the ERP and CRM business.
The search for the CEO of the new Australian Digital Health Agency has begun. The Agency is to be fully operational by mid 2016 and will combine the National E-Health Transition Authority and the work of the My Health Record managed by Department of Health .
Ian Black, the new CEO of Gentrack Group (NZX/ASX: GTK) starts today. He has previously been CEO of SAP NZ and COO of SAP in Australia and New Zealand.
