news briefs

ANZ Bank has established an International Technology and Digital Business Advisory Panel whose four members are representatives from Twitter , and Dimension Data and former executives from PayPal and Procter and Gamble .
UXC Limited (ASX: UXC) has signed a three-year $12 million plus, cloud based contract with water treatment and chemical distribution company Ixom, which was formerly a part of Orica (ASX: ORI).
The University of Swinburne ’s Opaque Multimedia team has won US $50,000 at the Microsoft Imagine Cup held in Seattle as part of the World Citizenship Award to continue developing their software. The group, including Liam McGuire, James Bonner, Chris Mackenzie and Norman Wang beat teams around the world when they were able to demonstrate how their Virtual Dementia Experience can stimulate the effects of ageing and dementia.
Civica UK has selected MarkManager from the Board of Studies, Teaching and Educational Standards (BOSTES) for a major global roll-out in a large skills proficiency testing customer environment. LANSA is the BOSTES commercialisation partner for marketing of the NSW government agency’s application software to the world on a SaaS basis.
A team from the University of New South Wales has defeated Germany to claim back-to-back Robocup SPL World Championships. The competition involves fully autonomous robots, with no remote control, competing against each other in 5-on-5 soccer. Each team uses the same robots, so the competition is focused on software and artificial intelligence development, not on hardware construction. Find out more about how they won here - and you can also watch the game! And be ready - the Robocup is coming to Australia in 2019!
The Department of Defence has signed a five-year software license and support deal with SAP worth $86 million. Under the deal, SAP will upgrade existing SAP Defence Forces and Public Security Solution, used in finance, logistics, and engineering and maintenance areas. The installation will occur in phases over the five years to 2020.
AusNet Services has partnered with Deakin University to launch the AusNet Services Women in Power Engineering Scholarships valued at $10,000 per year. The scholarships will be awarded to female applicants entering Deakin’s Bachelor of Electrical and Electronics Engineering (Honours) degree.
The University of Sydney in collaboration with The Monkeys has created a new virtual tour for prospective students. The tour shows the University through the eyes of current students, who narrate an interactive video of the campus and a snapshot of Sydney.
Aconex Limited (ASX: ACX) has entered into a multi-year partnership with CIMIC Group Limited (ASX: CIM), formerly Leighton Holdings Limited. Under the terms of the agreement, Aconex will also be acquiring the intellectual property, assets and certain customer relationships of INCITE Keystone, a platform owned and operated by CIMIC for $6.4 million.
Crowd Mobile Limited (ASX: CM8) has entered into an exclusive agreement AdsMetric Group Pty Ltd (AMG) to license and distribute AMG’s social messaging platform ‘Krafty’.
Australia’s Academic and Research Network (AARNet) has announced a partnership with the California Institute for Telecommunications and Information Technology in a move that will connect Australian researchers to a next-generation, private US Data sharing network.
Satellite operator SpeedCast (ASX: SDA) has inked a multi-year communications contract with international shipping company Vroon .
Telstra has opened a new R&D lab in Melbourne by the name of ‘Gurrowa’.
Up to $28.5 million is available for the Advance Queensland Research Fellowships , which supports original research that will positively impact on Queensland. Applications open Friday 4 September.

company watch

Beat the Q and Tech23 alumni Posse have announced the closure of an oversubscribed Series A $5 million funding led by the Westpac backed venture fund Reinventure and venture capital firm Exto Partners . The company plans to rebrand its flagship application Beat the Q to Hey You, effective 10 August 2015.
Medical management software startup HotDoc has raised $2.2 million Series A investment round led by AirTree Ventures . The company plan to use the funds to hire sales staff and new product developers.
Online construction equipment hiring platform iSeekplant has raised $1 million from a number of undisclosed angel investors to help drive growth and launch a new custom offering.
White label digital music provider Tuned Global has raised $5.1 million investment round from venture capital firm Exto Partners . This is the company’s second round of funding from Exto Partners.

people watch

Recently retired Telstra CEO David Thodey has been appointed Chairman of the CSIRO for a five year term. Thodey was the CEO of Telstra from 2009 – 2015. Before joining Telstra he had a 22 year career with IBM, ending as CEO. He starts in November.
