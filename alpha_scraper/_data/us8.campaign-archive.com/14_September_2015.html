news briefs

Malcolm Turnbull has resigned from his position as Minister for Communications and is mounting a challenge to become Prime Minister.
The latest round of successful applicants to receive funding under the Accelerating Commercialisation element of the Entrepreneurs' Programme include: Cloud Data Centre Ltd - $1 million for its OfficeBox platform transforming software and data delivery and storage; Cuoretech Pty Ltd - $490,000 for its Real-Time Cardiac Arryhthmia Mapping System; GET Trakka - $1 million for its Real Time GET Breakage & Ware Monitoring Enhancing Productivity and special congratulations to Tech23 Alumni Intersective Pty Ltd - $383,305 for its Practera Experiential Learning Software.
As you undoubtedly are aware, Australian Technology Park (ATP) in Sydney is either going to be reignited for tech or it is going to become the home to a bank. Ed Husic, Shadow Parliamentary Secretary to the Shadow Treasurer, spoke in Parliament today supporting ATP to stay tech. You can sign the petition to help save ATP for technology here .
The Federal Government has announced it is spending $18.5 million to establish the National Facial Biometric Matching Capability, a facial recognition technology to combat terrorism. From mid next year law enforcement and Government agencies will share and match photographs on identity documents to strengthen identity checking processes, while maintaining privacy safeguards.
The University of Queensland 's (UQ) Robotics Club is seeking more members to work on a project this semester creating a functioning prosthetic forearm with the intent of advancing open-source knowledge around the creation of 3D printed prosthetic limbs. So far, the 3D printed limb costs around $500 to make. The project has been funded through a UQ Sponsorship Grant, with robotics club member Fabian Vasuain acting as head engineer.
Boeing (NYSE: BA) has secured a $665.7 million contract with the Australian Defence Force (ADF) to provide digital communication system for land-based ADF deployed around the world.
Tomizone Limited (ASX: TOM) has signed a contract with Christchurch International Airport Limited to provide WiFi capabilities to the airport’s entire international and domestic terminals.
The Australian Communications and Media Authority (ACMA), has refreshed its strategy for addressing the growth in mobile broadband capacity in a discussion paper, ‘Beyond 2020 – A spectrum management strategy to address the growth in mobile broadband capacity’. The ACMA is inviting industry comment on the report, with submissions closing on 23 October 2015.
A new Australian IP Toolkit for Collaboration has been developed by the Department for Industry and Science and IP Australia to encourage greater collaboration between researchers and business.
A new partnership has been formed between the Australian Government , ACT Government and the Canberra Innovation Network to support science engagement activities in the ACT over the next four years, as part of the Government’s national Inspiring Australia programme showcasing STEM education. The Government is investing $15.95 million over four years to 2017-18 in the Inspiring Australia programme.
Telstra (ASX: TLS) and the Northern Territory Government have announced a $30 million, three year infrastructure co-investment agreement to build new telco infrastructure across the Northern Territory. Telstra has also committed an additional investment of $4.05 million over three years to deliver digital literacy initiatives, cyber safety programs and telehealth services.

company watch

Bailador Technology Investments (ASX: BTI) has invested $2.5 million in social media marketing platform Stackla as lead investor in a total of $3 million round. Stackla was founded in Sydney in 2012, headquartered in San Francisco and employs 48 people worldwide. Paul Wilson and Andrea Kowalski will represent BTI on the Board which also includes Founder and CEO, Damien Mahoney, Founder and CPO, Peter Cassidy, Tony Faure as Chairman, Paul Naphtali and Grant McCarthy.
Knowledge management software company Knosys (ASX: KNO) has successfully listed on the Australian Securities Exchange following its capital raising of $4 million.
iiNet Limited has been removed from the list of Australian Securities Limited, following implementation of the scheme of arrangement by which TPG Telecom Limited (ASX: TPM) acquired all of its share capital.
Bitcoin Group Limited (ASX: BCG) has received approval by the Australian Securities and Investment Commission for a $20 million public offer, with a proposed listing on the Australian Securities Exchange scheduled for Wednesday 11 November 2015.
migme Limited (ASX: MIG) has raised $10 million.
Uniseed , a venture fund operating at the Universities of Melbourne , New South Wales and Queensland has announced the exit of investee company Hatchtech , developer of next generation lice treatment. Hatchtech has entered into a commercial agreement with Dr. Reddy’s Laboratories (NYSE: RDY) which will include upfront and pre-commercialisation payments plus additional milestone payments on commercial sales. Hatchtech was supported by Uniseed from 2001 and then achieved additional investment from other VCs including GBS Ventures , QIC Bioventures and Biocomm , with the University Melbourne also investing from their endowment fund. Subsequent investors included OneVentures in 2010, and Blue Sky Venture Capital in 2013. Hatchtech’s technology was developed by Professor Vern Bowles at the University of Melbourne.
