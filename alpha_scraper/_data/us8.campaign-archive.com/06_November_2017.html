NewsBriefs

Up to 6,000 jobs will be lost (with 2000 more created) over the next three years at NAB, as part of a plan to slash expenses while investing in new technology, including using automation to replace work done by people. The cost-cutting measures are budgeted to deliver annual savings of $1 billion by 2020 as the company boosts technology spend by $1.5 billion.
Myer has launched the Myer Market as part of its omni-channel shopping strategy.
A project which aims to revolutionise personalised medicine through wearable sensor technologies like bracelets will receive up to $10 million as the inaugural winner of ANU's Grand Challenge Scheme. The five year project involves more than 60 researchers, and is the first winner under ANU's $50 million Grand Challenge Scheme, set up by Vice-Chancellor Professor Brian Schmidt with the ambition of solving "some of the biggest problems facing the world."
The eSafety Office is taking part in a global pilot with Facebook to prevent intimate images of Australians being shared online.
ABC News, in conjunction with co-production partner Torrens University, has launched a feature-length, fully interactive virtual reality experience that brings to life the experiences of World War II soldiers on the Kokoda Track.
Delivery of Victoria Police's new mobility managed service has commenced with Motorola Solutions. 59 officers have received the new service thus far, with the $50 million contract to deliver smart mobile technology to at least 10,000 officers.

Speedcast International (ASX: SDA) has been awarded a multi-year, multi-million dollar Australian government contract to provide the Australian Antarctic program with communications services. Speedcast will provide VSAT bandwidth, equipment and installation, as well as additional support to Geoscience Australia and three Australian Antarctic research stations - Mawson, Casey and Davis.
Startups selected to participate in the first cyber security accelerator program CyRise include Brooklyn Dynamics, CYDARM, NetCrypt and Cybercitadel. Each startup will receive $50,000 from joint backers Dimension Data, Deakin University and LaunchVic.
Trials of the Red Belly Blockchain at the University of Sydney have demonstrated that it can process financial transactions 50% faster than first anticipated. It can process over 660,000 transactions per second on 300 machines in a single data centre.
Telstra has chosen Smart Parking (ASX: SPZ) as its preferred supplier of parking technology .
Scott Morrison has announced that Australia is working to strengthen collaboration with the UK through an Australia-UK FinTech Bridge , which will assist Australian fintechs in the UK.

CompanyWatch

Superloop Limited (ASX: SLC) is to acquire connectivity services provider GX2 Holdings.
The separation of Domain Holdings Australia from Fairfax has now been approved by the Federal Court of Australia after it was approved by shareholders last week. 99.89% of shares voted by Fairfax last week were in favour of spinning off Domain. Fairfax will keep 60% of Domain, while the remaining 40% will be given to the publisher's existing shareholders. Domain is expected to commence trading on the ASX on 16 November 2017.
Telstra has acquired Australian-owned GPS and telematics fleet management solutions company MTData.
Brisbane-based intelliHR has raised $4 million in pre-IPO funding.

PeopleWatch

StartupAUS has rejigged its board and raised the number of women on the startup advocacy outfit’s 10 member board from two to four as led by chair Topaz Conway. The four new board members are: Managing Director of Blue Sky Venture Capital Elaine Stead; Investment advisor and strategist, Rachael Neumann; Vice-Chancellor and President of the University of Technology Sydney, Attila Brungs; and CEO of Startup Catalyst, Aaron Birkby.
Startup investor, mentor, founder Alan Jones is joining KPMG Australia’s High Growth Ventures team as Entrepreneur-in-Residence. Jones will continue to work with Blue Chilli as a mentor and advisor.
ICANN's new Vice Chair is Chris Disspain . Disspain was previously CEO of .au Domain Administration.
The Pearcey National Entrepreneur Award for 2017 went to Cyan and Collis Ta’eed of Envato. The Pearcey Medallist for 2017 is Emeritus Professor Mike Miller of the University of South Australia. The 2017 Hall of Fame winner is Senator (retired) Kate Lundy and Queensland ICT journalist Helen Meredith.
Four Indigenous entrepreneurs have been selected for Barayamal's three-month Budding Entrepreneurs program at Fishburners. The successful entrepreneurs are Jason Coulthard, Roma Williams-Pregarc, Dylan Mottlee and Phillip Harris.
