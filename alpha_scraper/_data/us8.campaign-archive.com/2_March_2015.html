news briefs

Monash University researchers along with collaborators from CSIRO and Deakin University have printed a jet engine. In fact Monash and their spin-out company Amaero , have printed two engines. One is on display this week at the International Air Show in Avalon, while the second is displayed in Toulouse at the French aerospace company Microturbo (Safran). The engines are a proof of concept that’s led to tier one aerospace companies lining up to develop new components at the Monash Centre for Additive Manufacturing in Melbourne, Australia. The Centre, AMAERO and the jet engine project have been supported by the Australian government via the Australian Research Council (ARC), the CRC program , Commercialisation Australia , the Science and Industry Endowment Fund ; Monash University and Safran .
The Australian Parliament is due to pass the Bill for a mandatory data retention scheme following the unanimous report of the Parliamentary Joint Committee on Intelligence and Security (PJCIS) on the fourth tranche of the Government’s national security legislation, the Telecommunications (Interception and Access) Amendment (Data Retention) Bill 2014. The PJCIS states that it considered carefully the rationale for a mandatory data retention scheme and that “such a regime is justified as a necessary, effective and proportionate response.”
NBN Co has chosen ARRIS Group, Inc . (NASDAQ: ARRS) to build its next-generation Hybrid Fiber-Coaxial (HFC) broadband network. The contract is estimated to be worth $400 million.
Allianz Insurance has selected Telstra (ASX: TLS) to deliver voice, data and mobile services worth $12.5 million to its customers over the next three years.
NEC Australia has implemented ICT infrastructure with the help of major ICT partner Cisco and ASCOM, SPOK and Wavelink for Victoria’s newly opened $447.5 million development of Eastern Health’s Box Hill Hospital following a competitive tender in 2012.
Scentre Group (ASX: SCG) has selected Optus to deploy free Wi-Fi at 21 Westfield shopping centres across Australia following two successful pilots in October 2014 at the newly redeveloped Westfield Miranda, NSW and Westfield Garden City, QLD. Shoppers will be given 1GB of data free for a three hour period each day.
The consortium of NSW universities and research institutes, Intersect , has invested $1.5 million to deliver 4,500 additional CPU cores to NeCTAR Research Cloud , boosting national capacity to approximately 32,000 cores. The NeCTAR Research Cloud removes the burden from researchers of operating their own physical computer infrastructure.
Australia’s first bitcoin debit card, CoinJar Swipe is now available. It can be used anywhere in Australia that accepts EFTPOS and allows you to withdraw your bitcoin as cash at any ATM in Australia.
Adelaide-based business accelerator program Innovyz has opened applications to businesses for its nine-month innovation program. Businesses working in the healthtech sector have until March 16 2015 to submit an application.
Seven Network Operations, a subsidiary of Seven West Media (ASX: SWM) is free to acquire shares to participate in a joint venture with Foxtel for the Presto Entertainment service after clearance from the ACCC . The ACCC determined that, following the proposed acquisition, the Presto Entertainment service would continue to face strong competition from other SVOD services such as Stan (a joint venture between Nine and Fairfax) and Netflix, which has announced it will launch in Australia this month.

company watch

Xero Limited (XRO) has received an injection of $141 million. Accel Partners has agreed to invest NZ$132.9 million (US$100 million) at NZ$20 (US$15.052) per share through a number of funds (the Accel Funds) under its management. Current shareholder Matrix Capital Management , Xero’s largest institutional investor, has agreed to invest an additional NZ$14.3m (US$10.8m). Upon completion of the transaction, Xero will have a cash balance of approximately NZ$285 million which will help them drive growth in the United States and United Kingdom. Former Dell Executive, Russ Fujioka has been appointed as U.S. President while Graham Smith, former Salesforce CFO will join the Board.
Melbourne IT (ASX: MLB) has entered into a Share Purchase Agreement for the purchase of 100% of the issued capital of Uber Global Group , with the initial value of the deal at $15.5 million, plus a potential earn-out based on EBITDA performance to 30 June this year.
Handkrafted has received $25,000 in seed funding from QUT Creative Enterprise Australia . This is in addition to the $100,000 received last month by the company from Sydney Seed Fund led by Garry Visontay, Benjamin Chong and Ari Klinger.
Speedcast (ASX: SDA) has acquired Geolink Satellite Services and part of CETel Group. The acquisition follows SDA’s acquisition of SatComms Australia and Oceanic Broadband, in the Australasia region in the past 12 months.
ParcelPoint has received $7 million in investment led by Blue Sky Alternative Investments Limited (ASX: BLA). Other investors included CVC Asia Pacific Managing Partner Adrian Mackenzie, John Symond and the Duncan family.
