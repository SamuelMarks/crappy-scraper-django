news briefs

Steve Dalby, Chief Regulatory Officer at iinet has blogged on iinet's stand against mandatory data retention and explained some of the terms, plans and costs associated with the plans canvassed by the Attorney General, George Brandis this week.
The Australian Payments Clearing Association (APCA) has released research showing that cash payments have declined 5% since 2005 and is predicted to accelerate, dropping a further 20% over the next few years before it plateaus in 2018. Figures from the Reserve Bank of Australia support this - in February 2014 there were 57.3 million cash withdrawals from ATMs - down from 60.0 million in February 2013. The APCA research also shows that the share of cash within all of the payment transactions made in Australia has dropped from 73% in 2005 to 59% in 2013, with a further share decline forecast to 43% in 2018.
Surf Accelerator is now open for applicants. The intensive 12 week, mentor driven program based on the Gold Coast is delivered by Silicon Lakes and funded by City of Gold Coast.
GovHack winners have been announced after the Red Carpet Awards were held in Brisbane over the weekend.
Westpac New Zealand is to release a new app which will use augmented reality and 3D imagery in September. The app was the winning concept from the bank's crowd sourcing initiative, The Westpac Global App Challenge - UK.
Telstra will become a customer of the Australian Securities Exchange (ASX) and co-locate at the ASX Australian Liquidity Centre. Telstra will provide global financial services organisations with connectivity to other major financial centres.
The ACCC’s inquiry into the pricing of mobile phone calls to different networks has found the prices mobile networks charge to receive calls and messages were too high and is looking at how they should be priced to promote competition in the market and benefit consumers.
ASG Group Limited (ASX:ASZ) has signed a five year contract, with a further three year option, to provide ICT services to the Australian Maritime Safety Authority.

company watch

migme (ASX:MIG) has begun trading on the ASX following its merger with Latin Gold Limited. migme was formerly mig33 (founded in 2006) and provides a social entertainment platform for emerging markets. Headquartered in Singapore, its founder and CEO is Steve Goh.
Farmbot , Pixc , and Opening Learning from Telstra’s start-up accelerator program, muru-D have received an investment from SYD Ventures.
Ezidebit which provides custom payment solutions has acquired StorMan in a multi-million dollar deal. Ezidebit claims to be the largest non-bank payment supplier in Australia and is rumoured to be considering a listing on the ASX.

people watch

Mark Britt has stepped down as CEO of Mi9 and into a new role with Catcha Group , leading their video streaming service across ASEAN. Britt will be replaced by Alex Parsons.
As part of AIMIA’s restructure, CEO David Holmes has been moved into an advisory position as he takes on a role at HostWorks. Holmes, AIMIA’s president, Tim O’Neill and Commercial Manager of AIMIA, Nick Toulson will lead AIMIA as it shifts more emphasis to the states.
Oztam has appointed Padraig O’Donovan as its new Digital Strategy Director. In his new role, O’Donovan joins OzTAM from Network Ten.
