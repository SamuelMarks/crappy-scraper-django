NewsBriefs

Sydney's startup ecosystem is set to have a new location with UTS' purchase of a Chippendale warehouse for $11.25 million. UTS Deputy Vice-Chancellor (Resources) Patrick Woods said that the space would be used to "further support the thriving precinct startup ecosystem".
Melbourne Airport has deployed TIBCO solutions to provide real-time information into airport operational systems.
Tech23 alumni Myriota, the University of New England and the Australia and New Zealand CRC for Spatial Information are collaborating on trials of low-cost livestock water tank level monitoring.
Aconex (ASX: ACX) will manage project information and processes for a Texas methanol production complex, under an agreement with Orascom Construction.
The Regional, Rural and Remote Communications Coalition met with members of Parliament last week to highlight concerns regarding telecommunications in the bush.
Queensland's biggest startup precinct has opened in Fortitude Valley. Over 80% of the government-backed Precinct has been leased.
NSW Health has rolled out Citrix technologies to provide health staff with remote and secure access to sensitive patient information, analysis of hi-res medical images across devices, and to support its bring-your-own-device initiative.
South Australia is funding local development of autonomous vehicle technology , with a $2.8 million trial of a driverless shuttle between Adelaide Airport's terminal and long-term carpark. It is one of seven projects to share in $5.6 million in the first round of funding from the $10 million Future Mobility Lab Fund. The other projects include autonomous cargo pods and driverless shuttles. Cohda Wireless will receive $2 million from the same fund for on-road trials in Adelaide. The trials will test how vehicles communicate with one another and with roadside infrastructure.

Science is a critical element for Australia's delivery of growth, jobs and economic prosperity - according to the Government's National Science Statement for 2017 . The Statement is designed to address Australia's lack of 'an explicit framework to bring our collective strengths together and to guide investment and decision making in the longer term.'
Optus is inviting entries to its second annual Future Makers program, with finalists to take part in a four month Accelerator Program. Entrants must submit a solution for areas including employability, education or wellbeing. Entries close 5 May 2017.
Australia Post, Alibaba and Blackmores are partnering in an initiative to increase the traceability of food products and combat the rise of ' counterfeit food ' being sold in China. The project will explore blockchain technology to help strengthen supply chains.
Optus will go live with IODM's (ASX: IOD) automated debtor management platform in April.
Family Zone (ASX: FZO) has partnered with interschool eSports tournament host Flaktest Gaming, with the latter to market Family Zone's products to private schools.
Crazy Domains (operated by Dreamscape Networks Limited - ASX: DN8) has joined the zipPay platform (ASX: ZML).
Innovation and Science Australia want to know your thoughts on how to position Australia as a ' leading innovation nation by 2030 '. Their newly-released issues paper identifies six challenges seen as central to its strategy. Submissions are open until 31 May 2017.
A consortium comprising South Australia's three public universities, TAFE SA and the Defence Teaming Centre are collaborating to deliver a 'defence-ready' workforce. The Defence Industry Education and Skills Consortium will consider tendering for the establishment of a Maritime Technical College in SA.

SpaceWatch

Australia needs a National Space Program as part of our national strategy. That's the message of the Space Industry Association of Australia 's (SIAA) latest whitepaper, ' Advancing Australia in Space '. The SIAA estimates that Australia could double the space sector's current annual revenue of $3-4 billion within 5 years with Australian Government commitment to the establishment of an Australian Space Agency.
You could be part of the search to find Planet 9 - a potentially new planet in our solar system. Australian National University (ANU) has launched a search for the planet , and is inviting anyone with web access to contribute. The possibility of a ninth planet on the outer edges of the solar system has been long-discussed but never proven. Citizen scientists will be able to use a website to search hundreds of thousands of images taken by the ANU SkyMapper telescope at Siding Spring Observatory.
An Australian researcher has received the American Institute of Aeronautics and Astronautics' 2017 Hypersonic Systems and Technologies Award. Professor Richard Morgan of the University of Queensland received the award for his work in developing high-speed wind tunnels for the lab study of hypersonic flows.
Astronauts and heads of space agencies will descend on Adelaide this September for the 68th International Astronautical Congress , running from 25-29 September.
As part of the international QB50 project , Australian nano-satellites or ‘CubeSats’ will (hopefully) blast off from the International Space Station tomorrow, 28 March. Follow the developments on Twitter .
