NewsBriefs

The Australian Cyber Security Centre is advising Australian organisations to apply newly released Microsoft patches for Windows XP, 8 and Windows Server 2003 as soon as possible to prevent infection from the Ransomware campaign which is impacting organisations globally.
Cre8tek Limited (ASX:CR8), the owner of Flamingo Customer Experience Inc, has signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) with Deloitte Australia to act as an integration partner or reseller of Flamingo for a six month period with the option to enter into a full partnership once potential product offerings and revenue streams are explored.
A new shark mitigation strategy has been released for WA, including a trial of personal shark deterrent devices, the use of drones by Surf Life Saving WA to monitor beaches, and two VR4 receivers to detect tagged sharks - to be deployed at Esperance. In shark news on the eastern coast of Australia, results of a six-month trial into shark nets have found that SMART (Shark-Management – Alert-in-Real-Time) drumlines are more effective and safer than nets. Five months into the trial, 29 target sharks were caught, with only one deceased. By contrast, the North Coast net trial caught only six target sharks (three deceased), along with a whopping 244 non-target animals, over half of whom were deceased.
Queensland-based Tech23 alumnus RedEye is partnering with US water technologies organisation, WaterStart, and has set up an office in Las Vegas.
Pushpay was the big winner at the 23rd Annual NZ Hi-Tech Awards , claiming the PwC Hi-Tech Company of the Year category and the IBM Innovative Company Award. RedShield Security received two awards – the Duncan Cotterill Innovative Software Product Award and the Kiwibank Innovative Services Award. The 2017 Flying Kiwi, Frances Valintine - founder and Chair of the MindLab by Unitec and recent founder of the Tech Futures Lab - was inducted into the Hall of Fame.
The Disaster Resilience Knowledge Hub has been created by the Australian Institute for Disaster Resilience to help prepare government and communities for natural disasters.
$1.8 million in funding has been announced for the installation of renewable energy infrastructure at the Australian Institute of Marine Science near Townsville.
$15.3 million will go towards transforming the pilot project, the Australian Geoscience Data Cube, into Digital Earth Australia over the next two years. Digital Earth Australia will provide access to Earth observation satellite data.
French cloud Infrastructure-as-a-Service provider OVH has opened its Asia Pacific headquarters in Melbourne , promising the creation of 80 jobs.
The Monash Children's Telesurgery Project will give doctors in Gippsland, Victoria a direct video link to leading experts in paediatric care. Over $500,000 in funding has been provided to the project by the Victorian Government to establish a direct, live video link from Monash Children's to regional operating theatres and emergency departments at Latrobe Regional Hospital, Central Gippsland Health Service and Bairnsdale Regional Health Service.

Envato has partnered with business productivity app AND CO to help its community with workflow.
Canberra's streetlights are set to be upgraded to provide a more energy efficient, smarter network as part of the Territory's goal to achieve zero net emissions.
Promapp will deploy its business process management software for the City of Joondalup in WA.
The Northern Territory Government has passed legislation putting MyFuel NT into effect. To be fully operational from November, MyFuel NT requires all fuel retailers to publish prices online via an online portal.
Sky and Space Global (ASX: SAS) has signed an MoU with WeFarm Ltd to explore the integration of the SAS nano-satellite communication platform into WeFarm's process of on-boarding small-scale farmers in isolated locations.
WA's Department of Education is partnering with the STEM Education Consortium in a $4 million, three-year deal that will focus on preparing students for employment in STEM areas .
RAC is expanding its partnership with French-based NAVYA, which will see RAC coordinate the on-site commissioning of NAYVA vehicles in Australia, New Zealand, parts of southeast Asia and Japan.
Rision (ASX: RNL) has partnered with iPad PoS management solution provider Revel Systems, providing the rostering company with a launchpad into the US market.
eMite (a subsidiary of Prophecy - ASX: PRO) has signed a partnership deal with Genesys to enable its cloud dashboard solution to be sold with Genesys' PureCloud.

BudgetWatch

The Federal Budget is allocating $24 million in scholarships for students to undertake STEM studies. The Rural and Regional Enterprise Scholarships will provide around 1200 grants, with students receiving up to $20,000 for 'priority fields' like STEM. This of course comes alongside recently-announced changes to Australia's higher education system, including a 1.8% increase in student contributions - cumulating to a 7.5% increase, and a lowering of the starting repayment threshold for loans to $42,000.
Included in this year's Budget is an additional $70.1 million over four years to drive the second stage of the Government's digital transformation agenda . $33.6 million over four years will be spent building whole-of-government digital platforms, with four pilot services and platforms to be delivered. $10.7 million will support the creation of a new cyber security advisory function within the DTA, and $15.2 million will deliver the second stage of the Govpass program.
AIIA has provided an overview of investments in ICT-related activity in this year's Federal Budget. CEO Rob Fitzpatrick said: “The government is taking a lead in Australia’s digital transformation through greater investment in ICT infrastructure and capability. Through its investments in data analytics, technology platforms, digital identity, cyber security, the expansion of the MyHealth record system, and building digital skills across the public service, government will be much better positioned to deliver efficient, quality services to Australians. These are smart investments that will enable the adoption of future technologies as government services evolve.” He also voiced concerns regarding the annual foreign worker levy or one-off payments for visas, noting a critical shortage of qualified people for jobs requiring skills in cyber security, cloud and data and analytics.
While acknowledging support for fintech in the 2017-18 Federal Budget, StartupAUS have stated that startups have ' dropped off the radar '.

CompanyWatch

iCetana , which spun out from research at Curtin University, has raised $8 million in a funding round led by Go Capital, with participation from the Delcor Family Office, angel investors and existing investor Yuuwa Capital. iCetana, which is transforming surveillance with its software, was a winner at Tech23 in 2010.
Another Tech23 alumnus, Abyss Solutions , has raised $1 million for global expansion. The funds will be used to hire more engineers.
iCollege (ASX: ICT) is to acquire Australian Capital College, based in the ACT. The move is part of iCollege's strategy to expand into the international student market, with the company stating that the recent 457 visa legislation changes 'have sparked a rally toward regional centres, such as Canberra'.
Japanese business intelligence and enterprise analytics software provider WingArc1st has acquired Melbourne-based Space-Time Research .
Founder of 1-Page (ASX: 1PG) Joanna Riley has been removed from the board in today’s vote.
