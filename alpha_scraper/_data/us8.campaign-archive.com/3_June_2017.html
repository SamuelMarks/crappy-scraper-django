NewsBriefs

Nine Australian and New Zealand universities signed a deal with London-listed IP Group , which will see $200 million made available to find and commercialise research ideas. The agreement involves IP Group Australia, the Group of Eight universities and the University of Auckland.
The ARC's Australian Laureate Fellowships for 2017 (round 1) include $2.7 million for solar-driven sustainable production of fuels and chemicals (University of Adelaide); $2.8 million for a project on sustainable electrochemical energy storage technology (University of Queensland); and $3.1 million for a new paradigm in immersive visualisation which aims to develop a new research tool that simulates seeing through the eyes of another to reduce the effects of stigmatisation and prejudice (University of New South Wales).
Australia's Chief Scientist Dr Alan Finkel announced a blueprint to optimise the National Electricity Market with his final report of the Independent Review into the Future Security of the National Electricity Market . The blueprint aims to deliver: future reliability; increased security; rewarding consumers; and lower emissions. The review concludes that a Clean Energy Target is the best way to reduce emissions while supporting security and reliability.
An inquiry into how to support regional NSW's startup ecosystem has been launched by the Committee on Investment, Industry and Regional Development. Nearly a quarter of Australian startup businesses were founded outside capital cities (according to Universities Australia and Startup Muster’s 2017 report). Submissions from the public are invited until Friday 21 July 2017.
What are the major problems facing the development of Artificial Intelligence (AI) ? The University of Sydney has launched a $7.5 million research centre in partnership with UBTECH Robotics to answer these questions.
Hansen Technologies (ASX: HSN) has signed a multi-year agreement with Sperian Energy Corporation for Hansen's business process outsourced services.
US offshore wind energy development group Deepwater Wind will use Aconex (ASX: ACX) to manage project information during the design and construction of the Block Island Wind Farm.
CSIRO's Data61 has delivered two reports on the potential for blockchain technology across government and industry in Australia, and its regulatory, technical and societal implications. The first report looks at four adoption scenarios of blockchain technology: aspirational, transformation, new equilibrium and collapse. The second report explores design alternatives in three use cases: remittance payments, open data registries and agricultural supply chains.

Cognizant and the University of Newcastle have co-innovated a virtual reality solution to change the way students learn anatomy and birthing techniques.
Corescan has licensed HyLogger , a fast and efficient drilling material scanner from CSIRO. Corescan operate a network of hyperspectral mineralogy labs across Australia, South East Asia, Canada, USA, Mexico, Peru, Chile and Argentina.
A marine pollution survey hailed as the world's largest is kicking off, led by a collaboration between CSIRO, the Oak Family Foundation and Schmidt Marine Technology Partners. Countries taking part include China, Bangladesh, Indonesia, Vietnam, the US, Australia, South Korea and Taiwan.
Planning is underway for WA's Wave Energy Centre of Excellence , as part of the WA Government's $19.5 million commitment to developing Albany as a renewable energy hub.
WA has formed a STEM Advisory Panel chaired by its Chief Scientist, Professor Peter Klinken. The panel aims to develop a state STEM strategy.
GetSwift Limited (ASX: GSW) has signed a commercial multi-year agreement with FRF Couriers.
A new space studies program will see $50,000 in SA Government funding for the University of South Australia's International Space University's Southern Hemisphere Space Studies Program.
The Federal Government has released a public consultation paper on proposed changes to telecommunications carrier powers and immunities laws.
The second phase of Tasmania's LoRaWAN wireless internet pilot in Launceston has been launched. The LoRaWAN network enables computer sensors embedded in everyday objects to interact with each other.
A $2 million donation will see the State Library Victoria establish Start Space , a centre to support early-stage entrepreneurs. The funding from Christine Christian will give entrepreneurs access to resources, services, programs and mentors.
Genome.One is partnering with US health network Sanford Health to develop a new self-assessment platform for US hospitals and clinics. Genome.One's self-phenotyping platform will link patient-provided data with research and genetic knowledge.
Allocate Software has signed a five-year contract with Eastern Health to implement its rostering, time and attendance software across all 50 Eastern Health facilities.

CompanyWatch

OneShift has been acquired by Programmed (ASX:PRG). Programmed invested in OneShift in 2013 and began the process of acquiring OneShift late last year in a private, off-market deal. Gen George who founded OneShift will continue to focus on Skilld (the small business hospitality recruitment app) and the newly announced tamme, an ad-tech platform for double sided marketplaces.
NAB Ventures and Reinventure have invested in open banking API platform startup Basiq .
Ubiquick has completed the acquisition of Paul Budde Communications, the Australian telecom research firm that Paul Budde started 40 years ago. Paul Budde will remain with the firm until the end of the year to ensure a smooth transfer and Uniquick will continue to keep BuddeComm trading under its own name.
Sydney marketing automation company, Autopilot has raised $12 million in funding , in a round led by Blackbird Ventures with participation from Salesforce Ventures, Rembrandt Venture Partners and Southern Cross Venture Partners.
Spark New Zealand is acquiring marketing automation provider Ubiquity , to blend Ubiquity's marketing software with Spark's big data and analytics software business, Qrious .
Vocus (ASX: VOC) has received a proposal from Kohlberg Kravis Roberts & Co to acquire 100% of Vocus' shares at $3.50 per share. The Vocus Board of Directors has formed an independent board committee review to assess the proposal, chaired by David Spence.
ROKT has completed a $15 million Series B investment led by Moelis Australia Asset Management, with participation from Time Inc and existing investors John Ho, Lachlan Murdoch, Greg Roebuck and Square Peg Capital. The funds will accelerate development of ROKT's transaction marketing technology, expand its management, extend international market growth and acquire strategic assets.
