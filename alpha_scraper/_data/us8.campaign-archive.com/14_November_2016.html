news briefs

Will you be watching the supermoon tonight? Otherwise known as 'perigee syzygy', it will be the biggest supermoon in 70 years. According to ANU astronomer Dr Brad Tucker, the best time to check out the moon will be sunset tonight, with a clear view to the eastern horizon.
The Office of the Chief Scientist has released a new paper, ' Busting the Myths about Women in STEM ', as well as a datasheet illustrating how Australia's gender STEM imbalance operates from the classroom to the workplace. Myths include : that girls are bad at maths; that women are disinterested in STEM careers; that the gender pay gap does not exist; and that the battle against sexism in STEM has been won.
Nominations are open for the inaugural BigInsights Data Innovation Awards for companies that have used data analytics and Internet of Things technology to innovate.
CSIRO has developed technology to allow diabetics to have their eyes tested by a GP. The diabetic retinopathy screening will mean patients don't need referrals to a specialist for the tests. A trial is to take place in Perth.
Adyen has deployed its infrastructure into Equinix's Melbourne and Sydney data centres.
Melbourne-based XENON Systems will supply a Lenovo NeXtScale system to the National Computational Infrastructure to improve their capacity and provide access to high-performance computing capability.
Queensland farmers can now use drones to spray crops due to a change in legislation. The legislation requires that UAV spraying operations are only performed by pilots who are authorised by the Civil Aviation Safety Authority and hold qualifications that demonstrate a suitable level of chemical application competency.
CSIRO is on the hunt for the next generation of supercomputer . The new computer is to replace the current Bragg accelerator cluster. The replacement will be capable of 'petaflop' speeds. The tender process will close on 19 December 2016, with the new system expected to be up and running in 2017.
Parkes radio telescope is working with two US telescopes to find out if civilisations exist elsewhere. The collaboration is part of the 10-year, $100 million Breakthrough Listen initiative, which is searching for intelligent life. Last week Parkes observed a newly-discovered earth-sized planet orbiting Proxima Centauri, the nearest star to our sun.
A new fabrication method could offer a way to create more efficient and cheaper solar electricity . According to ANU research, the method improves the performance of perovskite solar cells, which can combine with conventional silicon solar cells to produce more efficient electricity.
Optus ' new alliance with NAS Australia will expand the distribution of the SatSleeve product range into remote and regional Australia.
Pointerra Limited (ASX: 3DP) has signed a trial licence agreement with the CRC for Spatial Information (CRCSI) . As part of the trial, CRCSI will integrate Pointerra's technology with its QA4LiDAR quality assurance tool to add visualisation, data hosting and management capabilities.
Melbourne-based Amaero Engineering and partner Monash University have signed an agreement with Safran , a global aerospace and defence company, to print turbojet components at its factory in Toulouse, France.
Invigor Group Limited (ASX: IVO) is rolling out its Insights Visitor platform at locations including Zoos Victoria's Melbourne Zoo, Werribee Open Plains Zoo and Healesville Sanctuary, as well as an unnamed major Sydney CBD shopping precinct. The platform provides information about visitor behaviour.
Peppermint Innovation Limited (ASX: PIL) has signed a letter of intent with Globe subsidiary G-Xchange. Networks connected to Peppermint's platform will use GCash products and services.
The Federal Government has invested $1.6 million to develop printed solar panels which could reduce the cost of electricity. CSIRO , NSW-based startup Solafast and Melbourne printing company Norwood are partners on the project.

company watch

The reverse takeover of Excalibur by Dropsuite is set to be finalised after the announcement of a lodgement with the ASX. The Excalibur/ Dropsuite acquisition will offer up to 80 million shares, resulting in a raise of up to $8 million. Dropsuite expects the takeover to be finalised by 30 November, with a listing on the ASX also expected by this date.
Accelerus has acquired Xtreme Software’s Xuno school management software.
Swift Networks Group Limited (ASX: SW1) will raise $2 million in a placement to fund its acquisition of Web2TV and Living Networks.
Telstra Ventures has invested in US-based Boomtown which provides support software and technical services to technology providers and SMEs.
Wentronic will make a conditional proportional off market takeover bid for 83% of Cellnet's (ASX: CLT) shares. The offer values Cellnet at around $14.6 million, with Wentronic paying up to $12.26 million.

people watch

Dr Charles Day has been appointed CEO of the Office of Innovation and Science Australia (ISA). Day was previously Program Director for the University of Melbourne's STEM innovation precinct, the Carlton Connect initiative. He headed up Melbourne Ventures from 2004-2010 and co-founded Melbourne’s Accelerator Program . A Rhodes Scholar, Day completed his PhD in jet engine dynamics at Oxford University.
Three new ISA board appointments were also made, including: Professor Bronwyn Harch - Executive Director of the Institute for Future Environments and Professor of Applied Statistics at the Queensland University of Technology; Dr Rufus Black, President of Museum Victoria and Deputy Chancellor of Victoria University; and Dr Bronte Adams AM - Managing Director of Dandolo Partners. Two new members of the Cooperative Research Centre Advisory Committee were also announced: research-orientated cancer surgeon Professor Christobel Saunders; and engineer and CEO of Bellbery Ltd Kylie Sproston.
Suzanne Miller is to become the next Chief Scientist for Queensland. Miller is currently CEO and Director of QLD Museum Network. She replaces Dr Geoff Garrett AO, who has held the position since 2011.
Dr Tobias Feakin has been appointed as Australia’s first Cyber Ambassador to support the Prime Minister’s Cyber Security Strategy .
Steve Hoy will be the new CEO of Resolve Software Group beginning 5 December 2016. Hoy was formerly the company’s COO.
The new Chief Operating Officer of Kabuni (ASX: KBU) is Frans Tjallingii .
Manalto Limited (ASX: MTL) has appointed Gary Cox as a non-executive Director. This follows the recent announcement of new Chair Jim McKerlie, as part of a board renewal process. Cox has held leadership roles with Microsoft, News Corp subsidiary TrueLocal, and EMC.
Australia Post's Managing Director and CEO Ahmed Fahour will join the board of directors of global parcel express delivery and logistics company Aramex. According to Australia Post, "this is a key part of the strategic eCommerce alliance between Australia Post and Aramex".
