news briefs

Prime Minister Malcolm Turnbull has announced his cabinet including the following appointments - Christopher Pyne has been appointed Minister for Industry, Innovation and Science. Turnbull said this Ministry "will drive the Government's focus on investing in science; promoting Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematic education; supporting start-ups and bringing together innovation initiatives right across Government". Senator Mitch Fifield has been appointed Minister for Communications, Minister for the Arts and Minister Assisting the Prime Minister for Digital Government. Wyatt Roy is the Assistant Minister for Innovation and Paul Fletcher has been made Minister for Territories, Local Government and Major Projects.
Teaching coding in Australian schools is to become a reality following the endorsement of the Coding across the Curriculum initiative as part of the Foundation to Year 10 Australian Curriculum Technologies, endorsed by the Education Council . The Education Council members will work together to develop a national strategy to encourage more students to study STEM subjects. Four STEM education initiatives are to be funded by the Government - the development of innovative mathematics curriculum resources; supporting the introduction of computer coding across different year levels; establishing a P-TECH-style school pilot site; funding summer schools for STEM students from underrepresented groups.
There are almost 1500 signatures on the Save Australian Technology Park as a technology hub online petition. If you have yet to sign, you can do so here .
Localz , which provides micro-location technology has won IBM SmartCamp Australia 2015 . Bugwolf , CryptoPhoto , Level35 and Moroku were the other finalists.
NEXTDC has signed a three year $1.79 million contract with the Federal Government Agency for the provision of data centre services.
$14.2 million of funding from under the Capability and Demonstrator Program managed by the Defence Science and Technology Group has been awarded projects which develop and demonstrate innovative technologies that can enhance defence capability. The successful proposals were submitted by Armor Composite Engineering , CSIRO , Micro-X , Ocius Technology , University of Canberra , the Bureau of Meteorology and Deakin University .
NSW Police Force has selected Fujitsu for a fully self-contained Body Worn Video solution to help field officers to collect evidence and streamline administrative processes. Fujitsu has partnered with m-View Live Video to customise the solution to the needs of the NSW Police.
Service Stream Limited (ASX: SSM) has signed a five year agreement with the nbn worth $8-10 million which will see SSM responsible for allotted work in planning and implementing the construction of broadband infrastructure including Fibre to the Node, Fibre to the Premise and Fibre to the Basement.
The Department of Human Services is seeking expressions of interest to help select a commercial off-the-shelf integrated technology platform to support the Welfare Payment Infrastructure Transformation programme. Procurement of Systems Integrators will begin in 2016. The Expressions of Interest listing can be viewed on AusTender .
The Glebe Computer Project, which provides inner city residents with computer and internet access free of charge, has upgraded its collection of 12 computers with a $5,500 grant from the City of Sydney .

company watch

Congratulations to Blackbird Ventures for raising a $200 million fund to continue investing in Australian technology startups. Blackbird plans to keep investing in 8-10 companies per year but the new fund will now give them the ability to provide follow on funding from the seed and series A investments they make. First State Super and HostPLUS are both investors in the funds alongside successful tech entrepreneurs.
Bulletproof (ASX: BPF) is to acquire 100% of Infoplex Pty Ltd for $3.55 million cash.
Crowd Mobile (ASX: CM8) is to acquire Netherland based Track Holdings for $35.5 million.

people watch

zipMoney Limited (ASX: ZML) has appointed Jonathan Kelly as Director of Merchant Services. Kelly was most recently Head of Retail Services at PayPal . zipMoney has just listed on the ASX after raising $5 million via a reverse takeover of Rubianna Resources as announced earlier this year.
Stephen Lake has announced he intends to retire as Managing Director and Chief Executive Officer of GBST (ASX: GBST). Lake joined GBST in 2001 and drove the ASX listing in 2005 and has overseen the market capitalisation grow from $44 million to more than $300 million and revenues from $22 million to $114 million in that time. Lake will be involved in the selection of a new successor and will leave the company during 2016, when the handover to the new CEO is completed.
