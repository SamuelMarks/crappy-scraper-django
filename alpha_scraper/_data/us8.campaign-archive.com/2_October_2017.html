NewsBriefs

The Royal Australian Air Force (RAAF) and UNSW Canberra are partnering on a three-year space research and development program . The Government is investing $9.96 million to support the growth of Australian space technologies.
The Joint Standing Committee on the National Broadband Network (NBN) has released its report on the rollout of the National Broadband Network . The Committee recommends that the Australian Government directs and enables NBN to complete as much as possible of the remaining fixed line network using FTTC at a minimum (or FTTP), and require NBN to produce a costed plan and timetable under which that would be achieved. The Committee has also called for a full audit of the project's financial assumptions.
The Australian Academy of Science has launched their Vision for space and technology in Australia.
CSIRO has secured access to the NovaSAR satellite , which when launched will enable access to an advanced form of radar technology, S-band Synthetic Aperture Radar - which provides high resolution images of earth from space. The developer Surrey Satellite Technology Ltd has agreed to a 10% share of tasking an acquisition time with CSIRO. The agreement is worth $10.45 million over seven years, and gives CSIRO the right to direct the satellite's activity in Australia. It could assist with rapid natural disaster identification, infrastructure mapping, crop monitoring, and detecting of illegal deforestation.
The ANU's National Security College has won over a $500,000 grant from the Carnegie Corporation of New York to fund research into how new technologies for detecting submarines will impact nuclear weapons strategies in Asia.
Kyckr (ASX: KYK) has signed a Master Services Agreement with IBM.
The eight smart cities startups chosen to participate in BlueChilli’s CityConnect accelerator program receiving $25,000 in pre-seed capital include: Civic Switchboard, Coworkally, InnDox, NAQMap, Recrew, SciGround, Sirius and Smart Linc.
ICANN (Internet Corporation for Assigned Names and Numbers) is postponing the plan to change the cryptographic key that helps protect the Domain Name System. Recent data has shown that a number of resolvers used by ISPs and Network Operators are not ready for the Key Rollover .
Entries for the 2018 iAwards are open.
A NSW Government-funded business innovation centre has opened at Macquarie University.
A new innovation program will provide NSW businesses with grants of up to $250,000 to support joint R&D projects between NSW and Israel. Applications for the NSW-Israel R&D Tech Innovation Program close on 18 December.
Internet subscribers increased by 2.1% in Australia from the end of December 2016 to 13.7 million. Fibre connections have grown by 49.8% in the six months since December 2016, and an increase of 123% in fibre connections between June 2016 - June 2017.
The City of Adelaide and Government of South Australia are collaborating with SA Power Networks, Mitsubishi and Tesla to provide super fast direct current charging services with eight stations opening in Adelaide already and a further 11 stations to be installed by the end of November. The Electric Vehicle Charging Hub will be able to charge a Mitsubishi Outlander PHEV in 30 minutes, and will also house Tesla Superchargers, the first of a network of 50 charging points across South Australia.
The Federal Government has put aside $12 million and is seeking expressions of interest to upgrade the mobile telecommunications infrastructure between Wyong and Hornsby on the Central Coast rail line.
The ANU will lead a new project delivered through the Defence Materials Technology Centre to enhance Australia's defence capability by building industrial capability in sensor and on-board data processing for drones and small satellites .
UK online food delivery marketplace Appetise is preparing to enter the Australian market , opening a $4.8 to $6.8 million initial public offering ahead of a planned listing on the ASX in November.

CompanyWatch
