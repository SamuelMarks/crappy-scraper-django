news briefs

Two research teams, one led by Professor Andrew Duzrak and one by Professor Andrea Morello, working in the same laboratories at UNSW Australia have created two types of qantum bits or "quibits" - the building blocks for quantum computers - that each process quantum data with an accuracy above 99%. The two findings, obtained simultaneously with quite different systems, have both been published in the journal Nature Nanotechnology . The quantum bit devices were constructed at UNSW at the Australian National Fabrication Facility, with support from researchers at the University of Melbourne and the Australian National University. The research was funded by the Australian Research Council, the US Army Research Office, the NSW Government, UNSW Australia and the University of Melbourne.
Analytica Ltd (ASX:ALT) and SportsMed Global have agreed to pursue detailed planning for the integration of the PeriCoach pelvic floor exercise system and the SportsMed system. The SportMed system was originally developed in conjunction with the New Zealand Rugby Union and has offices in Australia, NZ, UK and is opening an office in the USA in early 2015. Analytica's lead product is the PericCoach ehealth treatment system which has been approved for use in Australia with product launched expected in Europe and the US in 2015.
The Victorian Government has announced an $18.8 million investment plan to increase the number of medtech companies operating in Victoria.
Swinburne University of Technology has partnered with Cisco to collaborate on research initiatives into the ‘Internet of Everything’. The research aims to pursue innovative approaches in learning environments to support student participation and education objectives.
The Federal Government has announced the 6,000 locations in Australia that have been nominated for the Government’s $100 million mobile black spot program .
According to the Australian Bureau of Statistics (ABS), Australians downloaded nearly one million terabytes of data in the three months to December last year. The ABS has also found that Australians download at speeds of 8 Mbps or greater with mobile wireless accounting for half of all connections.
Deakin University’s Motion Labs research centre has entered into a strategic partnership with Appearition Pty Ltd .
Yahoo7 has partnered with the Australian Securities Exchange (ASX) to become the exclusive sales representative for banner advertising across the ASX network on desktop and mobile sites.
The Federal Government has released a new cloud computing policy replacing its 2013 version to encourage government departments and agencies to adopt cloud procurements. Under the guidelines, departments will need to adopt the cloud where it is fit for purpose, provides protection of data and delivers.

company watch

Australian entrepreneur Simon Hackett has made a major investment in Oakdale Resources (ASX:OAR) purchasing one quarter ($1.5m) of Oakdale’s $6 million initial public offering. Oakdale Resources attracted Hackett's investment "because it appears to have located high quality and easily extracted graphite in an accessible location, just as market demand is about to pick up.... We consider graphite is a disruptive example of mining that has genuine 'green' aspects because graphite enables so much development of sustainable technology,” Hackett said.
Following shareholder approval for Cuscal to acquire SPS, Bendigo and Adelaide Bank and Mastercard will both assume a 10% stake in Cuscal. Bendigo and Mastercard jointly launched SPS in 2006. The SPS merger will firm Cuscal's standing as the only independent ADP licenced wholesale payments company.
Ingogo raised $9 million recently with Australian equity-based crowd-funding platform VentureCrowd contributing $1.2 million. Ingogo was founded in 2011 by Hamish Petrie, who sold his previous business Moshtix to News Limited in 2007. While Ingogo began as a taxi booking and in-app payments model, it evolved in 2013 to become a full mobile payments business. Ingogo is targeting an initial public offering in 2015.

people watch

The Commonwealth Scientific and Industrial Research Organisation (CSIRO) has appointed Dr Larry Marshall as its new Chief Executive effective January 2015. Marshall will join CSIRO in January 2015, replacing Dr Megan Clark who has led CSIRO for the past six years. Marshall is currently Managing Director of Southern Cross Venture Partners and is an experienced entrepreneur, holds 20 patents and has a doctorate in physics from Macquarie University. Marshall will be an industry leader at Tech23 2014.
Seven West Media (ASX:SWM) has appointed Clive Dickens as its new Chief Digital Officer effective 2015. Dickens is currently at Southern Cross Austereo as Director of Digital and Innovation.
Xero has appointed Andy Lark as its new Chief Marketing Officer. Lark was previously Chief Marketing Officer at Commonwealth Bank (ASX:CBA).
