NewsBriefs

A collaboration between Defence and industry will develop the ' Flight Recorder ', a Black Box for soldiers. Industry partners Myriota ( Tech23 alumnus) and IMeasureU were selected to develop the Flight Recorder, and will receive $700,000 from the Next Generation Technologies Fund to develop it.
Graphene-reinforced smart composites will be developed through a collaborative project between Swinburne University and Imagine Intelligent Materials . The six-month project is supported by a $20,000 Swinburne University Seed Grant.
Surgeons at a Queensland hospital have become the first to transplant a 3D printed shinbone (tibia) into a patient. The model was wrapped in biological tissue in an effort to engineer new bone.
Optus is using BroadSoft Business to deploy its new cloud-based Unified Communications solution for service providers.
A new flight data app has been created by Qantas pilots and GE Aviation. FlightPulse provides pilots with data that helps them fly more efficiently and reduce carbon emissions.
$33.8 million will be invested to modernise SmartRide , Transperth's cashless ticketing system. A new system will be introduced to provide for expected growth as part of METRONET.
The ABC has announced a $1 million, one year Podcast Fund . Submissions will be called for in the coming months.
A Senate Committee report has recommended that Parliament pass the telecommunications reform package . ACCAN and the Regional, Rural and Remote Communications Coalition are happy with the recommendation.
The Vocus-owned Australia Singapore Cable will be made available to customers in Equinix 's International Business Exchange data centres in Melbourne and Sydney.
Diversity Council Australia and the University of Sydney Business School have published a report on challenges faced by culturally diverse women in accessing leadership roles. The research was supported by Aurecon, Google, Deloitte and CBA.
Brazil and Australia have signed an Agreement for Cooperation on Science, Technology and Innovation.
RMIT University won first place in the 2017 National Instruments Autonomous Robotics Competition in Sydney.
Australians are encouraged to have their say on the safety regulation of drones in a new discussion paper.
The world's first automated life jacket, a cooling device for mine workers, and a drone with improved flight and hover capacity are amongst the 85 businesses who will receive $10 million in Advance Queensland funding . View the recipients here .
If you're a Western Australian-based startup , business or individual working on location-based products or services, you can apply for the second round of SPURonWA grants , which opens today. Applications close on 6 October 2017.
Progress has been made on improving online gambling protections , with two measures (limits on inducements and ease of account closure) agreed in-principle by gambling ministers last week.
The potential for Australia's critical commodities like lithium and cobalt that support our 'high-tech' lifestyles is under investigation in new analysis by Geoscience Australia and University of Western Australia's Centre for Exploration Targeting.
The suicide of a teenager following a reported online and offline bullying campaign has led to the Office of the eSafety Commissioner and the Carly Ryan Foundation reinforcing a holistic approach to dealing with cyber bullying and online issues.
Sendle has signed a major deal with eBay.
RMIT will be home to the 'world's first research centre on the social science of blockchain'. The RMIT Blockchain Innovation Hub will be led by Professor Jason Potts.
Windlab (ASX: WND) has secured a 10-year agreement with Queensland government-owned corporation CS Energy to purchase the renewable energy and some of the large-scale generation certificates produced from Kennedy Energy Park Phase I. The park is a 60.5MW hybrid renewable energy project in far north Queensland.
Queensland's Metro North Hospital and Health Service has deployed the Nutanix Enterprise Cloud Platform across its five hospitals.

CompanyWatch

Updater (ASX: UPD) has raised $50 million via an institutional placement.
Online share registry platform Registry Direct Limited will list on the ASX. The company has lodged its Prospectus with ASIC to raise up to $6 million via an IPO.
Asia Pacific Data Centre (APDC) has been in ongoing discussions with 360 Capital Group regarding the development of its alternative proposal to acquire APDC Securities. The aim of the discussions is to maximise value by 360 Capital "providing a certain offer capable of consideration by APDC securityholders which is superior to the unconditional cash offer by NEXTDC".
Superloop (ASX: SLC) is to acquire fixed wireless internet service provider NuSkope and its associated entities RA-WIFI and RA-ADSL. Consideration for the strategic acquisition includes $7 million in cash plus $3 million in Superloop shares.
Predictive decision support system for agricultural growers, CropLogic, will list on the ASX tomorrow.

PeopleWatch

Dr Heather Smith will move from the Department of Communications and the Arts to be the Secretary of the Department of Industry, Innovation and Science.
Bede Hackney has been named as ANZ Country Manager for Tenable . Hackney was previously ANZ Managing Director at Nimble Storage.
Tim Hoffman is to be Megaport's (ASX: MP1) Chief Technology Officer . Hoffman previously led the Global Network team at Twitter and led the development of New Zealand's communications infrastructure over the past decade.
Mark Ratcliffe has joined the 2degrees board. Ratcliffe was previously Managing Director and Chief Executive of Chorus.
Telstra has appointed Vicki Brady as Group Executive, Consumer and Small Business. She replaces Kevin Russell in the role.
