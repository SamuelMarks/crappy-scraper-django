NewsBriefs

Westpac has partnered with Stone & Chalk to launch data accelerator FUELD , connecting early-stage startups to powerful data-sets on Data Republic's data sharing platform. Westpac's venture capital firm Reinventure will provide mentorship, Amazon Web Services will provide infrastructure, and former CEO of Fishburners Murray Hurps will run the FUELD program.
Monash University has launched a $3 million incubation facility as part of its Food Innovation Centre. Greek yoghurt company Chobani is the first client to use the incubator.
$6.61 million from the NSW Medical Device Fund will go towards medical innovations including SpeeDx's first single test which detects sexually transmitted infections and antibiotic resistance ($2.5 million); MedLogical Innovations' ProFocal-Rx, which allows over 30% of prostate cancer patients to have less invasive therapy and preserve their prostate gland ($1.25 million); Baymatob's patch for real-time monitoring of pregnancy and labour ($1.47 million); and Western Sydney Local Health District's Mu Catheter to reduce hypertension ($1.39 million).
A brain-computer interface (BCI) has improved motor function in stroke patients, according to University of Adelaide research. The BCI produced a 36% improvement in motor function of a stroke-damaged hand.
ANU plans to drive an 'artificial intelligence revolution' in Australia with a new 10-year program led by Professor Genevieve Bell. Bell will lead a new Autonomy, Agency and Assurance Institute, co-founded with CSIRO's Data61. On another note, Bell has also just been announced to give this yearâ€™s Boyer Lecture .
A new 'one stop shop' for the medtech industry has been created by CSIRO, Monash University and Monash Health Translation Precinct. The M2 precinct in Victoria will help to accelerate the development of medical technology.
nbn is changing its "check your address" function to clarify when consumers can actually order a service and connect to the NBN.
Stargroup Limited (ASX:STL) has signed a joint venture with DigitalX Limited (ASX:DCC) to jointly tailor and offer a "Two-Way ATM" solution for buying and selling Bitcoin .
LaunchVic has opened its fourth round of funding , with up to $3 million to invest per application from Australian or international accelerators and teams with track records for running accelerators. Applications close Tuesday 10 October (the same day that Tech23 kicks off!).
Optus Satellite has extended its services contract with Seven Network for transponder capacity on the Optus D1 satellite. The term was extended to 2021, with financial details not disclosed.
National eSmart Week kicks off today, hosted by the Alannah and Madeline Foundation.
A ' virtual power plant ' launched last week in Queensland is the result of a partnership between Energy Queensland's Energy Services division and GreenSync Pty Ltd. The plant is a cloud-based, load control system to manage extreme electricity demand during severe temperature events.
The successful teams for the latest CSIRO ON Prime program have been announced. Innovations selected include wearable medical technology, a teen mental health and resilience program, and a bee tracking conservation device.
Monash University has licensed a lymphatic drug targeting technology platform to PureTech Health. The technology was developed by Professor Chris Porter's group at the ARC Centre of Excellence for Bio-Nano Science and Technology at the Monash Institute of Pharmaceutical Sciences. The technology will be developed and moved towards clinical trial by PureTech Health through a subsidiary startup, Glyph Biosciences, in collaboration with Dr Porter's lab.
The City of Melbourne has partnered with the Jiangsu-Suzhou Science and Technology Town, RMIT University, the University of Melbourne, the Victorian Government and Australia China Association of Scientists and Entrepreneurs to open the Jiangsu-Victoria Innovation Centre . Suzhou High-Tech Venture Capital Group will provide up to $80 million over three years.

CompanyWatch

Citadel Group Limited (ASX:CGL) has acquired Charm Health - supplier of specialist oncology e-health systems - for an upfront payment of $8.2 million .
Brisbane-based managed cloud services provider Fluccs has acquired Exigent Australia, Virtual DataCentre and 10TB Servers.
Ten Network's administrators have progressed the sale and recapitalisation transaction with CBS Corporation. The refinance component of the transaction was completed last week, with CBS funding around $142.7 million by way of loan.
