NewsBriefs

Blockchain technology is being used to prevent illegal fishing and human rights abuses. WWF has partnered with ConsenSys, TraSeable and Sea Quest Fiji to introduce the technology to the Pacific Islands' tuna industry. The hunt is on to find a retailer to partner in the project and use blockchain to complete the tuna's traceability story. Customers will be able to scan a QR code with their smartphone and trace tuna from 'bait to plate'.
CSIRO, the Australian Science Teachers Association and Questacon are partnering on the Stem X Academy , a five-day residential learning program for primary and secondary science teachers.
The Victorian Government is partnering with Stone & Chalk and YBF Ventures (formerly York Butter Factory) to launch two new financial technology hubs . Stone & Chalk's hub will be located at the Goods Shed North in Docklands, while YBF Ventures hub will be located at 520 Bourke Street.
The Federal Government has issued a direction to ACMA to implement new protections and will provide $8.7 million over three years to support measures to improve customer service during transition to the NBN.
Car owners can opt-in to the Vehicle Tracking Initiative by having hidden GPS tracking devices installed in their cars free-of-charge at participating dealerships. The one-year trial to live-track stolen cars is funded by the National Motor Vehicle Theft Reduction Council, which itself received a two-year funding package of over $590,000 from the Victorian Government.
The SaaS accelerator Acceleprise is expanding to Melbourne in partnership with Adir Shiffman and Rohit Bhargava.
The Victorian Government will not continue with the Commonwealth Mobile Black Spot program - instead investing $11 million to build new mobile towers across regional Victoria, in partnership with the telecommunications industry.
The Australian Federal Police has put a call out for technology to allow the public to provide audio and video media, imagery, email or location data to police.
The Victorian Government will build a new wind farm with battery storage to power the expansion of Stawell’s Nectar Farms, as part of a 15 year support agreement with Neoen Australia to deliver the Bulgana Green Power Hub.
Victorian farmers will receive $30 million in government funding to help manage energy costs through improved efficiency and new technology. $20 million will be allocated to grants to support farmers to invest in energy efficiency and generation technology and the remaining funds are to be distributed to initiatives such as energy efficiency assessments and linking farmers and businesses to educational resources and apps.
The Federal Court of Australia has dismissed Vodafone Hutchison Australia's application for a judicial review of the ACCC's conduct in holding a public inquiry and making a draft decision not to declare a domestic mobile roaming service. You can read Vodafone’s response here .
The first Defence Cooperative Research Centre (CRC) for Trusted Autonomous Systems was announced late last year, with inaugural members including BAE Systems Australia, RMIT University, DefendTex and the Department of Defence, represented by Defence Science and Technology Group.
The Federal Government has announced a program of work to implement a new Universal Service Guarantee to give Australians access to voice and broadband services.
Enex TestLab has signed an agreement with James Cook University to build Australia's first testing and certification centre for IoT devices , monitors and sensors. The agreement is supported by Huawei.
Vodafone will improve the identity verification of prepaid mobile customers after an ACMA investigation found the company failed to verify over 1000 customers before activating their prepaid mobile services. An enforceable undertaking accepted by ACMA means Vodafone is required to improve its processes.
Melbourne firm AMOG Technologies has contracted with Thales on new research into stealth technology for submarine periscopes and optronic masts.
Afilias Australia - a wholly owned subsidiary of Afilias plc - has been appointed to provide registry services for the .au domain from 1 July 2018, as part of auDA's Registry Transformation Project.
Smart Parking (ASX:SPZ) has received a letter of intent from the City of Adelaide to provide Smart Parking sensors, smartphone guidance and payment application and maintenance services. The company has also received a contract award notice from the City of Moonee Valley in Melbourne, for the installation of 1500 parking sensors.
ACMA has introduced new regulations to allow Australian road traffic authorities to roll out intelligent transport systems that enable vehicle-to-vehicle, vehicle-to-person or vehicle-to-infrastructure communications. The Radiocommunications (Intelligent Transport Systems) Class Licence 2017 will support the use of complying wireless technologies and devices.
ActewAGL Retail, Harvey Norman Commercial Division, ITP Home Energy, Power Saving Centre, Solargain and SolarHub were successful in receiving over $3 million in government grants to support the roll-out of solar battery storage in Canberra. A further two companies, EPC Solar and Evergen, will continue to operate under the program.
The Greater Launceston Transformation $3.5 million Project - a collaboration between state, federal and local governments, the University of Tasmania and Telstra - will transform city planning processes and develop a community co-designed innovation hub. Launceston is a primary test site for emerging 5G technologies and will be a first-comer for a full Telstra 5G rollout.
The Australian Securities and Investments Commission has licensed the first crowd-sourced funding (CSF) intermediaries under the new CSF regime. Seven companies have been issued with Australian Financial Services licence authorisations to act as intermediaries able to provide a crowd-sourced funding service. This enables eligible public companies to use the CSF scheme to raise capital by offering shares to investors via the intermediaries' online platforms.
A tripartite agreement between UNSW, Maoneng Australia and Origin Energy will see 100% of the university's energy supplied by photovoltaic solar energy. The 15 year solar supply agreement will enable UNSW to achieve its goal of carbon neutrality on energy use by 2020.

PeopleWatch

In a cabinet reshuffle in December, Michaelia Cash was named Minister for Jobs and Innovation. Senator Cash was previously Minister for Women, a role now taken on by Kelly O'Dwyer. Michael Keenan will join Cabinet as Minister for Human Services and Minister Assisting the Prime Minister for Digital Transformation. Australia's Deans of Science have since expressed their disappointment at the exclusion of science in the reshuffle. President of the Australian Deans of Science asked: "Why would Innovation be separated from Science?"
Former CSIRO scientist Dr Nico Adams will be the Director of Swinburne's Factory of the Future. Adams, who is an Industry 4.0 specialist and previously worked for Data61 and the Innovative Manufacturing CRC, will start at what Swinburne is calling "the first Industry 4.0 University-based enterprise in Australia" next month.
Alfred Lo is now a Partner at Tank Stream Ventures. Lo is also Investor-in-Residence at Cicada Innovations and was previously Head of AMP New Ventures.
Jamie Leach has taken on the role of General Manager (Australia and New Zealand) at SMG. Leach was previously CEO of Tech23 alumnus Gruntify.
Veeam has appointed Gary Mitchell as Vice President for ANZ. Mitchell was previously Director of APJ at Perfector Mobile.
Ana Bordeianu is joining Vodafone Hutchison Australia as Customer Operations Director. Bordeianu was most recently at Vodafone Romania.
Jody Weir is to start a new role as Head of Agility at THE ICONIC. She was previously Head of Agility at AMP and Executive Manager Agile Capabilities at Commonwealth Bank.

CompanyWatch

Orica (ORI: ASX) is to acquire GP Holdco (GroundProbe) from Crescent Capital Partners for $205 million. GroundProbe provides critical monitoring and measurement technologies to help the detection of rock-fall. Groundprobe's technology emerged from research undertaken at the University of Queensland.
Tech23 alumnus ReadCloud (ASX:RCL) plans to raise $6 million by listing on the ASX later this month.
Envirosuite Limited (ASX: EVS) has acquired Montreal-based environmental technology provider Odotech.
Mumbrella has been sold to Diversified Communications for an undisclosed sum.
Digital greeting card startup Cardly has raised $230,000 from QUT Creative Enterprise Australia.
Equinix is acquiring data centre company Metronode for $1.035 billion.
Flurosat - a Tech23 2017 alumnus - has raised $1.5 million from CSIRO’s Main Sequence Ventures, Airtree Ventures, and investors led by Cotton Research and Development Corporation.
Equitise has partnered with Xinja to allow investments in Australia's "first independent, 100% digital (soon to be) bank", as part of a $500,000 capital raising contributing to Xinja's $15 million institutional investment round.
Blockchain-based services marketplace CanYa has completed Australia’s second-largest initial coin offering , raising $12 million in cryptocurrency Ethereum over a 30-day period via a crowdsale of its CAN tokens.
Adelaide-based Entech Electronics will double its local production capacity with the help of a State Government Future Jobs Fund grant of $3.5 million .
Following its most recent capital raise of $51 million in a round led by Blackbird Ventures, Canva is now worth US$1 billion.
