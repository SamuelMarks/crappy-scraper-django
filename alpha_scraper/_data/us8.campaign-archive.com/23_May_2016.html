news briefs

900 from New Zealand's tech industry celebrated in Auckland at the 2016 Hi-Tech Awards gala dinner. Vista Group were named Hi-Tech Company of the Year; 90 Seconds won Most Innovative Company and Katherine Corich and Sam Morgan were inducted into the NZ Hi-Tech Hall of Fame.
Myer and eBay have partnered to create what they call the 'world's first Virtual Reality Department Store '. Using eBay Sight Search, over 12,500 products from Myer can be browsed and selected. Customers can receive their own ' shopticals ' (specially designed virtual reality viewers). Sydney visitors can book a place at the VR Department Store .
Netball fans will be able to watch games on their mobile thanks to a new five-year partnership between Netball Australia and Telstra.
Artificial intelligence is being used to run a complex experiment developed by ANU and UNSW ADFA physicists. The experiment creates an extremely cold gas trapped in a laser beam, known as a Bose-Einstein condensate. The team cooled the gas then handed control of the three laser beams to the artificial intelligence. The machine learnt the experiment itself in under an hour. The findings are published in the Nature group journal Scientific Reports .
The Australian Federal Police (AFP) executed two search warrants in Melbourne. Opposition frontbencher Senator Stephen Conroy and the home of an opposition staff member were raided, according to Shadow Attorney-General Mark Dreyfus. The allegations were the subject of a referral from nbn in December 2015. The Communications Minister Senator Mitch Fifield has announced that nbn had informed him that the leak had been referred to the AFP, but "as an AFP investigation was underway, I did not advise other Ministers or the Prime Minister of this matter."
Soprano Design has signed an agreement with Orange Belgium to roll out their Soprano Mobile Enterprise Messaging Suite to enterprise and government customers.
If you have a comment on Telstra's proposed variation to the Migration Plan, the ACCC is inviting submissions to the discussion paper by 20 June 2016.
The ACCC has proposed to deny authorisation to the Australian Payments Clearing Association (APCA) and some of its members to mandate an industry-wide security initiative using 3D Secure security measures to reduce online payments fraud. The ACCC commented that this would require significant costs and reduce competition for online Australian businesses.
The State Library of NSW is partnering with Inside History magazine to create a new website, including a photo-dating tool to help genealogists and family historians to trace their ancestors. Up to 200 images from the State Library's collection, dating from 1788-1955, will be arranged in a chronological timeline.
A hypersonic test flight was conducted successfully by an international team including University of Queensland, Defence Science and Technology Group, the US Air Force Research Laboratory, Boeing and HiFiRE (Hypersonic International Flight Research Experimentation Program). The experimental rocket, HiFiRE 5B, reached speeds of Mach 7.5 (9,200kmph) and a height of 278 kilometres from earth. It was launched from the Woomera Test Range in South Australia. Hypersonic flight would enable travel from Sydney to London in under two hours.
St John Ambulance Australia (NSW) will use VMtech for fully managed hosted infrastructure, network monitoring and managed backup.
Coviu and Chinese medical tourism services company SunWo Medical Management have entered into a Memorandum of Understanding which will licence Coviu’s digital service delivery platform to support health consultations for Chinese citizens.
Kuwait-headquartered Sayed Hamid Behbehani & Sons Co (SHBC) has chosen Aconex Limited (ASX: ACX) to manage information and processes on the US$928 million expansion of Al Farwaniya Hospital in Kuwait City.
An early prototype of a quantum computer has been tested by the University of Western Australia and University of Bristol. The research was published in the journal Nature Communications .
Web Marketing Experts and its mobile-focused offshoot Appscore have announced plans to add 70 new jobs to their Victorian workforce in the next year, with the opening of their new headquarters in Melbourne last week.
Over 50 terabytes of image and video data taken during a deep sea expedition to investigate World War II shipwrecks is now being processed, in a project led by the Western Australian Museum and Curtin University. The organisations have joined with the Pawsey Supercomputing Centre to manage the massive volumes of data.
Haptically-Enabled Robotics (or HER) remote ultrasound technology was developed by Deakin University's Institute for Intelligent Systems Research and Innovation, with funding and technical support from Telstra. Haptics-enabled robotics gives sonographers the ability to conduct ultrasounds on patients remotely, which could improve access to diagnostic tools for Australians in remote and regional areas.
Noosa Shire Council has selected Promapp 's cloud-based process management software to support the organisation's digital transformation.
Invigor Group Limited (ASX: IVO) has partnered with media company The Big Smoke to produce a joint digital advertising product and promote the Shopping Ninja brand.
Oneview Healthcare (ASX: ONE) has signed a multi-year contract with NYU Langone Medical Centre to deploy Oneview's software at its 374 bed Helen L. and Martin S. Kimmel Pavilion in New York.
iCar Asia Limited's strategic relationship agreement with carsales (ASX: CAR) - announced earlier this month - is now in effect.
ReachOut Australia has launched a portal for parents of teenagers with factsheets on bullying, sex, and other social issues; and videos from child psychologists. It features a peer-to-peer forum where parents can connect with each other anonymously.

company watch

Data Republic has secured $10.5 million in a Series A investment round, with Westpac Reinventure, NAB Ventures and Qantas Loyalty confirmed as strategic equity investors. It is NAB Ventures' first investment .
Graph database company DGraph Labs has announced a US$1.1 million seed funding round co-led by Bain Capital Ventures and Blackbird Ventures, with participation from Atlassian's Mike Cannon-Brookes and ex-Googler Mark Cummins. DGraph founder Manish Jain previously worked at Google in the Web Search and Knowledge Graph Infrastructure group.
Northern Iron Limited (ASX: NFE) has executed a binding heads of agreement with Israel-based technology and materials company Dotz Nano Limited to acquire 100% of Dotz's issued capital. Dotz holds the exclusive worldwide life of patent licence for the commercialisation of the patented technology from Rice University (Houston, USA). The technology aims to disrupt the Graphene Quantum Dots market. NFE will undertake a capital raising of not less than $3,500,000 through the offer of NFE shares.

people watch

Damon Rees has been appointed to the newly created Government Chief Information & Digital Officer (GCIDO) role for the NSW Government. Rees was previously at Macquarie Bank, where he was the Chief Digital Officer, having earlier served as the Chief Technology Officer and interim Chief Information Officer at Woolworths, and as Westpac’s Head of Integrated Delivery. The GCIDO will be the senior technology role within the NSW Government, reporting to the Secretary of the Department of Finance, Services and Innovation. Individual agencies will retain Chief Information Officers but all major ICT projects across government departments will require approval and oversight by the GCIDO.
James Deverell is to lead CSIRO’s new business advisory service to help Australian and international businesses to make science and technology investments.
CyberArk has appointed Bruce Nixon as Channel Manager for Australia and New Zealand, and Adel Eid as Senior Director, Channels for Asia Pacific and Japan. Based in Sydney, Nixon was previously with FieldAware and Aruba Networks.
