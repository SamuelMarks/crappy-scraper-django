NewsBriefs

Telstra and Cohda Wireless have conducted Australia's first test of Vehicle-to-Pedestrian technology over a mobile network in South Australia. In the trial, vehicles interacted with pedestrians' and cyclists' mobile phones, providing early-warning collision detection and alerts via an app installed on their mobiles.
A new Australian Smart Cities Consortium was launched at the University of Adelaide. Under a new agreement with the City of Prospect, the Consortium will focus on 'smart parks', using new low-band wifi and other technologies to gather information about the use of public spaces.
The Australian Bureau of Statistics is teaming up with GovHack to challenge Australians to come up with ideas for an innovative 2016 Census data application or product.
The plug has been pulled on a proposal for a coal-fired power station in north Queensland as an expensive and inefficient option for additional electricity generation.
Queensland has launched the “world's largest” EV fast-charging network – the world’s longest in one state.

Phase two of ITP Renewables’ lithium-ion battery trial - a program that looks to evaluate battery storage systems used to help households and businesses manage and store electricity - has been launched at the Bruce Campus of the Canberra Institute of Technology.
3.325 MW of solar photovoltaic plants have been commissioned in ten remote Aboriginal communities in the Northern Territory.
WA-based Aegis Aged Care has adopted the Nutanix platform to support its growth plans, after more than doubling in size over the past 7 years due to a shortage in available aged care beds and WA's aging population.
Brisbane Airport Corporation's (BAC) new online presence will be delivered by Acquia as part of the Airport's modernisation project. BAC is investing $3.8 billion over a decade on redeveloping the Airport.
After receiving an ambit claim from Brisbane City Council for loss and damage against TechnologyOne (ASX: TNE) in excess of $50 million , TechnologyOne is making a counterclaim for the same amount. This follows the termination of the ten-year LGS contract between the Council and TechnologyOne.

CompanyWatch

AGL (ASX: AGL) has announced a $US10 million strategic investment in US-based August Home , a sector leader in smart locks and smart home access products and services.
GlamCorner, a Sydney-based online rental service for designer fashion, has raised $4.2 million in seed funding from AirTree Ventures.
Brian Flannery has invested $10 million into Brisbane-based electric car charging company Tritium.
9 Spokes (ASX: 9SP) has raised $12 million from sophisticated and institutional investors. Proceeds will fund the company's expansion into North America, 'accelerate product roadmap' and expand channel partners.
NEXTDC (ASX: NXT) has announced an unconditional all-cash off-market takeover offer to acquire all securities of Asia Pacific Data Centre Group (ASX: AJD).
Skyfii Limited (ASX: SKF) has acquired key assets from Wicoms Wireless in an all scrip deal at a total value of $247,000.

PeopleWatch

Gabriele Famous has been appointed as Chief Marketing Officer for Aconex (ASX: ACX). Famous previously was Vice President, Global Product Marketing at Zendesk.
Martin Duursma has been appointed as a team member at Main Sequence Ventures, the CSIRO Innovation Fund . Duursma joins Bill Bartee, Tech23 2017 Industry Leader Phil Morle and Mike Zimmerman on the team.
Former Red Balloon CEO Nick Baker has been appointed as CEO of Outdoria and Go See Australia, both divisions of Melbourne-based market platform Marketplacer.
Robert Yang is Seagate Technology 's new Regional Vice President for Asia Pacific sales.
SEEK International's Head of China Simon Rosenberg has moved to become VP Operations at Crimson Consulting.
