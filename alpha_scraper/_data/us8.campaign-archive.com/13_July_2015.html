news briefs

The Queensland Premier , Annastacia Palaszczuk, has announced her Advance Queensland initiative outlining a $50 million "Advance Queensland Best and Brightest Fund" to attract and retain scientific and entrepreneurial talent. As part of the Best and Brightest initiative, the government will undertake a future schools review, conducted by a team of independent experts, into the teaching of STEM and how it could be expanded in Queensland schools. She announced three partnerships to help with commercialisations: UQ and Emory University (Atlanta, US), Siemens and Translational Research Institute , and Johnson and Johnson and QUT . There is also a $76 million Business Investment Attraction package with the aim to encourage Queensland startups including a $12 million Queensland Commercialisation Program to support proof of concept projects and a $40 million Business Development Fund through which the Government will provide seed co-investment to match and encourage greater angel and venture capital investment in Queensland businesses.
Buildkite has entered into an agreement with Shopify (NASDAQ: SHOP) to provide continuous integration tools for their web, mobile and desktop development teams. Melbourne headquartered, Buildkite was founded in September 2013 by Keith Pitt and Tim Lucas.
Covata (ASX:CVT) Safe Share is to be distributed by Colt Technology Services (LON:COLT) through Colt Financial Services Extranet.
Piivot is Sydney’s new digital creative knowledge hub. It’s a collaboration between UTS , the NSW Government , the Commonwealth Bank , Microsoft , ARUP , and Fishburners . Piivot aims to grow and support startups in Sydney, with Ultimo in particular to become a globally competitive hub of digital creativity. The Piivot online portal will be launched later this month.
252 new research projects have been awarded $86.9 million through the Australian Research Council ’s Linkage Projects scheme to connect industry with university researchers. Projects include: A/Prof Ivor Tsang from UTS with Mission Australia - Interaction Mining for Cyberbullying Detection on Social Networks; $394,000 to Associate Professor Lawrence Cavedon from RMIT with SEEK -User-Adaptive search and evaluation for complex information-seeking tasks; $295,467 to Victoria University to improve access to electronic health data for medical research while still ensuring patient privacy; $435,000 for Prof Dikai Liu from UTS with Roads and Maritime Services - An Intelligent Robotic System for Underwater Structure Maintenance.
The Australian Department of Defence has extended its contract with Optus ’ C1 satellite services in delivering managed professional satellite services until 2020.
Congratulations to the 10 business leaders accepted into the Springboard Enterprises Australia 2015 accelerator: Charlotte Petris Invoicebid ; Alison Hardacre Healthkit ; Belinda Adams Oscar Hospitality Solutions ; Tania Jolley DNA Security Solutions ; Carolyn Mee c4mee Productions ; Sarah Mateljan PD Online ; Rebecca Lee Virtualiis; Rebecca Wilson Starts at Sixty ; and Zoe Pointon Open Agent .
Applications for the National Roads and Motorists Association Jumpstart Program are now open until 6 August 2015.

company watch

Sydney-based recruitment start-up The Search Party has acquired employer review platform JobAdvisor for an undisclosed amount.
M2 Group (ASX: MTU) has increased its stake in Aggregato Global Limited by investing $4.9 million, increasing its shareholding to 61.2%, and has appointed two new Directors to the Aggregato board – Michael Simmons and John Allerton.
SpeedCast International Limited (ASX: SDA) has acquired the assets of NewSat (ASX: NWT) from its receivers for $12 million. The assets include the land and buildings at NWT’s teleport facilities in Adelaide and Perth and the associated plant and equipment.
Online screening and verification services company CV Check Ltd (ASX: CV1) is set to list on the Australian Securities Exchange . The company has issued shares at $0.20 per share and is looking to raise $11 million.
REFFIND Limited (ASX: RFN) has commenced trading on the Australian Securities Exchange following a raising of $8 million in an initial public offering. REFFIND was founded in 2014 by Digital4ge who now own 48% of the listed entity. Jamie Pride, who was once CEO of realestate.com.au will be Managing Director while his Digital4ge Co-Founder Ben McGrath will be Chief Customer Officer and Executive Director. REFFIND which offers employee engagement technology, boasts board members - recruitment pioneer Geoff Morgan and former Westpac NZ CEO Peter Clare.

people watch

Geoff Roberts has been appointed as the new Chief Financial Officer of SEEK Limited and will join the company in November 2015, replacing John Armstrong, who will be stepping down at the end of 2015 after 13 years with SEEK. Roberts has been the Managing Partner of Deloitte Victoria and Group CFO of AXA Asia Pacific for seven years.
Dr Ian Gibson, former founding CEO of Intersect , has joined the University of New South Wales as Associate Dean in Industry and Innovation. Working inside UNSW’s engineering faculty, Dr Gibson will oversee the development of engineering research relationships with industry and be the primary contact for companies wanting to work with the university.
The Attorney-General’s Department is on the search for a new CIO. Applications close Thursday 23 July 2015. While the City of Melbourne is on the lookout for a Chief Digital Officer to head up its new ‘Smart City’ office. Applications close Monday 27 July 2015.
John Wylie has joined the Board of Pexip after investing $5 million in the Norwegian-headquartered company which specialises in visual collaboration. Wylie, an investment banker, is also currently Chairman of the Australian Sports Commission.
Jon Cumming has been appointed the ACT ’s first Chief Digital Officer. Cumming previously worked with the New Zealand Government to deliver digital solutions for government.
RMIT University 's 2015 industry mentoring program for undergraduates and postgraduates to share their experiences and professional careers is now open. Mentoring options include Women in IT Engineering and IT Mentoring, Career Mentoring, Pride Mentoring and more. ​
