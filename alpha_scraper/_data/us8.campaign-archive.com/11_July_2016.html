news briefs

Australia received a disappointing report card from the World Economic Forum's Global Information Technology Report 2016 , slipping two spots to 18th position in the Networked Readiness Index. Factors that impacted the downgrade include affordability as "fixed broadband subscriptions remain particularly expensive". The report notes that "there is room for improvement in the level of businesses' adoption of ICTs, as the country still relies heavily on mining industries."
Forcing computers to share could improve efficiency of large data centres by 25%. ANU researchers have developed new system mechanisms to improve energy efficiency. While computer servers that run searches and interact with users are waiting for requests, other processes can be performed.
Tech23 alumni TrademarkVision and IP Australia have launched an internal trade mark search engine. TrademarkVision redeveloped the Australian Trade Mark Search System; IP Australia also leveraged their partnership to enhance further search-related tools.
Take part in this year's Startup Muster , which measures the Australian startup ecosystem.
Victoria will be home to two new wind farms , with the Victorian Government signing contracts with ACCIONA Energy and Windlab. Meanwhile, in South Australia, the first stage of Neoen Australia's Hornsdale Wind Farm was opened. It has begun generating energy into the grid.
Applications for Google's Impact Challenge for Australian non-profits close this Wednesday 13 July 2016.
A new wholesale relationship has been announced between Optus Satellite and designer and provisioner of satellite-based voice and data communications URSYS. URSYS will use Optus' D2 satellite's capacity, and access third party international satellite providers as well as Optus' Belrose-based teleport facilities.
Nuix has partnered with litigation support and big data analysis services company FRONTEO USA. Nuix will support FRONTEO's eDiscovery and digital evidence processing .
Applications for the it SMF Industry Awards for Excellence in IT Service Management close this Friday 15 July 2016.
New Zealand will integrate digital technology into its curriculum. The Government will hold consultations with stakeholders and design new curriculum content ready for integration into the New Zealand Curriculum and Te Marautanga o Aotearoa in 2018.
myki operator NTT Data has won the almost $700 million contract for the operation of myki for the next seven years.
And moving from Victorian to NSW public transport - travel in Sydney and its surrounding regions will be paperless from 1 August 2016. All travellers will need an Opal card or Opal single trip ticket. This will pave the way for a 2017 customer trial for tapping on with credit and debit cards.
The Good Guys are aiming to improve their B2B solutions , announcing a collaboration with IBM Commerce which went live in April.
Seven Western Australian startups will each receive $40,000 after being selected for the Vocus Upstart program. The startups include blockchain tech Veri.Vote ; tourism and experience app Everythere ; online marketplace PrevYou ; B2B supplier discovery platform Filtered ; big data analysis company for the legal system, Jurimetrics ; VR/AR startup Immersia VR ; and school activities' marketplace Yabble . The startups will be based at Spacecubed.
9 Spokes (ASX: 9SP) has announced a channel agreement with Deloitte UK to enable the latter to launch cloud-based service 'Propel'.
There has been much discussion of electronic voting following the drawn-out Australian election, with both the Prime Minister and Opposition Leader giving their support for electronic voting. The AIIA has also voiced their support , calling the current system 'archaic'. You can read an earlier Parliamentary report on electronic voting completed after the 2013 election.

company watch

Telstra Ventures has made a strategic investment in Cloopen, a Chinese-based provider of communications APIs.
Airwallex has secured US$3 million in a pre-Series A funding round.
Kogan.com Ltd (ASX: KGN) was admitted to the ASX last week.
Invigor Group Limited (ASX: IVO) has acquired the US patent to the biometric identity verification services by the My Verified ID group for $600,000. The acquisition includes associated filings to the technology.
Medical technology company Avita Medical Ltd (ASX: AVH) has raised $9 million in a partially underwritten Rights Issue to support the company's US launch and product commercialisation. Avita manufactures portable cell-harvesting devices which can treat burns, chronic wounds and skin defects.

people watch

UNSW has appointed astronomer Professor Brian Boyle as Deputy Vice-Chancellor Enterprise . Professor Boyle will lead a new portfolio to 'drive the University's innovation agenda' as part of UNSW's 2025 Strategy. The new role will have responsibility for engagement with government, business and industry. Professor Boyle is currently Acting Deputy Vice-Chancellor Research.
An investigation into the controversial Catalyst episode 'Wi-Fried' has resulted in reporter Dr Maryanne Demasi's suspension from on-air editorial assignments while ABC TV reviews the program.
Jason Baden is moving from Juniper Networks to Brocade , where he takes on the role of Senior Director for Australia and New Zealand.
B2B matching site Matchboard is expanding into the UK market, appointing Steve Charlton to lead the charge. Matchboard will operate under the brand 'AboutMatch' in the UK.
