NewsBriefs

Curtin University is partnering with Hitachi to create and deploy IoT solutions to enhance student experience and provide insights into campus operations. The partnership will use the university as a "living laboratory" to enable researchers to collaborate on data-driven research.
The CEO of ANZ, Shayne Elliott, has announced that ANZ Bank will adopt a scaled Agile approach . Citing its experience launching Apple Pay in 2016 from concept to viable product in about 10 weeks, ANZ is hoping that by building capabilities through small, collaborative, self-directed teams it will deliver continuous improvement to their customerâ€™s experience. ANZ is already using Agile to deliver around 20% of its technology and digital projects (FYI - AgileAus kicks off in Sydney on 22-23 June, 2017!).
The launch of Telstra Labs is being described as the next phase in the company's 'Innovation Lab journey'. Telstra Labs will comprise innovation and tech capability in an environment designed for 'rapid experimentation' and implementation. Telstra has launched a publicly-accessible GSMA Open IoT Lab out of its Melbourne space, Gurrowa. A new muru-D program will also be opening in Melbourne, based out of Gurrowa Lab.
Complexica has won a tender at Pfizer Australia to deploy its What-If Simulator.
The ACCC proposes not to declare a wholesale domestic mobile roaming service. The draft decision is based on concerns that a declaration would actually increase costs in some areas. Submissions are invited on the decision until 2 June 2017.
A two year project to supply fuel cell vehicles with low-emissions hydrogen will use CSIRO research in separating pure hydrogen from mixed gas streams. The project recently received $1.7 million from the Science and Industry Endowment Fund, to be matched by CSIRO .
Xref 's (ASX: XF1) platform has been integrated with the recruitment technology of San Francisco-headquartered SmartRecruiters.
New digital initiatives are in place to help those affected by ex-Severe Tropical Cyclone Debbie. Advance Queensland Community Digital Champions are helping with disaster recovery efforts in their local communities. Inaugural digital champion Juliette Wright is the founder and CEO of GIVIT, an online donation portal where charities can request specific items for those in need. Local businesses and industry can also access a new online hub, Go Local .
California-based construction solution provider Procore Technologies has opened its first international office in Sydney.
New technology is set to change the experience of those visiting Lanyon, a historic site in Canberra , with a new digital guide in the final stages of testing. Drone technology is also enabling staff to check on maintenance needs of areas with difficult accessibility.
Agile projects and methodology were praised by the Assistant Minister for Cities and Digital Transformation, the Hon Angus Taylor MP, who reported that colleagues quickly grasped the benefits of Agile over waterfall "when I ask them whether they would prefer a 3% or a 58% chance of telling the PM that their project is going well".
The Western Australian Government is planning to upgrade the radio communications system for Perth's urban rail system , with a $120 million budget to move to the digital spectrum. Five shortlisted companies will be asked to respond to a request for proposal.
A package of reforms to Australia's free-to-air broadcasting sector has been announced by the Australian Government. The package includes more restrictions on gambling advertisements during live sports across all platforms, abolishing broadcasting licence fees, and a review of Australian and children's content.
Body-worn cameras for frontline police are being rolled out in South Australia, with $5.9 million invested.
Ten new research projects have received a total of $3.7 million under the Australian Research Council Linkage Projects scheme . Funded projects include: low-cost and scalable commercial smart windows - Griffith University's Professor Huijun Zhao and Confirmation Australia ($513,210); a hybrid fibre-optic/RadioFrequency catheter system and driving software to help cardiac electrophysiologists perform non-invasive lesion assessments - Edith Cowan University's Professor Kamal Alameh and Lazcath Pty Ltd ($360,000); and a scalable computer architecture based on nanoscale motion on a silicon chip that could extend computing performance in environments (like space) where intense radiation causes digital electronics to fail - University of Queensland's Professor Warwick Bowen and Lockheed Martin ($334,710).
Subsidiary of Vista Group International, Movio Limited, has signed a multi-year agreement with Epsilon for access to the Movio Media platform .
Energy storage company Redflow has announced its largest single sale of ZBM2 zinc-bromine flow batteries , worth around $800,000, for multiple sites in the Pacific Islands. Redlow partner Vertiv will provide the batteries for an energy storage solution designed by Hitech Solutions .
Fairfax editorial staff are taking seven days of industrial action to protest the company's decision to cut 25% of its journalists, which amounts to 125 full-time equivalent positions. Follow the tweets at #fairgofairfax .

CompanyWatch

Melbourne IT (ASX: MLB) has successfully completed an institutional entitlement offer, raising approximately $19 million . The company announced a fully underwritten enquiry raising of around $30 million to partly fund the proposed acquisition of WME Group for around $39 million.
Israeli-based technology company Mobilicom (ASX: MOB) has listed on the ASX following completion of a $7.5 million IPO.
Brisbane-based legaltech company Lawcadia has received $500,000 from the Queensland Government's Business Development Fund. Lawcadia will use the funds to expand its legal procurement business.
Stockspot has announced a Series B capital raising of $3 million , led by Graham Tuckwell with growth investor Alium Capital, and participation from Danny Bhandari and existing shareholders H2 Ventures.
Fairfax Media (ASX: FXJ) has received an unsolicited, non-binding indication of interest from a consortium including TPG Group and Ontario Teachers' Pension Plan Board to acquire Fairfax for a combination of cash and scrip consideration in a newly listed vehicle.

PeopleWatch

Singtel Optus has appointed Simon Ractliffe as Head of Cyber Security. Ractliffe was previously Director and General Manager South Asia-Pacific at SecureWorks.
Data to Decisions CRC has appointed Andrew Stead as a board director. Stead is Director Product Incubation at IAG.
Stuart Bird is Optus' Managing Director, Wholesale and Satellite. The appointment follows the retirement of Robert Parcell last month. Bird was previously with Telstra.
CSIRO has appointed Dr Peter Mayfield as Executive Director for Environment, Energy and Resources. Prior to joining CSIRO's Energy Technology group in 2010, Mayfield held several roles at BHP.
Jonathan Swift is Whispir 's new Head of Product. Swift was previously with Frontier Software.
Urbanise (ASX: UBN) has named Henry Arundel as CEO and Vivienne Selzer as CFO. Arundel is currently with Ventia Pty Limited.
