news briefs

The University of Wollongong will build an $80 million Centre for Molecular and Life Sciences Research, ‘Molecular Horizons’. The purpose-built collaborative research centre will be equipped with world-leading technologies, centred around the revolutionary $7 million Titan Krios cryo-electron microscope. It will house around 150 researchers.
Accenture has won the $19.9 million open tender contract to help The Australian Bureau of Statistics to transform the way that they collect, analyse and access statistics. The new data collection systems will be installed, configured and tested by December 2017. The blame-game continues between the ABS, Nextgen and IBM over who is responsible for the Census failure.
Archaeologists at ANU and Monash University are trialling new technology to build a 3D virtual-reality map of the Plain of Jars in Laos. The CAVE2 technology uses drone footage to create a virtual replica of archaeology dig sites.
Redflow Limited (ASX: RFX) has completed its first customer deployment of ZCell batteries at an off-grid property in South Australia owned by the family of Alan Noble, Engineering Director for Google Australia and New Zealand. The batteries integrated 12 Tindo Solar panels and two Victron battery inverters by ZCell installer Off-Grid Energy Australia. They continued to work during the recent power failure in South Australia.
The University of Sydney Business School is establishing the Sydney Social Innovation Hub. Students from all faculties will be able to collaborate with academics, community and industry. The Hub's Expert-in-Residence is former IBM CFO and COO Cameron Barnes and Head of the Innovation Hub is James Meade.
An Israeli trade mission will showcase technologies designed to help people with disabilities. The trade delegation is led by PresenTense Israel in partnership with the Australia Israel Chamber of Commerce NSW.
In other trade mission news, this week the ICT Agency of Sri Lanka is leading a trade mission to Australia.
Xref Limited (ASX: XF1) has partnered with Expr3ss to integrate their cloud-based platforms. They will combine applicant selection and employee reference checks.
Ten hospitals are deploying enterprise information platform OnBase by Hyland. The hospitals include Mater Misericordiae Limited Brisbane, Melbourne Health and The Royal Children’s Hospital Melbourne.
Flight Centre New Zealand has implemented the Nutanix Enterprise Cloud Platform.
8common Limited 's (ASX: 8CO) subsidiary Business Information Services has won the NSW Department of Industry's Expense Management System tender.
The 'first global trade transaction' between two independent banks has been undertaken by Commonwealth Bank , Wells Fargo and Brighann Cotton using blockchain, smart contracts and IoT technologies. The transaction comprised a shipment of cotton from Texas, USA to Qingdao in China using Skuchain's Brackets system.
The Federal Government is investing $7.4 million over two years in the medical technology and pharmaceutical sectors. The funding will be delivered through MTPConnect , established under the Australian Government's Industry Growth Centres Initiative. 14 groups will work on projects to tackle barriers to industry growth, including: a global investment program to fund Australia's life science sector; a training program for early stage entrepreneurs and scientists; and a system to support the development of Australia's emerging digital health sector.
The Australian Government has launched IP NOVA , an app enabling users to explore and analyse Australia's intellectual property data.
The Instillery Group has partnered with Zscaler to become a provider of Zscaler cloud security solutions in New Zealand.
Optus ' new relationship with provider of the Early Warning Network, Aeeris (ASX: AER) will support remote worker solutions using Optus Satellite Services and the Thuraya SatSleeve+.
Biotechnology company Benitec Biopharma Limited (ASX: BLT) has announced a strategic agreement with NantVentures, the private investment arm of NantWorks and California Capital. The companies are planning an oncology-focused research and development collaboration.
Debtor management provider IODM (ASX: IOD) has partnered with accounting firm SV Partners. Under the deal, SV Partners will integrate its payment solution business, SmartFee, into the IODM debtor management platform.
An Australian consortium has entered into an agreement with NSW Government for the $16 billion partial lease of Ausgrid .
TasTAFE have three new DigiLabs to improve digital literacy skills. Located in the Hobart, Launceston and Burnie campuses, the DigiLabs are funded by the Tasmanian Government through a Skills Tasmania grant, and include 3D printers, virtual reality glasses and games, as well as a virtual office.

people watch

Professor Michael Aitken AM has received the $250,000 Prime Minister's Prize for Innovation 2016 . After developing SMARTS, market surveillance software acquired by NASDAQ, Professor Aitken is applying the same technology framework to health, mortgage and other markets. He is CEO and Chief Scientist of Capital Markets Cooperative Research Centre and Professor of ICT Strategy at Macquarie University.
Gerd Schenkel is the new CEO of Tyro Payments, after leaving his role as Executive Director Telstra Digital. Schenkel also founded UBank. The current CEO, Jost Stollman who is the founder of Tyro is to remain as full-time Executive Director and board member.
Dr Simon Poole has received the prestigious TelSoc 2016 Charles Todd Medal “for outstanding contributions to telecommunications”. In 1988, Dr Poole moved to Australia and became joint founder of the Optical Fibre Technology Centre at the University of Sydney, which subsequently became part of the Australian Photonics Cooperative Research Centre. Dr Poole has created and led two very successful start-ups in Australia: Indx in 1995 and Engana in 2001. Engana was acquired by Finisar, and now employs 220 people in Sydney and a similar number in China. In 2008, Dr Poole started the New Business Ventures Group within Finisar to generate high value added businesses.
TIBCO Software has announced Daniel Churches as Regional Vice President for Australia and New Zealand. Churches was previously at Verizon Enterprise Solutions.
Martin Mackay has been named President and General Manager of CA Technologies ' Asia Pacific and Japan region. Kenneth Arredondo will transition to a new role in the company next year.
Ecommerce site Temple & Webster (ASX: TPW) has made permanent the interim appointments of Mark Coulter as CEO and Mark Tayler as CFO.

company watch

Simon Hackett has purchased a 10% stake in Evoke , a chauffeur-driven Tesla service which is to expand its Sydney fleet and begin operating in Melbourne in the new year.
AtlasTrend has completed a $2.8 million capital raising to grow its online investment platform. Investors included SocietyOne and several ex-Macquarie Group senior executives. Greg Mackay, former Macquarie Group Executive Director, has joined the company's advisory board. The capital raising was supported by tax incentives for startup investors offered under the National Innovation and Science Agenda.
SafetyCulture has raised US$23 million in a Series B investment round led by venture capital firm Index Ventures, with participation from Blackbird Ventures and Atlassian Co-founder Scott Farquhar, who led the Series A round in 2014. Read Luke Anear's reflection on his pitch and on investor relations. He comments: “Investors want to buy a great company as early (cheaply) as possible, in order to see the greatest gain. As a founder and CEO, I don’t want to release any of our equity unless it helps us achieve our long term goals as a company. Therein lies the dichotomy between investors and founders with a long term vision.”
Property Connect Holdings Limited (ASX: PCH) has raised $2 million via a private convertible note.
Deloitte has acquired Plenary Networks. Established by Managing Director Peter Stojkovski in 2012, Melbourne-based Plenary develops network and security focused solutions and assists with network automation and orchestration for medium to large scale organisations. Stojkovski will become a Deloitte Consulting partner.
