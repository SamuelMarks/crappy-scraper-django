news briefs

Optus Business and Cisco will partner to create a fund to support the development of disruptive capabilities for Australian business and government. The companies aim to invest $12 million over three years in the local development of capabilities focused on cyber security, cloud, the Internet of Things (IoT) and the network of the future. Optus and Cisco signed a Letter of Intent to establish the fund earlier this month.
The Safe Connections program is to be expanded , with up to 20,000 safe smartphones provided over three years to women experiencing domestic violence. The program is supported by Women's Services Network (WESNET), the Federal Government and Telstra. Telstra is to provide the increased number of smartphones as well as $30 pre-paid starter kits and technology safety information. Those experiencing domestic violence can access online help at 1800respect.org.au or call 1800 RESPECT.
Clemson University (based in South Carolina, U.S.) has chosen Cohda Wireless to supply MK5 onboard and roadside unit hardware and software for a Connected Vehicle technology trial. The project is supported by US Ignite , a White House initiative run by the National Science Foundation. Cohda Wireless units will be used on the South Carolina Connected Vehicle Testbed.
Veritas has signed a multi-million dollar contract with Equinix for dedicated cloud data centres in Sydney and Melbourne to host its cloud-based archiving service, Veritas Enterprise Vault.cloud.
The Federal Government has introduced a Bill to provide tax incentives for early stage investors. Incentives will provide investors with a 20% non-refundable carry-forward tax offset for qualifying investments, capped at $200,000 annually for each investor and their affiliate; and an exemption from capital gains tax for qualifying investments held between one and ten years. The incentives are expected to apply to investments made on or after the date of Royal Assent and 1 July 2016.
nbn has released The nbn Digital Health at Home Report , which indicates that Australians caring for both children and elderly parents are the most likely to take advantage of telehealth services. 50% of respondents use telehealth for convenience, while one in five use it when they are unable to access a doctor.
NEC Australia is partnering with Japanese company WingArc1st and Melbourne-based Genix Ventures to launch a data analytics platform for enterprise customers. The partnership leverages the 2015 Japan-Australia Economic Partnership Agreement. NEC Australia is now also a strategic partner to Microsoft's Azure Internet of Things program .
15 recipients of the Australian Government's Entrepreneurs' Programme have been awarded commercialisation support, including $572,445 for biotechnology company Minomic, which has developed a new diagnostic test for the early detection of prostate cancer; $350,986 for MetaCDN Pty Ltd’s StreamShark, a next generation video streaming platform; $525,000 for RFID Technologies Pty Ltd’s chipless RFID for pharmaceuticals authentication; and $572,000 for Agriwebb Pty Ltd’s development of software for the livestock industry.
Digital Realty will invest up to US$68 million in the establishment of a new data centre in Deer Park, Melbourne. The centre will create up to 450 construction jobs and 35 ongoing full time roles. It will be the company's third data centre in Victoria.
Metricon Stadium is the first wi-fi connected ' smart stadium ' in Queensland. Huawei's Agile Stadium Solution was implemented by partner Fujitsu Australia, and includes 3D planning tools within the Wi-Fi designing and programming.
South Australian-based technology company Parashift has been selected to provide Ego Pharmaceuticals with a document management system.
IP Australia is running an online competition to find ' the best idea in Australia ' and inspire innovators and entrepreneurs to 'maximise their intellectual property (IP) rights'. The winner will be announced on 26 April, World IP Day.
SBS is to sub-license the FIFA World Cup to Optus, who will transmit live all 64 matches from the 2018 Men's World Cup, as well as the 2017 FIFA Confederations Cup and 2019 FIFA Women's World Cup over TV and internet-enabled devices.
The Dementia Friendly Home App was developed by Alzheimer’s Australia Vic and Deakin University, with $100,000 provided by the Victorian Government. The app helps families and carers to create a suitable home for those with dementia. It is available on iPads and Android tablets for $2.99.
Two University of Sydney machine-learning academics have raised concerns about the future role of artificial intelligence (AI) devices, following the defeat of the world champion Go player. Dr Michael Harre commented: "The technology has developed to a point that it can now outsmart a human in both simple and complex tasks. This is a concern because Artificial Intelligence technology may reach a point in a few years where it is feasible that it could be adapted to areas of defence where a human may no longer be needed in the control loop: truly autonomous AI."
Bluechiip Limited's (ASX: BCT) sample tracking technology has been implemented in the Autism CRC Biobank.
RCR Tomlinson Ltd (ASX: RCR) has won an Early Contractor Involvement contract to provide design and engineering expertise for Origin Energy's proposed large-scale solar farm in Dalby, Queensland.
MedAdvisor Limited (ASX: MDR) has signed a Memorandum of Understanding with Arrow Pharmaceuticals. Under the agreement, Arrow will promote the MedAdvisor platform to its customers.
Norwood (ASX: NOR) has signed a Memorandum of Understanding with Vietnam Posts Telecommunications Group (VPNT) as a precursor to a full commercial agreement. VNPT will promote and market World Phone to its customer base.
Auria Wireless Pty Ltd - a subsidiary of Etherstack plc (ASX: ESK) - has been awarded a contract with the NSW State Emergency Service to provide tactical radio communications network equipment.
In 2016 the Prime Minister’s Prizes for Science will include a new award: the Prize for New Innovators , which recognises the commercial successes of Australia's early career innovators.
Macquarie University has implemented BlackBerry's AtHoc Networked Crisis Communications Suite to enable university members to communicate effectively in the event of a crisis, both remotely and on-site.

people watch

Victor Kovalev has joined Redbubble as Chief Technology Officer based in Melbourne. He was previously VP of Engineering at Indiegogo based in Silicon Valley. Chris Nunn has transitioned from his Board role to take on an executive role as CFO as the company prepares for more growth.
Telstra has made two new appointments: Stephen Elop is to take on the newly created role of Group Executive Technology, Innovation and Strategy, while Kevin Russell was announced as Group Executive Telstra Retail. Elop was CEO of Nokia until Microsoft's purchase of its mobile and devices business, when he became Executive Vice President of the Microsoft Devices Group. Russell was most recently Country Chief Officer and CEO Consumer, Australia, at SingTel Optus.
Appointments to the new Innovation and Science Australia Board include: Scott Farquhar, Co-founder and CEO Atlassian; Daniel Petre AO, Partner AirTree Ventures; Paul Bassat, Co-founder Square Peg Capital and Co-founder SEEK; and Dr Chris Roberts, Non-Executive Director ResMed. Bill Ferris AC was already appointed as the Chair.
News Corp Australia CEO Peter Tonagh is to take over as Foxtel's Chief Executive after current Foxtel CEO Richard Freudenstein announced he would step down.
Tquila ANZ has appointed Jo Masters to the newly created position of CEO, based in Sydney. Masters was previously Vice President, Sales and Marketing at Object Consulting.
The new CEO for Davidson Technology is Brendan Kavenagh. He was previously General Manager of Peoplebank Victoria.

company watch

Aconex Limited (ASX: ACX) will acquire cloud and mobile collaboration service provider Conject Holding GmbH for €65 million ($96 million). To finance the acquisition and provide additional working capital, Aconex also completed a placement of ordinary shares, raising around $120 million.
Martin Hosking’s Redbubble has finalised a $12 million pre-IPO funding round led by Blackbird Ventures as it prepares to list on the ASX. 158 people work at Redbubble, with 90 based in the Melbourne headquarters.
WiseTech Global have released their Prospectus to raise $100-$150 million through a listing on the ASX. Richard White, the founder, will own between 46.1%-54.5% of the company following the listing.
CSC has acquired The Dalmatian Group, a Canberra-based information and cyber security consultancy. The Dalmatian team will join CSC's Consulting group based in Canberra.
Melbourne IT (ASX: MLB) will acquire data and analytics provider InfoReady for $15.4 million. Melbourne IT also intends to sell its International Domain Name Registration Business for $8.1 to $8.5 million. The company has announced plans to raise $15 million of new equity via an underwritten institutional placement.
Pacific American Coal has entered into a Heads of Agreement to become the largest shareholder in graphene developer, Imagine Intelligent Materials Pty Ltd.
