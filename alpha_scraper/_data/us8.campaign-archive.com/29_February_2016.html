news briefs

The Organisation for Economic Cooperation and Development (OECD) has released Going for Growth 2016 which identifies 'structural weaknesses' in Australia's educational outcomes, as well as tax revenue structure and public infrastructure. The performance of Australian high-school students in PISA tests in science and reading proficiency was noted as "only around the international average", with high variation across students. The OECD recommended improvement to all levels of education, with reform needed in the supply and quality of early childhood education as well as primary and secondary education. You can see country notes on Australia here and access the full report here .
The Australian Government will establish a landing pad in Shanghai, China. Five landing pads in total will be created, with San Francisco and Tel Aviv already announced and two final landing pads to follow soon.
A new report, 'Tomorrow's Digitally Enabled Workforce' , based on research by the CSIRO and Data61, was produced in conjunction with the Australian Computer Society (ACS), ANZ Banking Group, the Commonwealth Department of Employment and Boston Consulting Group. The report addresses work trends, including the exponential growth of technology and the rise of entrepreneurism.
Submissions for the new Entrepreneur visa are open until Friday 18 March. The visa will be introduced by the Federal Government in November 2016.
The Queensland Government will create a $12.5 million Cyber Security Unit to respond to cyber threats against Queensland Government IT.
Etsy has partnered with Australian company Sendle , which offers carbon neutral door-to-door delivery.

The Government has released the 2016 Defence White Paper and announced an additional $29.9 billion in Defence funding over the next decade. The funding will include investment in "enhanced intelligence, surveillance and reconnaissance, space, electronic and cyber capabilities."
The location of a fast radio burst (FRB) has been identified for the first time using CSIRO radio telescopes in eastern Australia, as well as the National Astronomical Observatory of Japan's Subaru telescope in Hawaii. In one millisecond, FRBs emit as much energy as the sun emits in 10,000 years. Scientists are hoping to discover what causes them.
In response to recent media coverage, nbn has stated that the company is on track to meet its 2020 targets.
OnMarket BookBuilds has partnered with WYZA - a digital publishing firm for over 50s - to allow WYZA's subscribers to use OnMarket.

The Tasmanian Government has launched free Wi-Fi across the state. 42 access points are active with the number expected to reach 150.
Deloitte has partnered with Talegent to produce a game-based, interactive assessment for its graduate recruitment.
Under a license agreement with GenghisComm Holdings, Department 13 (ASX: D13) has been granted a US patent on Cooperative Subspace Coding (CSC) technology to enhance D13's drone defence capabilities and advanced communication networking. The technology addresses a central part of D13's Counter Drone Defence Solution for dealing with swarms of drones, as well as the company's strategic networking solutions products.
TopBetta Holdings Limited (ASX:TBH) has signed a strategic commercial advertising deal with Fairfax Media's Australian Community Media division to promote its sport and racing Fantasy Tournaments.
Wangle Technologies Limited (ASX:WGL) is to design and build a microchip prototype for Israeli based Power Skills.
A new investment in a five-year collaboration between Monash University, CSIRO and the Australian Synchrotron will boost research into biomedical sciences, materials research, engineering and geosciences. MASSIVE (Multi-modal Australian ScienceS Imaging and Visualisation Environment) is a high performance computing facility designed to process complex data. It can build three dimensional X-ray images at micrometre resolution. Monash University has invested a further $4.1 million into the new $5.7 million project in order to fund a new high performance computing capability, M3. M3 will provide computing capacity to the Faculty of Medicine.

company watch

OntheHouse (ASX: OTH) has decided not to proceed with a joint venture with realestateVIEW to merge their Consumer Online Division. Onthehouse will now sell or close the Onthehouse.com.au and media sales businesses. If a sale agreement is not reached, they will be closed by April 2016 at an estimated closure cost of around $1.7 million.
Medical device company ResMed (ASX: RMD) will acquire clinical software applications company Brightree for USD$800 million. Brightree will continue to operate as a separate entity.
Private Media has sold Women's Agenda to its founding editor - now publisher - Angela Priestley.
Chapmans Limited (ASX: CHP)'s wholly owned subsidiary, Chapmans Opportunities Limited (COL) will invest $1.75 million in Whizz Technologies Pty Ltd, giving COL an 8.9% direct holding in WHIZZ. WHIZZ provides a platform to connect customers with qualified cleaners.
Stackla has announced a successful Series A round of with Bailador Technology (ASX:BTI) contributing $2.5m of the $6.1m round.
Health informatics company Alcidion , which is backed by Blue Sky Private Equity and Allure Capital, has listed on the Australian Securities Exchange (ASX: ALC) through a reverse takeover of mining shell company Naracoota Resources. The listing gave Alcidion an initial market capitalisation of $33.76 million.
