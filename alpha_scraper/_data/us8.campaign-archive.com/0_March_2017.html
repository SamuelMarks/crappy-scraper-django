NewsBriefs

A $730 million Next Generation Technologies Fund has been launched to develop creative solutions for Australia's defence capability. The first program run out of the fund is the Grand Challenges, where Defence will put forward a wicked problem and ask Australians to find a solution.
Corporate accelerator Slingshot is partnering with Qantas on a new travel startup accelerator program. The AVRO Accelerator will see startups working with Qantas Group mentors on ideas to streamline the travel experience, solve business problems and make the most of new technologies. Up to 10 teams will be chosen for the 12-week program. Applications close 27 April 2017.
Congratulations to Sydney-based Tank Stream Labs and Melbourne-based York Butter Factory for joining forces ! Members of the respective spaces will be able to work across both spaces for free.
Australian agtech startups are invited to enter the Future Food Asia Awards on Friday 26 May 2017. The Awards are looking for pre-Series A startups whose innovations address food challenges.
Congratulations to Carolyn Mee and her team at Sound Scouts ( Tech23 2014 alumni) for winning the Health and Wearable Technology Award at the 2017 SXSW Accelerator competition in Austin, Texas. Sound Scouts detects hearing loss in children through an interactive app.
A school devoted to STEM will open in Western Sydney in 2021. According to the NSW Government it will be the first of its kind in NSW, and a collaboration between the Catholic Education Diocese of Parramatta and Sydney Science Park developers Celestino.
BrainChip Holdings (ASX: BRN) has signed an agreement with French-based technology transfer company Toulouse Tech Transfer for exclusive rights to the JAST learning rules and algorithms developed by CERCO, a French public research lab. The technology will be implemented in BrainChip's new Spiking Neuron Adaptive Processor technology.
Silicon Valley-based accelerator, 500 Startups , is launching a 500 Melbourne office after receiving almost $3 million from the Victorian Government’s LaunchVic Round 2 Funding Round . The 500 Melbourne core accelerator program will accept 20-25 startups annually from Australia and the APAC region. This round also gave $384,000 to Startmate, Australia’s successful startup accelerator.
A new eSmart Schools program will tackle cyber safety and digital literacy in Tasmania. In partnership with the Alannah and Madeline Foundation, 23 Tasmanian Government schools will trial the program.

Launceston now has a city-wide Internet-of-Things LORA network (a long range low-power wide area network). The Tasmanian Government contributed $100,000 to the project, and the network will be run out of startup hub and incubator Enterprize Launceston. Definium Technologies manufactured the wireless broadcast locations powering the network.
NRMA Business Insurance has entered a pilot partnership with Prospa. NRMA's customers will be able to access Prospa 's small business lending services.
Up to $5 million in funding is available for local councils to support smart tech projects . Applications are open until 30 June.
New technologies - and anti-competitive responses to those challenges - are causing headaches for consumer regulators, according to ACCC Chairman Rod Sims. Read the full speech here .
Vocus has moved away from its three-tier datacentre infrastructure and adopted Nutanix 's enterprise cloud platform to manage its business support systems.
The Department of Human Services has boldly claimed that it is now ' cyber resilient ', following recent investments in cyber security capabilities.
Australia's Landing Pad in Singapore was launched last week. The network includes facilities in Berlin, San Francisco, Shanghai and Tel Aviv.
Applications are open for the SproutX Accelerator program . Backed by SproutX's $10 million venture fund, startups can access $40,000 in capital funding as well as the usual deal - office space, mentoring, and distribution opportunities. Applications close 8 May 2017.

EnergyWatch

Last week was all about energy, with South Australia announcing its $550 million plan to secure the state's power. The SA Government plans to build Australia's largest battery and a government-owned 250MW gas-fired power plant for emergency backup power. Expressions of interest are invited to build the grid-scale battery; the first project to be financed under the new $150 million Renewable Technology Fund.
A virtual power plant is now live in South Australia. Dubbed the first in Australia, AGL Energy Limited's Virtual Power Plant project will operate as a 5MW solar power plant by installing batteries that talk to each other through a cloud-based platform to form a connected system.
The Victorian Government is investing an additional $20 million in large-scale energy storage initiatives . The funding will support energy storage companies specialising in technologies including batteries, pumped hydro or solar thermal. These companies will partner with network businesses to boost energy storage capacity by up to 100MW by the end of 2018.
A focus on Australia's energy supply continues with the Federal Government's announcement of the planned Snowy Mountains Scheme 2.0 . The plan would increase generation of the scheme by 50%, which when complete could add 2000MW of renewable energy to the National Electricity Market.
South Australian energy storage company 1414 has closed its seed funding round after raising $2.5 million from professional investors in a heavily over-subscribed offer. The funding will be used to speed up its first commercial applications of the thermal energy storage systems, and to prepare for the IPO and listing on the ASX later this year.

PeopleWatch

Silicon Valley-based Kara Frederick has joined Reinventure as General Partner. Frederick will assist Australian fintech companies looking to expand to the US market.
PM-Partners has named Ken Sheargold as CEO. Existing Co-founders and Partners Pete Swan, Nik Samuelson and Steve Hewitson will move to newly-created Board roles. Sheargold has held Executive Director roles at Telstra, NSW Premier and Cabinet, Public Works and Service First.
Certus Solutions has appointed Michael Vulcan as Victorian State Manager. Vulcan was previously with IBM Commerce Group.
