news briefs

A report "Boosting High-Impact Entrepreneurship in Australia", has been released by the Office of the Chief Scientist . The report finds that Australia has one of the highest rates of business creation in the world, but few startups have the capacity to grow beyond the local level. The report, prepared by Colin Kinner, identifies a mix of programmes in universities, from harnessing entrepreneurs as role models to hands-on learning through incubators, accelerators and overseas placements, as the best approach.
The Melbourne Accelerator Program (MAP) has entered into an agreement with Australia Post worth $1 million over three years. The partnership will lead to the creation of two new MAP places, as well as providing a scholarship to the Wade Institute of Entrepreneurship, both situated in the University of Melbourne and three specific outreach programs targeting regional & rural areas, women and social entrepreneurs. Australia Post has also announced a $20 million capital fund to invest in emerging eCommerce businesses and plans to co-locate its accelerator at Lab-14 premises alongside MAP.
SydStart conference, which was last year acquired by Matt Barrie, CEO, Freelancer, is now to be known as StartCon after being enticed by the Victorian Government to move to Melbourne.
Brisbane is the location for the Mining Equipment, Technology and Services (METS) Industry Growth Centre. The METS Industry Growth Centre will be one of five centres established as part of the Commonwealth Government’s Innovation and Competitiveness Agenda. The Commonwealth will provide $3.5 million per year over four years and the Queensland Government will provide $6 million plus staffing support to the centre, based at the Queensland University of Technology Gardens Point campus.
The ACS Foundation is expanding its “BiG Day In™” career information conferences for high school students to include primary school children after a successful trial at Hilltop Road Public School in Merrylands, Western Sydney which hosted the first official event for the Jaguar Primary Schools Challenge™ exposing the school’s 320 Year 4, 5 and 6 students to STEM based activities including building a model F1 racing car from cardboard and racing it; Minecraft activities; a Robo Cup; coding classes and computational thinking. To ensure the conversation continues, parents and teachers were invited to attend the day as observers. From this pilot exercise, the ACS Foundation hopes to find the presentation partners and sponsors to design a nationwide program for BiG Day In Junior.
Funding of $7.3 million has been allocated to 20 Australian companies under the Entrepreneurs' Programme . Jumbo Vision International's CADwalk Global received $1 million; Scio Technologies Pty Ltd received $490,000 for its Pre-gestational Diabetes Platform; Tech23 alumni, Clevertar received $420,000 for its Relational agents for health coaching.
The Western Australian Public Trustee has extended its contract with Unisys to provide continued support maintenance and enhancements for its Management Accounting Trust Environment application.
Monash University has selected Mellanox Technologies CloudX platform to provide the network 100Gb/s end-to-end OpenStack Cloud.
Optus Business has signed a three year multi-million dollar deal with Accor Hotels to supply fixed voice and mobile services to 200 hotel properties across Australia.
Data#3 has signed a multi-million dollar agreement with Edith Cowan University to provide scalable and future proof communications platform, from strategy, design and implementation through to ongoing management.
Bill Shorten has announced the Labour party will fund $4.5 million in grants for coding programs to improve STEM education if in government. Grants of $150,000 each will be offered to a range of organisations including Code Club Australia , Code Camp and Tech Girls Movement for specific projects.
The Federal Government has partnered with Pollenizer to create DataStart - a program which will see 20 startups get a chance to present their ideas to a panel of judges in a showcase event in Canberra on Monday 18 January 2016. Successful start-ups will then enter a nine month incubation program.
Slingshot has partnered with health fund HCF to launch Catalyst, an accelerator program focused on health tech startups. Startups will have the chance to secure round investment of up to $100,000 from the Slingshot Venture Fund.
bigtincan has announced its product capability support for iOS 9 devices. The Boston based Australian start up and Tech23 alumni will also be providing a trip to the US for one Tech23 company this year.

company watch

REA Group Limited (ASX: REA) is to acquire iProperty Group (ASX: IPP) which is a real estate portal based in Malaysia. REA holds a 22.67% stake and Catcha Group holds a 16.7% stake in iProperty.
iSignthis Ltd (ASX: ISX) has completed a $10.45 million capital raising seeing a number of new institutional investors being added to the register.
Australian digital startup Unlockd has raised $50 million in its third capital raising ahead of its product debut in Australia and the US.
Rimon Advisory has announced a new seed capital fund called Rimon Investments .
Chinese wireless technology company Enice Holdings Limited (ASX: ENC) has begun trading on the ASX.
Cloud based legal services company LawPath has raised $1.3 million from Mardoch Ventures; Brook Adcock from Adcock Group and a syndicate led by Ben Keeble, principal at Fort Street Advisors.
SEEK Co-founder Paul Bassat is launching a new $200 million venture capital fund backed by James Packer, super funds and other individuals bringing the total fund raised or planned to almost $1 billion. The fund will aim to identify and invest in the next SEEK, carsales.com or Atlassian .

people watch

Dr Alan Finkel AO has been appointed Australia's next Chief Scientist. Finkel is currently Chancellor of Monash University and is President of the Australian Academy of Technological Sciences and Engineering . He will be replacing Professor Ian Chubb AC who has served since May 2011.
Cris Nicolli is set to retire as Managing Director and CEO of UXC Limited (ASX: UXC) in 2016 after 12 years with the company. Nicolli will continue as CEO until the acquisition of UXC by CSC is completed. If the CSC transaction does not take place, Nicolli will continue until the appointment and handover of a successor.
