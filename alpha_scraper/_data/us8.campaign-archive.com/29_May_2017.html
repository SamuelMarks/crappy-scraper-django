NewsBriefs

CSIRO's Data61 has developed a prototype wearable device to capture how an individual's unique energy generation pattern can be used as a form of authentication. To avoid draining the battery life, researchers have combined gait recognition with kinetic energy harvesting, which translates a person's motion into electrical energy.
The NSW Government's Digital Strategy was released last week by Victor Dominello, NSW Minister for Finance, Services and Property. The Strategy sets out three priorities: customer experience, data, and digital on the inside, and aims to deliver 70% of government transactions via digital channels by 2019. Four key areas of work which support the delivery of priorities include technology, cyber security, legislation and delivery capability.
40,000 ICT jobs were created in the last two years, according to the Australian Computer Society 's 2017 Digital Pulse report. The report indicates that over 80,000 new ICT professionals will be needed by 2022 to drive future technology-led growth. Lots more work needed on the diversity front too, with women representing only 28% of the ICT workforce.
The Australian Square Kilometre Array Pathfinder (ASKAP) telescope has found its first ' fast radio burst ' from space - a short, sharp spike of radio waves that last only a few milliseconds, which are thought to come from major events billions of light-years away. Only a couple of dozen have been found thus far.
A three year, $1.4 million trial in Tasmania will use GPS to track and monitor high risk family violence offenders .
Corporate Australia can now connect to the startup and scaleup community through a new platform, CoVentured . Slingshot is behind the platform, with over 24 businesses signing on as founding members, including Optus, Westpac, Woolworths, Australia Post, EnergyAustralia and LendLease. The platform's strong search and filter functionalities are designed to help corporates find and engage with startups.
Communications Alliance has released a proposal to transfer the management of Australia's telecommunications numbering arrangements from government to industry. Comments on the paper are due by 23 June.
Australia's video game development industry is being left out in the cold, according to the Interactive Games & Entertainment Association (IGEA). The CEO of the IGEA Ron Curry has written an open letter to Mitch Fifield, Minister for Communications and the Arts, stating: “The Australian video game development industry has huge potential as a weightless and clean export. It’s an intensively creative industry but also highly innovative and technical, and usually at the cutting edge of technology.” The IGEA has been waiting over a year for the Federal Government’s response to a report on the future of Australia’s video game development industry.
CSIRO has released a technology roadmap to support growth in the mining equipment, technology and services sector.
Telstra is to be FOX SPORTS' network partner of choice, using Telstra's Distributed Production Network (DPN) to enhance FOX SPORTS' outside broadcasting model . The deal will see Telstra's DPN connect 29 sporting stadiums to Remote Production Hubs in Sydney and Melbourne.

Zonge Engineering and Research and Cira Pty Ltd have been awarded $50,000 under the SA Government’s Innovation Voucher Program to develop a new high-powered geophysical transmitter system to allow greater depths of geological analysis.
nbn has awarded a contract to Xref Limited (ASX: XF1) to be the candidate referencing solution provider for all nbn hiring. Xref has also integrated with cloud-based recruiting software provider iCIMs.
NSW will get an additional 10 SMART drumlines as the shark net trial ends two weeks earlier, due to increased whale sightings.
The ACCC has finalised its decision on the regulation of high-speed internet services supplied by non-NBN fixed line networks. The decision permits non-NBN networks to pass on the proposed Regional Broadband Scheme charge on their customer lines to help fund nbn's non-commercial regional fixed wireless and satellite services.
People who record or share intimate images without consent could be jailed for up to three years and fined $11,000 under the new Crimes Amendment (Intimate Images) Bill 2017.
The Cyber Victoria Program has been launched to grow the state's cyber security ecosystem. The program will be delivered by LifeJourney, the company behind the Cyber Maryland program in the US. LifeJourney's Asia Pacific headquarters are now in Melbourne, with the company to partner with Optus to strengthen Victoria's cyber workforce.
Drones are being used by a team of Indigenous rangers protecting 22,500 hectares of their country around Archer Point on Cape York. The land includes parts of World Heritage-listed areas: the Wet Tropics and the Great Barrier Reef. The Yuku Baja Muliku Rangers are thought to be the first Indigenous ranger group in Queensland to receive Civil Aviation Safety Authority (CASA) certification to use drones commercially. CASA has also launched an app - ' Can I fly there ' - to keep drone users in safe zones.
MedAdvisor (ASX: MDR) will integrate with California-based iHealth's mobile healthcare solutions and apps.
A new online resource for end of life care has been launched : the palliAGED website collates research evidence including new medications, clinical decision making tools, models of care, and ways to support patients and their families.
eSports Mogul Asia Pacific (ASX: ESH) has entered into strategic marketing agreements with two Asian esports media groups, DreamCasters TDC and FPS Thailand, to promote the company's ChallengeMe online tournament platform .
Trade Me is partnering with Afterpay to allow sellers to offer interest-free deferred payments.
A Dutch dairy farm has deployed six Redflow ZBM2 zinc-bromine flow batteries to store self-produced solar energy to support its milk production.
Applications are invited for industry-led proposals for the 19th CRC selection round . Applications close 12 July 2017.
Citizen scientists will be mobilised to help Australian universities and scientific organisations - with $4.4 million in Citizen Science Grants awarded to 13 projects. Projects include: a CSIRO-led project where citizen scientists collect and analyse water samples to improve satellite-based water quality detection; RMIT's investigation into the environmental effects of sunscreen on marine ecology in Port Phillip Bay; and research by South Australian Museum scientists into microbat populations.

PeopleWatch

Slingshot 's new new Chief Commercial Officer (CCO) is Graham Christie. Christie is the co-founder of Big Mobile.
OneVentures has appointed Sakura Holloway and Sarah Meibusch to its healthcare investment management team. Holloway has previously held roles with UTS and the Garvan Institute, while Meibusch was previously with Shire Pharmaceuticals.
AGL is promoting its CIO , Simon Moorfield, to the Executive Team.
Karen Stocks is heading to Google headquarters in the US to lead the Global Measurement Solutions team. She was previously head of Twitter Australia and held a number of roles at Google.
AirTree Ventures has recruited Anil Sabharwal , Google's Vice President of Product.
Atlassian's new Chief Marketing Officer (CMO) is Robert Chatwani . Chatwani was previously Chief Revenue and Marketing Officer at Teespring in San Francisco and CMO of eBay North America.
Vocus Communications non-executive chairman, David Spence is joining the board of NetComm Wireless Limited (ASX: NTC) as a Non-executive Director. David Stewart will also join later this year after retiring from CEO and Managing Director of NetComm in 2016.
Kate Burleigh is leaving her role as Managing Director at Intel ANZ.
Kate Vale, Spotify 's founding Managing Director for Australia and New Zealand, has departed the streaming company to pursue opportunities outside of business.
