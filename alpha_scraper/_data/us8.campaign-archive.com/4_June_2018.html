NEWS BRIEFS
TEG Live and ESL have partnered with Visit Victoria to host Melbourne Esports Open until 2022. The inaugural Open will be held in September at Melbourne and Olympic Park and include interactive gamer experiences and a showcase of the latest esports technology.
Coworking space Tank Stream Labs has joined forces with Blockchain Professionals Sydney to develop a blockchain-focused hub at its original Sydney site.
The Federal Government has appointed four regional innovation facilitators based in regional Western Australia, northern Queensland, western Victoria and eastern South Australia, to connect regional incubators with grants, funding opportunities and resources.
T he City of Sydney Council has allocated $4.5 million to renewable energy incentives including grants for installing solar panels and the formation of a ‘solar concierge’ that will provide impartial renewable energy advice to building managers.
Australia's submarine and warship builder ASC has embarked upon a digital transformation program to guide its next phase of building development. ASC has entrusted the first phase of the project to Accenture, who are tasked with defining the scope of the project.
RMIT University has launched a series of online courses to help individuals adjust to continual digital disruptions in business in partnership with REA Group, Tigerspike and Paper Giant.
The NSW Government will deliver a $3.9 billion annual allocation to the NSW Police Force in the forthcoming State Budget. Of this sum, $12 million will be invested in facial matching services as part of the National Facial Biometric Matching Capability scheme.
Airservices Australia will deploy information sharing platforms in Australia’s busiest airports to enable air traffic controllers and operations staff to collaboratively view synchronised, real-time data. In a move set to save the aviation industry $52 million over the coming decade, US-based company Saab Sensis will assist Airservices with the initial software implementation at Brisbane Airport in 2019.
Family-run and small businesses are set to receive advice on improving their digital capabilities under an $18 million Federal Government initiative . This funding allocation resulted from findings that only one in five small Australian businesses have a digital business strategy.
Seventeen remote Northern Territory Indigenous communities will reduce their reliance on an irregular supply of diesel fuel, with the Federal Government providing them with access to a total 5.6 MW of solar energy . In conjunction with the Australian Renewable Energy Agency, the Government has allocated $31.5 million in funding towards this $59 million Solar Energy Transformation Program project.
The Australian Research Council has launched the Training Centre for Innovative BioEngineering at the University of Sydney. A consortium of university, government and health industry partners will begin work on personalised implant technologies to combat musculoskeletal degeneration.
The latest round of the Queensland Government’s Small Business Digital Grants Program sees $1 million distributed between 220 Queensland small businesses, helping them develop competitive digital strategies.
Brisbane startup hub The Precinct will undergo a multi-million dollar expansion , with Queensland Government funding allowing the hub to accommodate 27 new tenants.
New Zealand startup Aquafortus has signed a licensing agreement with Texas-based PetroH20 Recovery worth $53 million for the first three years, and $33 million each subsequent year.
COMPANY WATCH
Fintech startup Brighte has raised $18.5 million in a Series B round led by Airtree Ventures. The round also saw participation from Scott Farquhar’s Skip Capital, the Milgrom Family, and funding from existing backer Grok Ventures, Mike Cannon-Brookes’ venture capital outfit, which led a $4 million Series A round for the business last September.
Events company AmazingCo has announced a $2.3 million raise led by Melbourne-based Rampersand VC, with supporters including Macdoch Ventures, Aconex founders Leigh Jasper and Rob Phillpot, Luxury Escapes co-founder Adam Schwab and Richmond AFL captain Trent Cotchin.
MYOB (ASX:MYO) has cancelled a $180 million deal to acquire the Australian and New Zealand assets of Reckon's Accountant Group after discovering new information about the company.
Correction: In the 28 May edition of The Watch, Concept Safety Systems Pty Ltd was incorrectly referred to as the ASX-listed entity “ASX:CSS”. Concept Safety Systems is not listed on the ASX. “ASX:CSS” is an unrelated listed entity that is in no way connected to Concept Safety Systems. We apologise for any confusion caused by this error.
