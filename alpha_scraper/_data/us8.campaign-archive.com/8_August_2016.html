news briefs

The @7olympics app has not been winning any medals with users, instead garnering criticism on social media today due to streaming problems. Follow #7olympics on Twitter to read more.
Tech23 Alumni company Robological was recently named as a finalist in the Amazon Picking Challenge in Leipzig, Germany. Robological, along with researchers from the University of Canberra and collaborators from the University of New South Wales, competed with their 'microRecycler' robot .
Telstra and Ericsson have successfully delivered high-quality live footage to a T20 cricket audience at a large stadium, using LTE Broadcast technology. The trial broadcast ran continuously for five hours without any system or device failure.
Lockheed Martin (NYSE: LMT) will open a $13 million Science Technology Engineering Leadership and Research Laboratory (STELaR Lab) in Melbourne, the first of its kind outside the US. Scheduled to open in early 2017, STELaR Lab researchers will explore hypersonics, autonomy, robotics and command, control, communications, computing, intelligence, surveillance and reconnaissance. The Lab will be situated in Melbourne’s emerging technology district between University of Melbourne and RMIT.
The Australian Competition and Consumer Commission is undertaking a market study on the communications sector, examining competition and efficiency issues. The study will consider the potential to improve economic regulation where warranted.
The Australian Census will take place on Tuesday 9 August 2016. ThoughtWorks has joined the list of those voicing concerns . In an open letter to David Kalisch, Tom Sulston writes: "It is our belief that claims that the risk of data leaks are low may not be correct, as the ABS has reported 14 data breaches in the last three years. In light of the security threats observed in recent years, we are afraid that no matter how strong the security capability of the ABS, the risk is real and should this data leak the impact would be immense."
Brennan IT has selected Commvault Software and Huawei's OceansStor as its Backup-as-a-Service providers.
Xref Limited (ASX:XF1) and Verify have begun a pilot integration , bringing together their respective candidate reference checking and human resources technology.
Equinix has completed its US$28 million Port Melbourne Data Centre expansion.
A website with an adaptive learning engine is being used to battle high suicide rates among Australian farmers. "The Ripple Effect" website, part of a beyondblue project and created by digital specialists Sandpit, is a highly responsive accessible resource with content based on real experiences within regional farming communities.
The University of Queensland has opened the Nihon Superior Centre for the Manufacture of Electronic Materials, to develop a range of minute parts suitable for use in electronics like mobile phones and hybrid cars. The researchers will focus on developing solder alloys which connect the different electronic parts together.
Platform technology company Yojee Limited (ASX: YOJ) has signed a Memorandum of Understanding with manufacturing and agricultural machinery supplier Heiniger Australia. Yojee will provide logistics services using its blockchain enabled software.
BSA Limited (ASX: BSA) has secured a three-year services agreement with Ericsson for the provision of field services into the telecommunications market .
Visual effect production studio Animal Logic has partnered with the University of Technology Sydney to establish the UTS Animal Logic Academy, offering a Master of Animation and Visualisation postgraduate degree.
Animoca Brands (ASX: AB1) has signed a licensing agreement with DreamWorks Animation to develop a portfolio of book apps for Asian markets. The portfolio will include How to Train Your Dragon , Kung Fu Panda , Madagascar , The Croods and Trolls . The book apps will be distributed across Apple iOS and Google Android platforms.
Under an agreement with NAB (ASX: NAB), iSignthis Ltd (ASX: ISX) will offer paydentity integrated with card acquiring/settlement services for merchant customers in Australia and New Zealand.
The Queensland Government has signed an agreement with the US state of Nevada , with plans to work collaboratively in sectors including technology and innovation. The agreement aims to include pathways for market access for startups and established tech companies.
AGL will build a virtual power plant in Adelaide using intelligent solar batteries installed in 1000 homes. The batteries will store excess power which the customer can draw on, reducing power costs and grid demand. The system will be driven by the Sunverge VPP platform.
South Australia's bioscience agency is to be renamed TechInSA and will have its scope and operations expanded to cover not only bioscience but other high-tech industry sectors.
The Northern Territory Library has a new library management system, Libraries NT .
Seatfrog has joined travel tech startup community Amadeus Next, giving them access to 130 airlines who could benefit from their realtime seat-selection technology.
Optus Wholesale is now allied with 2SG Wholesale for the distribution of M2M, IoT and GPS products and capability to the wholesale reseller market.

company watch

Commercial legal services disruptor LegalVision has secured $4.2 million in a Series B financing round, led by Gilbert + Tobin.
SpeedCast International Limited (ASX: SDA) has acquired WINS Limited from Eutelsat Communications and Maltasat for an all-cash consideration of EUR 60 million and an equity raising of at least $62.1 million to partially fund the acquisition.
Micro-donation technology company Activistic Limited (ASX: ACU) has received firm commitments for a capital raising of $3.65 million via a private placement.
