news briefs

Big week in cyber with the Prime Minister Malcolm Turnbull launching Australia's Cyber Security Strategy to invest over $230 million over four years ‘to enhance Australia’s cyber security capability and deliver new initiatives.’ The Strategy includes five central elements, including: a national cyber partnership between research, government and business; strong cyber defences; global responsibility and influence; growth and innovation; and a smart cyber nation. Download the full report here .
CSIRO’s Data61 and the Australian Institute of Company Directors (AICD) have entered a Memorandum of Understanding to 'lift' digital and cyber literacy of directors and boards across Australia. Under the agreement, Data61 and AICD will develop a cyber security education and training program.
CSIRO have also had a busy week with the announcement of a Climate Science Centre in Hobart, Tasmania as a result of ongoing consultation and feedback. Working closely with the Bureau of Meteorology, the CSIRO is also planning to deepen its existing partnership with the UK Meteorology Office. CSIRO has also announced the latest ON Accelerator round. Eleven teams of scientists and researchers from Australian universities and CSIRO have been chosen to take part in the next round of CSIRO's ON Accelerator including facial-recognition technology that provides real-time pain assessment for patients who cannot verbally communicate, and a GPS-free collision avoidance sensor using Unmanned Aerial Vehicle (UAVs) and drone devices.
MassChallenge is partnering with Microsoft Australia and the Australian Government to provide a startup accelerator for Australian startups. The Government is contributing $280,000, which will support idea-pitching competitions, startup boot camps and local and international mentoring.
A new quantum computing lab has opened at the University of New South Wales (UNSW). This follows the Government's $26 million investment in the Centre of Excellence for Quantum Computation and Communication Technology (CQC2T). The new labs will double the productive capacity of the CQC2T at UNSW.
Start up big data visual exploration app Zegami has launched. The University of Oxford spin-out company was founded by Australians Samuel Conway and Roger Noble, as well as Oxford University and Steve Taylor. Zegami attracted 500,000 pounds in seed funding from investors including Parkwalk Advisors and Oxford Sciences Innovation. The sales and implementation team will be based in Adelaide, through the support of Blue Crystal and Coritsu Group.
Mitre 10 and BAI Communications Pty Ltd have selected performance and productivity software provider Expense8 .
ClipChamp received $499,000 and First2Click received $250,000 from the latest round of the Accelerating Commercialisation element of the Entrepreneurs' Programme.
Telco complaints as a proportion of services in operation increased 29% in January-March 2016, but dropped 13.8% from the same time last year, according to Complaints in Context , a new report by the Telecommunications Industry Ombudsman and Communications Alliance.
Veriluma - currently being acquired by Parmelia Resources (ASX: PML) - has entered into a License Agreement with RPM Group International (RPMG) to provide its patented predictive intelligence technology for application across RPMG's client base.
Researchers from the Australian National University and University of California Berkeley have discovered new properties in a nanomaterial which may offer potential for highly efficient thermophotovoltaic cells to harvest heat in the dark and turn it into electricity. The research is published in Nature Communications .
A multilingual library smartphone app deployed on the Civica Spydus platform has been launched by Fairfield City Council to help non-English speaking residents of Western Sydney.
Queenslanders had a first look at prototype high-temperature superconductors at the Banyo Pilot Plant Precinct, a Queensland University of Technology (QUT) research facility in Brisbane. The superconductors are part of a research agreement between Siemens, QUT and the Government's Defence Science and Technology Group.
MGM Wireless (ASX: MWR) has launched SchoolStar, a school mobile app which provides 2-way, personalised in-app messaging with automatic failover to SMS. It is accessible to parents and students registered with the school.
CSC and Transport for NSW have signed a three-year, over $100 million contract, under which CSC will provide network managed services across the department's 12000 sites.
In health and aged care technology news, Charm Health will install its CHARM oncology information management system through the day hospital network of Icon Cancer Care . Meanwhile in Western Australia, not-for-profit aged care and retirement services provider Bethanie has gone live with Promapp business process management software. A $2.5 million linear accelerator was also launched in WA to reduce oncology patient wait times at Fiona Stanley Hospital. In Victoria, new technology to help hospital doctors and GPs share critical medical information will be made possible through a $1.6 million funding boost from the Victorian Government.
The Australian Government Shared Services Centre is the first customer of SAP Australia 's SAP HANA Enterprise Cloud service in Canberra.
You will eventually be able to register a website with a direct .au address such as 'yourname.au'. The auDA Board has accepted the recommendation of the 2015 Names Policy Panel to introduce direct registrations in .au.
The Australian Competition and Consumer Commission has published its annual reports on the telecommunications sector for 2014-15 .
A whole-of-government website for the Northern Territory is now available at nt.gov.au . Chief Minister Adam Giles called it 'one of the biggest digital transformations undertaken by the NT Government'.
Optus Satellite and RigNet Australia have entered a wholesale partnership , under which RigNet will provide services to the Australasia region.
BluGlass (ASX: BLG) has collaborated with HC SemiTEK, a Chinese LED chip manufacturer.
Visionstream has won a five-year Telstra contract estimated at $350 million. The Network Integrity and Facilities Management contract will supply maintenance and building services to over 40,000 exchange and network assets in Australia.
New online tools aiming to help protect the Great Barrier Reef have been launched by the Queensland Government. The tools help building and construction sectors meet erosion and sediment control requirements.
The State Library of Queensland has digitised the first minute book of the Anzac Day Commemoration Committee, formed in 1916.

company watch

REA Group Limited (ASX: REA) has acquired Flatmates.com.au for $25 million plus potential earn out payments over the next two years. The purchase will be funded from cash reserves. Flatmates co-founder Thomas Clement will continue in his role as CEO of Flatmates.
Medical technology company Medibio Limited (ASX: MEB) has acquired Australian, New Zealand and Israeli patents from Heartlink Limited. The patents were held under exclusive licence on the 'method for diagnosing psychiatric disorders'.
Bugcrowd has closed a $15 million Series B funding round led by Blackbird Ventures, with other new investors including Industry Ventures and Salesforce Ventures. Existing investors Rally Ventures, Costanoa Venture Capital and Paladin Capital Group also participated in the round.
Daintree Networks has been acquired by Current, powered by GE (NYSE: GE) for more than $100 million . Daintree’s employees in California, Massachusetts and Melbourne will be integrated into the new structure.
Unlockd has raised $15 million from a Series A funding from investors including Seven West Media board member Peter Gammell, Virgin Australia board member Sam Mostyn, Lachlan Murdoch and PLC Ventures.
Quickflix (ASX:QFX) has enlisted voluntary administrator Ferrier Hodgson after a re-structure and cost-cutting attempt that proved unsuccessful in setting them apart from increased competition.
Telstra Ventures has made a strategic investment in NGINX. Read more from Telstra Ventures' Managing Director Mark Sherman on the investment.

people watch

iiNet founder and former CEO Michael Malone has been appointed to the Board of nbn as a non-executive director. He replaces Simon Hackett who resigned to focus on Redflow Limited.
Data61 CEO Adrian Turner and Doug Elix AO have been appointed as joint chairs of the Government's new Cyber Security Growth Centre.
Matthew Hyland has been named CEO of UltraServe , with founder Samuel Yeats to take a position on the company's board. Hyland's past roles include CFO at NextGen Group and CFO and Head of Operations at Fox Sports Australia.
Cirrus will open a new office in Canberra and has named Lisa Thorburn as General Manager-Federal.
Mark Buckman is to become Managing Director, Customer and Retail at Foxtel. Buckman was previously Chief Strategy Officer of Accenture Interactive in London and, prior to that, Group Managing Director, Media and Marketing and Chief Marketing Officer at Telstra.
MSM Corporation International Limited (ASX: MSM) has appointed Sophie McGill as Executive Director to the company's board. McGill is a Partner at Media Venture Partners.
