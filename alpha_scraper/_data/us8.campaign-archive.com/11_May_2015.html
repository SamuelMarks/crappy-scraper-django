news briefs

The Federal Government has announced plans to trial an opt-out, rather than opt-in myHealth Record option as part of a $485 million budget promise to redevelop the significantly underperforming Personally Controlled Electronic Health Record (PCEHR) system. Less-than one-in-ten Australians are currently signed up to the PCEHR system, which is opt-in. The National E-Health Transition Authority will be replaced with the Australian Commission for eHealth from July 2016, and a transition taskforce will be established to manage the transition between the two. The PCEHR operations and associated governance arrangements will also transition from the Department of Health to the new Commission, with the e-health policy function remaining with the Department.
The Communications Alliance Internet of Things (IoT) Think-Tank is to meet for the first time in Sydney to set the direction for the IoT program, which is expected to focus on creating an environment that allows the full potential of IoT services to be realised in Australia and the opportunity for Australian companies to be early beneficiaries of new business models through IoT. The Bureau of Communications Research within the Federal Department of Communications will sit on the Executive Council of the Think Tank as an 'active observer', alongside other members - Intel , IBM , Telstra , Alcatel-Lucent , Huawei , Ericsson and Hewlett-Packard , RoZetta Technology , KPMG and Creator Tech .
A computer algorithm which was used to interpret the changing levels of a protein called CA125 in 46,237 women has detected twice as many women with ovarian cancer as conventional strategies, according to the latest results from the largest trial of its kind led by University College London . The new approach to ovarian cancer detection developed by UNSW Vice-Chancellor Ian Jacobs could lead to widespread screening for the disease that kills about two in three sufferers in Australia.

Nextgen Networks has signed a data connection deal with Roy Hill Project in the Pilbara. Under the deal, Nextgen will be supplying Roy Hill with data connectivity between their communications systems in port Hedland and their Remote Operations Centre in Perth.
