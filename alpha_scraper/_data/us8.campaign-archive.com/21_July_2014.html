news briefs

Australia has been ranked 17 on this year’s Global Innovation Index. The index, which is published annually by Cornell University, INSEAD and the World Intellectual Property Organization, surveys 143 economies around the world using 81 indicators to evaluate a country’s innovation capabilities and results. You can see Australia’s results here . Switzerland, the United Kingdom and Sweden topped this year’s Global Innovation Index. Australia was ranked 19 on the Index in 2013.
The NSW Supreme Court has ruled in favour of Telstra in their case against NBN Co over when consumer-price-index (CPI) adjustments apply to the price that Telstra charges NBN Co to access its infrastructure. The decision could mean NBN Co would need to pay Telstra an extra $200 million. NBN Co has the option to appeal.
Epworth HealthCare has opened two new hospitals in Camberwell and Hawthorn with the help of TechnologyOne’s OneHealth Software.
The Australian collaborative economy has been valued at $46 billion according to a Deloitte report commissioned by Google Australia. The full report is here .
Australian space scientists will be launching a new Advanced Instrumentation and Technology Centre (AITC) at The Australian National University (ANU). The opening of the centre means scientists will no longer need to go offshore to build satellites; and positions Australia at the forefront of its ability to engineer equipment right from the design stage through to launch-pad. ANU has also signed two contracts totalling over $11 million for projects at the AITC.
Communications Minister Malcolm Turnbull has released a report which reviews the telecommunications access regime and rules relating to the operation of NBN Co.
Data Tools has entered into a long-term partnership with United Kingdom based company, Postcode Anywhere . The partnership was made by Data Tools to allow users to search for addresses efficiently by finding potential matches using keystrokes.
Fujitsu has entered into an agreement with Businesslink on behalf of the Department of Family and Community Services to provide a cloud based email service for 27,000 mailboxes across NSW Government agencies. Businesslink believes that this new agreement will provide staff with increased functionality and remote access through mobile technology.
Researchers at Deakin University and Japanese University, Aizu have teamed up to develop a new invention to reduce music piracy. The new multimedia watermarking process is said to lead authorities back to the source of the piracy without damaging the quality of the product.

company watch

QUT Creative Enterprise Australia has invested in online fashion retailer, Fame and Partners . Fame and Partners was founded by Nyree Corby and Elton Stewart and launched last year. Fame and Partners is a vertically-integrated, personalised eCommerce destination for special occasion dresses.

people watch

Alcatel-Lucent have welcomed Matthieu Destot as its new Vice President of Sales for Asia Pacific. In his new role, Destot will be responsible for growing the business in the Asia Pacific.
Mitrais has appointed Des Kearse as an Australian Sales Manager for its Software Development business unit. He will be based in Brisbane.
Kim Duffy has stepped down as Softlink’s CEO. Duffy is now a Non-Excutive Chairman. Softlink has elevated the current COO, Nathan Godfrey as its new Managing Director.
