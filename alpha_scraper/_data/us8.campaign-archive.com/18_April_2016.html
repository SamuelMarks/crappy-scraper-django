news briefs

Congratulations to Professor Thomas Maschmeyer and the team from University of Sydney spin-off company Gelion Pty Ltd, who have gained a commitment from Armstrong Energy valued at $11 million. Armstrong Energy is one of the UK's major utility-scale solar energy companies. "The idea is to build houses with batteries inherently included as part of their structure, and also to serve as a buffer for the grid, enabling an ever greater share of renewables to be connected," said Maschmeyer, who is also the director of the Australian Institute for Nanoscale Science and Technology, which launches this month at the University of Sydney.
Just in case you missed it, U.S. President Barack Obama has spoken about University of Melbourne research ! The Stentrode (stent-based electrode) is the size of a small paperclip and will be implanted in the first in-human trial at The Royal Melbourne Hospital in 2017 in the hope that those with spinal cord injuries will be able to walk again using this brain-machine interface . Led by Dr Thomas Oxley, 39 academic scientists from 16 departments were involved across The Royal Melbourne Hospital, The University of Melbourne and The Florey Institute of Neuroscience and Mental Health.
In welcome news for Sydneysiders dreading another long weekend with its inevitable traffic congestion, the NSW Government is partnering with National ICT Australia (Data61) to provide real-time data on congestion incidents and information about alternative routes. Data from Opal, GPS devices, traffic signals, and buses will be used to improve travel times and the transport network.
In further NSW road news, the NSW Government has announced a Smart Innovation Centre for Western Sydney . The Centre will be 'a new research and development hub for emerging transport and road technology.' Expressions of interest are sought from industry, investors and academics.
Imagine Intelligent Materials Pty Limited (Imagine IM) believes that Australia will become the first country to use graphene in the large-scale manufacture of an industrial product. Sydney-based Imagine IM has entered into a licensing agreement with Geofabrics Australasia Pty Limited . Geofabrics will become the exclusive licensee of Imagine IM's graphene coating technology for applications in geotextiles.
A $100 million innovation partnership will boost research and development funding for Australian research through the new Torch Innovation Precinct at UNSW. The partnership is supported by Chinese companies and the Ministry of Science and Technology's 'Torch' high technology industry development program. An initial $30 million investment has been secured by eight Chinese companies to support Australian research in advanced materials, biotechnology, energy and environmental engineering. The first group of UNSW Torch industry partners will set up incubator spaces on UNSW's Kensington campus.
Optus and Vocus Communications Limited (ASX: VOC) have announced an exclusive three-year mobile agreement. This extends a previous relationship with M2 Group, which merged with Vocus earlier this year.
Robots and renewable energy are among the winners in the latest round of Australian Renewable Energy Agency (ARENA) funding. The Australian National University (ANU) has won $4.7 million in funding for renewable energy research. The funded projects will examine how robots could capture data for solar PV installation diagnostics, estimate the power produced by rooftop solar PV, and look at how consumers can take charge of their energy use through batteries while being rewarded for helping to manage the electricity grid.
The SHOW INNOVATION online video and podcast show has launched, with interviews from over 20 Australian innovators. It is founded and hosted by Sidney Minassian. Interviewees include Dr Ian Oppermann, Chief Data Scientist at the NSW Data Analytics Centre (which has recently received a new advisory board - see this weeks People Watch for more).
NAB is partnering with PwC to help businesses reduce time spent on GST compliance. PwCs digital platform Airtax will integrate expense and income data from NAB customers through their transaction accounts to assist in managing their GST requirements. NAB Labs has also developed a Visa Debit card for customers using the Airtax platform.
Mint Payments Limited (ASX: MNW) has signed a five-year strategic partnership agreement with Singapore's Network for Electronic Transfers (NETS). Mint will deliver a NETS branded unified mPOS to enable merchants to accept NETS and credit/debit card payments.
Telstra has added a Video Monetisation Solution and Online Video Solution to its global media portfolio. In other Telstra news, the Australian Competition and Consumer Commission's (ACCC) annual report on Telstra's compliance with its Structural Separation Undertaking was tabled in Parliament last week . Its been a busy week for Telstra, who also make this weeks People Watch and Company Watch.
Code for Australia is partnering with the Victorian Government to launch the Code for Victoria Innovation Challenge , which will see technologists collaborating with the State Government on their current challenges. Code for Australia will receive $365,000 to place three teams from their Innovation Fellowship into selected departments for six months.
Under a new contract , YPB Group Limited (ASX: YBP) will provide its proprietary VariSec technology to protect a southeast Asian country's ePassports.
TPG Telecom Limited (ASX: TPM) and I-MED Network have signed a multimillion dollar commercial agreement for TPG to deliver a dedicated high-speed network connecting all I-MED locations nationwide.
Surveillance cameras and drone data will be used to work out how climate change is affecting our forests. Dr Tim Brown from the ANU has helped to launch the Australian Phenocam Network . You can see an interactive version of the Canberra panorama here .

company watch

Airtree Ventures has invested in video production marketplace 90 Seconds, which has raised US$7.5 million in Series A financing led by Sequoia India. The round was also supported by SKY TV NZ, Beenext and Fotolia.com. Founded in 2010 in New Zealand by Tim Norton, 90 Seconds has a global team of 70 based in Singapore, London, Tokyo, Manila, Sydney and Auckland.
Telstra will sell a 47.7% stake in Chinese online business Autohome to Ping An Insurance Group for $2.1 billion. Telstra will retain a 6.5% interest in the company.
Linius Technologies (ASX: LNU) will join the ASX this month through a reverse takeover of Firestrike Resources Ltd (ASX: FIE). The company has raised $3.5 million through an oversubscribed issue.
Renewable energy company Greenearth Energy Limited's (ASX: GER) wholly owned subsidiary, Vivid Industrial , has completed its 100% acquisition of Ilum-a-Lite Pty Ltd.
Quantum Resources Limited (ASX: QUR) and the vendor of LogiTag Systems Limited have agreed to terminate the Share Purchase Agreement.

people watch

An advisory board to the NSW Data Analytics Centre has been announced . The board, to be chaired by CIO of the ASX Tim Thurman, includes Mary O'Kane (NSW Chief Scientist and Engineer), Professor Hugh Durrant-Whyte (Director of the Centre for Translational Data Science at the University of Sydney) and Alex Scandurra (CEO of Stone & Chalk).
Deanne Weir is to join Foxtel as Executive Director Channel Aggregation and Wholesale, as of June 2016. Weir was Group Director, Corporate Development at Austar for ten years, and is currently Chair on the boards of Ai-Media, Hoodlum and Moshcam.
Former AMP CEO and Managing Director and Chairman of Stone and Chalk Craig Dunn is joining the Telstra Board as a non-executive Director.
Deloitte Australia has appointed John Preston as Director of Commerce, Deloitte Digital. Preston was previously Commerce Technology Group Director (Asia Pacific) for DigitasLBI.
Empired Limited : (ASX: EPD) has appointed John Bessey as Group General Manager, Sales and Marketing.
