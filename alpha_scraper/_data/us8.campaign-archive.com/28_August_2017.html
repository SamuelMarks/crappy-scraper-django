NewsBriefs

Victoria has launched its Cyber Security Strategy and claims it is the first state in Australia to do so. As part of the Strategy, the Victorian Government plans to appoint a Chief Information Security Officer, strengthen partnerships across levels of Government and the private sector, and develop a workforce plan to attract and retain skilled cyber security public sector workers.
Telstra's Security Operation Centre (SOC) has opened in Sydney. A Melbourne SOC will follow later this month, with further locations to open in 2018.
New ICT procurement reforms have been announced with the release of the ICT Procurement Taskforce Report.
Dubbo motorists are invited to take part in Australia's first digital driver licence trial this November.
A social humanoid robot has interacted and assisted Air New Zealand customers as part of an experiment to see how social robotics can improve customer experience at the airport. The robot, Chip CANdroid, is Commbank 's social humanoid robot.
Applications are open for mentees to enter the Working Lunch program, a new initiative to encourage entry-level women to keep working in the game development industry.
Aussie cloud computing company and host of the Watch, Joviam , has expanded to the United States, launching its services out of San Francisco.
Last weekend 450 students from 40 schools across Queensland competed in the Young ICT Explorers competition , which allows students in remote Queensland areas to compete at a national level.

The recording and distributing of intimate images of a person without their consent is now an offence in the NSW Crimes Act 1900. It is also an offence to threaten to record or distribute intimate images or 'revenge porn'.
$125,000 of new grants will go towards supporting ACT community organisations to establish or enhance their digital capacity. Successful recipients include the Indigenous Reading Project and the ACT Deafness Resource Centre.
Ultradata will provide core retail banking services for Teachers Mutual Bank Ltd .
A new 64-slice CT scanner will be included in the new Karratha Health Campus in Western Australia.
The ACCC is warning the public about scammers pretending to be from the nbn.
A partnership between Telstra and WA Police aims to support victims of domestic violence , with the State Family Violence Unit taking delivery of 100 smart phones with in-built duress capability.
The eSafety Office has launched Young & eSafe , a new youth engagement platform. According to research released by the eSafety Commissioner, 50-60% of young Australians aged between 12-17 have witnessed racist or hateful comments about cultural or religious groups online.
Sky and Space Global (ASX: SAS) and Sat-Space Africa have signed a binding commercial contract for the provision of narrow-band services. Sat-Space Africa is the company's first wholesale operator.
UNSW is home to Australia's first quantum computing company , Silicon Quantum Computing Pty Ltd. The NSW Government is the latest to join the $83 million venture, with a pledge of $8.7 million from its Quantum Computing Fund.

EnergyWatch

South Australia has invited renewable energy projects that provide power in categories including firming renewable generation, bulk energy storage, and bioenergy. Applications under the $150 million Renewable Technology Fund are open until 28 September 2017.
Magnis Resources Limited (ASX: MNS) has signed a Memorandum of Understanding with German Lithium-ion battery consortium TerraE-Holding GmbH for the supply of raw materials. Magnis will supply raw materials to the TerraE Gigafactories.
Ongoing laboratory test work between Hazer Group Ltd (ASX: HZR) and the University of Sydney has confirmed the production of 99.95% ultra-high purity synthetic graphite from graphite concentrate. The aim is for synthetic graphite to become 'a high-quality alternative product' within growing markets where ultra-high purity levels are required, as in lithium-ion batteries.
The Federal Government, through the Australian Renewable Energy Agency, is providing up to $12 million in funding for a 30MW large-scale battery . The battery will be designed, built and owned by ElectraNet.
The Federal Government has asked SolarReserve Australia Pty Ltd for project details about its proposal to construct a 150MW concentrated solar thermal power plant at Port Augusta in South Australia.
Imagine Intelligent Materials ' new graphene factory has opened in Geelong. The Advanced Manufacturing Centre also announced a $250,000 grant to co-fund projects commercialising graphene at an industrially-relevant scale.

PeopleWatch

Jayne Leighton is CSIRO 's new Chief Information Security Officer. Leighton most recently built and led the platform security capability for Defence's End User Computing project.
Kordia 's new appointments include Michael Warnock as Country Manager for Cyber Security and Tim Steele as Channel Manager for Cyber Security in Australia.
Tquila ANZ has appointed Janet Michie as Telstra Channel Manager. Michie was previously with Telstra.
Gail Williamson is WiseTech Global 's new Chief Growth Officer. The company also named Piers Shervington as Head of Corporate Affairs. Williamson has previously worked at Henderson Global Investors, ABN AMRO and AMP, while Shervington was previously at Cochlear.
Dilshan Sivalingam is Zscaler 's first Alliance and Channel Manager for ANZ.

CompanyWatch
