news briefs

3D Graphtech Industries has entered into an agreement with CSIRO to investigate research opportunities in the application of graphite and graphene inks in 3D printing and fused filament fabrication. 3D Graphtech is owned equally by Kibaran Resources Limited (ASX:KNL) and the 3D Group.
The Federal Government has partnered with Australia Post and the Department of Human Services to trial digital mail delivery.
The Australian Tax Office has delivered guidance on the taxation treatment of Bitcoin and other crypto-currencies in time for people to complete their 2013-14 income tax returns. Under the guidance paper and rulings, Bitcoin transactions are treated like barter transactions with similar taxation consequences.
The Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade (DFAT) has released its first procurement under the International Communications Network (ICN) program. The five-year ICN program includes modernising and upgrading DFAT’s communications network.
Mobile Embrace (ASX:MBE) has partnered with Telenor Group to provide direct carrier billing - a secure mobile payment allowing Telenor Group’s mobile subscribers to charge purchases directly to their phone bill.
Veda (ASX:VED) has signed a five-year multi-million dollar agreement with IBM to extend its outsourcing relationship.
The City of Melbourne has made IBM their official ‘Innovation and Technology’ partner. The three-year partnership was made to enhance Melbourne Fashion Week by leveraging the benefits of mobile devices, social media and data analytics.
WiseTech Global has announced e-Novator will join its global partner program to provide Central and Eastern European logistics industries localised access to advanced management systems.
A class action has been initiated by ACA Lawyers against Telstra, Optus and Vodafone for issuing illegal late fees.
The Bureau of Meteorology has made plans to purchase a new supercomputer and datacentre following upgrades to its data link, boosting speeds from 80 gigabits per second to 200. The supercomputer has been scheduled to operate for five years from July 2016 with small upgrades made two years into its operation.
Dataweave has been selected by the New South Wales Government to implement and support the deployment of its Identity Management solution to manage user identities across all NSW government enterprise resources in the firewall and beyond the cloud.
Vodafone has announced plans to modernise and consolidate its core network with the help of Ericsson.
Minister for Communications, Malcolm Turnbull, has released a draft of the Australian Communications and Media Authority Direction 2014 for public consultation. The draft works to provide NBN Co with the license to provide fixed wireless in areas surrounding major cities.
The Western Australian Department of Water has selected NEC to help them to manage the increased demand on water caused by population growth.
Scientists at the Australian National University have discovered a way to manufacture hydrogen as a fuel. Hydrogen is used to launch space crafts and is a zero-carbon replacement for petroleum products.
NBN Co is currently trialling fibre-to-the-node technology using fibre optic cables with Telstra’s copper lines in a streetside node cabinet.

company watch

Congratulations to Tech23 alumnus BugHerd for securing $1M in Series A funding from Tank Stream Ventures and Starfish Ventures , in conjunction with US-based 500 startups .
Upcoming listings on the ASX include: BPS Technology , owner of Bartercard; smarter building services platform, Urbanise.com Limited ; and digital customer engagement platform for SME merchants, Rewardle Holdings Limited .
ECAL has received $2million investment from Oxygen Ventures . Founder and CEO Patrick Barrett pitched at 'The Big Pitch' competition run by Oxygen Ventures in May 2014 and was one of two winners to secure pre-termsheets.
Melbourne's GetViable has been acquired by BigColors , the Hong Kong-based startup crowdfunding platform for an undisclosed amount. GetViable's platform will be integrated by Bigcolors over the next several months.

people watch

Sam McDonagh has been appointed by Airbnb as its new Country Manager of Australia and New Zealand. McDonagh was previously an iiNet Executive and is the co-Founder of Quickflix .
