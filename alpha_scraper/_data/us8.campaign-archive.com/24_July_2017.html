NewsBriefs

Nuclear science has helped prove the earliest Aboriginal occupation in Kakadu National Park. Powder x-ray diffraction was used to identify minerals present in artefacts. These methods, alongside extensive optically stimulated luminescence dating conducted at the University of Wollongong, indicate that occupation dates back to some 65,000 years ago.
Geoscience Australia has released the seafloor mapping data collected during the first phase of the search for missing Malaysia Airlines flight MH370. The data is some of the first high resolution data of this region.
The Australian Government is to sign the International Solar Alliance which aims to deepen cooperation on solar research and development, financing mechanisms, and diffusion of solar technology amongst countries in the tropics.
A US cube satellite carrying miniaturised GPS technology developed in Australia has been deployed from the International Space Station. The technology was developed by UNSW in partnership with Defence Science & Technology Group.
Mt Stirling Victoria’s visitor hub will be fully powered by a micro-hydroelectricity system through a $210,000 state government grant, replacing a large diesel generator.
Techstars Adelaide have announced the first cohort of startups to go through its accelerator. The startups are building businesses around technology such as intrusion detections (Cron Systems), noise reduction for drones (Dotterel), and artificial intelligence hacking (Nimis Cybersecurity).
Optus has announced that it will invest up to $1 billion to improve mobile coverage in regional and remote parts of Australia. As part of its new strategy, Optus will build 500 new mobile sites across regional Australia, upgrade more than 1,800 sites from 3G technology to 4G technology, and ensure that more than 200 sites are 4G enabled. Optus will also continue to deploy its satellite small cell technology.
Metronode will spend more than $150 million on the development of three new data centres in Melbourne’s Derrimut, Sydney’s Silverwater and Wollongong’s Unanderra.
Not-for-profit GP clinic Inala Primary Care has developed an app with the help of Random Hacks of Kindness Brisbane to provide better immunisation services for refugees . The app enables doctors to get the correct vaccine dosages and combinations for patients of different ages.
The South Australian Government is funding a $2 million project to develop a 3D digital model of the Adelaide CBD and its surrounding suburbs. The 3D model will be used to review planning strategies and decisions.
Sydney is home to one of four new IBM data centres . Other centres are located in London and San Jose.

Adtran has signed a supply agreement with nbn to help deliver high-speed broadband access. Adtran will provide the software, hardware and services needed to support the NBN rollout.
Agriculture Victoria has formed a partnership with AgriTask, an Israeli-based agricultural technology company, to deliver a farm management and decision support tool for Victorian farmers.
The SA Police Academy is now using a $480,000, 300-degree virtual reality firearms simulator . The simulator will train cadets, refresh frontline officers' skills and provide advanced training for STAR Group.
Samsung Pay and ANZ have partnered to enable customers to make secure contactless purchases.
South Australian firm LogiCamms has signed a Memorandum of Understanding with French company Dassault Systèmes to map capability across multiple industries in the state.
Mirvac - which acquired Australian Technology Park (ATP) - and Melbourne-based York Butter Factory are establishing ‘ Hoist ’, an innovation hub in Sydney’s ATP which will initially offer 50 co-working spaces to startups and corporates.
Congratulations to our hosting provider, Joviam , who has been named as a Gartner Cool Vendor in Cloud Infrastructure.
The winners of the Communications Alliance ACOMM Awards were announced last week, with Alexander Osborne (Head of Regulatory at VHA) named as Australian Communications Ambassador 2017. You can view all the winners here .
Civica has secured a $6.3 million contract with Lake Macquarie City Council to deliver its Authority 7 local government software to help improve the council’s business processes and deliver a better customer experience.
97% of households with children own video game devices according to new research by Bond University and the Interactive Games and Entertainment Association . The Digital Australia Report 2018 lead author Professor Jeff Brand found that the research showed games increasingly are serving other uses, including social connectedness, stress reduction, and physical and mental health benefits.
Fintech hub Stone & Chalk is expanding to Melbourne with CEO of Fintech Victoria to lead the Victorian-based operations. Stone & Chalk Melbourne will be housed alongside agtech innovation hub and accelerator SproutX, and will offer 60 desks for fintech startups.
The ACCC has welcomed the entry of more competitors into the telecommunications market, as it undertakes a broadband speeds monitoring program.

CompanyWatch

Village Roadshow Ltd (ASX: VRL) and Robert Kirby, Co-Executive Chairman and Co-CEO of Village Roadshow will invest a combined total of $1.5 million into Linius Technologies (ASX: LNU), a Melbourne-based virtual video developer.
Nuheara (ASX: NUH) - which has recently been ramping up sales channels in the US for its intelligent wireless earbuds, IQbuds - has raised $9 million through completing an institutional placement.
NextDC (ASX: NXT) has acquired a 14.1% stake in Asia Pacific Data Centre Group (ASX: AJD).
Melbourne-based retail technology startup Brauz has raised $2.25 million in seed funding . Brauz will use the funding for R&D and to build its in-house development team.
Speedcast International Limited (ASX: SDA) will acquire remote communications and professional services provider UltiSat for up to US$100 million . As part of the purchase, the company will create a new government division.
