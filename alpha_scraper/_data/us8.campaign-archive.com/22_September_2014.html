news briefs

The Centre for Eye Research Australia (CERA) has opened the Automated Stem Cell Facility and brought Australia’s unique new stem cell robot to life. Using adult stem cells, sourced from the skin cells of patients, CERA researchers produce eye cells for disease modelling which allow for new drug therapies to be developed.
David Miles AM has been appointed to lead the review of the Australian Government’s Cooperative Research Centres (CRC) Programme. Miles, the former Chair of Innovation Australia will start the review this month with the final report and recommendations expected by early 2015. The CRC programme began in 1991 under the Hawke Government. Since then, a total of 209 CRCs have been funded by the Australian Government which has committed more than $3.8 billion through the CRC programme. There are 36 CRCs currently in progress ranging from Cell Therapy to Space Environment Management. The review’s terms of reference and information about how to make a submission can be found here .
Westpac has partnered with BlueChilli to launch an Innovation Challenge competition that offers $40,000 to the startup that uses technology in smart ways to solve problems for businesses in the property & real estate industry. Five startups will be selected to pitch their idea to Westpac and the winner will receive $40,000 in cash.
JanssenHatch is seeking innovative solutions to improve early disease detection and monitoring; access to and sharing of health information; connectivity between Australians and healthcare providers and services. Four finalists will each receive $10,000 and the opportunity to collaborate with industry experts to workshop their ideas and business plans. A single winner will then be chosen to receive $100,000 to kick-start commercialisation of their idea.
Yahoo7 is integrating the PLUS7 service with the Fetch TV platform, allowing Fetch TV subscribers access to some free-to-air TV shows. PLUS7 offers video streaming with full-length episodes from Channel Seven, 7mate and 7TWO, as well as content from other partners.
SmartTrans Holdings (ASX:SMA) has signed agreements for its mobile billing to be integrated into apps with four Chinese smartphone game developers.
After an eight month competitive tender, the Australian Communications and Media Authority has selected ZOAK Solutions to build and manage Australia’s registry of telephone numbers.
Telstra has won a five year contract to provide Melbourne’s Etihad Stadium with an in-stadium wireless and IPTV network solution allowing spectators across the venue access to content and competitions and sponsors with advertisement and personal messaging opportunities.
The Australian Communications Consumer Action Network has launched the Digital Ready Digital Business Kit and the DigitalReady.org.au website aimed at small businesses and not-for-profit organisations in the Arts, Recreation and Education services sector.
The Department of Finance has selected Acquia to provide an open cloud platform for the development and delivery of its govCMS system. The govCMS system aims to help agencies reduce costs and increase their agility as they engage citizens with government services.
The Government is appointing a Children’s e-Safety Commissioner and is preparing a legislation to enhance online safety for children to be ready for Parliament by the end of the year. The commissioner will receive $7.5 million for online safety programmes in schools and $100,000 to support Australian based research and information campaigns on online safety.
Tech23 alumnus OrionVM has been awarded a Gold Stevie® Award in the New Product or Service of the Year, Software – Cloud Infrastructure category in The 12th Annual American Business Awards.
Analysis released by CSIRO on Australia’s galleries, archives and museums has revealed cultural institutions risk losing their relevance if they are unable to keep up with the changes in digital technology.

company watch

Global Payments Inc . (NYSE: GPN) has entered a definitive agreement to acquire Ezi Holdings Pty Ltd (Ezidebit) for $305 million. Founded in 1998, Ezidebit is the largest Australian non-bank merchant acquirer and integrated technology-enabled payment solutions company.
Ezeatm Limited (ASX:EZA) has executed an agreement to sell its ATM business to DirectCash Payments Inc., valuing the ATM business at $14.1 million. Consideration includes $10.26 million cash and cancellation by way of selective buy-back of shares.

people watch

Square Peg Capital is going global! Planning to invest over $100 million in businesses across the globe, Square Peg has announced that Matt Rockman has moved from Melbourne to Los Angeles and Dan Krasnostein has moved from Melbourne to Tel Aviv. Also joining the Square Peg team is Philippe Schwartz in Boston and Chris Hitchen (founder of Getprice) in Munich. Arad Naveh is joining as a Venture Partner in Israel. The news comes after Square Peg led the Series A for Feedvisor - a Tel Aviv based Algorithmic Repricing Platform late last month.
Jack Singh has been appointed as Director, NICTA Victoria. Prof. Singh joins NICTA from La Trobe University where he has been Director of the Centre for Technology Infusion.
Clipp has appointed Todd Forest, former Microsoft Executive, as its new CEO. Clipp has been selected as one of the Tech23 2014 companies.
Orion Health has appointed Andrew Ferrier as its new Chairman, succeeding Andrew Clements who announced his leave at the company’s annual general meeting.
