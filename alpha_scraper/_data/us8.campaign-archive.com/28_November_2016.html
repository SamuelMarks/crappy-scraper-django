news briefs

Australian broadcasting network SBS has chosen Ericsson for its media delivery services . Ericsson will combine and prepare content from multiple distributors worldwide and deliver it in a format ready for SBS to communicate.
NetComm Wireless Limited (ASX: NTC) has signed a Master Equipment and Services Supply Agreement with nbn for the supply of Distribution Point Units. Supply is likely to commence in 2018.
Queensland biofutures companies now have access to a $5 million development fund to help develop their ideas into commercial products.
Australia’s largest intelligent vehicle trial is to be held in Ipswich. 500 Ipswich motorists will be recruited to have their vehicles retrofitted with cooperative intelligent transport systems (C-ITS) technology to trial vehicles and infrastructure that can talk to one another, as well as to test cooperative and highly-automated vehicles on South East Queensland roads. The Queensland Government’s Cooperative and Automated Vehicle Initiative is co-funded by the Motor Accident Insurance Commission. It will be delivered with the support of a number of organisations including Ipswich City Council, Bosch Australia and QUT’s Centre for Accident Research and Road Safety – Queensland.
The new $1.2 million Manufacturing Technologies Centre has opened in Adelaide's northern suburbs.
Congratulations to the winners of the Victorian Schools Games and Apps Challenge . Winners include grade 6 students Maddy and Teisha of Princes Hill Primary School for their app named Foundit and best overall winner Jason of Braemar College for a game called Anxiety.
New legislation will be introduced to repeal the Competition and Consumer Act 2010 's telecommunications-specific anti-competitive conduct laws . Vodafone has expressed its concern with 'the proposal to repeal one of the longstanding cornerstone protections of competition in the telco sector in such a rush.'
From 3D printed sternums to 3D paper weaving, CSIRO 's Advanced Manufacturing Roadmap identifies opportunities for growth for Australia's manufacturing industry.
Alibaba Cloud is launching international data centres in Sydney and three other regions: Japan, Dubai, and Frankfurt.
The ACCC is taking action against Meriton Property Services Pty Ltd over their alleged interference with TripAdvisor 's 'Review Express' service.
The Government has responded to the Productivity Commission's report on public safety mobile broadband , voicing its in-principle support for the findings and recommendations.
Perth-based Fleetsu has won $45,000 ($30,000 in cash with $15,000 credit for AWS’ web hosting services) in Motorola Solutions' public safety hackathon.
Technology companies, startups and digital providers are invited to provide feedback on making Federal Government ICT contracts accessible to small innovators.
Technologies to counter radio frequency emitters will be developed as part of the $4.9 million Royal Australian Navy (RAN) 'Cuttlefish' project . The emitters will help the Navy to 'sustain a presence in hostile and threatening environments.' BAE Systems Australia has received funding to assist RAN to develop and demonstrate the technology to enhance defence capability.
IBM and the Government reached a commercial-in-confidence settlement regarding the #censusfail earlier this year. The MacGibbon Review into the 2016 eCensus was tabled in Parliament last week, with the Government accepting all recommendations.
ProVision , an Australian network of independent optometrists, has chosen 1stAvailable (ASX:1ST) to manage its online appointment bookings system.
US-based subsidiary of Telstra’s Ooyala is partnering with Sky Sports to deliver Premier League game clips and highlights packages.
Shark Mitigation Systems Limited 's (ASX: SM8) Clever Buoy shark detection system will be installed at City Beach in Perth for a trial lasting until the end of March 2017.
Shoe manufacturer Mizuno is using dorsaVi 's (ASX: DVL) ViMove Run solution in a pilot program in UK retail stores to better inform shoe selection. The solution uses sensors to wirelessly measure and record movement and the impact of up to nine variables while the customer runs on a treadmill.
iSignthis Ltd (ASX: ISX) has announced an agreement with Cyprus-based Leverate Financial Services to provide their Paydentity solution .

NZME Limited (ASX: NZM) has filed a submission with the New Zealand Commerce Commission (NZCC) regarding the issues raised in the NZCC's draft determination of the proposed merger between NZME and the NZ subsidiary of Fairfax Media Limited (ASX: FJX). The joint submission with FJX raises concerns that the NZCC 'has failed to properly take into account the diversity of opinions that will continue post-transaction in an increasingly converged digital world.'

company watch

Open source data platform for cloud-based solutions Instaclustr has closed a $7 million investment round , led by Bailador Technology Investments (ASX: BTI).
Estonian-based global job search company, Jobbatical has raised $1 million from AirTree Ventures .
Western Australian loan lender company Enably has raised $33.5 million from US investors Richard Driehaus and hedge fund Corbin Capital Partners. Enably aims to move towards larger loans.
eSports Mogul Asia Pacific (ASX: ESH) has completed an ASX listing after an oversubscribed $7 million capital raise.
Sky and Space Global (ASX: SAS) has received firm commitments for an oversubscribed $7 million capital raising , comprising a $5 million placement and $2 million priority offer to shareholders. The funds will help the company to fast-track commercialisation of its technology and kick-off the next phase in the rollout of 200 nano-satellites.

people watch

Julie Inman Grant is the new Children's eSafety Commissioner . Commencing in January, the role will focus on tackling 'revenge porn', or the non-consensual sharing of intimate images. The Office of the Children's eSafety Commissioner will also be renamed the Office of the eSafety Commissioner, reflecting its expanded focus on online safety for adults. Minister for Women Michaelia Cash said the new role will "help women manage technology risks and abuse". Inman Grant was previously Twitter's Director of Public Policy, Australia and southeast Asia.
Blockchain-based software solutions group DigitalX Limited 's (ASX: DCC) will be led by Australian-based CEO Leigh Travers and US-based President Neel Krishnan, with co-founders Alex Karis and Chief Trading Officer William Brindise to step down from the board. The new management team has completed an oversubscribed capital raising of $1.62 million, with the new board personally contributing around $350,000. The company’s wholly-owned subsidiary, AirPocket LLC, signed an agreement with Servicio UniTeller, giving AirPocket access to UniTeller's network of cash-out locations in Mexico.
Don Campbell has joined Saasabi in an advisory role. Campbell was previously CTO at Cognos.
ShoreTel (NASDAQ: SHOR) has announced four internal management appointments in its operations in Australia and New Zealand. The appointments will form part of the company’s strategic restructure into cloud services. Craig Howe has been promoted to Manager Channels, ANZ; Richard Ruth has been appointed Client Sales Director, ANZ; John Jackson will lead ShoreTel’s technical design and delivery team; and Bradley White has been appointed Manager Customer Success, ANZ.
IT cloud security company, Zscaler , has appointed Sean Kopelke as Country Manager, Australia and New Zealand. Kopelke was previously at FireEye.
