news briefs

New York headquartered, LiveTiles (ASX: LVT) has entered into a Co-Marketing Agreement with Microsoft Corporation under which LiveTiles Design will be marketed to all United States Office 365 commercial customers with more than 250 seat licenses. LiveTiles was developed originally by nSynergy which was acquired by Rhipe (ASX: RHP) in 2014 for $25.35 million. Rhipe took a 12.5% investment stake in LiveTiles at the time it acquired nSynergy for $25.35 million at the end of 2014. Led by co-founder Karl Redenbach (who also is the founder of nSynergy), LiveTiles listed on the Australian stock-exchange through a reverse listing earlier this year.
Matthew Ho (founder of Tech23 Alumni, Native Tounge ) who is leading the Change.org petition to save ATP as a Tech park has recently received support from Tom Forgan, the founding CEO of ATP and from Stan Jeffrey, the founding director of ATP Innovations .
CSIRO has launched an eight person energy generating swing known as the Infinity Swing to kick start a conversation about energy sustainability in Australia. Users can hop on the swing and see their movements turned into energy to power a light and sound installation. The swing will be at Federation Square in Melbourne from Monday 16 - Saturday 21 November 2015.
Optus Business has signed a three year $115 million contract with the Department of Immigration and Border Protection to supply end-to-end telecommunications and managed IT services.
Zendesk is to open their Asia Pacific Software Engineering Centre in Melbourne encouraged by investment from the Victorian Government .
Macquarie Telecom 's subsidiary, Ninefold is to close its doors with the last day of operation being January 30, 2016.
The Victorian Government may be forced to pay up to $20 million to more than 40,000 school teachers who were made to lease laptops from the Education department after the Victorian Federal Court ruled the scheme illegal under the Fair Work Act.
The Australian Research Council has announced its Major Grants for 2016 awarding $357.7 million across four schemes funding 899 projects. With 17.7% of those who apply for grants receiving funding, you might find this article in The Conversation interesting reading - Are we funding the right researchers in Australia?

company watch

Clean tech energy retailer, Solco Limited (ASX: SOO) is to acquire Redback Investments Australia which owns 20% of energy storage company Redback Technologies Limited for US$200,000 plus Solco shares based on a valuation prepared by an independent expert expected to be in the range of $800,000 to $2million. It is proposed that Solco will market, sell and distribute Redback's products and services. Redback partners with Microsoft and is housed at the University of Queensland 's ilab incubator .
Fairfax has invested in Homepass , founded by Daniel Oertli. The mobile property inspection app will be embedded into Domain . Oertli, who was previously the CIO of REA Group , was the founder of Tech23 2013 alumni Roomz , which was acquired by REA Group.
Australian mobile invoicing app, Invoice2go , has secured US$15 million in funding through a new round of capital raising led by Ribbit Capital , bringing its total funding to $50 million.
Document processing startup Shoeboxed Australia has closed $775,000 angel investment round led by Smorgon Capital , with participation from Andrew Bird and Jono Herrman, Steph Hinds of Growthwise , and the BlueChilli Venture Fund .
The Australian Competition and Consumer Commission has announced it will not oppose the proposed merger of Vocus Communications (ASX: VOC) and M2 (ASX: MTU).
