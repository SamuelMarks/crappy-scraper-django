news briefs

NICTA and Los Alamos National Laboratory have begun a 12 month Cooperative Research and Development Agreement (CRADA). Under this CRADA agreement, funded jointly by U.S. Department of Energy and NICTA, Los Alamos is tasked to build model reduction methods for coupled power grid and natural gas systems. NICTA’s role is to build a standalone software module for modelling and optimizing joint gas and electricity network design and operation.
Australian-born math genius Terence Tao is one of five people to win the inaugural Breakthrough Prize in Mathematics , for his contributions to harmonic analysis, combinatorics, partial differential equations and analytic number theory. Facebook founder Mark Zuckerberg is among the founders of the competition, which aims to generate excitement about science as a career. A child prodigy, Tao received his bachelor's and master's degrees at the age of 16 from Flinders University.
Five Canberra-based businesses have been accepted into the inaugural 2014 GRIFFIN Accelerator program . Congratulations to Made for me (3D printing marketplace), Quizling (Mobile quizzes in the classroom), Symberra (modelling platform for big data), Enabled Employment (recruitment site for people with special needs), and Snapknock (mobile enabled marketplace). The program is focussing on start-ups servicing government, education and health.
Vodafone has partnered with Dick Smith Electronics to offer Vodafone postpaid products and services in over 276 Dick Smith locations across Australia, doubling their national reach for postpaid.
The Australian Sports Technologies Network (ASTN) has officially launched its HeadStart Accelerator Program in Geelong aiming to incubate and invest in up to 40 new sports technology and IT start-up businesses over the next four years. HeadStart’s establishment was supported by funding from Enterprise Geelong (part of the City of Greater Geelong) and the Department of Employment (formerly DEEWR). A non-investment intake of seven start-ups recently commenced the program. HeadStart has plans to raise $2 million into an investment fund over the coming 6 month period to fund investments of $20,000-$50,000 for a 10-15% equity stake in each HeadStart start-up.
The finalists for the 2014 ACOMM Awards have been announced! The awards are a joint project of Communications Alliance and Communications Day and recognise exemplary achievement across 12 categories of endeavour. The 2014 ACOMMs will be presented at a gala industry event in Sydney on 30 July. View the full list of finalists .
In partnership with the SA Government, Internode has launched Adelaide CBD’s free wireless network .
The Victorian Government has launched a website to collect contributions about their digital economy strategy.
Pantha Corp has announced that it will be partnering with MuleSoft to provide better support and tools for its clients, allowing it to remain competitive.
The Australian Communications and Media Authority have granted SubPartners the right to use the Southern Sydney Cable Protection Zone for the landing of APX-East, a four fibre-pair system delivering 10Tbps per fibre pair. It will connect Sydney to California with planned branches to a number of Pacific Islands.
NBN Co and Telstra have agreed to pilot the planning, design and construction of 1000 nodes across Queensland and New South Wales, ahead of the anticipated large scale integration of Fibre-to-the-Node (FTTN) technology into the NBN.
The Federal Government has confirmed that the maximum amount that can be reimbursed under the Export Market Development Grants scheme as an initial installment is $60K. The grant reimburses exporters who have made eligible marketing expense claims through the scheme.

company watch

Nimble has closed a $10 million funding round attracting the company's first institutional investors - Acorn Capital and Monash Investors. Private investors also participated in the funding round, including previous investors iSelect Founder and chairman Damien Waller and iSelect director Les Webb. Founded in 2006 and headquartered in Queensland, Nimble offers loans of between $100 to $1200 which can be approved and paid in an hour and must be paid back within 16-50 days.
Intel Capital, the merger and acquisition arm of Intel Corporation has invested in New Zealand's Performance Lab which specialises in real-time exercise measurement, analysis and virtual coaching software. Financial details of the investment weren't released.
