news briefs

STAT Innovations won top prize in the 2015 MedTech’s Got Talent entrepreneurship challenge, hosted by The Small Technologies Cluster (STC). The coveted $40,000 cash prize will help STAT Innovations fast-track its Eyenaemia screening tool – a tool that enables a ‘selfie’ to detect the risk of anaemia, a condition that affects around two billion people worldwide. STAT Innovations co-founded by Monash graduate and Alfred Health’s Dr Jennifer Tangand and Monash medical student Jarrel Seah, can use any camera-enabled smart device to take a photo of the inner lining of the eyelid and use a cloud-based algorithm to rapidly determine the risk of anaemia.
The nbn has released its updated 2016 Corporate Plan .
Although Quickflix (ASX: QFX) has pulled out of a deal to acquire a Chinese content production company, they have confirmed they are still intent on developing a China strategy.
NEC Australia has won a four year contract to manage the 12,000 users of the 160 global sites of the Australian Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade 's Global Support Centre Services.
Metronode has signed an agreement enabling Telstra ’s business customers access to its 10 Australian data facilities located in seven cities.
DeakinDigital , a subsidiary of Deakin University has announced an international partnership with Credly to offer digital credentials for professional skills.
Urbanise.com Limited (ASX: UBN) is entering into a customer agreement with Interactive who will use the Urbanise platform and remote monitoring devices as part of their DC Care program to help customers to remotely monitor their data centre facilities.
Huawei ’s $30 million National Training and Innovation Centre has officially opened in Chatswood and will be used to train its customers and partners on the latest high tech ICT equipment.
The City of Sydney has drafted a Tech Startup Action Plan available online at sydneyyoursay.com.au until 10 November 2015.
HCL Technologies has been appointed as the strategic IT partner to SAI Global (ASX: SAI) for five years.
Yahoo7 has announced the commercial launch of Tumblr in Australia and New Zealand, with Woolworths , Telstra , Monash University and Studio Canal already on board as launch partners. Tumblr has more than 4.6 million users in Australia.

company watch

Isentia (ASX: ISD) has announced the acquisition of content marketing company King Content in a $48 million deal. King Content will remain a separate brand to Isentia, with offices in Sydney, Hong Kong, Singapore, the UK and the US.
Palette formerly known as SwatchMate has closed $1 million Series A funding led by Palette Chairman, Adam Lewis, ex-Managing Director of Mckinsey Australia .
Alcidion , provider of intelligent informatics software to support clinical decision making in the hospital environment has been acquired by Naracoota Resources (ASX: NRR) in a reverse takeover. The transaction is backed by Allure Capital , the private investment vehicle of Nathan Buzza, BlueSky Private Equity (ASX: BLA) and Paterson Securities Limited . Alcidion which is valued at $12 million has been led by Professor Malcolm Pradhan and Ray Blight since they founded the company in 2000. Alcidion's Miya Platform is used by every ward in Victoria's Western Health's three acute hospitals. Alcidion raised $2 million in Series A led by BlueSky in 2012 which was complemented through $1.964m in funding from Commercialisation Australia.
Sydney startup Simply Wall St has closed $600,000 funding round led by Mike Quinn from Innovation Capital , with participation from Sydney Angels and their Sidecar Fund.
TPG Telecom Limited ’s (ASX: TPM) acquisition of iiNet Limited (ASX: IIN) now has the green light from the Australian Competition and Consumer Commission .

people watch

Energy Action Limited (ASX: EAX) has appointed Mark de Kock as its new Non Executive Director. Kock previous roles include Executive Director for Strategy at Vocus Communications (ASX: VOC).
Myer (ASX: MYR) has appointed Mark Cripsey as its first Chief Digital and Data Officer. Cripsey was previously General Manager of Coles Online.
The Federal Government has reappointed Timothy Pilgrim as Australia’s Privacy Commissioner for a further year. Previously, Pilgrim was appointed as acting Australian Information Commissioner for a three-month period. His latest appointment will commence 19 October 2015.
South Australia is on the search for a Chief Information Security Officer. The newly created role will be located within the Attorney-General’s Department, working for the portfolio’s shared services unit Justice Technology Services. Applications are open until Wednesday 3 September 2015.
