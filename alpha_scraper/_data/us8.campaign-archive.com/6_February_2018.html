NewsBriefs

Australian Research Council's Centre of Excellence for Nanoscale BioPhotonics has developed a 3D printable ‘clip-on’ that can turn any smartphone into a fully functional microscope.
DTA has released the first component of the Trusted Digital Identity Framework outlining requirements for security, usability, privacy protection, accessibility, fraud protection and risk management.
ANSTO is providing expertise and irradiation services for Sydney-based OncoSil Medical to help provide radioactive phosphorus for implantable medical devices to treat pancreatic cancer .
The Notifiable Data Breaches scheme is now in effect. The new privacy laws regulate the reporting and notification of eligible data breaches with penalties for not notifying of data breaches including fines of $360,000 for individuals and $1.8 million for organisations.
After winning an international competition to have the DNA of a dingo sequenced, UNSW scientists are set on sequencing the genome of a German Shepherd to help prevent hip dysplasia .
QUT is testing a novel technology developed by US company Mercurius Biorefining to convert biomass into jet and diesel fuels .
95 percent of Tasmanian devils immunised prior to being released into the wild have generated a robust antibody response to the deadly devil facial tumour disease according to a new study.
Researchers from the University of Melbourne and Museums Victoria have CT scanned all 13 known Tasmanian tiger joey specimens to create 3D digital models.
University of Melbourne researchers are using smartphones to assist patients with bipolar disorder by monitoring and predicting swings between their manic and depressive episodes.
RMIT has partnered with fintech hub Stone & Chalk and Accenture to develop an eight week course on blockchain strategy .
Vault Systems and ID Exchange have announced a partnership to present an option for self-sovereign cloud-based storage.
A new Institute for Forest Products Innovation hub has been launched at the University of South Australia 's Mount Gambier campus with $4 million funding from the Australian and SA Governments.
Nine Western Australian agtech companies have been chosen to participate in the State's first agtech-focused accelerator program run by AgriStart.
For the first time in its 30 year history Western Australia 's biggest fishing event, Swanfish, has incorporated 'catch, click and release' technology to collect data on local fish.
The ACCC is inviting the views of consumers, media organisations, digital platforms, advertising agencies and advertisers after outlining the key issues it will be considering in its digital platforms inquiry .
Legislation passed in Victorian Parliament last week will allow driverless vehicles to be trialled across the state.
Lockheed Martin Australia has welcomed the official opening of its new Australian corporate headquarters in Canberra.
Tech23 alumnus Fleet Space Technologies have won a grant from the Dutch Government for an agricultural IoT project, that will will monitor rainfall and optimise irrigation.

CompanyWatch

ANZ has taken an equity stake in Data Republic and will begin using the data exchange platform to develop insights about its customers.
Identity verification startup Everproof has closed a $1.5 million seed round backed by Reinventure.
Energy management solution provider Simble Solutions (ASX:SIS) has listed on the ASX having raised $7.5 million in an IPO.
Real estate startup Soho has raised $1.65 million from BridgeLane Group. Soho is founded by Airtasker co-founder Jonathan Lui.
Monash spin out Cincera will develop therapies for metabolic syndrome and type-2 diabetes-related diseases, has launched with a $7 million venture capital commitment from the Medical Research Commercialisation Fund.
After several weeks suspended, logistics software company GetSwift (ASX: GSW) has resumed trading on the ASX.
Retail marketing startup Shping has launched a $46 million ICO after raising millions in its pre-sale.
Linius Technologies (ASX: LNU) has successfully raised $10 million in an over-subscribed capital raising.

PeopleWatch

John Kearney has joined TIBCO Software as regional Vice President. Kearney has previously headed up Zuora.
CEO of Jobs for NSW Karen Borg is exiting her role after residing over the official opening of the $35 million Sydney Startup Hub.
Alan Noble is leaving Google after ten years of heading up engineering to focus on the expansion of his Adelaide-based ocean research not-for-profit, AusOcean . Anil Sabharwal will lead Google's engineering efforts locally and also work with AirTree Ventures portfolio companies as a Venture Partner.
After 20 years in the role, John Croll has resigned as CEO of Isentia . The board will undergo a global search for a replacement.
Vocus (ASX:VOC) CEO Geoff Horth is leaving “by mutual agreement” after two years at the helm of the telco. The role of Interim Group CEO will be filled by Michael Simmons while Deputy Chairman Bob Mansfield AO oversees the search for a new CEO.
Education software company Tribal Group has announced the appointment of Peter Croft as Asia Pacific Managing Director. Croft has worked previously with the NSW Government in the Health sector.
