news briefs

South Australia Police's (SAPOL) facial recognition system will be delivered by NEC Corporation under a $780,000 contract. The turnkey system is based on NEC's NeoFace facial recognition software and enables police to monitor, scan, detect and search images of suspects. NEC Australia's Melbourne-based R&D team is working with SAPOL to develop applications using facial biometrics technology.
Trading Technologies International (TT) has launched its trading platform in the Australian Liquidity Centre. Customers of TT will have access to the new platform through ASX’s point-to-point fibre network - ASX Net .
NAB and the University of Melbourne are partnering to study innovation in Australian businesses. According to the NAB Labs Innovation Index, WA, SA, NT and QLD are the most innovative states. 1 in 2 firms identified a lack of time as the main barrier to innovation and business change.
A tender process for Victoria Police's technology overhaul will see police given access to mobile devices, to be used in partnership with a new intelligence system to be rolled out progressively over four years.
Scientists are using pre-industrial air bubbles in polar ice to measure the sensitivity of the earth's land biosphere to changes in temperature. CSIRO senior scientist Dr David Etheridge explains that the result will be important in the years to come because it indicates how the biosphere responds to temperature changes - like the expected future warming of the planet.
If you watched last week's Caribbean Premier League (CPL) Twenty20 tournament in Florida, you may have noticed flashing cricket stumps and bails - designed by South Australian technology innovation company, Zing International .
The ACCC will regulate access to wholesale superfast broadband services , declaring a five year 'superfast broadband access service' (SBAS). The SBAS declaration allows retailers to access non-NBN network services including AAPT's FTTB network and some of Telstra's fibre networks.The declaration aims to increase competition and gives retailers the chance to enter superfast broadband markets.
The ACCC also released its NBN Wholesale Market Indicators Report.
A new discussion paper is calling for views on how consumer information about broadband speed and performance can be improved. The ACCC is concerned about the 'current lack of clear information about broadband performance in advertising and other material'.
Telstra has extended its contract with Visionstream for the delivery of Wideband Services across Australia. The contract will generate $250 million in revenue during its initial three year term.
Edith Cowan University has deployed a next-generation Cisco network service. The new platform is managed by Data#3's managed services team in a 3+2 year agreement.
Deakin University has used Citrix technology as part of its cloud-based IT strategy. The rollout began in late 2015, with the university deploying a virtual environment, giving students and staff access to over 80 applications including ArcGIS, ArchiCAD and Solidworks.
Victorian metropolitan and regional health services can apply for funding to set up or expand telehealth services through the State Government's $5 million investment in telehealth. The funding is designed to enable specialist clinics to deliver patient consultations securely online.
Spookfish has selected NEXTDC Limited (ASX:NXT) to colocate its processing infrastructure and public cloud connectivity services.
You can now access geospatial data and info on NSW's renewable energy resources with the NSW Renewable Energy Resource Map.
Before summer, five new VR4G shark listening stations will be installed in the water on the NSW south coast at Kiama, Sussex Inlet, Mollymook, Batemans Bay and Merimbula. A further five will be added at Crescent Head, Old Bar, Hawks Nest, Redhead and Bondi. If a tagged shark swims within 500 metres of the stations, an alert is sent to @NSWSharkSmart and the Shark Smart app. So far 29 sharks and 88 bull sharks have been tagged.
Canberra Hospital is installing solar panels and new lighting to reduce costs and the carbon footprint as part of $3.3 million in ACT Government funding to help the hospital become more energy-efficient.
Crowd Mobile Limited (ASX:CM8) is planning to enter the Chinese market through the Beijing subsidiary of its subsidiary SmartTrans Technology (ASX:SMA).
A rare toadlet has been re-discovered in the Adelaide Hills through a new smartphone app that tracks frogs by their calls. FrogSpotter has helped to find the rare Bibron's Toadlet at Stirling, Aldgate and Athelstone. The app allows you to record frog calls and note the GPS location, habitat, and weather, as well as take photos. Users then upload the data to a national database.
FirstWave Cloud Technology Limited (ASX: FCT) is integrating a new cloud security service provided by Cisco System Inc.
Western Health has signed a $2.35 million, three-year Memorandum of Understanding with Alcidion Group Limited (ASX: ALC) to use the company's health technology across its three facilities - Western Health at Footscray, Sunshine Hospital at St Albans, and the Williamstown Hospital. The Miya platform gives clinicians access to patient information and clinical data on mobile devices.
Syntonic Limited (ASX: SYT) has extended its strategic software licensing agreement with Tata Communications.
iSignthis Ltd (ASX: ISX) has entered an agreement with Ixaris, under which iSignthis will integrate its Digital Know Your Customer service to provide Ixaris with remote identity verification and compliance to the EU's 4th AML Directive, which takes effect next year.
Mobile Embrace Limited (ASX: MBE) is expanding into Norway through an agreement with Telenor Digital.
Black Moth Vision Systems and SRX Global Australia are collaborating on the manufacture of several products.

company watch

MYOB (ASX:MYO) has acquired Auckland headquartered enterprise resource planning software provider, Greentree, for a total consideration of NZ$28.5 million.
Megaport Limited (ASX: MP1) will acquire Germany's second largest internet exchange operator , PEERING GmbH - which operates under the brand ECIX. Megaport will also acquire Bulgarian-based OM-NIX. The total acquisition cost is $3.1 million.
VivoPower USA - the subsidiary of Arowana International Limited's (ASX: AWN) operating company VivoPower International - has closed an agreement to finance and manage the build of a 43MW DC utility-scale solar photovoltaic project in North Carolina, United States. The project has a 10 year power purchase agreement with Duke Energy Progress Inc. VivoPower has executed binding agreements for New Energy Solar Fund to acquire a majority stake in the project for US$41.7 million.
Manalton Limited (ASX:MLT) has acquired Hearis, an enterprise social media management platform from Queensland-based Generation Co.
Fairfax Media Limited (ASX:FXJ) has outlined the details of the creation of a Domain Group segment to be reflected in the 2016 Full-Year Results.
Tabcorp Holdings Limited (ASX:TAH) is to acquire gaming systems company INTECQ Limited (ASX:ITQ) for approximately $115 million.
