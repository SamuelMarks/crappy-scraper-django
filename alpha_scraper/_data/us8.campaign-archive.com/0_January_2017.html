In response to the recent U.S. travel ban on citizens from several Muslim-majority countries, including war-torn Syria, Berlin's Mayor Michael Müller warned about the danger of building walls, saying: “We cannot let all our historical experience get trashed by the very people to whom we owe much of our freedom: the Americans. I call on the President of the USA not to go down that road to isolation and ostracism.” Canadian Prime Minister Justin Trudeau reacted to the ban by stating that “Diversity is our strength”, to which Atlassian’s Co-founder Mike Cannon-Brookes responded by tweeting : “This is what I’d like to see from @TurnbullMalcolm. We are also a country built on immigrants. Let’s not forget that.” TechSydney has condemned the US travel bans and called on the Australian Government to do the same.

NewsBriefs

Prime Minister Malcolm Turnbull has called cyber-attack "the new frontier of warfare”. Read last week's doorstop with the Prime Minister and the Minister Assisting the Prime Minister for Cyber Security, Dan Tehan.
Commonwealth Bank has trialled its private, permissioned blockchain platform for the end-to-end issuance of bonds. The bank issued a cryptobond for the Queensland Treasury Corporation using its capital markets blockchain platform.
Perform has awarded Telstra a six year, multi-million dollar deal to deliver a global media network solution for the Women's Tennis Association tournaments .
The Queensland Government is launching a 'refreshed' ICT strategy. The strategy, Advancing our Digital Future , has been developed by the Queensland Government Chief Information Office and will replace the current ICT strategy 2013-17.
Chorus will extend its ultra-fast broadband (UFB) rollout to a further 169 areas in New Zealand, under an agreement with Crown Fibre Holdings. Chorus estimates the cost of the UFB2 communal network to be $370-410 million.
The $1 million Advance Queensland Founders' Fellowships will give 10 local researchers and clinicians the chance to commercialise their work.
Japanese drone company Terra Drone will open a regional office in Brisbane's River City Labs innovation precinct.
As students return to school, parents are being encouraged to talk to their children before posting images of them online. The advice comes from the Office of the Children's eSafety Commissioner and Western Sydney University, who have partnered for the #talkb4sharing education initiative.
The CSIRO will open an office in San Francisco to support the Government's National Innovation and Science Agenda.
Skyfii Limited (ASX: SKF) have signed a five-year contract with Aliansce Shopping Centre in Brazil to roll out its IO platform of data analytics and marketing services.
South Australia is establishing a new organisation to produce defence-related R&D. The Defence Innovation Partnership comprises the South Australian Government, the Defence Science and Technology Group (DSTG), Adelaide University, Flinders University and the University of South Australia. DSTG will provide $150,000 per year. One of the program aims is to provide a route to market for commercialisation of future tech.
In the last six months of 2016, 32% of cyberbullying complaints received by the Office of the Children's eSafety Commissioner involved specific threats to a child's safety. More details can be found in the Office's Quarterly Report .
Optus Business has announced a five-year, $75 million partnership with Flight Centre Travel Group that will help deliver global security, mobility, expansion of data connectivity, as well as communications and collaboration services.
Norwood Systems (ASX: NOR) has signed a partnership agreement with loyalty-program provider Affinion International . Affinion will include Norwood’s World Apps in its portfolio aimed at targeting brands in the financial services industry, retail, travel, and commerce sectors.
CSIRO's Data61 has developed a national fire behaviour knowledge base called Amicus . It will enable calculation of expected fire danger and predict fire behaviour via a simple interface.

PeopleWatch

Angela Clark is to join Commonwealth Bank as the Executive General Manager Small Business. Clark was previously at ABC, and is to contribute at March’s The Deep Dive .
Dr Genevieve Bell is now a Professor in the ANU College of Engineering and Computer Science. She joins the university from Intel, where she served as Vice President, and was the first woman in the company to be appointed an Intel Senior Fellow - a position she is to retain. Bell will also collaborate with CSIRO's Data61.
Airtree Ventures has appointed James Cameron, senior investor at Accel Partners as a new partner. Airtree has also hired San Francisco tech marketer Julia French as its Head of Community.
The new chair of Internet Australia is Anne Hurley, replacing George Fong, who takes on the role of honorary secretary. Hurley is former CEO of Communications Alliance. Chris Winter has also joined the board.
The new Premier of NSW , Gladys Berejiklian has reshuffled her Cabinet, with Matthew Kean now Minister for Innovation and Better Regulation.
Tyro Payments Limited has appointed Kareem Al-Bassam as Head of Product. Al-Bassam founded PayPal Credit in Australia.
ASI Solutions has appointed a National Education Adviser. Michael Eggenhuizen was previously Director of ICT at Parramatta's King's School.
ABC's Chief Operating Officer David Pendleton is leaving the ABC .
Group Executive of New Business at Telstra, Cynthia Whelen has been appointed Chairman of its Foxtel pay-TV business.
Agile Australia 2017 captain Adam Boas is the new CTO of Melbourne-based education software company MyXplor . Adam Boas was previously at Culture Amp .

CompanyWatch

Tech23 alumni and Sydney-based Local Measure has raised $4.5 million in capital to support growth and global expansion. The company will open offices in Dubai and Los Angeles. Participants in the round included private investors, funds and private equity firms in the Asia Pacific region.
NZME Limited (ASX: NZM) is continuing to progress the New Zealand Commerce Commission (NZCC) process regarding the proposed merger with Fairfax New Zealand, the subsidiary of Fairfax Media Limited (ASX: FJX). The final NZCC decision is due by 15 March 2017.
GreenSync has secured an $11.5 million Series B funding round led by Federal Government-owned Clean Energy Finance Corporation and Southern Cross Venture Partners .
Westpac’s Reinventure has invested in Doshii , which connects apps to any POS.
