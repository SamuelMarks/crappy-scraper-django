NewsBriefs

CSIRO has signed a five year, $35 million research agreement with Boeing . The organisations will work on areas including space sciences, advanced materials and manufacturing.
A member of the Australian Federal Police (AFP) has accessed a journalist's telecommunications data without a warrant. The AFP has notified the Commonwealth Ombudsman regarding the breach. AFP Commissioner Andrew Colvin said: "The AFP understands the importance of individual privacy and we support this as a fundamental right in Australia. However, in the 21st century, access to metadata is central to the vast majority of successful crime investigations. It is critical that our law enforcement and intelligence agencies can access it."
Monash University has announced a tender for the supply of 45-55 GWh of electricity from a renewable energy source.
Unions NSW and Airtasker have come to a new agreement to improve workers' rights and conditions, in what Unions NSW calls a "world-first" for the gig economy. They commented on Twitter: "Fairness matters. Our @airtasker agreement means minimum pay rates, injury insurance & an independent umpire. We're only getting started."
Tech23 alumnus Saber Astronautics received a $40,825 grant from Jobs for NSW which has helped the company to develop a commercial spacecraft mission plan for Fleet Space Technologies. Saber's technology will help the company fly 100 IoT satellites.
Australia and China have agreed to enhanced cyber security cooperation . Both countries agreed that neither would "conduct or support cyber-enabled theft of intellectual property, trade secrets or confidential business information with the intent of obtaining competitive advantage." An agreement has also been made to establish a "mechanism" to discuss cyber security and cyber crime issues, in order to prevent incidents that could create problems between Australia and China.
Grey Innovation is taking part in the feasibility stage for the development of water quality monitoring technology for the Great Barrier Reef Catchment.
Fraser Coast Regional Council has deployed the Nutanix Enterprise Cloud Platform.
A $10 million investment program inviting researchers to develop innovative solutions to defeat improvised threats has been announced as part of The Grand Challenges program. The Grand Challenges program is a key element of the $730 million Next Generation Technologies Fund designed to provide game-changing capabilities for the future Australian Defence Force.
Nominations are currently being accepted from companies and individuals at the forefront of naval and commercial maritime innovation for the Maritime Australia Industry Innovation Awards.

A new report from IP Australia shows Australian universities are ranked 11 in the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD), ahead of countries like Israel and Switzerland, for university-industry collaboration resulting in international patent applications. The research found that collaboration between Australian businesses and universities accounts for a significant proportion of our international patent applications.
The Western Australian-based Western Barley Genetics Alliance, made up of the Department of Agriculture and Food and Murdoch University, with funding support from the Grains Research and Development Corporation, was a key contributor to an international consortium that recently produced the first complete genome map for barley , enabling breeders to develop better quality barley varieties that can be used for clearer, tastier beers.
BroadSoft has signed a multi-year strategic agreement with Telstra, enabling customers to connect via the cloud.
Properties at the end of high voltage powerlines in the most acute bushfire-risk locations in Victoria will be given the opportunity to go off the grid and reduce bushfire risk by making powerlines shorter. Those eligible for a a Stand-Alone Power System will be granted access to renewable energy such as wind and solar panels, batteries for storage and backup generators.
$259 million will be invested over five years to consolidate public health systems and open up accessibility to allow clinicians to treat patients across the Northern Territory.
auDA has ended its exclusive negotiations with AusRegistry for the management of its registry operations beyond 2018. A strict by-invitation tender exercise will be undertaken. AusRegistry will be invited to participate in the tender process.
Titjikala is the first community to receive mobile phone reception under a three-year, $30 million co-investment program between the Northern Territory Government and Telstra.
Skyfii Limited (ASX: SKF) has announced a master services agreement with Woolworths of South Africa to roll out the company's IO Software as a Service platform subscription data analytics services to 500 stores, with 126 stores already live.
Pilot testing of Peppermint Innovation's (ASX: PIL) mobile bill payments technology has commenced with Metro Gas Sales in the Philippines.

CompanyWatch

IoT for energy company Wattwatchers has raised $2 million from the Southern Cross Renewable Energy Venture Capital fund (REVC). The funds will help to expand the company's technology partnerships, increase production volumes, expand customer support and recruit new staff. REVC is managed independently by Southern Cross Venture Partners , whose managing director Dr Mark Bonnar will join Wattwatchers' board.
Smart Sparrow - a Tech23 alumnus - raised $4 million in funding to expand its instructional design and courseware platform. Moelis Australia Asset Management came on-board as a new investor in the deal structured by returning investor OneVentures. Returning investor Uniseed also participated.
EnviroSuite (ASX: EVS) has sold its entire Pacific Environment Consulting practice (PEC) for $15 million to Environmental Resources Management (ERM). The transaction will be conducted via a sale of the shares in EnviroSuite's three PEC subsidiaries. All PEC staff will transfer to ERM.
Airwallex has raised US$13 million in Series A funding. Chinese company Tencent Holdings, Sequoia Capital China and Mastercard all participated in the round. The funds will go towards international expansion.

PeopleWatch

The new CEO of $80 million venture BioCurate is Dr C. Glenn Begley . BioCurate was launched last year by Monash University and the University of Melbourne and is supported by the Victorian Government. Begley was most recently Chief Scientific Officer of Akriveia Therapeutics in California.
Andrew Rothwell is joining Data Republic as Chief Operating Officer. Rothwell co-founded Tyro Payments in 2003.
Professor Sue Thomas, Provost and researcher from The University of New England, will be the new Chief Executive Officer of the Australian Research Council.
JD Peterson is now Chief Growth Officer at Culture Amp. He was previously Chief Marketing Officer at Trello.
Amazon Web Services has appointed Matt Allen to the role of Startup Business Development, based in Melbourne. He was most recently at Lookahead Search.
Fintech company DirectMoney (ASX: DM1) has appointed Andrew Goodwin as Chief Financial Officer. Goodwin was previously Partner at Draycap.
Agile Australia Conference Captain Alex Stokes is now Partner at Luna Tractor. Stokes has previously had roles at Envato, REA Group and Australia Post.
