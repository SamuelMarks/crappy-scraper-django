news briefs

The Federal Government has released the strategy Boosting the Commercial Returns from Research , acknowledging that when it comes to collaboration between business and researchers, Australia’s performance falls away. “Despite our strong history in research, Australia ranks last in the OECD on the proportion of businesses which collaborate with research institutions on innovation. Similarly, in 2011, Australia ranked second last of 17 OECD countries on new-to-the-world innovation,” Minister Pyne said. Key actions to be taken under the strategy include: publishing priorities to align areas of national research excellence with Australia’s industrial strengths, global trends and community interests; develop simpler and more transparent research block grant arrangements; identify further opportunities to enhance collaboration between publicly funded research agencies and industry; review research training arrangements; examine the operation of the R&D Tax Incentive; develop whole-of-government policy for opening access to publicly funded research publications and data.
Back pain patients monitored and treated using wearable movement sensors from dorsaVi Ltd (ASX:DVL) have had a significant and sustained improvement in pain and functional ability, according to new data from a 12 month trial involving 112 patients at Austin and Epworth Hospitals.
The ACT’s emergency radio communications network will be upgraded over the next three years, with $14.728 million allocated in the upcoming ACT Budget .
CSIRO has developed a new technology known as Eddy, which keeps track of electricity use, collects and analyses the data, and makes recommendation to help users save money. The technology is currently being commercialised by HabiDapt which is trialling it in Perth, and in conjunction with Ergon Energy Retail in Queensland.
ATP Innovations and The NSW Office for Health and Medical Research have opened applications for their Medical Device Commercialisation Training Program, which aims to assist participants develop the skills needed to commercialise medical devices.
The Western Australian Government has allocated $21.6 million funding in the 2015-16 State Budget to the Pawsey Supercomputing Centre . The funding, which runs until 2020-21, will be used by the centre for scientific research requiring high level computational, visual and data management resources.
Canberra’s GRIFFIN Accelerator has announced the successful participants for the startup incubator. The companies include: Hact , Solution Solution , OzGuild , Dilkara , myBUZZ App , SignOnSite , and Solar Bare .
Empired Limited (ASX: EPD) has secured a five year contract worth $20 million with Rio Tinto (ASX: RIO) to provide application managed services and project services.

company watch

Tidy Me has raised $750,000 from Airtree Ventures . Founded by Stacey Jacobs, Tidy Me enables you to book a cleaner for your home.
DWS has acquired Symplicit Pty Ltd for an up-front payment of $8.5 million, followed by further payments up to a total of $6.5 million based on performance over a three-year period. Symplicit is a UX digital design consultancy which will provide DWS with $8.5 million in annual revenue. All 50 staff, including management team, are expected to move to DWS.
Amcomm (ASX: AMM) has set Monday 15 June as the new date for the shareholder vote on its proposed merger with Vocus Communications (ASX: VOC) and has sent a letter to its shareholders with information relevant to the vote.
Tripalocal , a muru-D company, has raised $850,000 round of funding led by China based investor company Euler Capital LLC .
Ingogo has closed $12 million Series C funding, with $4.2 million raised through VentureCrowd . Ingogo has now raised more than $28 million in its four years.

people watch

Hills Limited (ASX: HIL) has announced its Group Managing Director and CEO, Ted Pretty will be leaving the company. Pretty will be replaced by Grant Logan, current Chief Operating Officer, effective immediately.
