news briefs

Eight startups have been accepted into the Melbourne Accelerator Program 2015 . Startup Accelerator - EIRA Biotech - delivery of therapeutics into the cochlea; Hypetap - influencer marketing platform; Jobbop - ICT hires in under a fortnight; MobilKamu - car-buying online platform based in Indonesia; NoteXchange - online exchange program for student notes; Nuraloop - Headphones; Parkhound - finds spare parking spaces; Relectrify - energy storage using advanced battery management systems. The founders of these eight companies receive Entrepreneurial Fellowships from the University of Melbourne with other benefits including $20,000 in funding; office space and trips to Silicon Valley.
Curtin University has become the fourth Australian university to join global education platform, edX . edX was established by Harvard and MIT universities in 2012.
Tech23 alumni Maestrano has opened its second office in the United States, within 18 months of the business first opening its doors in Sydney.
OtherLevels Holdings Limited (ASX: OLV) and Switch Digital Pty Ltd have entered into a strategic partnership to deliver mobile audience engagement solutions.
Tesla has opened its third retail store in Australia and its second in Melbourne. The new store, situated in Richmond, is owned by local investors Michael Gannon and Gus Cooper, both of whom purchased the building complex for $20M in June 2010.
Presto movie and TV Subscription Video on Demand streaming service will be distributed by Quickflix (ASX: QFX) following an agreement between Quickflix and Foxtel.
The Royal Flying Doctor Service is to be the launch partner for Activistic Limited (ASX: ACU). Founded in 2012 and formerly One Cent Call, Activistic completed a reverse takeover of Acuvax Limited in mid-May 2015 and raised $6M. Activistic has executed an agreement with US mobile billing aggregator Mobile Giving Foundation for charities and groups in the US to use Activistic's micro-donations platform. Through the Foundation, Activistic's micro-donation services will be available to all Verizon Wireless , AT&T Mobility , Sprint , T-Mobile USA and US Cellular customers.
Deakin University has signed an MOU with Max Healthcare , which runs 10 hospital branches in India. Both partners will put "big data" to work across a large array of medical records - including admissions, diagnosis and computerised registries - with the aim of identifying critical safety issues and assisting clinical efficiencies.
