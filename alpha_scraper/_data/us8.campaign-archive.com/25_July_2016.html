news briefs

$1 million in Advance Queensland funding will be provided to develop and test Remotely Piloted Aircraft Systems technologies. The technology will be developed through a partnership between The Boeing Company, Boeing subsidiary Insitu Pacific, Shell's QGC project, Telstra and local SMEs. The project will create 100 new aerospace industry jobs in Queensland, and is part of the $10 million Advance Queensland Platform Technology Program.
The winners of the tenth annual 2016 Communications Alliance & CommsDay Awards included Optus Mobile, who took out an Innovation Award for Cash by Optus Wearables and also the Best Mobile Solution Award. The Internet of Things Innovator was won by NetComm Wireless Smart Home Gateway, and TollShield took home the Emerging Innovation Award for Symbio Networks fraud mitigation software.

The new home for University of Wollongong's business incubator and accelerator, the iAccelerate Centre , has been opened. The Centre was supported by $16.5 million from the NSW Government's Restart NSW Illawarra Infrastructure Fund. The three storey, 4000 square metre building has space for over 280 entrepreneurs.
A University of Queensland study is trying to discover how adults with hearing loss use telecommunications to support their general health. The study will look at aspects of e-health and investigate potential improvements.
In more UQ news, the Centre for Children's Burns and Trauma Research has partnered with digital health company iPug to develop Cool Runnings , a smartphone app to educate parents about preventing burn injuries in children. And an early career researcher at UQ is refining an app-connected device that encourages stroke survivors to speak more frequently. The CommFit pedometer measures vibration through the collarbone using an accelerometer to indicate how much the wearer has talked during the day.
The winners of the Optus Future Makers were awarded $50,000 grants , including Marita Cheng for 'Teleport' - an affordable telepresence robot which allows young people with a spinal cord injury or debilitating disease to work remotely; Marina Paronetto for 'Biz', a peer-to-peer mobile app designed to help teenage girls learn about business; and Dr Rowan Tulloch for 'The Game Change' software, which helps university and school teachers to gamify their classrooms.
Aconex (ASX: ACX) has signed a three year enterprise agreement for UK and Irish contractor Farrans Construction to use their collaboration platform.
ZEN Energy has won a $1 million tender to install lithium ion batteries at the State Library of South Australia, the Art Gallery of SA and Adelaide High School.
Telstra's variation to the nbn Migration Plan has been approved by the ACCC.
The Australian Red Cross Blood Service will use Oracle Customer Experience to support its new Donor Relationship Management system to help match blood demand with blood types.
ResApp Health Limited (ASX: RAP) is planning a clinical study at Massachusetts General Hospital in Boston. The company is developing a smartphone medical application for the diagnosis and management of respiratory disease.
Submissions are invited in response to the National Research Infrastructure Capability Issues Paper, released by Australia's Chief Scientist Dr Alan Finkel.
Optus Business has announced a new three year ICT services agreement with QBE Australia that will see Optus connecting 4000 QBE end users across 42 locations throughout Australia using the Optus network.

CSIRO is working with Enirgi Group to develop its MagSonic technology , which produces magnesium using up to 80% less energy and 60% less carbon dioxide emissions. The technology uses a supersonic nozzle, and aims to take advantage of Australia's reserves of magnesite ore.
The Internet of Things Alliance Australia (IoTAA) was launched as an independent not-for-profit entity last week. The IoTAA grew out of an IoT Think Tank created by Communications Alliance early last year. It will initially be hosted and supported administratively by the University of Technology, Sydney.
Transport for NSW has migrated to 8Common's (ASX:8CO) Expense8 expense management platform on a SaaS basis.
The Adaptive Variable Speed Limit system is now fully automated on the M80 Ring Road in Victoria. An algorithm assesses live traffic conditions and regulates traffic speed.
Noveda Technologies - which is to be acquired by Buddy Platform Limited (ASX: BUD) - has been selected as a provider of real time energy monitoring for a new grant program in New York.
Touchcorp has signed a software development and transaction services agreement with Change Up Holdings Limited. Having completed the first phase of the development services, Touch's initial payment is $11.25 million in cash and shares.

company watch

Adcorp Australia will take a 15% investment in video startup business Shootsta for approximately $1 million in a mix of cash and in-kind services. Adcorp will also take a seat on the Shootsta board.
Mobile platform services provider Syntonic (ASX: SYT) has commenced trading on the ASX after raising $2.2 million through its public offer.
Brisbane-based medical device company Oventus has listed on the ASX with a fully subscribed IPO underwritten by Bell Potter, and with strong support from Ord Minnett, Baillieu Holst and Peloton Capital.
Blue Sky Venture Capital has participated in a $13.6 million financing round in ecommerce provider aCommerce . MDI Ventures and DKSH also participated in the raise. The investment was made by a fund managed by Blue Sky Venture Capital, a division of Blue Sky Alternative Investments (ASX: BLA).
Ezycollect has raised $1.25 million in a round led by Tank Stream Venture Capital.

people watch

ABC TV has appointed Josh Faulks as Head of Partnerships and Policy beginning in September. Faulks has previously had roles at Communications Alliance and as Head of Corporate Affairs with Salmat Ltd. Most recently, he was Deputy Chief of Staff to the Attorney General and Leader of the Government in the Senate.
Greg Hunt is moving from the Environment portfolio to become the Minister for Industry, Innovation and Science. Paul Fletcher will become the Minister for Urban Infrastructure. Craig Laundy will become the Assistant Minister for Industry, Innovation and Science.
Labor has appointed Michelle Rowland as the Shadow Minister for Communications , a portfolio previously held by Jason Clare. Rowland is a lawyer who specialised in communications before entering Parliament. Clare will become the new Shadow Minister for Trade and Investment, as well as taking on the job of Shadow Minister for Resources and Northern Australia. Stephen Jones will continue in his role as Shadow Minister for Regional Communications. Senator Stephen Conroy has been moved out of Defence, and into the role of Shadow Special Minister of State, as well as becoming Labor's Shadow Sports Minister. And Kim Carr is the Shadow Minister for lnnovation, Industry, Science and Research.
Telstra's Chief Operations Officer Kate McKenzie is to retire after 12 years at Telstra to pursue new directions. Brendon Riley, Group Executive Global Enterprise and Services, will be interim COO until a permanent replacement is found.
Renai LeMay has left Delimiter for a role as a business writer/editor for CTO Group in Canberra.
David Stewart, CEO and MD of Netcomm Wireless, was named Australian Communications Ambassador 2016 at last week's ACOMM Awards .
CSIRO's new Director of Health and Biosecurity is Dr Rob Grenfell. Grenfell was previously with Bupa.
Patrick Devlin joins SimpliVity in the newly-created role of Regional Sales Director ANZ. He was previously MD ANZ and Pacific Islands at Ruckus Wireless.
