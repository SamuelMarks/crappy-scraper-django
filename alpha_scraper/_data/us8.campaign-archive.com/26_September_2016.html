news briefs

A smartphone device to detect irregular, stroke-causing heart rhythm abnormalities is being piloted in Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander communities. The AliveCor/ Kardia Heart Monitor program is run by the Poche Centre for Indigenous Health at the University of Sydney, and funded by the National Heart Foundation. The pilot will provide a snapshot of atrial fibrillation rates in Aboriginal people.
Marita Cheng , Tech23 alumni and 2Mar Robotics and Robogals founder is an awardee of the 2016 Asia Game Changers at the United Nations. She will be honoured at the Asia Game Changer Awards Dinner and Celebration in October for bringing more women into the tech and engineering space.
The Federal Government has allocated $23 million to its Incubator Support Initiative , giving incubators the opportunity to apply for grants of up to $500,000 for startups to realise their global potential.
Telstra (ASX:TLS) and Ericsson have teamed up to bring 5G to Australia with the first trialling system conducted in Melbourne.
Toowoomba Regional Council's contact centre and telephony applications infrastructure will be supplied by QPC .
NAB has formed what it calls an ' international banking innovation alliance ', signing agreements with Israel's Bank Leumi and the Canadian Imperial Bank of Commerce. The alliance will provide opportunities for the banks to share information, innovation strategies, and support networks with startup partners. The partners may also co-develop products and services.
71% of Australian IoT devices don't provide a privacy policy that adequately explains the management of personal information - according to the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC). The review was part of the Global Privacy Enforcement Network's annual privacy sweep. The OAIC is encouraging businesses, including startups, to adopt a 'privacy-by-design' approach.
WA-based disability services provider VisAbility will deploy Promapp's cloud-based business process management software.
A new partnership between Boeing and the University of Queensland (UQ) will see 30 staff from Boeing Research and Technology Australia establish a presence at UQâ€™s St Lucia campus. They will collaborate with UQ researchers and students on topics like cabin disease transmission and bioterrorism countermeasures, unmanned aircraft and autonomous systems, and environmental monitoring technologies (for areas including the Great Barrier Reef).
Middle Eastern construction company ALEC will standardise on Aconex solutions (ASX: ACX) for managing information and processes across all of its projects.
Optus Business is collaborating with Palo Alto Networks to launch Managed Advanced Threat Protection services operating on the Palo Alto Networks Next-Generation Security Platform. The collaboration between Palo Alto Networks, Singtel and Optus aims to protect organisations by 'monitoring, isolating and preventing' suspicious applications.
Sportstech company GuardLab will establish its Asia Pacific headquarters in Melbourne.
RayGen Resources has received over $1 million in funding as part of the Victorian Government's $20 million New Energy Jobs Fund grants. The funding will support the design, construction and manufacture of a new automated production line for its solar technology. This is one of 24 grants to be announced in the next few months.
Victoria Police personnel will be able to access on-the-spot mental health and wellbeing services through equipt , a new mobile app. equipt was developed through a $300,000 joint investment from the Victorian Government, Victoria Police and The Police Association.
Curtin University has opened entries for the OzAPP Awards for startups with mobile, web, and cloudapp concepts. Prizes include a trip to Las Vegas. Entries close on 28 October.
