NewsBriefs

Eight Australian businesses have received a total of $5 million in commercialisation grants under the Federal Government's Entrepreneurs' Programme. Full Profile received $1 million for its Blockchain-enabled agricultural commodity management and finance solution; Good Thnx received $230,464 for a platform which thanks customers and employees through charitable donations; Healthcare Logic received $1 million for commercialising a patient level information system; Ignite Energy Resources received $1 million for converting end of life waste plastics to sustainable fuels and chemicals; Interact Technology was awarded $458,582 for a software communications and engagement platform for the health industry; 1414 Degrees received $560,000 for a thermal energy storage commercial demonstrator; Style Atlas was awarded $383,322 for an online fashion fitting solution, and Verton Technologies Australia received $393,252 for a way to revolutionise crane operations.
Food Standards Australia New Zealand has called for submissions on an application to permit a genetically modified rice line that produces a form of provitamin A.
A consultation process seeking broad input on Australia’s space industry capability has been launched by the Federal Government.
The Australian Centre for Robotic Vision Team ACRV won the 2017 Amazon Robotics Challenge at RoboCup in Nagoya Japan. For winning the finals, the Centre, headquartered at Queensland University of Technology, will receive $80,000USD.
Graphene applications company Imagine Intelligent Materials (Imagine IM) has received a $100,000 grant from Transurban to fund 'smart' motorways through scalable, graphene-based sensors. The grant will be used to build prototype sensors to be installed under motorways to monitor traffic movement, road conditions, and eventually even enable energy storage or generation. Imagine IM presented at last week's inaugural IMPACT7 event in Melbourne.
A renewable hydrogen refuelling station will be implemented in Victoria with $1 million provided by the Victorian Government to Moreland City Council and hydrogen utility company H2U. Construction is set to begin next year, with the $9.37 million project to establish Australia's first commercial-scale hydrogen refuelling station.
The Federal Government has 'increased its program of work to ensure customers have a positive experience on the NBN '. ACMA is to conduct research and collect data on the NBN customer experience.
In other ACMA news, research shows that Australian children are watching over 10 hours of screen content per week. Children aged 0–14 were watching 92 minutes of scheduled broadcasts on television per day in 2016 compared to 122 minutes in 2005.
Queensland has launched Renewables 400 , an initiative to support energy security and next generation large-scale renewable and battery storage projects. Under a reverse auction process, companies can bid for government support for renewable generation and storage projects.
The South Australian Government is on the hunt for new technology to replace the current paper-based SA Transport Subsidy Scheme. Applications close on 10 August 2017.

The first trial of a driverless shuttle bus in NSW is to begin this month through a collaboration between the NSW Government, HMI Technologies, NRMA, Telstra, IAG and Sydney Olympic Park Authority. The trial will extend to public use at Sydney Olympic Park once the first stage of tests and safety checks is completed in an off-road environment.
Flinders and Cape Barren islands are getting a telecommunications boost as part of an $11 million upgrade project with the Federal Government, Tasmanian Government, Flinders Island Council and Telstra.
Agtech and food accelerator program Rocket Seeder is launching in partnership with Monash University's Food Incubator.
Cyber crime is now the seventh biggest global economic threat, and will cost Australia an estimated $16 billion over the next decade, according to Minister for Defence Industries Martin Hamilton-Smith.
A pilot project to develop solar battery storage kits has received $800,000 from the Victorian Government. A consortium of five companies led by ARVIO will receive the funding to manufacture a solar storage control system and software technology kit.
Adelaide's GigCity Adelaide network was launched last week.
Commonwealth Bank has provided an update on AUSTRAC's filing of legal proceedings last week. CBA stated that a coding error created after a software update on their Intelligent Deposit Machines (IDMs) led to the IDMs not creating the Threshold Transaction Reports needed by AUSTRAC.
Boral Australia will deploy MuleSoft 's Anypoint Platform.
Queensland agtech company BlockGrain is trialling its automated platform with nine grain brokers. BlockGrain received $95,000 from the Advance Queensland Ignite Ideas Fund earlier this year.
The ACCC has published a consultation paper inviting submissions on NBN Co's revised variation to its Special Access Undertaking.
Applications for BlueChilli's CityConnect smart cities accelerator program are open.
WA's Department of Fire and Emergency Services is investing $7 million in a new computer aided dispatch system through Motorola Solutions.
CSIRO is partnering with Ruralco to apply digital technology to Australian agriculture . The partnership will explore the use of drones in long-range livestock detection; nutrient and fertiliser management; the development of long-range sensing; and adaptation of geospatial tools.
Vodafone plans to spend almost $2 billion in 2017 on its mobile network and technology.

PeopleWatch

John Sheridan is now the CIO and CISO for the Australian Government Department of Finance. He was previously First Assistant Secretary Technology and Procurement and Australian Government Procurement Coordinator for the Department of Finance.
Finalists for the WA Innovator of the Year program have been announced.
Paul Slaven is AirTrunk 's new Chief Development Officer. Slaven was previously Senior Construction Manager at Amazon.
At last week’s inaugural IMPACT7 , Dr Lee Reid was awarded the People's Choice Award for his brain surgery planning tool, CONSULT, QUT's Mathilde Desselle won the Collaboration for Impact Award for her presentation on biofabrication and the hospital of the future, and Monash University's Michelle McIntosh won the Greatest Potential Impact Award for her inhalable oxytocin project which could reduce the deaths of new mothers in third world countries.
Former boss of Pandora Jane Huxley is to become Managing Director of Spotify ANZ.
Securonix has opened its first operational base in Sydney, and will be led by Chris Poulos, VP for Asia Pacific and Japan, and Michael Livingstone, Country Manager for ANZ.

CompanyWatch

LiveTiles (ASX: LVT) has raised $11 million via a share placement.
Webjet is to acquire JacTravel Group for an enterprise value of £200 million.
Westpac has invested $40 million in zipMoney (ASX: ZML). Their strategic relationship will be used to explore the rollout of Zip's products and services across Westpac's payments network.
Catapult Group International (ASX: CAT) is to acquire SportsMed Elite and Baseline athlete management system products from Brisbane-based SMG Technologies. The total transaction consideration includes upfront cash of $1.4 million, $0.25 million of cash and $0.25 million of CAT script, and up to $2 million of deferred cash consideration over the next two years.
