news briefs

nbn and Telstra have signed a contract worth approximately $1.6 billion for the planning, design, construction and construction management services within the existing Telstra Hybrid Fibre-Coaxial (HFC) footprint. The deal will provide broadband to over three million homes. The Australian Competition and Consumer Commission (ACCC) has expressed concern regarding Telstra's involvement in the rollout of the NBN network.
Tasmania will implement a new emergency services system to manage medical, fire, police and emergency incidents. The Tasmanian Government has awarded Fujitsu the $6.5 million contract for the initial acquisition and implementation of the emergency services computer aided dispatch (ESCAD) system. Under the contract, Fujitsu will also provide five years of support and maintenance after the system's implementation, bringing the total cost to $15.3 million. The state currently has three non-integrated CAD systems; an integrated communications and dispatch system was recommended in the 2013 Bushfire Inquiry.
Crowd Mobile Limited (ASX:CM8) has signed an agreement with Sequoia Financial Group Limited (ASX:SEQ) to design and maintain a Q&A app allowing consumers to receive general investment advice from Sequoia experts for $9.99 a question. A similar agreement has been signed between Crowd Mobile and CIA for a CIA Tax app.
Isentia (ASX:ISD) has signed a new agreement with the Copyright Agency , providing Isentia clients with retention and access to a tailored 12-month archive of relevant media and real-time access to stories. The Copyright Agency will use this agreement as a basis for future agreements with other media content aggregators.
CSIRO has begun rolling out electric cars and electric bikes across seven sites to enable petrol-free motoring within CSIRO’s pool of fleet vehicles. With the addition of solar PV panels at the sites, the aim is to generate enough renewable energy to charge and run the cars, making them emission free.
The Two Feet program is launching in Sydney and Brisbane after starting in Melbourne last month. Run by The Difference Incubator (TDi) and subsidised by NAB, Two Feet is a social enterprise incubator.
The City of Sydney has awarded a $25,000 grant to the Indigenous Digital Excellence Initiative (IDX) to enable Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander entrepreneurs to boost their digital technology skills. IDX is a not-for-profit partnership between the National Centre for Indigenous Excellence and the Telstra Foundation. The funding will be used to hold the 2016 IDX summit in Sydney.
At the end of 2015 there were approximately 12.9 million internet subscribers in Australia, marking a 2% increase on the previous year. The number of fibre connections doubled between December 2014 and December 2015 to 645,000 subscribers.
University of Queensland has installed four solar-powered electric vehicle (EV) chargers at its St Lucia and Gatton campuses, marking the first network of public EV chargers extending outside of Brisbane.
Optus plans to change the structure of its Consumer and Enterprise divisions to 'help drive Optus' transformation strategy and reshape operations for sustainable growth'. As part of this, Optus proposes to make a number of roles redundant.
A new data hub, SPUR , was launched in Western Australia bringing together selected Western Australian Land Information Authority (Landgate) services to support the development of new products, services and research projects. Landgate works in close partnership with the Cooperative Research Centre for Spatial Information.
The South Australian Government has signed an agreement with Inspur, a Chinese-based vendor of cloud computing and big data services. Under the agreement, the Government will facilitate Inspur's cloud data centre construction as well as its home server business, big data innovation centre, and telecommunications network management business.
Orion Health (ASX: OHE) has signed an agreement with Queensland health service Metro North to deliver its Referrals Management platform.
Stanfield Funds Management Ltd (ASX: SFN) has announced that Swift Networks Group has reached an agreement to resell Foxtel's services in aged care, lifestyle villages and hospitals across Australia.
INTECQ has signed a $2.4 million contract to supply its full suite of gaming systems across Federal Group's Wrest Point Casino in Hobart and Country Club Tasmania in Launceston. INTECQ's wholly owned subsidiary, eBet, was chosen to supply its business intelligence, gaming management and player loyalty systems. The contract also includes $350,000 per annum over the following six years of the contract term.
Buddy Platform (ASX: BUD) will launch its beta release of 'Parse Server by Buddy' - a mobile backend as a service (mBaaS) - later this month. Following Facebook's decision to decommission their hosted Parse product and move to the open source community, Buddy has been working with the Parse team at Facebook's California headquarters 'to stand up a high availability, fully scaled multi-tenant Parse Server offering and migration toolset that enables customers to simply migrate their applications.'
The Australian Electoral Commission (AEC) is migrating to 8common Limited's (ASX: 8CO) Expense8 solution on a Software as a Service (SaaS) basis.

company watch

Congratulations to Richard White on the successful listing of Wisetech Global Limited (ASX: WTC) on the ASX today. White still remains the main shareholder but has raised over $160 million, valuing Wisetech at over $1 billion!
Online wine seller Vinomofo has secured $25 million from Blue Sky Venture Capital. Blue Sky is the sole investor in the funding round. The investment will be used to accelerate Vinomofo’s growth plans.
Blue Sky Venture Capital’s VC2014 Fund LP - a division of Blue Sky Alternative Investments Limited (ASX: BLA) - has closed a $2.8 million investment in ELOQUII, a New York-based plus-sized women’s fashion ecommerce retailer. The investment is part of a $19.8 million Series B round of financing.
TopBetta Holdings Limited (ASX: TBH) has completed its strategic investment in Australian wagering technology company, Modern Wagering Systems Pty Ltd (MWS). TopBetta has acquired a 12.5% interest in MWS, with an option to acquire a further 12.5% within 12 months.
Mobile games company Animoca Brands (ASX: AB1) has raised $6.5 million via Institutional Placement. The company has also announced a Share Purchase Plan to raise up to $1 million.

people watch

Mick Liubinskas is moving to San Francisco in August with Telstra’s incubator, muru-d to help the incubatees expand their global networks. Good luck Mick on your new adventure!
Teresa Engelhard has joined the StartupAUS Board. Teresa is currently on the boards of RedBubble, Planet Innovation and Daintree Networks.
Professor Peter Andrews AO is the new Chairman of QUT's independent commercialisation arm, qutbluebox. Andrews was Queensland's Chief Scientist from 2003-2010. He replaces Hugh Nalder, who remains a member of QUT Council.
NEC Australia has announced a new leadership team. Mike Barber, former executive director of IT Solutions and Services, will take on the role of Chief Operating Officer. Current Managing Director Tetsuro Akagi will become CEO of NEC Asia Pacific. D'wayne Mitchell was appointed Director of IT Solutions and Andy Hurt is now Director of Communication Solutions.
Rebekah O'Flaherty is the new CEO of 3P Learning Limited (ASX:3PL) and will join 3P in July. She is currently GM Sales, Service & Marketing at Origin Energy and prior to that was Executive Director Telstra Country Wide and had roles overseas with HP.
Shayne Taylor is now Interactive Intelligence Group 's Channel Sales Manager for Australia. Taylor was previously Australia Country Manager at Allied Telesis.
Nimble Storage has appointed Cath Gentile as Senior Channel Development Manager for Australia and New Zealand. Gentile was previously at Citrix.
Xero Limited 's (ASX: XRO) Chief Product Officer Angus Norton is stepping down to return to the US. Chief Platform Officer Duncan Ritchie will resume responsibility for the global product team.
