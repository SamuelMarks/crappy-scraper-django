news briefs

The Australian Institute for Nanoscale Science and Technology will launch next month at the University of Sydney. The Institute will be headquartered at the new $150 million Sydney Nanoscience Hub .
Fire & Rescue NSW will receive two new drones as part of a multi-million-dollar technology rollout that also includes two custom-built mobile command centres and the installation of over 180 mobile data terminals in NSW fire trucks. The drones will provide firefighters with real-time images of dangerous areas, while the mobile data terminals will provide fast access to weather information, pre-incident plans, navigation and maps.
The Queensland Government has partnered with Data61 to improve government service delivery. Data61 produced the recent Digital Economy Fast Forward report .
An international team of scientists - led by Professor David Moss at Swinburne University of Technology and a colleague in Canada - has set a new record for the complexity possible on a quantum computing chip. The record in the number and complexity of entangled photons that can be generated on a chip may help to "crack the code to ultra-secure telecommunications of the future". It also has applications for quantum information processing, imaging, and microscopy.
A team of researchers, led by the Australian National University's Dr Yuerui (Larry) Lu, has created the world's thinnest lens . At one two-thousandth the thickness of a human hair, the lens could lead to flexible computer displays and miniature cameras. The study was published in the journal Light: Science and Applications .
A new Massive Open Online Course (MOOC) for students interested in science, technology, engineering and maths (STEM) has launched . 'Question Everything: Scientific Thinking in Real Life' was developed by a team of Brisbane Grammar School maths and science teachers as well as online learning specialists from the University of Queensland.
The Queensland Building and Construction Commission has selected MuleSoft 's AnyPoint Platform for a business transformation initiative.
Imagine Intelligent Materials Pty Limited (Imagine IM) has entered into a contract with Geelong-based engineering company Austeng to build Australia's first commercial graphene manufacturing plant. The pilot plant will have an output capacity of up to 10 tonnes per year. Imagine IM plans to use two tonnes in the first year of operation for products aimed at customers in Australian manufacturing. Graphene can be used in RFIDs, smart packaging, super capacitors, composites, ITO replacement, sensors, and memory.
Telstra has partnered with Cisco to develop its first Software-Defined Networking (SDN) products for Australian customers. SDN and Network Function Virtualisation technologies are used to deliver both the platform and products. The first product, Internet Virtual Private Network, will be available later in March, while the second product, Cloud Gateway Protection, is available in beta. Further products will be available later in the year.
Applications are now open for the Victorian Government's $4.5 million Intelligent Transport System (ITS) Transport Technology Grants Program. Applications close on Friday 15 April 2016.
Optus Business has delivered guest WiFi services at two of Mirvac's (ASX: MGR) flagship shopping centres - Broadway Shopping Centre (Sydney) and Orion Springfield Central (Brisbane).
A digital hospital project is being implemented at Brisbane's Princess Alexandra Hospital. The Queensland Government has lauded Princess Alexandra as "Australia's first public digital hospital". Electronic Medical Records (EMR) are now available instantly to healthcare staff across the hospital, replacing paper records with real-time patient information sent to a secure EMR. Almost 6000 staff received training with more than 1600 new digital devices integrated across the hospital. The Queensland Government has invested $200 million over four years to establish the foundations of the digital hospital system, which will be implemented across the state. Metro South Hospital and Health Service contributed over $30 million towards the Princess Alexandra Hospital project.
The Victorian Government has partnered with Australia Post to assist businesses to access China's eCommerce market through China's online sales platform, Tmall (owned by the Alibaba Group).
The NSW Government's Electricity Supply (Advanced Meters) Bill 2016 will support a voluntary, market-led rollout of smart meters.
As part of an $85 million investment through the Western Australian Government's Royalties for Regions program, mobile phone coverage and high-speed wireless broadband has been extended to Carnamah West, Bolgart and Yerecoin.
Landgate's new farm monitoring tool, Pastures from Space Plus , gives farmers access (via subscription) to high-resolution satellite technology. The tool, which integrates high-resolution satellite imagery, a stocking rate calculator, feed budgeting tool and Bureau of Meteorology rainfall data, was launched in Western Australia last week.
A new anaerobic digester at garden supplies company Richgro in WA is converting food and organic waste into electricity. The facility was made possible through $500,000 in Low Emissions Energy Development funding from the WA Government.
100 hi-tech manufacturing jobs will be secured through the final phase of Pfizer's $21 million Adelaide facility upgrade, to be completed by mid-2016.
The Digital Ready for Business program was launched in Hobart, Launceston and Ulverstone last week. The program partners with UTAS, TasTAFE and others to help young people, graduates and startups to develop and launch their businesses.
More than double the number of government agencies and institutions have participated in govCMS than was initially estimated. After one year in operation, govCMS supports 58 government sites for 28 agencies. The cloud-based initiative is built on the Acquia platform.
Queensland Rail has established a new Rail Management Centre (RMC) to manage its fleet and the metropolitan rail network. The Quintiq platform will provide 'a key role' in the new RMC, including Electronic Train Graphs and the delivery of dynamic timetabling and fleet allocation capabilities.

company watch

Culture Amp has closed a US$10 million Series B round led by Index Ventures with participation by Felicis Ventures and Blackbird Ventures. The funding will be used to launch advanced analytics features on the Culture Amp platform and finance the company's expansion into the UK and Europe.
Mobile Masters - a founding partner of Orion - has been acquired by CSE Crosscom Pty Ltd, a wholly owned subsidiary of CSE Global. The transaction extends CSE's current investment in Orion.
iWebGate Limited (ASX: IWG) has entered into an agreement with an entity managed by The Lind Partners, LLC, for a total funding commitment of up to US$8.575 million via a Convertible Note and Equity Funding Facility. The funds will be used to accelerate growth in core markets - including government, enterprise and small business - with AU$1 million to refinance its existing loan facility.
