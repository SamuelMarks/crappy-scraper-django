news briefs

The Australian Museum Eureka Prize winners include the Garvan Institute of Medical Research and Australian National University, whose DIY Droplet Lens won the ANSTO Eureka Prize for Innovative Use of Technology . The DIY Droplet Lens can be be cheaply constructed and fitted to a smartphone, transforming it into a mobile laboratory. The University of New South Wales Eureka Prize for Excellence in Interdisciplinary Scientific Research was won by the SEARCH Project, University of Melbourne for mapping a thousand years of climate history.
Minister Malcolm Turnbull hosted the Online Copyright Infringement Forum to discuss the issues raised by the Government in the Online Copyright Infringement Discussion Paper.
ICT Geelong and Australian Sports Technologies Network have both launched their annual investment pitching competitions for 2014. Between the two competitions there is more than $35,000 in prizes to be won, and five resident placements into the HeadStart Accelerator program to help high-potential startups grow their businesses in Geelong.
The panellists on tonight's Q&A Program on the ABC are all scientists! Marita Cheng, Engineer and founder of Robogals (and Tech23 Alumni); Suzanne Cory, Molecular biologist; Peter Doherty, Nobel Prize Winning Immunologist; Brian Schmidt, Nobel Prize Winning Astrophysicist and Ian Chubb, Australia’s Chief Scientist.
Thiess , incorporating Silcar, has agreed to expand and vary the existing Field Services Delivery contract it had with the NBN Co for an initial two years, with the option for a one year extension by NBN Co. The main scope for work is to connect customer premises to the NBN in Queensland, New South Wales and the ACT.
Communications Alliance has lodged an application with the Australian Competition and Consumer Commission to launch a ‘Follow-the-Money’ strategy to reduce the economic incentive to promote or encourage online copyright infringement in Australia.
The shortlist for the inaugural Australian Communications and Consumer Action Network and Australian Human Rights Commission Apps For All Challenge 2014 has been announced. It includes a range of apps that help young and old Australians with disabilities perform everyday activities.
Adelaide City Council has partnered with Cisco to become an Internet of Things Innovation Hub.
Australia's Chief Scientist, Professor Chubb, released his recommendations for a strategic approach to science and its related fields - Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics: Australia's Future. The Federal Government is working with Professor Chubb to convene a reinvigorated Science Council. The Council will advise the Government on its long term strategic science and research investment.
Kids Helplin e has launched online group counselling for Australian teenagers via social media. The project, led by University of Sydney Psychologist, Dr Andrew Campbell, is a response to perceived gaps in health services and growing demand to migrate mental health support online.
The Australian Competition and Consumer Commission will not prevent TPG from rolling out its FTTB technology to supply high speed broadband services to residents in metropolitan areas across Australia.
The Justice Health and Forensic Mental Health Network has adopted Orion Health’s electronic medical record solution to deliver complete electronic medical records across the New South Wales public health system. Patient details will now be held state-wide as opposed to being held in multiple paper files and electronic registers.
NEC Australia has secured a $150million contract with the Australian Department of Defence to deliver information technology infrastructure and services.
Liverpool City Council has selected NEC Australia to deploy WiFi and video solutions to provide a new CCTV network providing 24 hour recording of local streets, retails and parks across central Liverpool.
Hansen Technologies Limited (ASX:HSN) has announced Univisa S.A. has signed a five year license agreement to deploy Hansen’s ICC Customer Care and Billing Solutions for its operations in Ecuador.
Primary Opinion Limited (ASX: POP) has launched it's content delivery and distribution platform initially covering the intellectual property sector.

company watch

Istaclustr , which delivers hosted and managed DataStax Enterprise and Apache Cassandra™ services, has closed a first round investment offer for over $2 million in seed funding led by ANU Connect Ventures. DataStax, the company that delivers Apache Cassandra to the enterprise, also participated in the round. Istaclustr was founded by Doug Stuart and Ben Bromhead who previously founded Stratsec which was acquired by BAE Systems in 2011. Adam Zegelin is also a co-founder of Instaclustr. And Peter Lilley, who was a co-founder of Stratsec is Instaclustr's COO. Instaclustr’s first home was at Canberra’s Entry29 and now has a team of ten and an office in California.
Venuemob has raised $530,000 in its second round of investment led by Sydney Angels’ investor Adrian Bunter. Venuemob was initially funded $450,000 by Optus Innov8 in 2012.
Planet Tel has acquired ISPhone . Planet Tel will assume management of the company and new directors will be appointed.
Qanda Technology (ASX:QNA) has acquired Caramavan , an online hiring and rental marketplace for caravans in a share deal. This follows Qanda's acquisition of Drive My Car Rentals and Rentoid .

people watch

Oakton has appointed Shaji Sethu as its new Executive General Manager.
Singtel has appointed Allen Lew as CEO of Optus and SingTel’s Consumer Australia division effective from October 2014. Lew is the former head of SingTel’s Digital L!fe group. Paul O’Sullivan will move into a part-time role as Optus’ Chairman.
Mint Wireless Limited (ASX: MNW) has welcomed Anne Weatherston as its new Non-Executive Director effective immediately. Weatherston was previously the Chief Information Officer and a member of the Management Board at ANZ.
Tigerspike has appointed Aisha Hillary as its new Group Communications Director.
