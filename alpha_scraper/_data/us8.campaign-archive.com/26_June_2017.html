NewsBriefs

A telecommunications reform package has been introduced to NSW Parliament. The Bill aims to provide three outcomes. First, the Regional Broadband Scheme will provide funding for the 'loss-making' NBN fixed wireless and satellite networks in regional Australia. Second, Statutory Infrastructure Providers obligations will be created to enable Australians to order high-speed broadband regardless of their location. And third, the 'level playing field' rules will be reformed to ensure high-speed broadband network operators have separate wholesale and retail businesses. ACCAN has welcomed the changes.
Monash IVF Group has contracted SugarCRM to provide cloud software for patient enquiry management.
Data Governance Australia has announced the draft Code of Conduct which aims to benchmark the collection, use, management and disclosure of data. Submissions on the Code are open until 21 July 2017.
Victorian paramedics will receive high tech body cameras as part of a six month trial to help keep them safe. The trial will be rolled out in June and July to 27 branches, involving up to 550 paramedics and 150 cameras.
The NSW Government is investing $536 million over eight years in IT infrastructure and systems to support its healthcare sector . This includes digital infrastructure, digital patient records, a central repository for patients' public hospital pathology results, and the rollout of the Electronic Medications Management System.
CSIRO is tackling illegal fishing with the development of a notification system that alerts authorities when offending vessels arrive in port. The technology uses vessels' anti-collision devices, which can be detected by satellites.
An implementation plan for the National Relay Service (NRS) has been released by the Federal Government. The three stages of the plan include a 'rebalancing' of the NRS outreach function, improved data insights into usage and user requirements (and a registration process), and a tender for the continued provision of the NRS, with its annual funding allocation of $22 million to remain in place. ACCAN has expressed concerns over the announcement, including potential difficulties around a registration process, and the lack of provision to increase funding despite new relay options being introduced over the last three years.
Sky and Space Global launched three Diamonds nano-satellites from the Indian Space Research Organisation.
Three new robots will join Victoria Police to assist with surveillance, security and bomb disposal operations.
NetComm Wireless (ASX: NTC) and Telefónica have signed an IoT Device Supply Agreement . NetComm Wireless will supply a range of 3GM2M/IoT devices.
WA's shark monitoring network has two new satellite-linked receivers bringing the total now to 27.
$28.8 million will go to 13 projects in the third round of the Cooperative Research Centre Projects (CRC-P) initiative . Successful projects include: intelligent vision, sensing and data fusion for mining and exploration ($2.1 million); advanced printing technology for new generation flexible batteries ($2 million); and a big health data analytics and insights platform for the MTP sector ($1.9 million).
CubeSat INSPIRE-2 has successfully sent back to earth its first radio signal.
Qantas has announced the first startups and scaleups of its AVRO Accelerator program . Startups include roster-sharing tool for pilots, Aeroster and bill splitting platform easyshare , with scaleups including petminding services network Pawshake and loan-bidding marketplace LoanDolphin .
Few people have created greater impact in the world than Australian of the Year (2005) Fiona Wood , who will be keynoting at IMPACT7 (1 August 2017, Melbourne), drawing on her experience as a burns doctor assisting victims during the 2002 Bali bombings. In partnership with CSIRO, IMPACT7 spotlights emerging research that is creating a huge impact.
The NSW Government has allocated $2.5 million towards a world-class work health safety centre , aimed at translating research into improved work health and safety practices, as part of its 2017-18 budget.

PeopleWatch

The Children's eSafety Commissioner has been renamed to eSafety Commissioner to reflect their responsibility to tackle online safety for all Australians.
Prospa has named former Domain executive Damon Pezaro as its Chief Product Officer.
Campaign Monitor's CEO Alex Bard has "decided to embark on a new career journey in venture capital full time.”
Dropbox has appointed former SurveyMonkey Managing Director (APAC) Tony Ward as Country Manager for Australia and New Zealand.
Dr Elizabeth Eastland is to take up the position of Director Entrepreneurship at the University of New South Wales (UNSW). Dr Eastland has been at CSIRO for the past year as General Manager, Strategy, Market Vision and Innovation, CSIRO and prior to that created iAccelerate at the University of Wollongong. Dr Eastland is an Impact Leader at IMPACT7 .
Ansible has appointed Kanth Gudipudi as its new Technical Product Manager.
Mike Zimmerman has joined the CSIRO Innovation Fund alongside Phil Morle and Bill Bartee. Zimmerman headed up BuildingIQ and has been involved in the Australian VC early-stage tech space in his role at Technology Venture Partners.
Bevan Slattery has been named CEO of Superloop (ASX: SLC) after serving as Executive Chairman and Interim CEO. Michael Malone has been appointed Chair of the Board.
John Harvey OA, who so charmingly represented IBM over the years has passed away. The Australian Technical & Management College of which he was Chair, will establish a Scholarship in his name.
