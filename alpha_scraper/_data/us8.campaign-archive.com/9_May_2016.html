news briefs

The Young and Well Cooperative Research Centre (CRC) activities are due to finish on 30 June 2016. Awarded $27.5 million in funding in 2011, Young and Well CRC designed three streams of research investigating how technology could impact the lives of young people aged 12 to 25 and improve their mental health and wellbeing, and are on track to meet all 600 Commonwealth milestones. The Young and Well CRC, as an existing CRC, is ineligible to apply for funds under the Program to continue their work in its current form. Congratulations to Associate Professor Jane Burns, CEO, for the work she and her team have done in contributing to the state of mental health research globally and in making a difference to the lives of young people. Hopefully the Young and Well CRC may be rescued somehow by an industry-led CRC entity? Jane Burns will speak at Agile Australia 2016 on how to move "towards an inclusive workplace".
The Australian Information Industry Association (AIIA) has criticised the Budget's NBN funding and called on the Government to do more to improve the digital readiness of Australian businesses and the integration of STEM into the national curriculum.
The Space Industry Association of Australia has released a Green Paper on the impact of the Budget to Australia's civil space sector.
Bondi Labs will be joining Swinburne's Innovation Precinct in 2016. Jonathan Marshall founded Bondi Labs in 2012 to develop serious games for the plant biosecurity space in collaboration with the CSIRO, QUT, SBIT and the Crawford Fund.
CSIRO is teaming up with the National Astronomical Observatories of the Chinese Academy of Sciences (NAOC) to develop the world's largest single dish telescope - the Five hundred metre Aperture Spherical Telescope, or FAST . FAST's diameter is half a kilometre wide, and its 19-beam receiver will be designed and built in Australia by CSIRO engineers.
Civil surveillance in the ACT is to be reviewed. The review will look at how laws can keep up with technological developments and emerging devices, including smartphones, fitness trackers, drones, and practices including geo-tagging.
The Sunshine State is offering international startups up to $100,000 in grants to relocate. The Hot DesQ program aims to attract interstate and international entrepreneurs to Queensland. Hot DesQ is part of the $180 million Advance Queensland initiative.
You can now experience ziplining in New Zealand through virtual reality video technology. Tourism Rendezvous New Zealand (TRENZ) is launching 360-degree immersive technology, which uses a Samsung VR headset. The headsets will be used on sales trips, staff training, and potentially in their Queenstown, New Zealand store.
The potential of blockchain technology for government and industry will be examined in a new review by CSIRO's Data61 in collaboration with government agencies including Treasury. The study will take place over the next nine months, and will be headed by Liming Zhu and Mark Staples, with strategic foresight input from Stefan Hajkowicz.
XTD Limited (ASX: XTD) has renewed its partnership with APN Outdoor Group Limited (ASX: APO). Under the agreement, APN Outdoor will continue to provide XTD with advertising content for its digital screen systems in Melbourne and Brisbane metro rail networks.
Optus' social media will now be managed by We Are Social.
South Australia's Way2Go program has launched a website , promoting active travel to school.
The Queensland Government has become an executive member of the Open Worldwide Innovation Network.
In more tech news from Queensland, a new discussion paper suggests that Queensland has the potential to become the hub for a multi-billion dollar Asia-Pacific aerospace industry.
Mach7 Technologies Limited (ASX: M7T) has completed a pilot program of Mach7 Enterprise Imaging Platform with a US radiology group. The company is now rolling out to all 20 remaining sites. The subscription revenue for the first 12 months is expected to be around $650,000.
The Tax Incentive for Early Stage Investors (20% non-refundable tax offset and 10 year capital gains tax exemption) and New Arrangements for Venture Capital Limited Partnerships have passed the Senate .
Quberider, co-founded by UNSW students Solange Cunin and Sebastian Chaoui, is producing STEM education programs designed for high school students. The company will send the first Australian-owned experiment to the International Space Station (ISS) in June. The small payload , about the size of a large coffee mug, will launch aboard the SpaceX Falcon 9 rocket from Cape Canaveral, Florida. It will include dozens of data-driven software experiments developed by close to 1000 students from 40 high schools across New South Wales. Supported initially by UNSWs Michael Crouch Innovation Centre, Quberider is now part of Telstra's muru-D accelerator program. Quberider teamed up with American space company DreamUp, which is powered by NanoRacks, to deliver educational experiments to the ISS. NanoRacks operates via a Space Act Agreement with NASA that allows for commercial experiments to be undertaken on the US National Lab aboard the ISS.

people watch

The new Prime Ministers Industry 4.0 Taskforce has been formed to support Australias transition to a new economy. The Taskforce aims to connect the nation to the fourth industrial revolution, known as Industry 4.0, through close collaboration with German industry, and will meet with the German Plattform Industrie 4.0 and the US Industrial Internet Consortium. Taskforce members comprise a whole male cast, including: Swinburnes Deputy Vice-Chancellor (Research and Development), Professor Aleksandar Subic; Jeff Connolly, Chairman and CEO of Siemens Australia; John Ruthven, President and Managing Director SAP Australia and New Zealand; John Pollaers, Chairman of Australian Advanced Manufacturing Council; Stephen Durkin, CEO of Engineers Australia; and Keith McLean, Director CSIRO Manufacturing.
Brent Barnes has been appointed the new CEO of LBT Innovations Limited (LBT), a developer of clinical and diagnostic technology. Barnes takes the reins from Luisa Guthrie, who has been the CEO since she co-founded the company in 2004. Barnes has spent the last 11 years with Cochlear.
David Bennett is Australia's first IP Counsellor in China.
