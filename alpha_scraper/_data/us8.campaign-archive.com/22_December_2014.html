news briefs

The 2013-14 Communications Report from the Australian Communications and Media Authority (ACMA) has been released. 14.7 million Australians have a fixed line internet connection at home. Over 50% of 25-34 year olds are relying only on a mobile phone (and do not have a fixed line phone). In the 2013-14 year the volume of fixed-line data downloads increased by 53%. The volume downloaded on mobile devices rose by 97%! The majority of us are using multiple devices to get online with 68% of Australians using three or more different internet devices with the most popular being mobile phones (76%); laptop computers (74%); desktop computers (67%) and tablet computers (54%).
A digital Service NSW kiosk has opened in North Sydney, part of a pilot program to give customers more choice in how they interact with Government. The cashless kiosk will be staffed to help people complete a range of online transactions such as applying for a Seniors Card or renewing car registration.
Optus Business has partnered with Citycom Solutions to deliver the Citycom ‘ Assure ’ platform in response to the increasing regulatory oversight in the financial services industry and to address the issue of insider trading and financial malpractice issues.
Australian mobile phone carriers, along with the Emergency Call Person and ACMA, have collaborated to enable emergency service organisations to obtain automatic access to the location of a caller in life-threatening emergency situations.
CSIRO has partnered with Brisbane based dentist Chris Hart to develop the Oventur Clearway Device - a medical grade plastic mouthguard using 3D printing to address sleep apnoea. The device is currently available in Chris Hart's Brisbane practice and will be introduced in Sydney and Melbourne practices in the New Year.
Investor and entrepreneur Simon Hackett has transformed an Adelaide mansion into a co-working, incubator and events space called Base64 for its investments.
Innovation Bay is expanding into Perth. Go Capital founders Derek Gerrard and Geoff Pritchard will lead the Perth team in 2015.
As part of Prime Minister Abbott's ministerial reshuffle , Industry Minister Ian McFarlane has had Science added to his title to become Industry and Science Minister. He will be assisted by newly appointed, former engineer and Queensland MP, Karen Andrews.

company watch

Vocus Communications (ASX: VOC) and Amcom have entered into a Scheme Implementation Agreement under which Vocus will acquire the remaining 90% shares in Amcom. Three Amcom directors will join Vocus' board, while existing Chairman David Spence will continue in his role. Current Amcom Chair Tony Grist becomes Deputy Chair and James Spenceley CEO of the merged entity, with a market capitalisation of $1.1 billion.
Telstra (ASX: TLS) is in formal talks to acquire Pacnet .
Pawshake has raised $1.5 million in seed funding led by Airtree Ventures. Pawshake is the first company to receive investment from the fund following the company's launch in July 2014 by serial technology investors Daniel Petre and Craig Blair.
ParentPaperwork, an edu-tech startup to automate paper forms in schools, has closed a seed round of funding. The amount of the round is not being disclosed, but was contributed to by a small group led by Melbourne investor David Broughton, who will also join the company’s board of directors.
Fairfax Media (ASX: FXJ) is in talks to secure a radio merger with Macquarie Radio Network (ASX: MRN).
