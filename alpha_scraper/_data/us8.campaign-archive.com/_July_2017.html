NewsBriefs

The City of Melbourne has released its Draft Startup Action Plan .
The Royal Adelaide Hospital's Automated Guided Vehicles or RAH-bots are ready to start moving food and supplies around the hospital.
This year's EY FinTech Adoption Index 2017 has been published. The research suggests that one in three digitally active consumers use two or more fintech services. China has the highest global level of fintech adoption (69%), followed by India (52%) and the UK (42%), with Australia at 37%.
Zetaris is partnering with Melbourne Business School to provide analytics students with tools and resources.
Motorola will supply radio communications for the Gold Coast 2018 Commonwealth Games. Motorola Solutions' TETRA Digital radios and its Zeon Digital network will be used to provide communications and coverage to over 6000 radios.
EscapeNet has been appointed by the South Australian Government to deliver the $7.6 million GigCity Adelaide network .
Optus has committed $10 million to improving mobile coverage in Western Australia, with $3 million for Albany and along the Albany Highway and $7 million for Busselton, Margaret River and Bunbury.
AT&T has joined the Executive Council of Internet of Things Alliance Australia.
After LaunchVic provided funding for 500 Startups to establish a Melbourne base, the organisation is now urgently seeking more information after 500 Startups co-founder Dave McClure published an acknowledgment that he made inappropriate advances “towards multiple women in work-related situations”. LaunchVic has affirmed its zero tolerance for sexual harassment . CEO of 500 Startups Christine Tsai has apologised to LaunchVic, Minister Dalidakis and the Victorian Government.
A $1.6 million Digi-Tech Startup Grants initiative has been announced in Victoria. 150 secondary schools will receive two robots each and be provided with grants to buy specialised equipment, including microscopes, laser cutters, 3D printers and virtual reality goggles. The initiative will be delivered with Digital Learning and Teaching Victoria.
The Australian Defence Force has created an Information Warfare Division , consisting of four branches: information warfare capability, C4 and battle management capability, capability support directorate and the joint cyber unit. The Division will be led by Deputy Chief Information Warfare, Major General Marcus Thompson.
Community television broadcasters in Melbourne, Perth and Adelaide have another six months to transition their services to online streaming.

Complexica will deploy its Customer Opportunity Profiler system for DuluxGroup .
The Federal Government has directed the Australian Signals Directorate to use its offensive cyber capabilities to 'disrupt, degrade, deny and deter organised offshore cyber criminals.'
Oakton has delivered a cloud-based CRM platform for the Department of Industry, Innovation and Science.
InterSystems has been chosen by the Northern Territory Government to implement InterSystems TrakCare for its Core Clinical Systems Renewal Program.
The first companies to graduate from CSIRO 's ON Connect@Lindfield are: AGSOL (a solar-powered mill for people in poverty-stricken areas); Orbit Australia (PollenSmart app); 1BTN (IoT reordering solution); Connected Control Systems (IoT security for construction sites); and Cleverheart (pay as you go solar energy solution for developing countries).
The Federal Government has updated the occupations lists for temporary and permanent skilled visas. 36 occupations newly eligible for 457 visas (Temporary Work (Skilled) visa) and the Employer Nominated Scheme (subclass 186) were added, with 12 occupations removed from the lists.
Industry groups including Communications Alliance , AIIA and AMTA have endorsed the recommendations of the Parliamentary Joint Committee on Intelligence and Security to the Telecommunications Sector Security Reform legislation.
Village Roadshow (ASX: VRL) will deploy the MuleSoft Anypoint Platform to personalise entertainment experiences across digital channels.
Flexiroam Limited's subsidiary Flexiroam Asia has partnered with Korean Air to provide passengers with access to complimentary data roaming from July-September.

CompanyWatch

Fairfax Media (ASX: FXJ) has ceased discussions with Hellman & Friedman and a consortium including TPG Group and Ontario Teachers' Pension Plan Board, following their examination of unsolicited, preliminary, non-binding indications of interest.
Audinate - originally spun out of NICTA - has listed on the ASX (ASX: AD8), raising $21 million in a deal valuing the company at just over $72 million.
Hansen Technologies Limited (ASX: HSN) has acquired Enoro Holding A/S, a Nordic provider of custom information systems and meter data management for the energy sector.
EnviroSuite (ASX: EVS) has completed the sale of its Pacific Environment Consulting practice to Environmental Resources Management.
ELMO Software Limited has been admitted to the ASX (ASX: ELO).
