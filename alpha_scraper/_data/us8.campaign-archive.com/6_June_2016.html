news briefs

Under an exclusive agreement , IOT Group Limited (ASX: IOT) will distribute drones from Chinese manufacturer Shenzhen AEE Aviation Technology Co., Ltd. - including, UAVs, police and military drones - in Australia and Japan.
University of Wollongong's $18 million iAccelerate Centre is accepting applications for its next intake of startup companies.
A start-up joint venture between NAB's innovation hub NAB Labs and Telstra's Gurrowa Innovation Lab has produced Proquo , a new digital marketplace.
The ACCC has issued a final determination on nbn's revenue controls for 2014-15, and has stated that nbn's prices did not exceed maximum regulated prices during that financial year. The ACCC also published nbn's proposed variation to its Special Access Undertaking, and has invited feedback on this.
The universe is expanding faster than expected according to an ANU study. Astrophysicist Dr Brad Tucker suggests the speed could be due to dark radiation, an undiscovered force. The research will be published in the Astrophysical Journal and is available from here .
Three Victorian tech companies have announced partnerships, licensing deals and ventures at CommunicAsia 2016. flexAnswer was awarded a three-plus-two year Whole of Government contract for the provision of a Virtual Assistant system to all Singapore Government agencies. Optotech commissioned three new systems for Seagate in Malaysia, while Urbanise will partner with several companies and has selected Singapore as a regional base.
Smart drumline technology has caught four sharks as part of the NSW Government's shark strategy. The sharks were tagged with satellite and acoustic tags and then released. Trials of the smart drumlines will continue over winter.
The Queensland Government is trialling a data logging buoy to monitor acoustic pingers, which help to protect migrating whales. The technology will also be used to reduce the accidental capture of marine animals.
Data61 has launched ribit , an online platform connecting students to start-ups and businesses for work experience.
Empired has entered into an exclusive strategic relationship with Canadian-based eSCRIBE Software Ltd. Empired will head eSCRIBE's expansion into ANZ markets.
Rio Tinto is rolling out automated technology as part of its 'Mine of the Future' program. The company is working towards installing a fully autonomous, heavy haulage, long-distance rail system that they say will be a 'world first'.
Fujitsu has signed a global reseller agreement with Megaport, which will include the installation of a Megaport point of presence in the Fujitsu Malaga (Perth) and North Ryde (Sydney) Data centres.
Chorus and Nokia will extend their technology partnership to further invest in New Zealand's ultra high speed broadband program for another three years.
BPS Technology (ASX: BPS) has executed a merchant agreement to facilitate the selling and distribution of Morlife health products into China's market on Alibaba's 1688.com, through its master merchant agreement with SmartTrans Holdings Limited (ASX: SMA).
Brisbane Airport Corporation will extend its managed technology infrastructure service contract with Data#3.

medical watch

FUJIFILM Medical Systems - a division of FUJIFILM Australia Pty Ltd - has announced a contract to deliver a Cardiovascular Information System (iCVIS) to the Sunshine Coast University Hospital. iCVIS was developed by Alcidion for FUJIFILM and is built on the Miya platform.
In what ANU Vice-Chancellor Professor Brian Schmidt AC has called 'the future of medicine', a new personalised medicine centre will be opened in the ACT, with $7.3 million in ACT Government funding awarded to the ANU. Canberra Clinical Genomics is an ANU-ACT Health partnership that aims to cure patients with complex diseases by sequencing their genome.
A new $80 million enterprise in Victoria aims to accelerate the development of new medicines. The enterprise will bring together the University of Melbourne and Monash University, with the State Government contributing $10 million to the partnership.
Clarity Pharmaceuticals has raised $2 million to develop its clinical pipeline using its proprietary drug targeting technology. The investment included support from newly-appointed non-executive director Dr Chris Roberts, fellow directors Dr Alan Taylor and Dr Matt Harris, as well as current shareholders and collaborators, including the Australian Nuclear Science and Technology Organisation, the University of Melbourne, and ATP Innovations.
BlueChilli and Westpac's Innovation Challenge is offering $40,000 in funding to the next big idea in healthcare. Applications close on 19 June 2016.

company watch

Webjet (ASX: WEB) has acquired New Zealand-based Online Republic for NZ$85 million to be paid in cash and new Webjet shares.
9 Spokes has closed a $25 million capital raise . The cloud-based business software platform is set to list on the ASX on 9 June.
Isentia (ASX: ISD) has acquired media intelligence company Social Net Creator, and has completed a 'bolt-on' acquisition in Thailand. The company is finalising two further acquisitions in Vietnam and Hong Kong.
Utopia Gaming Systems - a division of Banktech - has acquired Global Gaming Industries.
Cre8tek Ltd (ASX: CR8) has acquired Flamingo Customer Experience Inc.
Mobile Embrace Limited (ASX: MBE) has completed a $12 million equity placement .
zipMoney Limited (ASX: ZML) has raised $20.6 million via a placement to institutional and sophisticated investors. The company has also entered into a non-binding indicative term sheet to acquire Pocketbook for $7.5 million.

people watch

SuiteBox has appointed David Whyte as Chairman and Craig Anderson as Director of the Board. Whyte formerly led AIG life operations in New Zealand and Australia. Anderson was formerly founder and managing director of Dual Insurance NZ.
Optus have named Ben White as Managing Director, Marketing and Product for Optus' Consumer business. White has been with Optus since 2006.
