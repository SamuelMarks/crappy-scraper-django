NewsBriefs

The South Australian Government has selected Tesla to provide a 100 MW/129 MWh Powerpack system to be paired with Neoen’s Hornsdale Wind Farm near Jamestown, South Australia. Upon completion by December 2017, this system will be the largest lithium-ion battery storage project in the world and will provide enough power for more than 30,000 homes, approximately equal to the amount of homes that lost power during the blackout period South Australia experienced in early 2017.
The first $50 million Defence Cooperative Research Centre (CRC) has been announced and will focus on Trusted Autonomous Systems to promote cooperation between people and machines during military operations. The CRC for Trusted Autonomous Systems will receive an annual funding of $8 million with a maximum of $50 million over a seven year period and will be chaired by Jim McDowell.
The Australian Bureau of Statistics (ABS) will collaborate with Deakin University to improve their data collection processes . The new agreement with the ABS builds on Deakin’s existing relationship with the agency, which last year set up its National Data Acquisition Centre at the University’s Waterfront Campus.
The Royal Australian Air Force’s E-7A Wedgetail airborne early warning and control capability is being upgraded to the tune of $582.5 million. Expected to be completed by mid-2022, the upgrades include new and more advanced combat identification sensors, tactical data links and communication and encryption systems.
Four Royal Flying Doctor Service bases and 300 remote area clinics will now receive broadband through the Sky Muster satellites.
The Bureau of Meteorology has released an Australia-wide, near-real time lightning viewer to help people seek safety in advance of an approaching thunderstorm.

The Victorian government-funded start-up body LaunchVic has announced that the 500 Melbourne program will be postponed to enable time for 500 Startups to rebuild the trust of the Australian community.
RSPCA Queensland has introduced Avaya video technology to enable their workforce to engage in better, faster collection, care, treatment and placement of animals.
SRA Information Technology has won the contract to build South Australia's new Dogs and Cats Online system – a one-stop online service for all registration payments, microchipping and breeder information for launch in July 2018.
The government of Solomon Islands has now officially signed a contract with Huawei to construct a new subsea cable linking Honiara and Sydney.
Family Zone (ASX: FZO) has partnered with Learning With Technologies to pre-install Family Zone software on devices sold to parents under school mandates.
ANZ (ASX: ANZ), Westpac (ASX: WBC), IBM (NYSE: IBM), and shopping centre operator Scentre Group have successfully digitised the bank guarantee process used for commercial property leasing. The trial used Distributed Ledger Technology to eliminate the need for current paper-based bank guarantee documents, resulting in a single source of information with reduced potential for fraud and increased efficiency.

CompanyWatch

Blue Sky Venture Capital, a subsidiary of ASX-listed Blue Sky Alternative Investments Limited, has won a $50 million mandate to manage a venture capital fund for the South Australian government. Dr Elaine Stead, Head of Venture Capital at Blue Sky Venture Capital, who is originally from South Australia, will lead the management of the SAVCF. Stead is an IMPACT leader at IMPACT7 on 1 August 2017, Melbourne.
Sydney based compliance, asset and workforce management platform Assignar has raised $3 million in Series A funding led by Our Innovation Fund.
Girl Geek Academy has raised $1.3 million to roll out its #SheHacks Victoria hackathon with $300,000 from LaunchVic and a further $1 million in support contributed from partners including the GO1 Learning Portal, an Australian-owned tech startup backed by Y-Combinator and Steve Baxter.
Our Innovation Fund has lead a $2 million Series A funding round in EFTsure , a secure payments data platform.
Property Management solution for short term rentals, MadeComfy , has secured $1.1 million from angel investors including ex-LinkedIn boss Cliff Rosenberg, Amaysim co-founders Rolf Hansen and Peter O’Connell, Hein Vogel of Investec Australia, and Manfred Hasseler.
Vocus (ASX: VOC) has permitted Kohlberg Kravis Roberts to conduct due diligence after their preliminary, non-binding proposal to acquire 100% of the shares in Vocus.

PeopleWatch

A search for a new CEO of Scale Investors is underway in light of Laura McKenzie’s resignation. In the meantime Sabine Dejey and Amanda Derham have stepped up as interim co-CEOs with the guidance of board member Lynne Thornton.
Hugh Bradlow, Chief Scientist and former CTO of Telstra has stepped down after 22 years. Bradlow plans to work more closely with the Australian Academy of Technology and Engineering (ATSE) once retired.
Unisys has appointed Murray Whitlocke-Jones to the new role of Partner and Alliance Director for Unisys Asia Pacific.
Viatek has appointed Naran McClung as Group Chief Technology Officer (CTO) and James Veness as General Manager of Sales.
Commvault has appointed Kaaren Lewin to lead its channel and distribution strategy across Australia and New Zealand. Lewin was formerly General Manager of Sales at Canon.
Peter White has been appointed as an Executive Director and CEO of EnviroSuite Limited (ASX: EVS).
