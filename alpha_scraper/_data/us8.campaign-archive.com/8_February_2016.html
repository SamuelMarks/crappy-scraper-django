news briefs

Although the overall number of people working at CSIRO is projected to be unchanged at the end of a two year period, up to 350 people may lose their positions in the interim as people will be redeployed or reskilled and some will be made redundant. While CSIRO is planning to reduce the staff in its Oceans and Atmosphere business from 420 to 355, CSIRO CEO, Larry Marshall has confirmed that the Cape Grim air pollution monitoring station and RV Investigator are not under threat from these changes as has been reported by some media outlets.
Association for Data-Driven Marketing and Advertising (ADMA) has acquired the assets of the Digital Industry Association of Australia (AIMIA) which was voluntarily wound up by its members in January 2016. ADMA plans to service AIMIA members under the AIMIA brand.
Bigcommerce and eBay have formed a partnership – known as eBayLINK – an integration two years in the making that will give Bigcommerce users in Australia and New Zealand the ability to list products and manage inventory on eBay using eBayLINK.
Applications for the latest rounds for CRC s (Round 18) and CRC Projects (Round 1) have opened, the first held under the Federal Government’s new CRC Programme guidelines released in December. CRC-P applications close on Thursday 17 March; CRC applications close on Thursday 31 March. Applications that are industry-led and focused on practical outcomes are welcomed.
The House of Representatives Standing Committee on Education and Employment is to inquire into and report on matters that ensure Australia’s tertiary system can meet the needs of a future labour force focused on innovation and creativity. Submissions for the Inquiry into Innovation and Creativity: Workforce for the New Economy are now open.
NEC Australia has opened its new Australian headquarters and Innovation Centre in Melbourne's Docklands precinct.
An Australian first study by Macquarie University of the country’s book publishing industry reveals innovation has resulted in a significant drop in onshore book sales.
The Federal Government's school app, Learning Potential has been upgraded with new content including ways to get your child excited about primary school; helping teenagers plan for success in year 11 and goal setting for life after school with useful links to career planning websites. It has had more than 111,500 downloads since launching in August 2015.
The NSW Government ’s MyServiceNSW has launched offering information, vehicle registrations, demerit points and fine payments, with more products set to roll out in the coming months.
Mint Payments (ASX: MNW) has signed a three year license and distribution agreement with travel agency software solution provider Tramada System. Under the agreement, Tramada will deliver Mint Payment’s fully integrated online payments processing service 'Minternet'.
The Australian Communications and Media Authority has announced the outcome of the 1800 MHz band spectrum auction. Optus spent $196 million, Telstra spent $191 million, TPG spent $88 million and Vodafone spent $68 million to all secure spectrum in the auction, resulting in total revenues (including withdrawal penalties) of approximately $543.5 million.
govCMS has implemented an Omni-channel and ‘Government as an API’ service for digital content via its govCMS platform. The feature is supported by Acquia Content Hub technology. The service allows content to be shared across sites and systems, and ensures consistency of content across channels. Digital content could also be exported and reused by third parties.
Four cultural attractions in Canberra can now be explored online, with the completion of the first stages of a digital project developed by the ACT Government in partnership with the Google Cultural Institute . The Canberra Museum and Gallery , Lanyon, Calthorpes’ House and Mugga-Mugga can each be discovered online using Gigapixel technology and the Street View feature. The next stage of the project will add digital interpretation resources. Visit the attractions online here .
The Government has announced amendments to the Telecommunications ( International Mobile Roaming ) Industry Standard 2013. Changes will give consumers ‘more choice’ in the ways they can opt-out of mobile roaming services and spend-management notifications.

people watch

Sydney Water has appointed George Hunt as its new CIO.
Stuart Lee is to leave Telstra (ASX: TLS) after 45 years! Lee, who is the Group Executive for Telstra Wholesale, will be replaced by Group Managing Director Telstra Business Will Irving. Lee has been a member of Telstra’s senior leadership team since 2005 and head of Wholesale since 2011. He will be leaving Telstra at the end of March.
