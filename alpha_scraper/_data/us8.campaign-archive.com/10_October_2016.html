news briefs

Melbourne fintech startup LOKE is making it easier for people to feed their ice cream addiction, powering the new mobile payment and loyalty app for Gelato Messina . LOKE has recently opened a Singapore office.
The Bureau of Meteorology has launched its weather app , which may be especially useful for our friends in Adelaide and Victoria at the moment - and for all grappling with the effects of climate change!
Visionstream has announced a four-year contract with Chorus New Zealand to deliver customer connection works to the Ultra-Fast Broadband.
Selfies are about more than vanity , according to research from the Australian National University. A study has found that more than 75% of selfies are posted by women?!
Australian data consumption is significantly increasing, according to the Australian Bureau of Statistics' recently released Internet Activity Survey . Internet subscribers have increased by over 4% in the past year, up to 13.3 million in June. The number of fibre connections has more than doubled to over 960,000 subscribers. Data downloads have increased by over 22% to 2.1 million Terabytes between April-June 2016.
Telstra and Cohda Wireless have successfully trialled Vehicle-to-Infrastructure (V2I) technology over Telstra's 4G network in South Australia. The trial is a step towards Vehicle-to-Everything technology, which includes vehicles talking to traffic lights, other vehicles and pedestrians. Cohda Wireless is also collaborating with Renesas Electronics Corporation on vehicle-to-vehicle and V2I communications.
nbn's second satellite, Sky Muster II , has launched from the Guiana Space Centre in French Guiana.
The first recipients of the $20 million New Energy Jobs Fund grants have been announced. SEA Automotive will receive over $500,000 to support its production of electric powered commercial vehicles.
2 x 15 MHz of unallocated 700 MHz digital dividend will be auctioned off by the Federal Government to support mobile broadband services. The ACMA will conduct the auction.
Optus has signed a Memorandum of Understanding with Nokia to collaborate on moving to 5G.
Data61's Cyber Security and Innovation Hub has opened in Victoria. The Hub has created 140 jobs over three years, including PhD student positions. Based in the Docklands' Goods Shed, it will also house a collaboration with Oxford University's Global Cyber Security Capacity Centre and Victoria's new Oceania Cyber Security Centre.
The NSW Government is increasing the number of smart drumlines off the NSW coast from 15 to 100. So far, 31 great white and five bull sharks have been tagged and relocated. Meanwhile the WA Government will use drone technology in a shark surveillance trial as part of its $33 million investment in the WA Shark Hazard Mitigation strategy. Surf Life Saving WA has received $88,000 to conduct the three-month trial.
A virtual creative school residencies program in Victoria will see $150,000 invested in six pioneering projects to support students in regional and remote areas. The program will allow students to connect and collaborate with creative practitioners through online tools and platforms. Virtual Creative Professionals in Schools is a pilot created through a partnership between Creative Victoria and the Department of Education and Training.
Crowd Mobile (ASX: CM8) has partnered with Viacom International Media Networks (VIMN) to develop new apps . The two year agreement gives Crowd Mobile a license to leverage some of VIMN's brands for new app development.
Automatic Vehicle Location technology will be rolled out in 700 Department of Fire and Emergency Services and local government firefighting appliances in Western Australia. The project is enabled by $12.34 million in State Government Royalties for Regions program funding.
The Townsville City Council is using an employee messaging app developed by Omni Market Tide Limited (ASX: OMT) to facilitate communication between the Council's CEO and employees.

company watch

Tech23 alumni HappyCo (previously Happy Inspector) has completed a Series A funding round of $7.5 million, with investors including 500 Startups, Point Nine, Flight Ventures and SaaS Syndicate.
News Corp Australia has acquired punters.com.au for an undisclosed amount. Punters was founded in 2008 as a blog. CEO and founder Luc Pettett and COO Heath Kilgour will remain in their positions.
Under a recently-approved Deed of Company Arrangement, US-based Karma Media Holdings will take control of Quickflix and its assets in exchange for payment of $1.3 million into a Deed fund.
Telstra has completed its $1.25 billion off-market share buy-back tender .
