NewsBriefs

Amongst the 24 recipients of commercialisation grants totalling $11.2 million under the Entrepreneurs' Programme were: Lucsan Capital , who received $1 million for their analytics product to predict the impact of climate risk on food supply chain; $408,000 to Morse Micro for their ultra-low power WiFi chip for IoT; Swift Performance , who received $371,000 for their athletic performance training hardware and cloud data solution; and $484,000 to scale Kasada ’s Polyform web application security business.
Agtech innovators are invited to apply for Cicada Innovations' new deep tech commercialisation program for the agriculture and food sectors, GrowLab - created in partnership with MLA Donor Company.
$500 million will go towards improving Australia's space-based intelligence , surveillance and reconnaissance capabilities. The funds will improve Defence's access to commercial satellites.
The finalists of the ACOMM Awards have been announced. This year the IoT Alliance Australia will offer the IoT Impact Award.
Two bomb disposal robots will be supplied to a state police force under a $1.7 million contract with XTEK (ASX: XTE).
You're invited to be part of an ACCC program to measure and compare broadband speeds nationwide. Hardware-based devices will be installed in around 4000 households over four years, performing remote testing to determine typical speeds on fixed-line NBN services.
Around 50 South Australian primary schools are participating in a rollout of 3D printing technology, as part of a joint initiative between the SA Government, public schools and Makers Empire.
Fintech businesses in Hong Kong and Australia could benefit from a Cooperation Agreement signed by ASIC and the Hong Kong Securities and Futures Commission (SFC). The agreement provides a framework to support and understand financial innovation in each economy, and will enable the SFC and ASIC to refer fintech businesses to each other via ASIC's Innovation Hub and the SFC's Fintech Contact Point.
Carly's Law has passed Parliament. The Criminal Code Amendment (Protecting Minors Online) Bill targets online predators preparing or planning to cause harm to, procure or engage in sexual activity with a child. It especially targets those who misrepresent their age.
Optus is partnering with Canberra Data Centres to deliver a Federal Private Cloud solution .
eCommerce shipping solution ShipStation has launched an integration with Sendle .

The Victorian Government is all about fintech with two announcements this week. The state is establishing a financial technology hub in Docklands. The Victorian Government has released a request for proposal to establish the hub at the Goods Shed North. Melbourne will also host a week-long fintech festival from 27 October - 3 November 2017.
PrimeQ will establish an Innovation Centre, 24/7 Technology Support Hub and graduate program supported by a $500,000 grant via Investment Attraction South Australia.
nbn has begun building Fibre to the Curb access technology , with a trial commencing in Coburg, Victoria.
The Federal Government today tabled the Productivity Commission's report into the telecommunications Universal Service Obligation . Access the report here .
AVCAL has released a new report revealing that VC investment as a proportion of GDP is 0.0023% of GDP in Australia, less than half the average for the world's leading nations (according to the OECD). The report, Venture Capital Effect: A report on the Industry’s Economic Impact on the Australian Economy , can be found here .
The Victorian Government is providing $654,000 in funding to support 9 Victorian studios to develop and release new digital games .
The Copyright Amendment (Disability Access and Other Measures) Bill 2017 was passed last week. The reforms aim to improve copyright licensing provisions for collecting societies and educational institutions.
The Civil Aviation Safety Authority has released the Terms of Reference for its review of drone safety regulation .

PeopleWatch

Tyro's CEO Gerd Schenkel is resigning after taking up the post in October 2016, with Non-Executive Director Rob Ferguson appointed Acting Managing Director. Current Executive Director Jost Stollman will become a full-time Non-Executive Director.
Brenton Smith has been named Vice President for Australia and New Zealand at Software AG . Smith was most recently VP and GM at Quest Software, Asia Pacific Japan and MD and VP at Symantec, Australia and New Zealand.
Previous head of Digital Transformation Agency Paul Shetler is joining Stone & Chalk as their Expert in Residence.
Dr Vivienne Conway was named Achiever of the Year at the Western Australian Information Technology and Telecommunications Alliance (WAITTA) INCITE Awards . Congratulations to Tech23 ’s great supporter and co-founder of Yuuwa Capital Matthew Macfarlane who won the inaugural Pearcey Western Australian Entrepreneur of the Year.
Lighthouse is to pivot away from property and focus on founder education, with Annie Parker announcing that the overlap with Jobs NSW's Sydney Startup Hub was too great to make both viable. Lighthouse has shared their learnings with Jobs NSW to support its growth. Parker will remain involved with Lighthouse in an advisory capacity but she is now on the hunt for her 'next big adventure.'

CompanyWatch

Evans & Partners Global Disruption Fund has lodged a PDS with ASIC to raise $100 million, with the ability to raise an additional $50 million through oversubscriptions. The investment committee includes David Thodey (Chairman of CSIRO) and Paul Bassat (Co-founder of Seek and Square Peg Capital).
ASG has submitted an expression of interest to acquire SMS Management and Technology Limited (ASX: SMX) for $1.80 per share.
Culture Amp has raised a Series C round of US$20 million led by Sapphire Ventures. Existing investors Index Ventures, Felicis Ventures and Blackbird Ventures also supported the round. Kevin Diestel, Principal at Sapphire Ventures, will join the Board of Directors. Culture Amp will grow its team in Melbourne, San Francisco, New York and London.
Audinate Group Limited has lodged a prospectus with ASIC for an IPO to raise $21 million.
IOT Group (ASX: IOT) has completed a $850,000 equity raising to fund inventory and order flow.
