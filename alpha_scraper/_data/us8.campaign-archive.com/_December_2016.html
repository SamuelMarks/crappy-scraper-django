news briefs

The newly-announced $200 million CSIRO Innovation Fund will support co-investment in spin-out and startup companies, as well as SMEs working on translating research generated in the publicly-funded research sector. The fund is part of the Government's National Innovation and Science Agenda, comprising $70 million in government funding, $30 million from CSIRO's WLAN program, as well as private sector investment. CSIRO CEO Dr Larry Marshall said: "It’s a virtuous cycle of investment in taking our best ideas from bench-top to beta to buyer." Congratulations to CSIRO for nabbing Bill Bartee to manage the Fund. Bartee is currently a Partner at Blackbird Ventures and on the Investment Committee at NAB Ventures, and has invested in some of Australia’s most successful tech companies. He will also be speaking at the inaugural The Deep Dive .
StartupAUS has launched its third Crossroads report on Australia's startup ecosystem. According to the report's snapshot of Australian startups, 17% have at least one female founder, 61% were founded by immigrants or the children of immigrants, and 57% took advantage of government grants during their development.
Telstra , Macquarie Telecom , Nextgen Networks , TPG , Verizon Australia and Sliced Tech have emerged as the first six successful tenderers on the Commonwealth's whole-of-government telecoms services panel . The panel is designed to provide a coordinated provision of services, including internet connection.
The NSW Government has launched a new innovation strategy with initiatives including the opportunity to pitch ideas to government advisers and industry experts through a 'Shark Tank' process; the creation of a Ministerial Innovation Committee; $10 million to grow NSW incubators and accelerators and $3 million of grants for startups in 2016-2017.
Year 10 students at Patterson River Secondary College in Victoria will be given the opportunity to explore technology subjects including electricity systems, solar generation, battery storage, data collection and analysis. The sensors and data analytics equipment to be used by students to measure and monitor energy use will be supplied by Melbourne-based energy companies Hux Connect, Watt Watchers and Greensense.
A continuing increase in digital data traffic is the main source of change in the communications sector, according to the ACMA's new Communications report 2015-16 .
Applications are now open for the second round of the Advance Queensland Innovation Partnerships program .
Battery company Redflow Limited (ASX: RFX) has extended its reach across regional areas and the Northern Territory, with seven new installers approved for its ZCell energy storage solution.
Chinese company Thermal Focus is partnering with CSIRO to market, sell and install CSIRO's low-cost heliostats, field control software and design software. A shared revenue stream will provide funding for further climate mitigation research. The partnership follows the announcement of China's plan to produce 5 GW of concentrating solar thermal electricity by 2020.
Arrow Electronics will distribute Cylance 's cyber attack prevention technology in Australia and New Zealand.
The ANZ Bank has launched its mobile payments system for business customers, BladePay , developed in partnership with ThumbzUp .
Cold gas might be responsible for the formation of giant galaxies - according to discoveries made by CSIRO and US radio telescopes.
The second round of the Federal Government’s Mobile Black Spot program will deliver funding for 266 new mobile base stations around the country.
An Aurionpro company, Cyberinc has opened its new Australian headquarters in Melbourne, and plans to create 50 new jobs. Their base is Goods Shed, Docklands, which is also the home of the Oceania Cyber Security Centre and the CSIRO’s Data61 Cyber Security and Innovation Hub.
The Western Australian government has announced a $2 million investment into the development of business in the West Pilbara region through the creation of the Karratha Enterprise Hub . The hub is an innovative co-working space for startups.
Ride Sharing app Uber has arrived in Tasmania. The launch of the app in Hobart was made possible by the Tasmanian Government’s legislation to allow ride-sharing in the state.
Two big data PhD research projects at Western Sydney University will explore the use of visual analytics: one will consider how visualisation can help to better interpret data in paediatric cancer treatment; the other will examine how virtual and augmented reality can make large-scale visual analytics more accessible. The projects are supported by funding from Oracle , which will enable an extension of existing research currently supported by the Cancer Institute of NSW's Big Data, Big Impact Grant, and also support a new project which will be open to PhD students.
Customers and small businesses in rural, remote and regional Australia have a new advocacy body for communications services. The Regional, Rural and Remote Communications Coalition includes the ACCAN, the National Farmers' Federation, the Country Women's Association of NSW, the Isolated Children's Parents' Association and AgForce Queensland.
Australia now has a Digital Currency Industry Code of Conduct. The Code was launched by the Australian Digital Currency & Commerce Association to help consumers identify digital currency businesses with best practice standards.
The ACCC has issued a draft determination proposing to deny Australian banks the authorisation to bargain with and boycott Apple on Apple Pay. The banks include Commonwealth Bank, Westpac, NAB, Bendigo and Adelaide Bank.
Australia Post is partnering with Digimatic Australia to pilot a program to run the Australian Pavilion on the Lazada Marketplace for Malaysia, Indonesia and Singapore.
The National Narrowband Network Co has partnered with Cisco with "a vision to build smart cities that are more responsive, efficient and sustainable.”

company watch

IoT startup Secure2Go will list on the ASX as it seeks to raise over $4 million through the placement of shares valued at $0.30 per share.
Israeli startup HearMeOut (proposed ASX: HMO) is set to be listed on the ASX on 6 December.
Trade Me has sold its accommodation website Travelbug and its online booking engine BookIt to Travel Booking Services 2016 for a confidential amount.
CIMIC Group has completed the divestment of its remaining 29% investment in Nextgen Group. Total proceeds will be between $190-210 million.
Solar farm proponent Genex has secured credit approval from its debt syndicate of up to $110 million to proceed with the construction of its Kidston solar farm , 270km northwest of Townsville in Queensland.
The company that owns Sky News in Australia, Australian News Channel, has been acquired by News Corp . The company was previously a joint venture between Seven West Media , Nine Entertainment Company and Britain's Sky PLC .

people watch

Among the recipients of the 2016 Victoria Prize for Science and Innovation and the 2016 Victoria Fellowships is Professor Min Gu from RMIT University, who was awarded the $50,000 Victoria Prize for Physical Sciences, recognising his groundbreaking research in optical data storage. The full list of recipients is available here .
NetComm Wireless' (ASX: NTC) CEO and Managing Director David Stewart has announced his retirement, effective from 23 December 2016. Chief Strategy Officer and Executive Director Ken Sheridan has been named Interim CEO.
Robyn Denholm is Telstra's new Chief Operations Officer. Denholm was previously Executive Vice President, Chief Financial Officer and Chief Operations Officer at Juniper Networks. Denholm will be based in Sydney. Acting COO Brendon Riley will return as Group Executive Global Enterprise and Services.
Mike Smith (formerly of ANZ) is the new Chairman of startup incubator York Butter Factory .
Paul Shetler has reflected on his sixteen months leading Australia's Digital Transformation Office . He identifies both an enthusiasm and fear for digital, adding: "Government's biggest challenge in the digital age is to completely upskill the public service so that it is well equipped to deliver the change that's needed."
