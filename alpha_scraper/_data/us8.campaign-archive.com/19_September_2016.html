news briefs

Agtech accelerator program SproutX has been launched in Geelong, VIC. Over the next two years, the VIC Government will provide $1 million through the flagship Agriculture Infrastructure and Jobs Fund to support the establishment of the program and enable a first tranche of Victorian participants. SproutX is a joint venture between the National Farmers Federation and financial services firm Findex.
Australian startup Pod has been named the exclusive global pet tracker for The Secret Life of Pets, a Hollywood animation from the team behind Minions.
The Australian Communications Consumer Action Network has released a mapping tool named 'Get Connected ' for consumers struggling to get an ADSL connection, and outlines steps they can take to get a service.
Tyro will provide up to 10 grants of $25k each to businesses with innovations in improving efficiency and growth in the small and medium enterprise sector under their Smart Growth Grant program .
Swift Networks (ASX:SW1) will provide internet and TV systems to Rio Tinto's Hail Creek mine, Brockman 2 village, and NT Link's Delamere Range facility.
HCL Technologies will provide application management services for WA-based energy provider, Synergy.
Sine has partnered with Hewlett Packard Enterprise Aruba Networks to provide instant Wi-Fi credentials on check-in at airports, hotels, schools, and events.

A scholarship program launched by the WA Government in collaboration with the Autism Academy for Software Quality Assurance, will offer up to 5 scholarships for students on the Autism spectrum so that they have better access to an education in IT.

Body Worn Video technology for general duty police officers is currently being rolled out in NT. The cameras will be equipped with a buffering component which means the moment the device is activated, the 30 seconds prior to activation is automatically recorded. The cameras are capable of recording for up to 72 hours of footage and have a 12 hour battery life.
A new innovation, design, and manufacturing hub will see world-first commercial, road registered, custom Australian solar electric cars built in Brisbane by Clenergy TeamArrow.
15 projects through the Advance Queensland Innovation Partnerships program will receive $9.65 million, with the successful recipients and their project partners contributing a further $15 million. Elevare Energy has been awarded almost $350k to look at improving the management of batteries in solar energy systems and a University of Queensland project led by Associate Professor Stephen Johnston will receive over $600k to develop a living koala genome bank.
Singtel and Optus Business has extended its telecommunication and managed services agreement with ANZ Bank to 2020.
