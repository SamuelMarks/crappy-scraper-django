NewsBriefs

The Report into Open Banking makes 50 recommendations on a regulatory framework; the type of banking data in the scope; privacy and security safeguards for banking customers; the data transfer mechanism and implementation issues, among others. The Government is seeking any further comments on the recommendations up until 23 March 2018.
The Productivity Commission has released its draft report into Competition in the Australian Financial System and is welcoming feedback until late March.
The Australian Human Rights Commission is undertaking a major project on the relationship between human rights and technology . The agency this week said it would look at "innovative ways" to make sure human rights are protected as technology advances.
Sydney-based data feed solution SISS Data Services has forged a partnership with UK technology platform digi.me.
The South Australian Government is partnering with CSIRO to conduct research into the social and environmental impact of the gas industry in the Otway Basin.
Blockchain startup Power Ledger has formed a partnership with US not-for-profit Helpanswers to bring renewable energy trading to North America.
The Federal Government has opened applications for individual grants of up to $7000 for researchers and businesses to expand their work overseas and to increase collaboration in science, technology and innovation . Applications are being accepted until 19 February 2018.
EnergyLab is seeking 16 startups to round out their third cohort. Those chosen receive at least $50k from the The Clean Energy Seed Fund, managed by Artesian Venture Partners and free rent in EnergyLab’s co-working space at UTS's Blackfriars campus.
PowerHealth has partnered with Sunquest Information Systems in its successful bid to provide laboratory-related software solutions for Queensland Health services.
The City of Adelaide has confirmed that TPG Telecom (ASX:TPM) will begin rolling out the Ten Gigabit Adelaide network this month.
Xref Limited (ASX:XF1) has partnered with US recruiting software company, Lever to allow users of Lever’s applicant tracking system to request candidate reference checks directly from their existing platforms.
A robotic window cleaner, a water filter made from agricultural waste, and a plastic made from prawn shells are among the winners of the 2018 BHP Billiton Foundation Science and Engineering Awards for school students.
The newly opened Tonsley Manufacturing Innovation Hub at Flinders University ’s Tonsley campus aims to help accelerate the adoption of advanced digital technologies by SA businesses.
Data61 is collaborating with Chinese self-driving technology company, ZongMu Technology in research aimed at finding a solution which gives autonomous vehicles “human sight” .
NBN Co has tasked satellite provider Speedcast International with delivering satellite services to regional and remote Australia. The 10-year contract is valued at $184 million.
Global entertainment company Technicolor has announced plans to establish a 500-person visual effects centre in Adelaide.
Technology developed by computer scientist Professor Carolyn McGregor AM originally used to track the vitals of astronauts in space , is currently being investigated by the WA Department of Health for its potential to help monitor premature babies .
The search for New Zealand's first Chief Technology Officer is still up for grabs with the first round of 60 applications not quite hitting the mark.
Among other projects shining a light on the experiences of the Stolen Generations in South Australia, Ara Irititja Aboriginal Corporation has received $97,850 to develop software improvements to their database - to give the wider Aboriginal community better access to family history information.

CompanyWatch

Cloud-based analytics and automation startup Nuonic has been bolstered by a strategic investment from KPMG Australia bringing their total investment to $1.3 million.
Biotech startup Elastagen , a University of Sydney spinoff supported by Cicada Innovations, has been acquired by global pharmaceutical company Allergan for $120 million.
Energy Made Clean and Lendlease (ASX:LLC) have been awarded a $6.8 million contract for Western Power’s renewable energy-powered microgrid project for Kalbarri, WA.
Melbourne real estate startup ActivePipe has raised $5.9 million in a Series A round led by NAB Ventures.
Peer-to-peer car sharing network Car Next Door has closed a $5 million Series B round , with the round comprising investment from existing backers Caltex (ASX:CTX), Investible, with a portion raised through equity crowdfunding platform Equitise.
Bygen has received $217K for their agricultural waste conversion technology; and ecoJet Engineering $96,500 for their micro gas turbine engine from the South Australian government.
Satellite tech company Kleos Space has announced a successful $2.2 million seed fund raising and its intention to list on the ASX in July 2018.
Former parliamentary secretary to NSW premier Gladys Berejiklian, Catherine Cusack , has been named the state's first parliamentary secretary for digital inclusion .

PeopleWatch

Best wishes to Kylie Hargraves , who is leaving the NSW Government after eight years. Her achievements include setting up NSW's overseas trade and investment offices and co-writing NSW's first ever International Engagement Strategy. Thanks from Slatterys for your support of Tech23 over the years.
Formerly at Dell EMC, Antoine Acklin has been appointed new CEO of Telco Together . His predecessor Renee Bowker will take up an Executive Director position on the Telco Together Foundation Board.
Georgia Beattie is leaving her Chief Executive role at Startup Victoria to head up REA Group’s (ASX:REA) new working space platform, Spacely.
Harbour ISP has appointed 25-year IT industry veteran Adam Simpson as CEO, replacing founder Charles Tym in the role.
River City Labs has appointed Cristo Pajust as head of their Accelerator Program. Pajust was previously CEO of Results on Air.
