NewsBriefs

Lendlease is to partner with the University of Melbourne on a new innovation precinct for Melbourne. The consortium will develop a $500 million mixed-use project for the University of Melbourne in inner-city Carlton. The 62,000-square-metre development will rise from the former Royal Women's Hospital site on the corner of Swanston and Grattan streets and will include commercial space as well as retail areas, childcare, collaboration space and accommodation for 528 students.
A new partnership between Bosch Australia and Monash University will see an AgTech Launch Pad facility established at Bosch Australia’s Clayton headquarters, with one hectare allocated to a smart farm. The smart farm will include space for cropping trials and early-stage prototype development, enabling the use of artificial intelligence, automation, robotics and advanced sensor technology.
The Smart Energy Council has been formed by the Australian Solar Council and the Energy Storage Council.
The world's largest lithium ion battery is set to be energised for the first time as it enters a phase of regulatory testing in South Australia. Tesla Powerpacks, connected to Neoen's Hornsdale windfarm, have now been fully installed on site ahead of schedule, with testing underway to ensure the battery is optimised and meets AEMO and South Australian Government requirements.
Canberra’s streetlight network will become more efficient, reliable and smart under a new contract with preferred tenderer Electrix for the upgrade of the ACT’s streetlight network.
A tender process for the latest round of the Government’s Mobile Black Spot Program is now underway. $60 million has been committed to 106 priority black spot locations, on top of the $160 million already committed under the first two funding rounds of the program.
NBN Co has pressed pause on the rollout of the Hybrid Coaxial-Fibre network to new areas as it explores new initiatives to raise the standard of service.
NBN Co also signed a new contract with phone and internet providers last week. The Wholesale Broadband Agreement requires NBN Co to meet 'enhanced service performance commitments' for activations and fault restoration, and includes a two-hour service level for hand-offs from NBN Co to RSPs, a new rebate for providers where NBN Co doesn't meet target fault restoration timeframes, and the trial of a new appointments system.
The Federal Government will now be required to pay invoices for contracts worth up to $1 million within 20 calendar days.
The ACCC will allow businesses in the new Eastern Energy Buyers Group to establish a joint energy purchasing group and run joint tender processes for electricity and gas.
Researchers from CSIRO have applied the Transport Network Strategic Investment Tool (TraNSIT) to 98% of agriculture transport across Australia. The results are available in the final TraNSIT agricultural report which was released last week. The TraNSIT tool identifies ways to reduce travel distance and time.
New Zealand's Coda Group has begun to deploy Promapp 's business process management software.
12 teams from nine countries competed in the Robotics Challenge last week, co-hosted by the International Atomic Energy Agency and CSIRO's Data61.
Sydney technology company Precision Tracking is currently facing off against fast food giant Domino's Pizza Enterprises in the Federal Court over the rights to technology utilised in Domino's GPS Driver Tracker.
In excess of one in 10 Australians had their personal information stolen by criminals in an “astonishing” hack on Uber accounts that the ride-sharing company covered up for more than a year. Uber’s CEO conceded that “none of this should have happened”.
According to the recently released 2017 Startup Muster report , over 35% of founders were born overseas, 25% are female (up from 16% in 2014), and almost 50% are not working full-time. Almost 97% of startups had less than 5 people in their founding team. The top startup industries are fintech, education, IoT and artificial intelligence. Going forward, participation in the report will be available throughout the year, according to CEO and Co-founder Monica Wulff.
A five-year contract worth $83.2 million has been awarded to Red Rock Consulting to deploy its enterprise resource planning software at TAFE NSW. The contract commenced in September and will be extended until 2023.
The Allens Hub for Technology, Law and Innovation will sit within UNSW Law to enable a strategic alliance between Allens and UNSW Sydney.
LiveTiles will set up a new global innovation and R&D centre and Asia Pacific headquarters in Geelong, creating over 500 jobs.
MPower (ASX: TAG) is collaborating with Jemena on the development and installation of new residential hybrid power systems in NSW.
Cirralto (ASX: CRO) has signed a Letter of Intent with Swedish-listed company West International AB, a technology provider of merchant payment solutions and services.
The NSW Government has launched the digital.nsw platform.

CompanyWatch

SelfWealth Limited (ASX: SFW) has listed on the ASX following its IPO raise of $7.3 million.
Macquarie Telecom Group Limited (ASX: MAQ) announced its plan to make an off-market bid through its subsidiary Macquarie Cloud Services to acquire all shares of Bulletproof Group Limited (ASX: BPF) for around $17.9 million.
Green energy company Cobalt Blue Holdings (ASX: COB) has raised $2.5 million.

PeopleWatch

Sydney-based robotics company and Tech23 alumni Abyss Solutions has welcomed Lashika Medagoda to the team. Medagoda has research, engineering and field experience with a variety of field robotic systems from the USA and Germany.
ICT body ACS has announced that Yohan Ramasundara will be the ACS President effective from 1 January 2018 for a two-year term. Ramasundara is the Head of Governance for IP Australia after recently returning home from a secondment as the Innovation Manager for International Operations in San Francisco with AusTrade. Ramasundara’s leadership team at ACS for 2018-19 includes Nick Tate, Ian Oppermann, Anthony Wong, Arnold Wong, Andrew Johnson and four branch representatives.
Professor Karen Hapgood will be the new Executive Dean of the Faculty of Science, Engineering and Built Environment at Deakin University. Professor Hapgood is currently the Head of the School of Engineering at Deakin, having joined the University in March. Professor Hapgood joined Deakin from Monash, where she was Head of Chemical Engineering for five years.
David Bonham will join data collaboration company IXUP (ASX: IXU) as Chief Financial Officer. Bonham was previously Bupa Dental Corporation's Managing Director Australia.
Marine scientist and ecotoxicologist Professor Emma Johnston is now President of Science and Technology Australia, taking on the role from outgoing President Emeritus Professor Jim Piper AO. Johnston is Dean of Science at the University of NSW.
Swinburne University has appointed its leadership team for the Innovation Precinct, with Dr John Morrison named Executive Director, Dr Elicia Wong named Director of Research Innovation and Entrepreneurship, and Dr Michelle Carr announced as Program Director of the Innovation Precinct.
