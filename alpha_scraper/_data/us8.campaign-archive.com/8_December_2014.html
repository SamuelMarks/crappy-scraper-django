news briefs

Nkosana Mafico from PeeP Digital has been voted the National Champion at the Global Student Entrepreneur Awards (GSEA), winning a trip to Washington in April to contest the global champion award. Mafico is a third year Business Management student at the University of Queensland who was born in Zimbabwe. PeeP was founded in 2013 by Greg Beaver and Professor David Ross and was accepted into iLab Germinate Program at the University of Queensland and recently was accepted into Telstra’s Muru-D program. PeeP has just launched Funetics , which Mafico believes has the potential to significantly improve English literacy rates.
The Financial System Inquiry final report has been released. Recommendations relating to innovation include: establish a permanent public–private sector collaborative committee to facilitate financial system innovation and enable timely and coordinated policy and regulatory responses; develop a national strategy for a federated-style model of trusted digital identities; clearer graduated payments regulation; facilitate crowdfunding for both debt and equity.
Presto Entertainment , a new service originating from a proposed Foxtel and Seven West Media (ASX: SWM) joint venture, is expected to see subscription on demand television content incorporated into Presto’s existing online subscription library in early 2015. The deal, which is subject to regulatory approval, will see each company hold a 50% interest in the joint venture.
Footprints, developed by Quanticare Technologies , has won the inaugural Janssen Health and Technology Challenge . Footprints sensor system predicts falls in the elderly before they occur. The company will receive a $100,000 grant towards developing Footprints as long as they agree to enter into a collaboration agreement with Janssen.
Following a global competitive tender, The New Payments Platform Australia – an independent body established by the Australian Payments Clearing Association – has selected SWIFT to build and operate new infrastructure for data-rich payments in Australia.
The Telecommunications Universal Service Management Agency has launched a free app for deaf, hearing and speech impaired people, which is now available as part of the National Relay Service .
The ARC Centre for Quantum Computation and Communication Technology , based at The University of New South Wales (UNSW) will receive $5 million over five years from the Commonwealth Bank . The Centre for Quantum Computation and Communication Technology is led by UNSW Scientia Professor and Laureate Fellow Michelle Simmons, who leads a team of more than 170 researchers in six affiliated universities working across both optical and silicon quantum computing and secure communications.
The University of New South Wales School of Photovoltaic and Renewable Energy and RayGen Resources have collaborated to achieve a world record of 40.4% for the highest conversion of sunlight into electricity at an outdoor test facility in the US. RayGen plan to use the results of the tests to work with companies like China Intense Solar to address air pollution.

company watch

Shoes of Prey has raised $5.5 million in Series A funding led by Silicon Valley based Khosla Ventures, Bonobos Co-founder and CEO Andy Dunn, and ThirdLove Co-founders Heidi Zak and Dave Spector, a former partner at Sequoia Capital. The company plans to use the funds to open a second factory, and facilitate a partnership with Nordstrom.
SocietyOne has announced a Series B capital raise from a consortium made up of Consolidated Press Holdings, News Corp Australia and Australian Capital Equity. The Westpac Group-backed venture capital fund Reinventure has also agreed to participate in the latest capital raising following its initial investment in February 2014. Co-founded by CEO and angel investor Matt Symons and Greg Symons, the SocietyOne platform allows people to borrow up to $30,000 unsecured, at a current interest rate of 9.95%.
Tech23 2014 alumni Bluedot Innovation , currently valued at $7.45 million, is set to raise Series A funding of between $1.5 million and $2 million to establish and expand its direct sales resources and enhance marketing and customer acquisition activities.
Uber has raised a $1.2 billion financing round, which it will use to make 'substantial' investments in the Asia Pacific region.

people watch

Peter Binks has commenced his role as the first Director of the Wade Institute . Binks' past roles include CEO at the General Sir John Monash Foundation , Director of Ceramic Fuel Cells Limited and the startup CEO of Nanotechnology Victoria. The Wade Institute for Entrepreneurship is to be based at Ormond College and will offer a one-year University of Melbourne Master of Entrepreneurship degree, as a result of a collaboration between the Faculty of Business and Economics, the School of Engineering and Ormond College. The Wade Institute was made possible from a $10 million gift by Geelong entrepreneur and Ormond alumnus, Peter Wade.
The Australian Computer Society has promoted Chief Operations Officer Andrew Johnson to the role of CEO. Johnson will be replacing CFO and interim CEO Kim Finch.
Bigcommerce has announced Chris Fry, former Head of Engineering at Twitter and Salesforce.com, has joined the company’s board of advisors.
