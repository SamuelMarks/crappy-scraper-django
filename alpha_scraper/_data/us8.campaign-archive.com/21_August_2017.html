LaunchVic has announced five grants totalling $1.4 million to support organisations to deliver services and programs that encourage Victorian migrants and refugees to develop their ideas and startup skills . Funded projects include Free to Feed, an incubator program for food entrepreneurism; Enterprising partnerships - a series of hackathons and a pre-accelerator to consolidate startup ideas run by Cultov8; YGAP - a program to support startups around problems affecting at risk or disadvantaged refugees and/or migrants; Laika Academy - workshops and programs for startups; and Hatch Quarter, which will create a playbook for first generation migrants and refugees to deliver meetups.

The Australian Competition and Consumer Commission (ACCC) has published guidelines for advertising broadband speeds, including clearly identifying typical peak speeds. Communications Alliance has voiced its support for the six best practice principles for broadband speeds as laid out by the ACCC, but is also assessing the 'cost and complexity' added to selling broadband services in Australia.
Drones have been used to record footage from the 20,000 square kilometre Kakadu National Park for the first time. The footage is to be used in Tourism NT's new video .
Imagine Intelligent Materials has partnered with Swinburne University to launch a Graphene Supply Chain CRC-P project . The strategic research initiative has received $1 million in Commonwealth funding. It aims to develop quality assurance throughout supply chains for materials using graphene for the advanced manufacturing sector.
South Australian company Consolidated Power Projects (CPP) will help construct the state's grid-scale battery . CPP will carry out the engineering and construction work to connect the Tesla battery to the Hornsdale wind farm and SA's power grid.
Sydney Trains is upgrading its network with Ciena 's 6500 Packet-Optical Platform. The deployment aims to support critical passenger safety measures.
A new training simulator designed to emulate track cycling at next year's Commonwealth Games is one of three sporting innovations sharing in $300,000 from the Advance Queensland Sport Science Challenge. $100,000 will go to Griffith University to develop the cycling simulation; Fusion Sport will develop a modular suite of athlete health and performance monitoring tools; and McBride Brown and Partners will create an interactive 3D coaching app for athletes.

The trial indicates a reduction in alcohol consumption, illegal drug use and gambling.
A new Anzac Portal aims to promote awareness of Australia's wartime history.

Last week the issue of digital delivery of government services was referred to the Finance and Public Administration References Committee. The inquiry will look particularly at whether planned/existing programs can digitally deliver services with due regard for privacy, security, quality and value; strategies for whole of government digital transformation; and digital project delivery. A report is due to be tabled on 4 December 2017.
