news briefs

ShipIT has partnered with Australia Post to help improve their ecommerce offering. The NZ based company, will enable Australia Post to help online retailers generate shipping labels directly from orders, reduce double entry during the order process, and speed up the shipping process.
The Government has passed the Telecommunications (Interception and Access) Amendment (Data Retention) Act (the Data Retention Act) requiring telco providers to keep metadata about their customers’ communications for two years. The legislation comes into effect on Tuesday 13 October 2015.
CSIRO has released its masterplan; Australia’s Innovation Catalyst for 2015-2020 which outlines how the organisation will become a global collaboration hub and boost Australia’s innovation performance. CSIRO has also created an ON program – an initiative to fast track CSIRO technology and ideas into the market.
Under a new licensing agreement, Australian aerospace company TAE will commercialise CSIRO ’s Guardian Mentor Remote (GMR) wearable technology system, allowing aircraft companies to undertake repairs and maintenance without having to fly in specialist engineers or mechanics. The GMR, which has already been trialled by Boeing and Aviation Australia works by connecting experts with onsite operators for real-time assistance using a headset and glasses.
The Australian Competition and Consumer Commission (ACCC) has announced in its draft decision to approve the nbn’s proposal to migrate Optus ’ HFC subscribers to the new multi-technology broadband, while parts of Optus’ HFC network are integrated into the NBN. The ACCC is now seeking submissions from nbn and interested parties in relation to its draft determination before making a final decision in August/September 2015.
CSIRO has signed a multi-million dollar agreement with the Breakthrough Prize Foundation to be involved in the largest ever data-sharing science project. Funding the search for life in the Universe project is cosmologist Stephen Hawking, Astronomer Royal Lord Martin Rees, SETI pioneer Frank Drake, Ann Druyan and Internet investor Yuri Milner. The search will use 25% of the 64-m Parkes radio telescopelescope’s time for five years from July 2016 and will return CSIRO the cost of operating the telescope during the observations as well as contributing to an upgrade of the data systems used for this and other science.
A $6 million Electron Microscopy Facility has opened at the Australian Nuclear Science and Technology Organisation to enable scientists to conduct research into nuclear materials and radiation, in particular the behaviour of materials for use in extreme environments.
The Bureau of Meteorology in Australia has awarded Cray a contract worth up to US$59 million to provide a new supercomputer to supercharge weather warnings and forecasts. The first phase of the contract is expected to be completed in 2016. Funding for the new supercomputer was announced by the Australian Government in the 2014-2015 Federal Budget .
ABC has announced that their retail strategy will move to eCommerce, meaning the closure of 50 ABC shop stores and 78 ABC centres. Up to 300 staff members will be impacted by this transition.
Mint Payments (ASX: MNW) has signed an agreement with eWay , whereby eWay will license Mint’s ‘card present’ capability to distribute their own mobile point of sales solution allowing customers to use a full spectrum of card payments.

company watch

Off-the-plan property marketplace startup Investorist has raised $2.5 million in Series A funding led by Netspace Co-founder Stuart Marburg, Redflex CEO Paul Clarke, Watersun Properties Founder Benni Aroni, and a number of undisclosed private investors. Salta Group , which led the startup’s $1.7 million seed funding round in 2014, also participated.
AirTree Ventures has led funding for both eCommerce startup Brosa , and rostering software startup Ento to the tune of $2 million Series A, and $1.2 million respectively.
iiNet (ASX: IIN) shareholders have voted in favour of the scheme of arrangement under which TPG Telecom Limited (ASX: TPM) will acquire 100% of the share capital of IIN that it does not already own.
Health.com.au has been sold to Geelong headquartered member-based, non-profit, insurance company, GMHBA . iSelect Limited (ASX: ISU) is to receive $42.1 million owing to it under a loan facility it has entered into under with GMHBA will acquire health.com.au Pty Ltd. ISelect and GMHBA have also entered into a multi-year agreement for iSelect to distrubute GMHBA's insurance products.

people watch

CSIRO has appointed Brendan Dalton as its new Chief Information Officer. Dalton was previously CIO at both the Clean Energy Regulator and former Department of Climate Change .
The Queensland Government is on the search for a Privacy Commissioner. Applications close Friday 31 July 2015.
