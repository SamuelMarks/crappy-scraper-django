NEWS BRIEFS
Scientists from Australian National University , including professor Daniel Shaddock, have designed satellite instruments in collaboration with NASA and Germany for the launch of the GRACE Follow-on mission , which will measure gravity from space in order to study the changes in water levels on Earth.
It was great to see Tech23 Alumni Abyss Solutions, who received $759k for their underwater robots, Clevertar ($470k) for their virtual agents and SmileyScope ($236k) for their use of VR to help give children needles amongst the 13 businesses who received grants in the latest round of $6.6 million from the Accelerating Commercialisation element of the Entrepreneurs’ Programme.
Ask Nicely , a platform for customer experience feedback backed by Blackbird Ventures last year, won the Emerging Company of the Year at the NZ 2018 Hi-Tech Awards . Invenco , which provides self-service payment solutions beat out other finalists – Pushpay, Vend, Serko and Straker to take home the Company of the Year. The Maori Innovation Award was won by Straker Translations. Claudia Batten was inducted into the NZ Hi-Tech Hall of Fame on the night.
The Federal Government has endorsed many of the Innovation and Science Australia’s 2030 plan’s recommendations in the 2018-19 Budget. These include almost $2 billion in long-term funding for national research infrastructure, $4.5 million to support Women in STEM and $65 million to establish the Consumer Data Right to support ethical use of data and protection of individual privacy.
Swinburne University of Technology has announced a partnership with Tableau Software to co-design and launch a hands-on course in business analytics and data visualisation.
Partnering with Chrysos Corporation, CSIRO have developed an x-ray technology for detecting and analysing unseen gold . Australian mining services company, Ausdrill, will be the first to adopt this technology.
CSIRO’s Data61 has been appointed to perform the role of a Data Standards Body for the Federal Government’s Consumer Data Right, which will give Australians greater control over their own data.
CSIRO’s Data61 is to form the Sixth Wave Alliance to develop a national robotics R&D strategy and create the critical mass required to address large-scale Australian and international challenges using robotics technologies.
The Australian Research Council is to launch an Industrial Transformation Training Centre for Advanced Technologies in Rail Track Infrastructure, headquartered at the University of Wollongong.
Griffith University, University of New South Wales, University of Technology Sydney and Sydney University have been selected to collaborate on defence projects with universities in the United States under the US Department of Defence Multidisciplinary University Research Initiative , to develop integrated quantum sensing and control for high fidelity qubit operations.
Australia’s first Defence Cooperative Research Centre for Trusted Autonomous Systems, established by the Next Generation Technologies fund, has been officially launched in Brisbane, enabling Australia’s industry and research sectors to develop autonomous technologies to maintain the Australian Defence Force’s capability advantage.
A trial of new ticketing technology , mobile Myki will be underway soon in Victoria. All going well by mid-2019, Victorians may be able to use their phones to pay for public transport.
Despite Space Agency Head Dr. Megan Clark’s earlier suggestion that Canberra is the logical choice for the Agency, Australia’s first astronaut, Dr Paul Scully-Power AM, will lead NSW’s case to locate the nation’s first space agency in Sydney.
COMPANY WATCH
SafetyCulture has raised $60 million in its Series C funding round led by New York investment firm Tiger Global Management, with previous investors also contributing, including Blackbird Ventures, Index Ventures, Morpheus Ventures and Atlassian co-founder Scott Farquhar.
Agtech startup OneCrop took home first prize at the Future Food Asia Awards, receiving US$100,000 in prize money.
Lithium-ion battery technology company UltraCharge (ASX: UTR) has raised $2.5 million via a share placement.
Sydney-based property management startup MadeComfy has raised $6 million in funding for international expansion, the majority of which came from Investec Australia.
Vervoe , a platform for testing and developing interview skills between employers and candidates, has raised $4.6 million in funding led by SEEK (ASX: SEK). Vervoe and SEEK are now in discussions for a strategic partnership.
ClearVue Technologies (ASX: CPV) has raised $5 million at its Initial Public Offering to commercialise its solar PV technology.
BluGlass (ASX: BLG) has raised $9.2 million from institutional, professional and sophisticated investors. BluGlass, which is developing and commercialising semiconductor technology, has announced that industry veteran Stephe Wilks is to join their Board.
Concept Safety Systems (ASX: CSS) has acquired Locatrix Communications .
PEOPLE WATCH
Lisa Frazier , who was previously Chief Digital Officer at the Commonwealth Bank of Australia and moved to the US a couple of years ago, will head up Well Fargo’s Innovation Group in San Francisco.​
Silicon-Valley based and former Facebook technologist Dr. Paul Herz has been appointed as Adjunct Professor for the faculty of Business and Law at Curtin University to provide guest lectures in entrepreneurship and engineering.
Mark Stickells has been appointed as the new Director of the Pawsey Supercomputing Centre in Perth, WA. Stickells’ most recent role was Director of Innovation and Industry Engagement at The University of Western Australia.​
Pureprofile (ASX: PPL) has appointed Melinda Sheppard as CFO, effective 25 June 2018. Sheppard is currently Senior Commercial Finance Manager at News Corp Australia.
The Open Data Institute Australian Network has appointed Tech23 Alumnus Jamie Leach as the Institute’s new CEO.
UNSW has appointed Marcus Marten-Coney , who was previously at BlueChilli, to lead its Founders 10x accelerator program.
Cameron King , Managing Director of digital revenue at News Corp Australia, has been appointed as IAB Australia Chair.
