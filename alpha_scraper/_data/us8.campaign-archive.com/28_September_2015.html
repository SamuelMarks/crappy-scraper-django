news briefs

Chief Scientist Professor Ian Chubb has launched a new report by the Australian Council of Learned Academics (ACOLA) entitled Technology and Australia’s Future . ACOLA Council member and President of the Australian Academy of Science Professor Andrew Holmes said “Australia’s response to new technologies needs to be agile, adaptive and optimistic – new technologies can offer Australia great benefits; it is up to the nation to seize the opportunity.” The comprehensive report makes 18 major findings, including the disruptive role that new information and communication technologies, especially data analytics, can have on existing business – from transportation to mining, healthcare and education. Other findings focus on the skills that Australia needs to invent and harness new technologies and develop competitive strengths that outlive the resources boom.
Under the Australian Labour Party 's new policy, startup loans will be offered to 2,000 students and graduates each year that want to establish a startup within a university-based (or similar) accelerator; 2,000 Startup Entrepreneurial Visas will be offered annually to global entrepreneurs looking to establish themselves in Australia; 2,000 Graduate Startup Entrepreneurial Visas will be made available annually for university graduates who have a credible and genuine startup business idea; and there will be the introduction of a trial of an ‘AusGov Challenge Platform’ which aims to provide a single portal for government agencies to submit challenges to which the public can respond.
Peter MacCallum Cancer Centre has replaced its five aging billing systems with PowerHealth Solution ’s enterprise patient billing system. The system will be used to recover an estimated $1.4 million per annum in rejected claims and delayed or uncollected payments amongst other things.
Telstra (ASX: TLS) has been selected for a three year multi-million dollar contract as an exclusive partner for Medibank ’s telecommunications services.
ASG (ASX: ASZ) has signed a five year, $23.8 million contract with the Department of Infrastructure and Regional Development to deliver Infrastructure Services, Desktop Services and end-to-end Service Management across the Department’s ICT environment.
The University of Melbourne ’s entrepreneurship program, the Melbourne Accelerator Program and The Nasdaq Entrepreneurial Centre are partnering to facilitate student exchange, alumni networking and future program development.
The University of Melbourne has launched a new website, Pursuit edited by Phillip Gardner as part of the University's new branding campaign, spotlighting the collaborations or “collisions” that lead to research achievements. Pursuit will be updated daily to dwell on future research activities of the University.
Tech23 Alumni Smart Sparrow has announced a partnership with Open Learning to supply educators with access to course-building software.

company watch

REFFIND Limited (ASX: RFN) has acquired employee recognition platform WooBoard Pty Ltd . The deal will see $1.25 million of REFFIND scrip, or shares, issued six months after completion of the acquisition, and an earn out of between 900,000 and 2.9 million REFFIND shares, assessed on a sliding scale 12 months from completion.
Melbourne medical school graduates Hon Weng Chong and Andrew Lin have announced a US $5 million round of funding for their connected medical kit, CliniCloud . The funding was led by Chinese internet company Tencent and Ping An Venture . In addition to the funding news, CliniCloud has announced a partnership with retail company Best Buy , which will carry its product in the United States and Canada.
Vocus Communications (ASX: VOC) and M2 Group Ltd (ASX: MTU) have announced a merger implementation agreement. Under the deal M2 shareholders would receive 1.625 Vocus shares for each M2 share. Furthermore if the deal is approved, M2’s CEO Geoff Horth will serve as the combined entity’s CEO, while Vocus CEO James Spenceley will sit on the board as Executive Director.
Melbourne FinTech startup Clover has announced the raising of a seed funding round led by superannuation fund Equipsuper for an undisclosed amount.
