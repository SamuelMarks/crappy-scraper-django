In your Watch this week: StartupAUS releases Crossroads 2017 report; new exec appointments for Slack and Elabor8; Blackbird Ventures & others invest in Mentorloop; and much more!

NewsBriefs

StartupAUS' Crossroads 2017 report has been released. In the foreword, Co-CEO of Atlassian Scott Farquhar writes that when it comes to talent, Australia is "falling behind", and that "to become a world-leader in innovation, Australia must attract world-leading talent." The report's recommendations including an expansion of skilled worker visas to include digital skills and startup needs; that the Federal Government should pay the R&D Tax Incentive on a quarterly basis; an expansion of startup precincts; and an extension of the digital technologies curriculum.
Western Australia has announced a $16.7 million New Industries Fund to support a range of initiatives , including Innovation Hubs and Science Industry Fellowships - all designed to foster collaboration between industry, academia and government. The Joondalup Hub is one of these initiatives, and has been provided with $800,000 to bring together the public, private and research sectors to work on cyber security.
The world's largest lithium ion battery has been launched at Hornsdale Power Reserve in South Australia. Tesla powerpacks, connected to Neoen's Hornsdale windfarm, are operational and delivering power to the National Energy Market. The battery was delivered well ahead of Tesla CEO Elon Musk's "100 days or it's free" deadline, coming in at 63 days after the grid connection-agreement was signed.
Imagine Intelligent Materials is partnering with NUVIZ - an American technology company that claims it released the first fully-integrated Head-Up Display (HUD) for motorcyclists - to deliver 'actionable information' for those working in infrastructure including dams, roads and mines by fitting the NUVIZ/Imagine HUD to construction helmets.
Victoria is set to become a "living laboratory" - with the announcement of the Gen V project by Murdoch Children's Research Institute. The $24.5 million initiative in partnership with the Paul Ramsay Foundation will invite parents of all babies born in 2020 and 2021 across the state to participate. The aim is to create a holistic picture of Victorian children’s health and wellbeing.
An Australian miniature satellite was successfully launched from the US last week. The buccaneer cube satellite was developed by Defence and the University of New South Wales. Communications have been established with the satellite, which was launched from Vandenberg Air Force Base in California on a Delta-II rocket. Buccaneer is designed to improve understanding of the outer atmosphere, especially the Ionosphere.
Macquarie Telecom (ASX: MAQ) is opening a new network operations centre (NOC) in Sydney's CBD this month.
Communications Alliance have announced a new industry code to help with the accuracy of public number customer data. A revised version of the Industry Code C555:2017 Integrated Public Number Database (IPND) (and its associated Guideline G619:2017 IPND Data) has been registered by the industry regulator, the Australian Communications and Media Authority (ACMA).
Perth is one of three cities worldwide chosen to host a trial of a new type of electric-powered autonomous vehicle. The trial will be run by RAC WA in partnership with the State Government, and will involve vehicles called Autonoms - produced by French company NAVYA.
Elenium Automation has opened its global headquarters in Tullamarine, Victoria. The company provides self-service and automation technologies for the aviation sector.
Feedback is sought on a discussion paper detailing Telstra's proposed variation to the NBN Migration Plan to facilitate the rollout of fibre to the curb technology. Submissions are due by 5 January 2018.
The Australian Federal Police (AFP) have acknowledged the findings in the Commonwealth Ombudsman's inspection report regarding access to a journalist's telecommunications data without a journalist information warrant. The report made a recommendation to the AFP that it reviews its approach to metadata awareness and training.
Tech23 alumni Bluedot has partnered with Transurban to help motorists to automatically track and pay for tolls on their smartphone. The LinktGO app uses Bluedot's location services technology.
The world's largest residential virtual power plant is being trialled in Canberra. Over 250 Canberra households and businesses are taking part in the trial, which is demonstrating Tech23 alumni Reposit 's software system and uses batteries from the ACT Government's Next Generation Energy Storage program.
Victoria will be home to the MedTech Actuator program's national headquarters, with the program offering startups up to $2.7 million over 15 months. Backed by Artesian, the Actuator will run programs to help up to 40 startups each year to commercialise.
A Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) to support the greater use of electric vehicles has been signed by mayors and ministers from South Australia, Victoria, Western Australia, the ACT, and the cities of Adelaide, Hobart, and Darwin. The MoU was signed with the Electric Vehicle Council.
Canberra drivers beware - the ACT has rolled out electronic chalking or licence plate recognition technology (LPR). LPR is a vehicle mounted camera system which enables parking officers to capture and record images of parked vehicles to determine if they have overstayed the time limit or parked unlawfully.
1414 Degrees' Gas TESS project is a recipient of the South Australian Government's Renewable Technology Fund. The company has been granted $1.6 million to develop a biogas storage solution with SA Water.
Datacom has started construction on its $22 million customer care operations, training and IT hub within TAFE SA's Tea Tree Gully campus. The SA Government is contributing over $5 million towards the centre through its Economic Investment Fund.
Sky and Space Global (ASX: SAS) has integrated cyber security solutions from Check Point Software Technologies into the SAS network.
Charles Darwin University (CDU) and the Centre for Appropriate Technology will receive NT Government funding for renewable energy R&D projects. The Centre for Appropriate Technology will receive $388,576 to develop an investment grade renewable resources database to help scaling of renewables in the Territory. CDU will receive funding for projects including maintenance of remote hybrid mini-grid systems ($466,380); modelling of future solar energy production ($231,316); and bioenergy for PV hybrid electricity generation ($413,197).
Swedish designer and manufacturer of prefabricated data centre buildings Flexenclosure has entered into a strategic partnership with Australian telecommunications provider Virtutel, with the intent to build up to 20 data centres across Australia. The first of these facilities will be launched in Albury, New South Wales, followed by more in regional cities across the country.
Equatorial Launch Australia (ELA) and Gumatj Corporation have secured land in North East Arnhem Land to establish ' Australia's first commercial space centre .' The Northern Territory Government signed a Project Facilitation Agreement with ELA, Gumajt and Developing East Arnhem Limited to support the first phase of the Arnhem Space Centre. Construction is expected to start early next year, pending further regulatory processes and environmental assessment. The first launch is slated for the end of 2018.
The Federal Government has formally directed the ACCC to commence an inquiry into digital platform providers such as Facebook and Google. The ACCC’s inquiry will look at the effect that digital search engines, social media platforms and other digital content aggregation platforms are having on competition in media and advertising services markets.
Michelle Rowland MP has questioned the timing of the NBN Co's announcement about delaying HFC connections to the national broadband network, revealing a timeline that shows problems were first identified two years ago.
Mobecom Limited (ASX: MBM) has signed a technology partnering agreement with Singapore-based payment service group Network for Electronic Transfers (NETS), enabling users to earn and spend airBux digital currency at NETS' QR code enabled terminals.
The ACCC has announced SamKnows as the successful tenderer to undertake its $6.5 million broadband speeds monitoring program, which will see broadband speeds recorded across 4000 Australian homes over the next four years.
Australian blockchain startups will now have access to part of a US$70 million ($92 million) investment fund to fuel development of local companies, provided by the company behind one of the world’s largest cryptocurrencies, NEM.
The winner of StartCon's 2017 Pitch Competition is CheckBox . Two startups tied to win StartCon's accelerator/incubator of the year - SheStarts and Tech Ready Women .

CompanyWatch

Mentorloop has closed a $725,000 oversubscribed seed round , with funding from Blackbird Ventures, rampersand and Tempus Partners.
Telstra Ventures has made a strategic investment in Headspin, a Silicon Valley based mobile application testing company.
Airtree Ventures is leading New Zealand company Thematic's seed round, which raised $1.75 million.

PeopleWatch

Arturo Arrarte is Slack's new Head of Growth - Asia Pacific. Arrarte was previously Salesforce's Director - Commercial Sales.
John Sullivan has been named as Elabor8’s new CEO . Sullivan was previously CTO at Vicinity Centres.
Q-Ctrl has appointed Robert Love as CTO. His previous positions include CTO of REFFIND and CTO - Innovation and Digital Ventures at PwC Australia, as well as General Manager - Product and Engineering at Pollenizer.
Kate Burleigh is now Country Manager for Amazon Alexa Skills. Burleigh was previously Intel ANZ's Managing Director for five years.
The Federal Government has announced senior appointments as part of its national security strategy . Mike Burgess has been named Director-General designate of the Australian Signals Directorate (ASD). Current ASD Director Paul Taloni will move to a senior position within the Office of National Assessments. Nick Warner AO PSM has also been announced as the new Director-General of the Office of National Assessments and Director-General designate of National Intelligence. Warner will drive the establishment of the Office of National Intelligence.
The recently formed Office of the Victorian Information Commissioner has welcomed Sally Winton as Public Access Deputy Commissioner and Rachel Dixon as Privacy and Data Protection Deputy Commissioner. The five year terms have already commenced. Dixon was most recently Head of Identity at the Digital Transformation Agency.
The inaugural NT Awards for Digital Excellence were held last week, with Adam Smith of Matera Technology taking out the NT Government Digital Innovation Award. The Minister's Gold Award was awarded to Georgina Davison of Alice Springs Public Library.
Staff at Huffington Post Australia have found themselves suddenly made redundant after the joint venture between HuffPost and Fairfax collapsed. Read the responses from Tory Maguire , Mat Whitehead and Luke Cooper .
